## 1. Introduction

### Overview
Thirteen years after the events of the first game, FBI agents Saga Anderson and Alex Casey arrive in the sleepy town of Bright Falls in rural Washington to investigate the murder of a former agent who disappeared at the same time as the famous crime fiction author, Alan Wake. The murder appears to be linked to a series of earlier ritual murders in the area. The agents soon realize that the case is anything but mundane as their investigations begin to uncover manuscript pages that predict the events happening around them, and the world begins to change to fit the story.

Compared to its predecessor, *Control*, and the original *Alan Wake*, *Alan Wake II* leans far more heavily into survival horror tropes and gameplay. Rather than a fast-paced action-adventure or power fantasy, it presents a tense, resource-focused horror narrative.

---

### The Story So Far
*This recap assumes you have either played the original Alan Wake or do not intend to do so, as it contains major spoilers for the first game.*

Alan Wake is the successful author of a series of thrillers starring a detective named **Alex Casey**. After writing six novels in seven years, Alan felt burned out and killed off the character in his final novel, *The Sudden Stop*. Following a two-year writer's block that strained his marriage, his wife, **Alice**, booked a holiday for them in Bright Falls in the Pacific Northwest. 

Alice secretly contacted a local psychiatrist, **Emil Hartman**, who ran a clinic called Cauldron Lake Lodge specializing in treating artists. Upon arriving, Alan went to get their cabin keys from **Carl Stucky**, but instead met a mysterious old woman who directed him to a cabin on an island in Cauldron Lake. Shortly after arriving, the lights went out, Alice was dragged into the lake by a dark force, and Alan dove in after her.

Alan awoke a week later at the wheel of a crashed car with no memory of the past week. He began finding pages of a manuscript called *Departure* that predicted his actions and described an entity known as the **Dark Presence**. He discovered that the cabin's island had actually vanished in a volcanic eruption in 1970. 

A kidnapper demanded the manuscript in exchange for Alice. Alan's agent, **Barry Wheeler**, arrived to assist him. When Alan met the kidnapper at Mirror Peak, the Dark Presence killed the kidnapper and cast Alan back into the lake. Hartman rescued Alan and took him to Cauldron Lake Lodge, attempting to gaslight him into believing Alice's death was a psychotic break. Alan escaped during a patient riot started by the **Anderson brothers** (Tor and Odin, former members of the band *Old Gods of Asgard*).

Alan discovered that Cauldron Lake is a threshold to the **Dark Place**, a nightmare dimension. The Dark Presence had taken over the body of **Barbara Jagger** and forced Alan to write *Departure* to set it free. Alan managed to write his own escape into the plot. Guided by **Cynthia Weaver**, the "lady of the light" who kept the town's power station lit, Alan reached the Cauldron Lake threshold. Using a switch called the **Clicker**, he destroyed the Dark Presence's physical form. To ensure Alice swam to freedom, Alan chose to remain trapped in the Dark Place.

### Walkthrough Structure
*Alan Wake II* split the narrative into two parallel stories:
*   **Return**: Saga Anderson's real-world investigation in Bright Falls, Watery, and Cauldron Lake.
*   **Initiation**: Alan Wake's attempts to write his way out of the Dark Place.

Around the halfway point of the game, players gain the ability to freely switch between Saga and Alan's stories. This walkthrough follows the parallel stories in a natural progression:
1.  **Invitation** & **The Heart**: The start of Saga's storyline.
2.  **Late Night** through **Haunting**: The start of Alan's storyline and the switch back to Saga.
3.  **Local Girl** through **Summoning**: The middle section of Saga's storyline.
4.  **We Sing** through **Gone**: The conclusion of Alan's storyline.
5.  **Summoning (Part 2)** through **Come Home**: The conclusion of Saga's storyline.

Throughout this walkthrough, key collectibles are tracked using the following abbreviations:
*   **MP**: Manuscript Page (Saga)
*   **CS**: Cult Stash (Saga)
*   **LB**: Alex Casey Lunch Box (Saga)
*   **NP**: Nursery Rhyme Puzzle (Saga)

## 2. Gameplay & Combat

### Gameplay Overview
*   **Return (Saga's Story)**: Set across Cauldron Lake, Bright Falls, and Watery. It is a semi-open world that opens up further as floodwaters recede. Saga utilizes the **Mind Place**, a mental space where she organizes clues on a **Case Board** and profiles suspects to progress investigations.
*   **Initiation (Alan's Story)**: Set in the Dark Place, styled after a nightmarish version of New York City. Traversal requires using the **Light Shift** mechanic to transfer light between sources and rewrite the local geometry in the **Writer's Room** by matching acquired plot elements to specific scenes.

### Combat Mechanics
Combat is resource-heavy. Damage dealt to enemies is significantly reduced until their **Darkness Shield** is burned away using your flashlight boost. 
*   Flashlight boosting consumes a "charge." A standard battery pack contains four charges (five when upgraded).
*   Saga's default pistol is weak, but she quickly unlocks more powerful weapons. Alan's default revolver is stronger, but ammo for his flare gun is extremely rare.
*   Taken enemies do not de-aggro or despawn when entering Safe Havens (such as Break Rooms); they will wait outside for you.
*   Avoiding combat when possible is highly recommended to conserve ammunition and batteries.

---

### Enemies Guide

#### Saga's Enemies (The Taken)
*   **Taken (Basic Grunts)**: Melee enemies that charge directly at Saga. Burn their shield and target their weak spots (head or the glowing **Source**). Cultist variants wear masks that block a single headshot.
*   **Taken Thrower**: Rapidly teleports and throws hatchets from range. They split into two weaker images when flashed. The hunting rifle is highly effective for taking them out from a distance before they aggro.
*   **Taken Bruiser**: Tough, slow enemies armed with heavy two-handed weapons. Evade their slow strikes and counter with shotgun blasts to the head at close range.
*   **Taken Diver**: Toughest non-boss Taken. They consist of two merged upper bodies. They attack with ranged shockwaves, can submerge to become invulnerable, and grapple you in close quarters (which costs one Hand Flare to escape). Engage with the rifle or crossbow at range, then switch to a shotgun.
*   **Taken Wolves**: Fast, dark, and difficult to target in the undergrowth. Use a shotgun to deal wide damage.

#### Alan's Enemies (Shadows)
*   **Hostile Shadows**: Most shadows in the Dark Place are simple illusions that dissipate when approached or flashed. Hostile Shadows, however, are physical, attack in melee, and sometimes throw projectiles. Burn their darkness shield and shoot them in the head with the revolver.
*   **Fast Shadows**: High-speed variations first encountered later in the game. Hard to track; use the flare gun to deal with them quickly.

#### DLC Enemies
*   **Painted (The Lake House)**: Special enemies that emerge from walls. They are immune to conventional damage; use a **Flashbang** to stun them and escape.

## 3. General Survival Tips

*   **Difficulty**: Normal difficulty in *Alan Wake II* is highly challenging. Do not hesitate to lower the difficulty if you find the combat frustrating, as there are no difficulty-specific trophies.
*   **Saving**: Save manually at thermos flasks in Break Rooms whenever possible. Do not rely solely on autosaves.
*   **Inventory Management**: Upgrade your inventory space as soon as possible. Excess items can be stored in **Shoeboxes** located in Break Rooms. 
    *   Use consumables regularly to free up space. 
    *   Crossbow bolts only stack 4 per slot, making them inventory hogs.
    *   First Aid Kits provide full heals but take too long to use during active combat. Keep them stored in Shoeboxes to use as post-combat heals inside Break Rooms.
*   **Supplies**: Resource spawns in containers are semi-randomized based on what you currently lack. If you are low on batteries or ammo, containers are more likely to yield them. Hoarding resources can cause containers to spawn empty.
*   **Upgrades**: Upgrade fragments are scarce. The best upgrades are:
    *   *Saga*: Reload speed and health siphon for the sawed-off shotgun, two-shot and magnetic draw for the crossbow, and shield-bypass for the hunting rifle.
    *   *Alan*: The "It's Personal" Word of Action upgrade, as Hostile Shadows frequently ambush you in close quarters.
*   **Charms**: Equip a **Coffee Mug Charm** before major fights to act as a second life. They break upon preventing death. Note that Alan's chapters do not contain boss fights.
*   **Break Room Reset**: Saga's wilderness combat encounters are often randomized. If an encounter drains too many resources, reload your previous Break Room save to reset the spawns.

## 4. Saga's Story: Return

### Return 0: The Cult
Following the opening cinematic narration, you gain control of a naked man emerging from Cauldron Lake. Walk forward, following the path deeper into the forest to the right while ignoring the distant lights of hikers who quickly disappear. Follow the path up the slope until you reach a clearing. Jog along the path and head up another slope. As you pass an abandoned shelter on your left, walk toward the lights in the distance. Sinister figures wearing deer masks will emerge from the shadows. They proclaim themselves to be the **Cult of the Tree**, subdue you, and tie you to an altar. They carve out your heart, fleeing only when disturbed by the returning hikers.

*   **Trophy Unlocked**: **Hunting Season** (automatic for completing the chapter).

---

### Return 1: Invitation

#### Cauldron Lake (First Visit)
**Return 1 Collectibles Checklist**:
*   **Maps**: 1 (Cauldron Lake Map)
*   **Manuscript Pages**: 1 (MP 1: "Message")
*   **Cult Stashes**: 1 (CS 1: Mobile Home Stash)
*   **Lunch Boxes**: 5 (LBs 1–5)
*   **Deer Heads**: 1 (Deer Head 1)

##### Get to the Crime Scene
FBI Agents Saga Anderson and Alex Casey arrive at the Cauldron Lake Campground to investigate the ritual murder. Your starting inventory includes a service pistol, 12 rounds of 9mm ammunition, and **Logan's Charm** (equipped, increases maximum health; unlocks the **Rustic Charm** trophy).

Walk forward to the campground noticeboard where Casey is standing. Examine the noticeboard to obtain the **Cauldron Lake Map** (unlocks the **Nightmare Territory** trophy). 

Follow Casey along the path to meet Deputy Mulligan at the barricade gate. Continue through the gate and down the slope to the crime scene, where Deputy Thornton provides a briefing. Nightingale, the victim, has had his heart removed. Examine Nightingale's corpse on the table to gather clues: bruising on the wrists, the chest trauma, and the heart left in a tray next to him. 

##### The Mind Place
Interact with the **Mind Place** menu. Place the gathered Cauldron Lake Murder Scene clues onto your Case Board under the *Murder at Cauldron Lake* line of investigation to open up new questions: *The Victim?* and *The Killer(s)?*. 

Exit the Mind Place and speak to Thornton to learn that the victim was Robert Nightingale, a former FBI agent. Re-examine the table to gather further clues:
1.  Blood spatter on the table.
2.  Footprints on the ground near the "2" and "3" markers.
3.  The cool box and beer bottles by the "4" marker.
4.  The tripod on the ground.

Exhaust your conversation options with Casey. Return to the Mind Place and place your new clues on the Case Board. The blood spatter and footprint clues go against *The Victim?*, and the cooler and tripod clues go against *The Killer(s)?*, unlocking the *Premeditated or Spontaneous?* and *Victim Came From...?* questions. Go to Saga's profiling desk in the center of the room and select the **Missing Person** and **Ritual Murder** topics for Nightingale. Saga deduces that Nightingale came from the lake. Place this new clue against *Victim Came From...?* on the Case Board.

##### Cauldron Lake Exploration
Before following Casey, explore the area to gather collectibles:
1.  **Lunch Box (1/21)**: Climb down the shortcut stairs to the south. Head left along the lower trail, and follow a side path to a viewing platform. Search the corner of the platform to find the lunch box containing two manuscript fragments (unlocks the **Hidden by the Trees** trophy).
2.  **Cult Stash (1/22)**: Walk to the clearing east of the crime scene to find an abandoned mobile home trailer. Just outside, next to a picnic table, is a locked chest. The note on the lid reads: *Wash hands, Take chicken out of Fridge, Take a nap*. Go inside the trailer and note the painted symbols:
    *   Above the bathroom sink (wash hands): Two overlapping triangles pointing up and down.
    *   On the side of the fridge (fridge): Two triangles pointing horizontally away from each other.
    *   On the bedroom wall (nap): Two overlapping triangles pointing vertically.
    *   Enter these symbols in order on the combination lock of the chest outside to unlock it (unlocks the **Secret Stashes** trophy). The stash contains 9mm ammunition and a **Trauma Pad**.
3.  **Deer Head (1/12)**: Go into the trailer's back lounge to find a mounted deer head. Interact with it to pet it.
4.  **Lunch Box (2/21)**: Go behind the mobile home trailer and climb the ledge to the north. Follow the trail north to find a lunch box containing one manuscript fragment.
5.  **Lunch Box (3/21)**: Backtrack to the main path and follow Casey northwest. At the fork in the trail, take the right path toward the campsite. Search the corner of the campsite near the wooden fence to find a lunch box containing three manuscript fragments.
6.  **Lunch Box (4/21)**: Continue past the Witch's Hut, cross the bridge to the left, and enter a small clearing. Read the entries in the diary notebook on the table, open the supply box next to it, and search the edge of the clearing to find a lunch box containing one manuscript fragment.
7.  **Manuscript Page (1/56 - "Message")**: Backtrack across the bridge and follow Casey east. Near the Witch's Ladle sign, you will find the path blocked by branches. Search the ground here to pick up the **Message** manuscript page (unlocks the **Not the Last** trophy).

Return to the Mind Place and review the *Message* page. Place the two new clues against the *Victim Came From...?* and *Anything Else Here?* questions. Profile Nightingale again under the topic *The Page* to realize he had something hidden inside him.

##### Returning to the Car
Follow Casey back to the crime scene and speak to Thornton. He will hand you the **Shortcut Key**. Use the key to unlock the iron gate just to the right of the General Store path. Follow the path up the slope:
1.  **Lunch Box (5/21)**: When you see the FBC monitoring station on your left, go behind it and climb the rocky ledge to find a lunch box containing two manuscript fragments.

Rejoin Casey at the path's end, read the note left by an outdoor enthusiast, and return to the car to drive to Bright Falls.

##### Elderwood Palace Lodge
Saga and Casey arrive outside the Elderwood Palace Lodge. Head inside and grab the **Bright Falls Map** hanging on the wall to the right of the entrance desk. 
*   **Radio Show (1/7 - "Tapio's Weather Report")**: Go down the corridor to the right of the reception. Enter Saga's room (the first door on the left) to hear the radio show broadcast.
*   **Song (1/14 - "Filth")**: Immediately after the radio broadcast ends, the radio will play the song.
*   **Deer Head (2/12)**: Go to the lodge's dining room, pass the door marked "Private", and pet the deer head mounted above the fireplace.

##### Bright Falls Exploration
Step outside the lodge to explore the town:
1.  **Cult Stash (2/22)**: Walk to the end of the street where a white delivery van is parked. Search the grassy area on the side of the van to find a locked chest. The lock uses a repeating electronic sequence; watch the flashing buttons and replicate the pattern to open the chest. It contains 9mm ammunition, shotgun shells, and a **Trauma Pad**.
2.  **Mayor Setter Sign (1/6)**: Head up Coal Street to the left of the lodge. Examine the *Mayor Setter Won't Roll Over* campaign sign standing on the grass.
3.  **Lunch Box (6/21)**: Head east toward the Oh Deer Diner, past it, and enter Founders' Park. Walk to the wooden bandstand and search the base of a tree nearby to find a lunch box containing five manuscript fragments.
4.  **Mayor Setter Sign (2/6)**: Cross the street from the park to find the *Setter is Better* sign standing outside the shops.
5.  **Deer Head (3/12)**: Go back to the Oh Deer Diner. Before speaking to the witnesses, enter the rear corridor and go through the first door on the right to find and pet the deer head on the wall.

##### Interview the Witnesses
Enter the main seating area of the diner and speak to the Bookers (Tammy and Ed) sitting at the corner table. They explain they are in town researching Alan Wake and witnessed Nightingale's body emerge from the lake. Profile the Bookers in your Mind Place under the *Cult of the Tree* and *Cauldron Lake* topics. You will deduce that Tammy found a necklace belonging to a cultist. 

Go to the Case Board and place the new clues under the *Previous Murders* case files, which unlocks the *The Cult of the Tree* and *Cult Stashes* cases. Closed cases can be marked as such. Speak to Tammy Booker again to receive the cultist's necklace. Talk to the waitress, Rose, who behaves strangely and claims to know Saga's daughter, Logan. 

Return to the Mind Place and add your new clues. Catch up with Casey and Sheriff Tim Breaker outside and follow them into the Bright Falls Sheriff Station.

#### Bright Falls Sheriff Station (First Visit)
**Sheriff Station Collectibles Checklist**:

*   **Manuscript Pages**: 2 (MP 2: "Saga Fights Nightingale", MP 3: "Saga Pursues Nightingale")

##### Examine Nightingale's Body
Go behind the front desk of the sheriff station and grab the **Sheriff Station Map** from the wall. Walk down the main corridor and enter the break room:
*   **Koskela Ad (1/6 - "Adventure Tours")**: Walk into the break room to trigger the television broadcast (unlocks the **The Koskela Brothers** trophy).

Go down the stairs at the end of the corridor and proceed into the basement morgue. Approach Nightingale's corpse on the autopsy table. Examine the body, clicking on the head, the chest wound, the upper arm, the hands, and the heart in the tray. 

Return to the Mind Place. Place the Robert Nightingale key image and the five autopsy clues onto the Case Board under *Murder at Cauldron Lake* to open the *Ritual Writing?* and *Cause of Death?* sub-topics. 

Exit the Mind Place. Saga will automatically pick up the **Saga Fights Nightingale With Light** manuscript page (2/56) from the morgue floor. Tim Breaker departs to find files. Suddenly, the power cuts out and Nightingale reanimates, killing a deputy. Saga is knocked down and loses her gun.

##### Escape the Morgue
1.  **Nightingale Encounters**: Nightingale cannot see in bright light. Immediately run to the illuminated corner of the morgue. Wait for him to leave the room.
2.  **Retrieve Weapon**: Walk out of the morgue, jog down the corridor, and grab your pistol from the floor where you dropped it.
3.  **Fight Nightingale**: Nightingale will enter the corridor. Aim your flashlight boost at him and fire your pistol at his head until he vanishes.

*   **Trophy Unlocked**: **Somebody's Home** (for surviving the morgue attack).
*   **Song Unlocked**: **Follow You Into the Dark** (2/14) plays during the transition.
*   **Manuscript Page (3/56 - "Saga Pursues Nightingale")**: Search the morgue floor where Nightingale was standing to find the page.

Profile Nightingale in the Mind Place under the *Where Did Nightingale Go?* topic. Saga deduces that Nightingale has returned to Cauldron Lake. Speak to Casey and Deputy Thornton. 

Step outside the station to return to your car. Cross the road to the harbor area and stand near the fisherman on the pier to listen to **Donna's Jerky Review** radio show (2/7). Use the car to return to Cauldron Lake.

### Return 2: The Heart

#### Cauldron Lake (Second Visit)
**Return 2 Collectibles Checklist**:
*   **Manuscript Pages**: 7 (MPs 4–10)
*   **Weapons**: 1 (Sawed-off Shotgun)
*   **Cult Stashes**: 3 (CSs 3–5)
*   **Lunch Boxes**: 3 (LBs 7–9)
*   **Nursery Rhyme Puzzles**: 3 (NPs 1–3)
*   **Nursery Rhyme Dolls**: 3 (Crow, Wolf, Hero Dolls)
*   **Deer Heads**: 1 (Deer Head 4)

##### Track Nightingale
Use the shortcut key to enter the fenced area and follow the path down toward the crime scene. Ilmo Koskela is waiting outside the FBC monitoring station, guiding FBC technician Steven Lin. You can go inside the station to read the Maintenance Note. 

Continue to the crime scene, which is now heavily flooded. Approach the makeshift sacrificial altar to find footprints leading south. Wade through the water, following the footprints past the Witch's Ladle sign to the massive tree trunk where the path previously ended. Aim your flashlight boost at the shimmering dark barrier on the tree trunk to burn it away, revealing the **Saga Finds a Safe Haven** manuscript page (4/56).

##### Investigate the Witch's Hut
Backtrack to the Witch's Hut. Examine the fuse box on the side of the hut to find a fuse is missing. Cross the bridge to the west and search the blue plastic supply bins in the clearing to find a working fuse. Insert the fuse into the Witch's Hut fuse box to restore power.
*   **Trophy Unlocked**: **Coffee Thermos** (for entering a Break Room / manual save point).
*   **Manuscript Page (52/56 - "Dark Poem 1")**: Grab the page from the table inside the hut.
*   **Manuscript Page (5/56 - "Saga Opens the Cauldron Lake Overlap")**: Search the shelves in the corner of the hut to find the page.

Go to the Mind Place. Profile Nightingale under the *Overlap* topic to deduce that the tree is a threshold, and that you must find Nightingale's heart and place it through the hole in the tree to open it.

##### Find the Heart
Head southeast and south from the Witch's Hut toward the General Store. As you enter the back room of the store, a Taken cultist will burst through the wooden wall.
1.  **Taken Combat**: Use your flashlight boost to burn away the shadow shield. Shoot the glowing red **Source** on his chest to deal critical damage, then finish him with headshots.
    *   **Trophies Unlocked**: **Darkness Coiled** (for burning a shield), **Chasing the Source** (for popping a Source point), and **Aimed Ahead** (for headshot kills).
2.  **Inventory Upgrade**: Enter the small room the cultist emerged from and grab the **Hip Pouch** lying on top of the filing cabinet to unlock four extra inventory slots (unlocks the **Grew Bigger** trophy).
3.  **Manuscript Page (6/56 - "Saga at the General Store")**: Pick up the page from the floor near the gun cabinet.
4.  **Sawed-off Shotgun**: Examine the locked gun cabinet on the wall. The combination note reads: *Check with Lady Fortuna at the counter*. Walk to the front counter and look at the lottery ticket note: the last number combination digits are `05 13 17 23 39 45`. Since the previous digits used were `23`, the next number in sequence is `39`. Enter **739** into the gun cabinet combination lock to claim the **Sawed-off Shotgun** (unlocks the **Bring It** and **All Accounted For** trophies).
5.  **Nightingale's Heart**: Walk to the open freezer unit in the corner, burn away the darkness shield, and collect the **Heart** from inside.

Return to the Witch's Ladle. Place the Heart into the circular hole in the massive tree trunk to open the Overlap.

##### The Overlap (Nightingale Boss Fight)
**Overlap Checklist**:
*   **Manuscript Pages**: 2 (MPs 7 & 8)

Step through the portal into the Overlap. Follow the path forward. You will find yourself looping through the same flooded path. On the second loop, look for a patch of dark substance on a rock in the water. Burn it with your flashlight to reveal the **Nightingale Enters the Lake** manuscript page (7/56). 

Continue along the path, drop down a ledge, and look for a tree with a red painted cult symbol. Pick up the **Nightingale in the Dark Place** manuscript page (8/56) from the ground next to it. 

Follow the red lights, drop down another ledge, and squeeze through the narrow gap between the rocks. Nightingale will attack you with a heavy tree branch.
1.  **Nightingale Fight Strategy**: Nightingale teleports close to Saga to strike. Perform a perfect dodge by pressing L1 just before his blow lands (unlocks the **This is the Moment** trophy). Use a healing item from a quick slot to unlock the **Felt Good** trophy.
2.  **Combat**: Flash him with your boost to expose his source point, then blast him with your shotgun.
3.  **Tornado Phase**: Once damaged, he will retreat and a red tornado will sweep through the area. Run along the paths to avoid the wind, collecting ammo from crates. Nightingale will ambush you from the brush; tap the escape button if grabbed, use a Hand Flare to repel him, and keep firing until he is permanently defeated.
    *   **Trophy Unlocked**: **Lawman** (for defeating Nightingale).
    *   **Trophy Unlocked**: **Into the Overlap** (for escaping the Overlap).
    *   **Song Unlocked**: **Wide Awake** (3/14).

Saga returns to the Cauldron Lake shore, finding a disheveled Alan Wake lying in the mud.

#### Cauldron Lake (Post-Overlap Exploration)
The floodwaters have receded, opening up the western and northern sections of Cauldron Lake.

##### Streamside
1.  **Manuscript Page (9/56 - "The Overlap")**: Follow the path east from the lake shore, ducking under a fallen tree. Search the ferns along the northern edge of the path to find the page.
2.  **Cult Stash (3/22)**: Walk south into the newly dried area. Search the driftwood to find a locked chest. Replicate the electronic flashing sequence on the lock to open it. It contains a **Hand Flare**, an **Inventory Upgrade** hip pouch, and a **Propane Tank**.
3.  **Manuscript Page (10/56 - "Saga Burns Away the Dark Substance")**: Head back to the main path, go north past the Witch's Ladle tree, and head west. Deal with the wolf that attacks from the shadows. Continue west to a campsite and grab the page from the picnic table.
4.  **Streamside Stash Key**: Shine your flashlight on the trees and rocks near the campsite to find painted yellow arrows. Follow the arrows to a rocky ledge where the **Streamside Stash Key** is lying.
5.  **Cult Stash (4/22)**: Backtrack to the campsite and search the trees behind the picnic table to find a chest. Use the Streamside Stash Key to open it. It contains a **Hand Flare**, **Shotgun Ammo**, and a **Trauma Pad**.

##### Picnic Area
1.  **Nursery Rhyme Puzzle (1/17)**: Walk west across the wooden bridge to a picnic area. You will find a nursery rhyme drawing on the ground.
2.  **Crow Doll (1/13)**: Grab the **Crow Doll** sitting on the picnic table.
3.  **Hammer Charm**: Read the rhyme on the ground: *One bird for light, Two for darkness...*. Place the Crow Doll on the Sun drawing. A crow will fly down and leave the **Hammer Charm** on the picnic table (increases stagger chance; unlocks the **Strange Reality**, **Shift in Reality**, and **I'll Find You** trophies). Pick up the Crow Doll before leaving.

##### Witchfinder's Station
1.  **Manuscript Page (11/56 - "Murder Sites")**: Follow the path northeast and climb the wooden stairs to the Witchfinder's Station. Search the picnic table outside to find the page.
2.  **Nursery Rhyme Puzzle (2/17)**: Locate the drawing on the ground outside the front of the station.
3.  **Hero Doll (2/13) & Wolf Doll (3/13)**: Go inside the station. Check the computer desk to find the passcode **2547** on a post-it note. Read the emails. Pet the **Deer Head (4/12)** mounted above the fireplace. Go upstairs, turn left into the bedroom, and collect the **Wolf Doll** from the cardboard box and the **Hero Doll** from the floor.
4.  **Coffee Mug Charm**: Return to the puzzle outside. Read the rhyme: *The Hero brave and strong left home... From the woods came the Wolf...*. Place the Hero Doll on the Boat symbol and the Wolf Doll on the Tree symbol. Collect the dolls, go inside the house, and follow the pawprints upstairs to find the **Coffee Mug Charm** in the playpen (prevents death once; unlocks the **Back to Life** and **Filling the Shape** trophies).

##### Streamside & Private Cabin
1.  **Lunch Box (7/21)**: Head east from the station. Search the bushes near the cliff edge to find a lunch box containing nine manuscript fragments.
2.  **Nursery Rhyme Puzzle (3/17)**: Continue southeast and duck under the fallen branches to find the Private Cabin. The puzzle drawing is on the ground outside.
3.  **Kalevala Knights Charm**: Read the rhyme: *A mother-Crow sits in her nest... to protect her home...*. Place the Crow Doll on the Egg symbol, the Hero Doll on the Heart symbol, and the Wolf Doll on the House symbol. Pick up the dolls and return to the small pond to the north to find the **Kalevala Knights Charm** next to a toy house (increases Hand Flare duration).
4.  **Manuscript Page (12/56 - "Cult of the Tree")**: Interact with the yellow generator outside the cabin and complete the timed button presses to restore power. Go inside the cabin (now a Break Room) and grab the page from the floor.
5.  **Cult Stash (5/22)**: Walk southwest from the cabin toward the stream. Find a locked chest on the ledge. The clue reads *Rock Rock Tree*. Look at the surroundings:

### Appendix: Final Draft (New Game Plus)

#### Overview
After completing the campaign, players can select the **Final Draft** mode from the main menu. This New Game Plus mode introduces the **Nightmare** difficulty level, new manuscript pages, alternative video logs, and a revised campaign ending.

##### Carry Over Details
*   **Saga's Weapons**: Saga's unlocked weapons (Sawed-off, Crossbow, Hunting Rifle, Pump-Action Shotgun) and upgrades are available from the Break Room Shoebox starting in *Return 2: The Heart*.
*   **Alan's Weapons**: Alan's Flare Gun and Double-barrelled Shotgun carry over and are available in the Shoebox starting in *Initiation 2: Casey*.
*   **Charms**: Saga's unique charms carry over. Duplicate unique charms are replaced with **Coffee Mug Charms** in the shoebox.
*   **Words of Power**: Alan's Words of Power carry over. You can continue to collect them up to the upgrade cap.
*   **Songs**: Unlocked songs carry over.
*   *Note: Key items (Screwdriver, Boltcutters) and consumables do not carry over.*
