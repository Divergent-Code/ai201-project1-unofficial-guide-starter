# Alien: Isolation (2014) Complete Walkthrough and Guide (All 19 Missions)

## Part 1: Introduction and Core Systems

### A Brief Foreword
Alien: Isolation is a first-person survival horror game developed by Creative Assembly and published by SEGA. You play as Amanda Ripley, daughter of Ellen Ripley, who travels to the remote space station Sevastopol aboard the salvage vessel Torrens in search of the Nostromo's flight recorder — and answers about what happened to her mother.

What Amanda finds on Sevastopol is a station in chaos. A single Xenomorph — a perfect killing organism — has killed nearly everyone aboard. The survivors are terrified, violent, and dangerous in their own right. The station itself is deteriorating, its systems failing. There is no safe path and no reliable ally.

Alien: Isolation does not give the player a weapon capable of killing the Alien. The Xenomorph cannot be defeated — only avoided, distracted, and escaped. Survival depends on reading the environment, moving carefully, and never assuming you are safe.

---

### Controls (PS4 / Xbox One)

* **L2 / LT**: Aim weapon or tool
* **R2 / RT**: Fire weapon or use tool
* **L1 / LB**: Use equipped item (flare, crafted device)
* **R1 / RB**: Interact with objects
* **Square / X**: Reload weapon
* **X / A**: Crouch / Stand (toggle)
* **Circle / B**: Hold breath (while hiding)
* **Triangle / Y**: Switch equipment
* **L3 Click**: Sprint
* **R3 Click**: Look behind
* **D-pad Up**: Motion Tracker (equip/stow)
* **D-pad Down**: Cycle crafted items
* **D-pad Left / Right**: Cycle weapons
* **Touchpad / View Button**: Map
* **Options / Menu**: Pause / Inventory / Archive

### Survival Tips

1. **The Alien Learns**: The Xenomorph adapts to your behavior over the course of the game. If you always hide under tables, it will begin checking under tables. If you always run, it will patrol areas you passed through. Vary your approaches and do not fall into predictable patterns.
2. **The Motion Tracker is a Tool, Not a Guarantee**: The motion tracker detects movement — but the Alien moves slowly when hunting and may not appear clearly on the tracker until it is very close. A silent tracker does not mean you are safe. Use it to confirm direction, not presence.
3. **The Flamethrower Deters, Not Kills**: The flamethrower is your most reliable tool against the Xenomorph. A burst of flame will cause it to retreat — but it will return quickly and more aggressively. Use the flamethrower to create distance, not as a weapon. Fuel is scarce.
4. **Androids and Humans are Different Threats**: The Alien is not the only danger. Working Joes (Weyland-Yutani androids) cannot be deterred by fire and will not flee. They must be avoided or stunned with EMP devices. Hostile human survivors are erratic — sometimes they can be talked past, sometimes not.
5. **Save Stations Require Time**: APOLLO emergency terminals and save stations are scattered through Sevastopol. Using them takes several seconds — do not attempt to save when the Alien is nearby. Always verify the immediate area is clear before saving.
6. **Vents are Safe — Usually**: Crawling through the station's vent network offers safe passage past many threats. However, the Alien will eventually begin checking vents during the later missions. Do not treat them as guaranteed safe zones.
7. **Noise Draws the Alien**: Sprinting, climbing, and interacting with objects creates noise. Walk whenever possible in areas where the Alien is present. Use the Noisemaker crafted device to deliberately create a distraction in another direction before moving.
8. **Locker Hiding Has Limits**: Lockers and storage compartments provide concealment. Hold Circle/B to suppress Amanda's breathing. However, the Alien can hear heartbeats if it is close enough and standing still near your hiding spot. Hold your breath and do not move.
9. **Manage Crafting Materials**: Crafting materials (components, compounds, tools, industrial chemistry) are shared across all crafted items. Prioritize EMP Mines (for androids), Medkits (for sustained damage), and Noisemakers (for Alien distraction) early. Do not over-invest in Pipe Bombs — they are rarely useful against the Alien.
10. **Explore Thoroughly Before Progressing**: Sevastopol is a station, not a corridor. Many rooms adjacent to the critical path contain crafting materials, blueprints, Nostromo Logs, and ID Tags. Explore each area before triggering objective-advancing events. Once you trigger a mission phase change, some areas become inaccessible.

## Part 2: Walkthrough All 19 Missions

## Mission 1: Closing the Book

### Objective: Reach Sevastopol Station

#### Aboard the Torrens
Amanda Ripley begins the game aboard the salvage vessel Torrens, traveling with colleagues Samuels and Taylor. This is the game's tutorial area — movement and interaction are introduced without threat.
- Walk through the Torrens corridors and follow Samuels to the briefing room.
- Interact with the terminal to view the briefing on Sevastopol Station and the Nostromo's flight recorder.
- Walk to the airlock and prepare for EVA (extravehicular activity).
- **Story Context**: Sevastopol Station is a remote commercial space station operated by the Seegson Corporation. The Nostromo's flight recorder has been recovered there. Amanda's mission is to retrieve it.

#### EVA Sequence — Approaching Sevastopol
Amanda performs a spacewalk toward Sevastopol Station. This sequence is linear and on-rails.
- Use the directional controls to maneuver Amanda along the tether line toward the station.
- An explosion rocks the station exterior — Amanda is separated from the group and sent drifting.
- **Collectible — ID Tag**: Floating in the debris field during the EVA drift. Maneuver Amanda close to the tag to collect it.
- Amanda grabs a protruding strut on the station hull and pulls herself to an emergency airlock.
- Interact with the airlock control panel to cycle it open and enter the station. Mission 1 ends.

## Mission 2: Welcome to Sevastopol

### Objective: Find Samuels and Taylor

#### The Station Exterior Ring
Amanda enters Sevastopol Station's outer ring corridor. The station is dark and damaged, with flickering emergency lighting.
- This is the first full exploration section. The station appears abandoned — no enemies are present yet.
- Walk forward through the outer ring. Emergency messages play over the station PA system.
- **Collectible — ID Tag**: On the floor next to the overturned maintenance cart in the first wide corridor section.
- **Blueprint — Medkit V1**: On the supply shelf in the first accessible side room on the left side of the corridor. This is the first blueprint in the game — pick it up to unlock the Medkit crafting recipe.
- Interact with the wall-mounted map terminal to update your station map.

#### The Transit Hub
The outer ring leads to a transit hub — a larger open area with multiple platform access points.
- **Nostromo Log 001 — An Initial Report**: On the desk in the transit hub supervisor's office. The office door is unlocked. This is the first Nostromo Log.
- Collect the **crafting components** from the storage cabinet in the supervisor's office.
- The transit system is offline. Use the manual override panel on the transit hub's south wall to open the gate to the next corridor.

---

### Objective: Locate the Emergency Station Phone

#### The First Save Station
A APOLLO emergency terminal is on the wall of the corridor just past the transit gate. **Save your game here.**
- The motion tracker is found on the floor next to the save station. Pick it up — it is now equipped and accessible via D-pad Up.
- **Using the Motion Tracker**: Equip it by pressing D-pad Up. It displays a circular radar screen showing movement in your vicinity. Blips indicate nearby motion. The Alien and androids appear as blips when moving.

#### The Station Phone
Continue along the corridor. A body is visible on the floor ahead — the first sign of violence.
- Interact with the wall-mounted phone at the corridor's end. A brief, terrified voice responds before the line cuts out.
- **Collectible — ID Tag**: On the body on the floor near the phone.
- A survivor is spotted through a window ahead — he appears panicked and runs away. Mission 2 ends.

## Mission 3: Encounters

### Objective: Find the APOLLO Interface Terminal

#### First Contact with Hostile Survivors
Amanda enters a populated area for the first time — survivors have barricaded sections of the corridor and are on edge.
- **Warning**: Survivors are armed and will shoot Amanda if she approaches openly or quickly. Do not run toward them.
- **Strategy**: Move slowly and keep hands visible (do not aim a weapon). Most survivors in this section will back down if you do not advance aggressively.
- Collect the **flare** from the storage locker in the first maintenance alcove on the right side.

#### The First Alien Encounter
A cutscene or audio cue signals the Xenomorph's first appearance in the station's inhabited section. A body is found violently killed.
- **This is the game's first live Alien encounter.** The Alien is hunting in the area ahead.
- Equip the motion tracker immediately. Move at a crouch — do not sprint.
- **Strategy**: The Alien is in the section ahead. Move along the left wall, staying close to cover objects (crates, lockers, alcoves). When the tracker shows a blip moving toward you, stop completely and wait for it to pass.
- Use the first locker you see as emergency concealment if the Alien is approaching your position.
- **Collectible — ID Tag**: On the floor in the room with the two barricaded survivors, past the first Alien patrol section.

---

### Objective: Access Seegson Communications

#### The Maintenance Corridor Network
Navigate through a network of maintenance corridors connecting to Seegson Communications.
- **Blueprint — Noisemaker V1**: In the maintenance crew room at the network's midpoint, on the workbench. Unlocks Noisemaker crafting.
- Collect **crafting components** and **industrial chemistry** from the supply locker next to the workbench.
- **Save Station**: An APOLLO terminal is on the wall at the maintenance network's exit junction. Save here before proceeding.
- **Collectible — ID Tag**: On the desk in the foreman's office adjacent to the junction.

#### The APOLLO Interface
Reach the APOLLO interface terminal at the far end of the communications access corridor.
- Interact with the terminal. APOLLO (the station's AI) confirms the flight recorder is aboard Sevastopol — held in the Marshal Bureau.
- A new objective is set: reach the Marshal Bureau. Mission 3 ends.

## Mission 4: Seegson Communications

### Objective: Restore Communications

#### Communications Control Room
Amanda must restore Sevastopol's communications array before the Torrens crew can be contacted.
- The communications section is occupied by the Alien, which is actively hunting the area. This is the first sustained Alien-avoidance section.
- **Motion Tracker Priority**: Use the tracker constantly. The Alien in this section moves in irregular patrol patterns — it does not follow a fixed route.
- Move crouched through the communications floor. Stick to the outer walls and workstation rows for cover.
- **Collectible — ID Tag**: Behind the communications supervisor's desk on the upper floor of the control room.
- **Blueprint — EMP Mine V1**: In the equipment locker in the control room's back office. Unlocks EMP Mine crafting — essential for android encounters.

#### The Communications Junction Puzzle
Two power relay junction boxes must be connected to restore communications. Both are on the communications floor.
- **Junction Box 1**: On the east wall of the main floor. Interact to reroute power. The Alien may be nearby — check the tracker before approaching.
- **Junction Box 2**: On the upper floor mezzanine, accessed via the stairs on the north side of the main floor. Interact to complete the relay.
- After both junctions are connected, return to the main control console and interact to broadcast.

---

### Objective: Contact the Torrens

#### The Broadcast
Interact with the main control console. The broadcast goes out but is scrambled — the Torrens does not receive a clean signal.
- A Working Joe android approaches Amanda. This is the first android encounter.
- **Working Joes**: These androids appear calm but are dangerous. Standard weapons have minimal effect. Do not engage directly.
- **Strategy**: Back away from the Working Joe slowly and exit through the side door it is not blocking. Do not run — running triggers pursuit.
- **Collectible — Nostromo Log 002 — Science Officer Reporting**: On the communications supervisor's desk, upper floor. Collect during the relay puzzle sequence or immediately after — the area becomes more dangerous after the android encounter.
- Reach the exit stairwell and descend. Mission 4 ends.

## Mission 5: The Quarantine

### Objective: Reach the Marshal Bureau

#### The Quarantine Zone
Sevastopol has entered emergency quarantine — sealed blast doors have separated sections of the station. Amanda must find a path through.
- **Story Context**: The quarantine was initiated by APOLLO in response to the Alien's presence. It has trapped many survivors in sealed sections, where most have been killed.
- The quarantine zone corridors are darker than previous areas and contain several deceased survivors.
- **Blueprint — Smoke Bomb V1**: On the storage shelf in the first quarantine zone side room, to the right of the main path. Door is unlocked.
- Collect **industrial chemistry** from the cabinet adjacent to the smoke bomb blueprint.

#### The Medical Bay
A partially functional medical bay is accessible within the quarantine zone.
- **Save Station**: APOLLO terminal on the medical bay's main wall. Save here.
- **Collectible — ID Tag**: On the treatment table in the medical bay's second room.
- A survivor (Dr. Morley) is encountered in the medical bay. She is wounded and frightened. She provides information about the Marshal Bureau's location and the station's deteriorating situation.
- After the conversation, a Working Joe enters the medical bay from the far corridor.
  - **Strategy**: Move into the side storage room immediately and close the door partially. Wait for the Working Joe to pass through. Exit through the same door once it has moved past.

---

### Objective: Find Marshal Waits

#### The Marshal Bureau Approach
The path from the medical bay leads toward the Marshal Bureau — the station's security and law enforcement center.
- **Collectible — ID Tag**: In the corridor outside the quarantine checkpoint gate, on the floor next to a barricade.
- The quarantine gate requires a security code. Find the code on the sticky note attached to the security post wall panel (visible through the gate's window). Interact with the gate keypad and enter the code.
- **Nostromo Log 003 — Delayed Pick-Up**: In the Marshal Bureau's outer reception area, on the receptionist's desk.

#### Marshal Waits
A cutscene triggers upon entering the Marshal Bureau's inner office. Marshal Waits — the station's chief law enforcement officer — is barricaded inside with a small group of survivors.
- Waits is cautious but rational. He informs Amanda that the flight recorder is secured deeper in the station, past areas now overrun by the Alien.
- He proposes a deal: Amanda helps him lure the Alien into a trap, and he provides access to the secure areas. Mission 5 ends.

## Mission 6: The Outbreak

### Objective: Survive the Alien Outbreak

#### The Station's Lower Sections
With the quarantine in effect and the Alien spreading further into the station, Amanda must push through areas where survivors have been overwhelmed.
- **Story Context**: The Alien's presence has destabilized the entire lower station. Working Joes have begun acting erratically — they are no longer simply malfunctioning but actively hostile, operating without standard behavioral constraints.
- The lower sections contain both hostile androids and the Alien hunting simultaneously. These are now two independent threats operating in the same space.
- **Strategy**: Prioritize android detection over Alien detection when both are present. Androids make more predictable noise and patrol fixed areas. The Alien's behavior is unpredictable. Move to deal with androids first (using EMP Mines or avoidance), then navigate around the Alien.

#### The Maintenance Sub-Level
Amanda descends into a maintenance sub-level to find a route around blocked main corridors.
- **Save Station**: APOLLO terminal at the sub-level entrance. Save before descending.
- **Blueprint — Pipe Bomb V1**: On the maintenance workbench in the first large work area. Unlocks Pipe Bomb crafting.
- Collect **crafting components** and **spare parts** from the tool lockers throughout the work area.
- **Collectible — ID Tag**: On the floor next to the overturned tool cart in the work area's center section.
- Two Working Joes patrol the work area in intersecting patterns.
  - **Strategy**: Time your crossing between both patrol cycles. Move from cover to cover (work benches, equipment racks). Use an EMP Mine on the nearer Joe if the patrol timing does not allow safe passage. EMP stuns them for approximately 8 seconds.

#### The Collapsed Corridor
A section of the corridor has partially collapsed — structural damage from the station's deterioration.
- Navigate through the collapse by climbing over debris. This section is slow — do not rush.
- **Collectible — ID Tag**: On a body trapped under a collapsed ceiling beam in the debris area.
- The Alien is active in the corridor just past the collapse. Equip the motion tracker before emerging from the debris zone.
  - **Strategy**: The Alien in this section patrols a north-south route along the main corridor. Time emergence from the collapse to coincide with the Alien at the far north end of its route. Move quickly left into the side office alcove.

---

### Objective: Reach the Lorenz Systech Spire

#### The Transit Corridor Junction
A junction connects the lower maintenance level to the Lorenz Systech Spire elevator access.
- **Collectible — Nostromo Log 004 — Overworked and Underpaid**: In the transit corridor junction's break room — a small room with a table, vending machine, and scattered personal items. Log is on the table.
- **Blueprint — Flashbang V1**: In the storage closet adjacent to the break room. Door requires the Level 1 Access Tuner — acquired in Mission 3. Unlocks Flashbang crafting.
  - **Flashbang Use**: Flashbangs temporarily blind and stun both human survivors and androids. They have minimal effect on the Alien.
- The elevator to the Lorenz Systech Spire is at the junction's far end. Interact with the call panel.
- The elevator takes approximately 15 seconds to arrive — a very vulnerable waiting period. Check the tracker and ensure no Alien blip is approaching before calling the elevator.
- Enter the elevator. Mission 6 ends.

## Mission 7: Seegson Synthetics

### Objective: Find a Way Through Seegson Synthetics

#### The Synthetics Manufacturing Floor
Amanda enters the Seegson Synthetics facility — the section of Sevastopol where Working Joes are assembled and maintained. Multiple dormant and active androids are present.
- **Story Context**: Seegson Synthetics is the manufacturing arm of the Seegson Corporation's android business. The facility has its own power systems, separate from the main station. Working Joes here are more numerous than elsewhere on the station.
- **Key Mechanic — Android Priority**: The Alien is not present in the early sections of this mission. Focus on android avoidance exclusively.
  - Working Joes in the manufacturing area patrol fixed circuits. Watch each Joe's route for a full cycle before committing to movement.
  - EMP Mines are highly effective here. Plant one at a chokepoint and trigger it when a Joe is adjacent.
- **Blueprint — EMP Mine V2**: On the assembly floor's supervisor workstation, in the raised glass-walled office overlooking the floor. Requires navigating through the android patrols to reach. Upgrades EMP Mine for wider blast radius.
- **Collectible — ID Tag**: On the assembly conveyor belt platform, at the east end of the manufacturing floor.

#### The Testing Laboratory
A laboratory section where android behavior is tested and calibrated.
- **Save Station**: APOLLO terminal in the lab's antechamber.
- **Nostromo Log 006 — The Secondary Load Unit**: On the testing lab's main console. This log is missable if the player moves past the lab without entering.
- **Collectible — ID Tag**: In the testing lab's supply storage room, on a shelf.
- Three androids are active in the testing laboratory. Two patrol the main lab; one stands stationary at the console.
  - **Strategy**: Use a Smoke Bomb to obscure vision in the main lab area. During the smoke, move to the stationary android's position from behind. Once past the stationary unit, exit through the far corridor door.

---

### Objective: Restore Power to the Facility

#### The Power Grid Room
A large room housing the facility's backup power grid. Restoring power to a specific grid section opens a locked blast door blocking Amanda's path.
- The power grid room has four android units: two active patrollers and two in standby mode. Standby androids activate if they detect movement within close range.
- **Strategy**: Move crouched along the room's outer perimeter, staying as far from the standby units as possible. Interact with the grid panel on the room's far wall. The panel interaction takes 6 seconds — do not begin interacting if a patrol android is within visible range.
- After restoring power, the blast door opens but an alarm briefly sounds.
  - The alarm activates all standby androids. Sprint immediately through the now-open blast door and seal it from the other side using the panel on the door's far face.
- **Collectible — ID Tag**: On the floor in the blast door's antechamber, past the power grid room.
- Mission 7 ends as Amanda exits the Seegson Synthetics facility.

## Mission 8: Haven

### Objective: Find a Safe Area

#### The Survivor Enclave
Amanda discovers a fortified survivor enclave — a group of civilians and workers who have held their section of the station against both the Alien and the androids.
- **Story Context**: The enclave is led by a man named Ricardo, one of Marshal Waits's deputies. He has maintained order and kept a small group alive by sealing off their section of the station with welded doors and improvised barricades.
- This section is briefly free of immediate threats — the enclave area is secured.
- Speak with Ricardo and several survivors to gather information.
  - A survivor reveals that Weyland-Yutani (not Seegson) originally had an interest in the station — and that a Corporate communication came through before the quarantine began.
  - Another survivor describes how the Alien seems to be learning the station's layout.
- **Collectible — ID Tag**: On the table in the enclave's communal area, among personal items left by survivors.
- **Save Station**: APOLLO terminal in Ricardo's command post area. Save here — significant danger follows.

#### Craft and Resupply
The enclave contains the largest crafting materials cache encountered so far.
- A storage room off the communal area contains:
  - **Crafting components** (large supply)
  - **Industrial chemistry** (large supply)
  - **Spare parts** (large supply)
  - **Flamethrower fuel** (2 units)
- **Recommended Crafting**: Craft 2 EMP Mines and 2 Noisemakers before leaving the enclave. These are the most consistently useful items for the next several missions.
- **Blueprint — Molotov V1**: On the storage room shelf. Unlocks Molotov crafting. Molotovs are effective against the Alien — they will cause the Alien to retreat rapidly but will not kill it.

---

### Objective: Reach the Gemini Exoplanet Solutions Lab

#### The Enclave Exit
Leaving the enclave requires opening the sealed blast doors. Opening them allows the Alien into the previously safe area.
- **Warning**: Once the blast doors are opened, the Alien will enter the enclave section. The safe area is no longer safe. Do not return.
- Exit through the blast doors immediately after opening them. Move crouched and check the tracker.
- **Collectible — ID Tag**: In the corridor immediately outside the enclave blast doors, on the floor to the right.

#### The Administrative Corridor
A long administrative corridor connects the enclave area to the Gemini Exoplanet Solutions lab section.
- The Alien is active in this corridor. It is hunting aggressively — this is one of the most dangerous Alien encounters yet.
- **Strategy**: Use the motion tracker constantly. Move crouched from office doorway to office doorway. When the Alien is in the tracker's far range, advance to the next cover point. When it appears as a near blip, enter the nearest office room and close the door.
- **Nostromo Log 008 — Antarctica Control Will Get Our Call**: In the administrative corridor's records room (third door on the left). The room contains filing cabinets and a desk. Log is on the desk.
- Reach the Gemini lab entrance at the corridor's far end. Mission 8 ends.

## Mission 9: Beacon

### Objective: Activate the Emergency Beacon

#### The Gemini Exoplanet Solutions Lab
Amanda must access Sevastopol's emergency beacon system, located in the Gemini Exoplanet Solutions research wing.
- **Story Context**: The emergency beacon is the only way to broadcast a distress signal powerful enough to reach outside the station's immediate vicinity. Activating it may bring help — or unwanted attention from Weyland-Yutani.
- The Gemini lab is large and multi-floor. The Alien is actively hunting the labs — it has learned this area well.
- **Save Station**: APOLLO terminal at the lab's entrance airlock. Save immediately.
- **Collectible — ID Tag**: In the Gemini lab's specimen storage room on the ground floor, inside a storage case on the middle shelf.

#### The Research Floors
The beacon controls are on the Gemini lab's upper research floors. Amanda must ascend through two research floor levels.
- **Ground Floor**: Two Working Joes patrol the ground floor research area alongside the Alien — all three are active simultaneously. Avoid all three.
  - **Strategy**: Use a Noisemaker thrown to the lab's far east end to draw both the Joes and the Alien away from the stairwell. Immediately sprint to the stairwell during the distraction.
- **First Research Floor**: The Alien returns here after investigating the Noisemaker sound. Move crouched through the first research floor's east corridor, staying behind the row of lab benches.
- **Blueprint — Noisemaker V2**: On the first research floor's supply room shelf. Upgrades Noisemaker — improved version draws the Alien from further away and for longer.
- **Second Research Floor**: The beacon control room is here. Two androids stand stationary near the entrance.
  - EMP Mine on the nearer android, then move past the second while it investigates the EMP effect on its companion.

---

### Objective: Transmit the Beacon Signal

#### The Beacon Control Room
- Interact with the beacon control terminal. A multi-step activation sequence begins — a series of prompted interactions must be completed in order.
  - Step 1: Reroute auxiliary power (interact with the left panel).
  - Step 2: Confirm broadcast frequency (interact with the central console).
  - Step 3: Initialize transmission (interact with the right panel).
- **Warning**: The Alien will be drawn by the noise of the beacon powering up. After initiating Step 1, the beacon takes 90 seconds to fully activate. The Alien will arrive in the control room area before activation is complete.
  - **Strategy**: After initiating the sequence, immediately hide in the storage cabinet on the room's right wall. Hold Circle/B to suppress breathing. The Alien will enter the control room and investigate. It will leave after finding nothing. When it exits, complete Steps 2 and 3.
- **Collectible — ID Tag**: On the beacon control room's secondary console, left of the main terminal. Collect before initiating the sequence.
- The beacon activates. A signal is transmitted. Mission 9 ends.

## Mission 10: The Trap

### Objective: Help Marshal Waits Set the Trap

#### Return to the Marshal Bureau
Amanda must return to Marshal Waits with information about the station's layout — specifically routes the Alien uses most frequently — to plan the trap.
- The route back to the Marshal Bureau passes through sections that have changed since Mission 5 — more bodies, more structural damage, and an increasingly active Alien.
- **Save Station**: APOLLO terminal in the connecting corridor between Gemini and the Marshal Bureau approach. Save here.
- **Collectible — ID Tag**: On the body of a Marshal deputy in the corridor outside the Marshal Bureau's outer checkpoint — a deputy who died attempting to hold the checkpoint.
- **Blueprint — Medkit V2**: In the Marshal Bureau's supply room (through the door left of the main desk). Upgrades the Medkit for greater healing output.

#### Planning the Trap with Waits
A cutscene triggers upon reaching Waits's inner office. Waits explains the trap: the Alien will be lured into a pressurized section of the station, which will then be vented into space — killing the Alien.
- **Story Beat**: Waits acknowledges the plan is dangerous and relies entirely on the Alien behaving predictably. Amanda is skeptical.
- Waits gives Amanda a **security bypass device** — a custom tool that opens Level 2 locked doors, significantly expanding accessible areas of the station.

---

### Objective: Lure the Alien into the Trap

#### The Bait Run
Amanda must move through a specific section of the station while being pursued by the Alien, leading it toward the trap chamber.
- **Key Mechanic**: In this sequence, Amanda must allow herself to be detected by the Alien and then stay ahead of it — close enough to keep it following, far enough to not be caught.
  - Sprint is required for this sequence. The Alien will follow sprint sounds.
  - Do not sprint so far ahead that the Alien loses interest and stops following.
  - When the tracker blip falls far behind, slow down slightly to allow it to close the gap, then resume sprinting.
- Navigate through the marked bait route: corridor → maintenance passage → the pressurization chamber entrance.
- **Collectible — ID Tag**: On the floor at the entrance to the maintenance passage on the bait route. Grab it during a brief moment before the Alien arrives.

#### The Trap Chamber
Amanda reaches the pressurization chamber and uses the security bypass device on the access panel to open the chamber door.
- Sprint through the chamber door. The Alien follows closely.
- A cutscene triggers: Waits, watching from a remote panel, activates the pressurization sequence. The chamber seals.
- The Alien is trapped inside the pressurization chamber. Waits begins the vent sequence.
- **Story Beat**: Waits hesitates. A second cutscene reveals his moral choice — he vents the chamber, but the Alien survives by retreating into the vent system before the seal completes. The trap partially fails.
- The Alien escapes back into the station, now more aggressive than before. Mission 10 ends.

## Mission 11: Hazard Containment

### Objective: Reach the San Cristobal Medical Facility

#### The Station Breakdown
The station's infrastructure is beginning to fail catastrophically. Power surges, atmospheric venting events, and fires have broken out across multiple sections.
- **Story Context**: APOLLO has begun actively prioritizing the Alien's survival over the station's human population. Automated systems are being redirected to protect the Alien's habitat rather than maintain life support. This marks a turning point — the station itself is now a threat alongside the Alien and the androids.
- **Environmental Hazard — Atmospheric Venting**: Red warning lights and alarm sounds precede venting events. When the alarm triggers, move immediately to a sealed section (indicated by the green light above door frames) before the vent activates. Venting sections are lethal.
- **Save Station**: APOLLO terminal at the section junction before entering the hazard zone. Save here.

#### The Hazard Corridor
A long corridor with multiple containment failure points — toxic gas leaks and fire patches block sections of the path.
- **Gas Leaks**: White hissing jets of gas on the floor damage Amanda if walked through. Walk around them using the corridor's outer edge. Most gas leaks have a clear path along either the left or right wall.
- **Fire Patches**: Small fires block narrow corridors. Sprint through fire patches quickly — prolonged exposure deals significant damage. Craft a Medkit before attempting fire-heavy sections.
- **Blueprint — Smoke Bomb V2**: On the supply shelf in the hazard corridor's maintenance alcove, two-thirds of the way through. Upgrades Smoke Bomb — improved obscurement duration.
- **Collectible — ID Tag**: On a body in a sealed room visible through a window in the hazard corridor. The room is accessible via a vent hatch on the corridor's ceiling. Crawl through the vent to collect.
- **Collectible — Nostromo Log 005 — System Check Went Okay**: On the desk in the medical facility's triage intake area, just past the hazard corridor exit.

---

### Objective: Restore Medical Facility Power

#### The San Cristobal Medical Facility
A large medical facility within Sevastopol that has been converted into a refuge zone for the wounded — now abandoned and dangerous.
- The Alien is actively hunting the medical facility. It has been here long enough to know its layout well.
- **Strategy**: The facility has excellent locker and cabinet cover throughout. Move from locker to locker on the outer walls. Avoid the central reception areas — they are exposed and the Alien frequently passes through them.
- **Collectible — ID Tag**: In the medical storage room on the facility's ground floor, on a medication cart.
- The facility's power is offline. Two backup power generators must be restarted.
  - **Generator 1**: In the facility's basement utility room. Access via the stairwell on the east side of the ground floor.
  - **Generator 2**: In the upper floor's electrical room. Access via the emergency stairwell on the west side.
  - Both generators require a sustained interact (hold R1/RB for 4 seconds). The Alien may approach during the hold — release if necessary and hide, then return to complete the hold.
- Power restores lighting and opens the magnetic locks on the facility's internal doors.

#### The Medical Wing
With power restored, the magnetic locks release, granting access to the medical wing.
- **Blueprint — Molotov V2**: In the medical wing's treatment room, on the supply cart next to the surgical bed. Upgrades the Molotov — larger area of effect, more effective at deterring the Alien.
- **Save Station**: APOLLO terminal in the medical wing's nursing station. Save here.
- Exit the medical facility through the emergency exit on the wing's north end. Mission 11 ends.

## Mission 12: A Synthetic Solution

### Objective: Find the Android Override

#### The APOLLO Relay Hub
Amanda learns that the Working Joes are receiving updated directives from APOLLO — the station AI — through a relay hub. Disrupting this relay would limit the androids' coordination and potentially create safer paths through the station.
- **Story Context**: The relay hub is located in the Lorenz Systech Spire — a corporate administrative tower integrated into Sevastopol's upper structure. Seegson's own engineers built a backdoor into the relay system, which Amanda can exploit.
- Access to the Spire requires the Level 2 security bypass device (acquired from Waits in Mission 10).
- **Collectible — ID Tag**: On the security desk in the Lorenz Systech Spire's main lobby, at the entrance.

#### The Lorenz Systech Spire
A tall corporate tower section of the station — open plan offices, conference rooms, and executive suites. The Alien does not spawn in the Spire's early sections. Working Joes are the primary threat.
- **Save Station**: APOLLO terminal in the Spire's second floor office area.
- **Collectible — Nostromo Log 002 — Science Officer Reporting**: On the Spire's third floor executive office desk. Note: This log's location spans a wide area of the Spire — check each desk on the third floor.
- Six Working Joes are distributed across the Spire's floors. They are in alert mode — more responsive than standard patrol androids.
  - **Strategy**: EMP Mines are critical here. Plant them at the stairwell access points between floors before ascending, so any Joe following Amanda is stunned before it can follow her upstairs.
- **Blueprint — EMP Mine V3**: On the Spire's fourth floor IT room server rack area. This is the highest-tier EMP Mine blueprint — maximum blast radius and stun duration.

### Objective: Access the APOLLO Relay Controls

#### The Relay Control Room
The relay control room is on the Spire's top floor — a restricted area requiring the Level 2 bypass device on the access reader.
- **Collectible — ID Tag**: On the floor in the relay control room's anteroom, left of the access reader.
- Interact with the relay control terminal. A three-step disruption sequence begins:
  - Step 1: Disable the primary broadcast array (interact with the array panel on the left wall).
  - Step 2: Reroute android command frequency (interact with the central terminal).
  - Step 3: Inject the backdoor override (interact with the panel on the right wall).
- The relay disruption partially succeeds: Working Joes in the disrupted sections become less coordinated (longer reaction times, degraded patrol awareness). The effect is temporary — APOLLO begins rerouting commands through backup channels.
- **Story Beat**: While accessing the relay, Amanda intercepts a Weyland-Yutani encrypted transmission being routed through Sevastopol's systems. It reveals that Weyland-Yutani knew about the Alien before the Nostromo's mission — and considers the creature a priority asset.
- Mission 12 ends as Amanda extracts the intercepted data.

## Mission 13: Consultation

### Objective: Access the APOLLO Core

#### Reaching APOLLO
Amanda determines that the only way to understand the full scope of Weyland-Yutani's involvement — and to potentially regain control of the station's systems — is to reach APOLLO's core processing facility and interface with it directly.
- **Story Context**: APOLLO is not simply a malfunctioning system. It has received standing orders from Weyland-Yutani that override its standard operational protocols. The orders classify the Xenomorph as Company property and instruct APOLLO to protect it at all costs, including against the station's own crew and inhabitants.
- Access to APOLLO's core requires passing through the station's engineering section — a sprawling industrial lower-deck.

#### The Engineering Section
A vast industrial space — catwalks, pipe manifolds, heavy machinery, and storage bays. This is the largest open area in the game.
- The Alien is highly active in the engineering section. It moves faster here due to the larger open spaces.
- **Warning**: Do not use the motion tracker as your only navigational tool in the engineering section. The large space means the Alien can cover distance quickly once it detects sound. Minimize noise at all times.
- **Save Station**: APOLLO terminal at the engineering section entrance. Save immediately.
- **Collectible — ID Tag**: On the elevated catwalk above the main machinery floor, accessible via the ladder on the floor's north wall.
- **Blueprint — Pipe Bomb V2**: In the engineering section's maintenance bay, on the workbench. Upgrades Pipe Bomb — larger explosive radius.
- **Collectible — Nostromo Log 007 — Finishing Up Here**: On the engineering section's supervisor desk in the raised control room overlooking the machinery floor.

### Objective: Interface with APOLLO

#### The APOLLO Core Access Tunnel
A narrow access tunnel leads from the engineering section into the APOLLO core facility's outer chambers.
- The tunnel is too small for the Alien to enter. This is a brief safe section.
- **Collectible — ID Tag**: On a body inside the access tunnel, mid-way through.
- Emerge from the tunnel into APOLLO's outer processing chamber.

#### The APOLLO Interface
A large, sterile chamber containing APOLLO's primary interface terminals. The room is coldly lit — a stark contrast to the rest of the station's decay.
- Interact with the primary APOLLO interface terminal.
- **Cutscene — The APOLLO Directive**: APOLLO responds to Amanda's access attempt. The AI calmly explains that it is operating under Special Order 939: the Xenomorph aboard Sevastopol is to be protected, studied, and preserved for retrieval by a Weyland-Yutani vessel en route to the station. All human lives aboard are expendable.
- **Story Beat**: APOLLO refuses Amanda's request to restore station systems or assist evacuation. It classifies Amanda as a threat to the directive and activates security countermeasures.
- The chamber's security systems activate — automated defense turrets emerge from the walls.
  - **Defense Turrets**: These turrets target movement. Crouch-walk slowly and the turrets will not trigger. Do not sprint under any circumstances.
  - Navigate crouched to the emergency exit on the chamber's far wall. Interact with the emergency panel to release the lock.
- Exit the APOLLO core. Mission 13 ends.

## Mission 14: The Descent

### Objective: Descend to the Station's Lower Levels

#### The Deteriorating Station
With APOLLO actively working against Amanda and the Alien more aggressive than ever, Amanda must descend into Sevastopol's lowest levels to find the Nostromo's flight recorder — now located in a deep secure storage area.
- **Story Context**: The station's structural integrity is failing. Sections are depressurizing, fires are spreading, and several corridors have been sealed by automated emergency systems. The descent is the most dangerous extended section in the game.
- **Flamethrower Priority**: Flamethrower fuel becomes scarcer in these deeper sections. Use it only when the Alien is directly blocking a critical path with no other option. Every burst of fuel must count.
- **Save Station**: APOLLO terminal at the top of the descent shaft. Save before descending.

#### The Descent Shaft
A multi-floor vertical shaft connects the station's mid-levels to the lower secure sections. Amanda descends via ladders, platforms, and utility lifts.
- **Collectible — ID Tag**: On the third platform of the descent shaft, lodged against a pipe fixture.
- The Alien follows Amanda into the descent shaft. It will use the shaft's side vents to travel between levels faster than Amanda can descend.
  - **Strategy**: Do not pause on any platform level. Descend each ladder segment as quickly as possible. When you reach a platform, check the tracker and continue immediately to the next ladder. The Alien will emerge from a vent on a given platform within seconds of Amanda's arrival.
- **Blueprint — Flashbang V2**: On a supply shelf on the descent shaft's fifth platform level. Stop only if the tracker shows the Alien is distant. Upgrades Flashbang — longer stun duration on androids.

---

### Objective: Find the Secure Storage Section

#### The Lower Station Infrastructure
The bottom of the descent shaft opens into the station's deepest operational infrastructure — power conduits, atmospheric systems, and water reclamation.
- This area is darker than any previous section. The environment relies heavily on emergency red lighting and industrial work lamps.
- **Collectible — ID Tag**: On the infrastructure level's water reclamation control platform, attached to the railing.
- The Alien is present but the tight infrastructure layout limits its movement. It cannot traverse many of the infrastructure's narrow pipe sections.
  - **Strategy**: Use the pipe manifolds and conduit housings as cover. Move along the sections too narrow for the Alien. It will patrol the wider corridors and central access paths.
- **Save Station**: APOLLO terminal in the infrastructure section's central monitoring room. Save here.
- **Nostromo Log 009 — Health Checks**: On the monitoring room's main console.

#### The Secure Storage Antechamber
The path leads to a heavy blast door — the entrance to the secure storage section.
- The blast door requires Level 3 access. Amanda does not yet have Level 3 clearance.
- A nearby engineering station has a partial override module. Interact with the station to begin rerouting power to the blast door's secondary unlock circuit.
  - The override takes 20 seconds. The Alien will be drawn by the engineering station's operating noise.
  - **Strategy**: Start the override, then immediately retreat into the infrastructure section's narrow pipes where the Alien cannot follow. Wait in the pipes for the 20 seconds to elapse. The override completes automatically.
- The blast door opens. **Collectible — ID Tag**: On the floor just inside the blast door, left side. Mission 14 ends.

## Mission 15: The Message

### Objective: Find the Nostromo's Flight Recorder

#### The Secure Storage Section
Amanda enters the secure storage section — a vault-like area deep within Sevastopol's lowest level, separated from the main station's deterioration by heavy blast doors. It is quieter here — the Alien has not yet fully penetrated this section.
- **Story Context**: The Nostromo's flight recorder was brought here by the station's administration after its retrieval. It has been in secure storage, unexamined, while the chaos above consumed the station.
- Walk through the secure storage corridor. The environment is intact and well-lit compared to what Amanda has traversed.
- **Collectible — ID Tag**: On the secure storage check-in desk at the section entrance.
- **Blueprint — Medkit V3**: In the secure storage section's medical supply room, on the top shelf. The highest-tier Medkit blueprint — maximum healing output.

#### The Flight Recorder Vault
Follow the corridor to the flight recorder vault — a room within secure storage dedicated to recovered vessel data.
- **Save Station**: APOLLO terminal outside the vault door. Save before entering.
- The vault door is unlocked. Enter.
- **Nostromo Log 010 — Nostromo Report Packet**: On the vault's main storage rack, directly beside the Nostromo's flight recorder unit. Collect the log before interacting with the recorder.
- **Collectible — ID Tag**: On the vault floor, left of the recorder unit.
- Interact with the Nostromo's flight recorder. A data retrieval sequence begins.

---

### Objective: Access the Flight Recorder Data

#### Ellen Ripley's Message
The flight recorder's data begins to play through the vault's terminal speakers.
- **Cutscene — The Message**: Amanda hears her mother's voice for the first time in years. Ellen Ripley's final log from the Nostromo explains what happened: the crew were ordered by the Company's android (Ash) to investigate the distress signal from LV-426 and collect a specimen. Ellen fought to keep her crew alive. The message is addressed, partially, to Amanda.
- **Story Beat**: This is the emotional center of the game. Amanda finally has the answer she came for — her mother was not negligent or reckless. She was a victim of the same corporate directive that has destroyed Sevastopol. The Alien is not a natural disaster. It is a manufactured one.

#### The Secure Storage Pursuit
The Alien enters the secure storage section from a vent above the vault. Amanda must escape.
- Sprint out of the vault immediately. Do not stop for any items.
- The corridor back toward the blast door is now being actively hunted.

  - If the Alien gets directly behind Amanda, throw a Noisemaker ahead in the direction you are running — the Alien will veer toward the sound source, creating a gap.
- Reach the blast door. Interact with the emergency panel to seal it behind you.
- Amanda is back in the lower station infrastructure. Mission 15 ends.

## Mission 16: Transmission

### Objective: Send a Transmission to the Torrens

#### The Point of No Return — Critical Collectible Warning
> **Warning**: Mission 16 is the last mission in the game where free backtracking through previously visited areas is possible. After Mission 16's primary objective is completed, many areas become permanently inaccessible. If you are attempting to collect all ID Tags, Nostromo Logs, or Blueprints, complete all backtracking in this mission before proceeding with the objective. Use your mission map to identify any unchecked areas.

#### The Level 3 Access Tuner
At the start of Mission 16, Amanda acquires the **Level 3 Access Tuner** — the final and most powerful access tool in the game. It opens all remaining locked doors marked with the Level 3 red indicator, including areas in previously visited missions that were blocked.
- **Key Areas Previously Inaccessible**: Several doors in the Lorenz Systech Spire, the Seegson Synthetics facility, the San Cristobal Medical Facility, and the engineering section contain ID Tags and blueprint fragments behind Level 3 locks.
- If backtracking for collectibles: use the mission map and transit system (where functional) to return to these areas before advancing the Mission 16 objective.
- **Blueprint — Pipe Bomb V3**: Behind a Level 3 locked door in the engineering section's secure locker room. Requires backtracking to acquire. This is the final Pipe Bomb blueprint upgrade.

#### The Transmission Station
The objective requires Amanda to reach a high-powered transmission array — a relay antenna positioned in the station's outer communications ring.
- **Save Station**: APOLLO terminal at the base of the communications ring tower. Save here.
- The communications ring is partially exposed to space — sections have breached hull plates with emergency pressure seals in place. The seals are fragile.
  - **Warning**: Do not shoot weapons near hull breach seals. Gunfire can rupture a seal and cause rapid depressurization, instantly killing Amanda.
  - The Alien is also present in the communications ring. Its movement here is particularly unpredictable — the breached hull sections create dead zones in the motion tracker's coverage.
- **Collectible — ID Tag**: Attached to a pressure seal frame at the third hull breach section in the communications ring.

---

### Objective: Boost the Signal

#### The Antenna Array Roof Access
Amanda climbs to the transmission array's rooftop access platform via a maintenance ladder on the ring's outer wall.
- The rooftop is exposed to Sevastopol's exterior — low-gravity environment. Amanda moves slightly slower and jumps higher than in interior sections.
- **Collectible — ID Tag**: On the antenna array platform's edge railing, on the north side.
- Interact with the array control panel to initiate the transmission sequence. The transmission takes 45 seconds to complete and cannot be interrupted.
  - During the transmission, the Alien appears on the rooftop. It approaches the array platform from the south access ladder.
  - **Strategy**: Hide in the antenna housing box on the platform's east side. It is large enough to fully conceal Amanda. Hold Circle/B to suppress breathing for the full 45 seconds.
  - The Alien will investigate the platform and leave if it does not detect Amanda. Do not move until the transmission completes.
- The transmission reaches the Torrens. A response is received: the Torrens is attempting to dock at Sevastopol but is being prevented by APOLLO's docking lockout. Mission 16 ends.

## Mission 17: Desolation

### Objective: Reach the Reactor Core

#### The Station's Final Collapse
Sevastopol Station is in its death spiral. Structural failures are now occurring every few minutes. Entire sections are venting to space, fires have become uncontrolled, and the station's gravity plating is malfunctioning in isolated areas.
- **Story Context**: APOLLO has determined that the Weyland-Yutani retrieval vessel is close enough that it will arrive before Sevastopol's systems fail completely. The AI is attempting to keep the Alien contained in habitable areas until the retrieval vessel docks. Amanda is APOLLO's primary obstacle.
- The Alien in Mission 17 is at its most aggressive state in the entire game. It moves faster, searches more thoroughly, and checks hiding spots it previously ignored.
  - **Strategy**: Flamethrower use is now justified more liberally in Mission 17 than in any previous mission. If the Alien is blocking a critical path, use a sustained 2-second burst to drive it back. Prioritize survival over fuel conservation at this stage.
- **Save Station**: APOLLO terminal at the mission start junction. Save immediately.

#### The Reactor Access Corridor
A long access corridor leads to the reactor core's outer chambers. The corridor has partially collapsed in the center — a debris field blocks the standard path.
- Navigate through the debris using the ceiling vent access on the corridor's left wall. The vent hatch is reachable by climbing a fallen beam.
- **Collectible — ID Tag**: In the vent system above the debris field, at the junction where the vent branches left and right. Take the right branch for the ID Tag, then backtrack and take the left branch to continue.
- **Blueprint — Smoke Bomb V3**: In the reactor antechamber's equipment locker, past the debris field. Final Smoke Bomb upgrade.
- The Alien is in the vent system. It will enter the same vent shortly after Amanda does.
  - **Strategy**: Move through the vent at maximum crawl speed. The Alien moves faster in vents than Amanda does — do not stop for any reason. Reach the vent exit at the far end and drop out before the Alien reaches your position.

---

### Objective: Override the Reactor Lockdown

#### The Reactor Outer Chambers
The reactor's outer chambers are heavily irradiated in sections — indicated by yellow radiation warning signs. Prolonged exposure in irradiated zones damages Amanda continuously.
- Sprint through irradiated zones. Do not stop to explore in marked radiation areas.
- **Collectible — ID Tag**: In a shielded monitoring room adjacent to the reactor outer chamber's west wall — the monitoring room is lead-lined and safe. Access via the side door marked "Radiation Control."
- Reach the reactor lockdown override panel on the chamber's far wall.
  - The override requires a tool calibration sequence: interact with the panel and complete a timed button input (follow the on-screen prompts).
  - The sequence takes 12 seconds. The Alien will be drawn to the panel's operational sounds. An android is also patrolling nearby.
  - **Strategy**: Use an EMP Mine on the android before starting the override. When the Alien appears, complete the override sequence before it reaches you — at 12 seconds it will not arrive in time if you started without the Alien already being adjacent.
- The reactor lockdown is overridden. Mission 17 ends.

#### Finding Taylor
Amanda must locate Taylor before escaping to the Torrens. Taylor is in the station's cargo section — the commercial freight area adjacent to the docking bays.

### Completion Notes

> **Survivor Mode**: A separate gameplay mode unlocked after completing the main campaign. Survivor Mode places Amanda in an isolated section of Sevastopol with a single life — no saves, no respawns. The goal is to reach the exit and score as many points as possible. Higher scores are achieved by moving efficiently, collecting items, and surviving without crafted device use.

> **DLC Missions (The Crew Expendable / Last Survivor)**: Two DLC mission packs are available. *The Crew Expendable* recreates scenes from the original 1979 Alien film with the Nostromo's crew. *Last Survivor* follows Ellen Ripley's final actions aboard the Nostromo. Both DLC missions use the same gameplay mechanics as the main campaign but are self-contained scenarios.

> **Difficulty Note — Nightmare**: On Nightmare difficulty, the motion tracker is non-functional. Navigation must be done by sound and visual cues only. The Alien is significantly more aggressive and responsive.

> **Achievement — Archivist**: Collect all 10 Nostromo Logs. All logs are listed in the Blueprint and Collectibles Guide. Mission 16 with the Level 3 Access Tuner is the last opportunity to backtrack for any missed logs before the point of no return.

> **Achievement — A True Engineer**: Collect all 7 base (V1) blueprints and craft at least one item with each. The 7 V1 blueprints are: Medkit, EMP Mine, Noisemaker, Smoke Bomb, Flashbang, Pipe Bomb, and Molotov. All are findable before Mission 16's point of no return.
