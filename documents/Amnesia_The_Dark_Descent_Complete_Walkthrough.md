# Amnesia: The Dark Descent (2010) Complete Walkthrough and Guide

## Part 1: Introduction and Core Systems

### A Brief Foreword
Amnesia: The Dark Descent is a first-person survival horror game developed by Frictional Games. You play as Daniel, a young man who wakes on the floor of Brennenburg Castle with no memory of who he is, how he arrived, or what has happened to the castle around him. A note in his own handwriting tells him to descend to the inner sanctum and kill a man named Alexander. He does not know why.

As Daniel explores the castle, memory fragments return in the form of notes he wrote to himself — notes that reveal a story of obsession, cruelty, and a bargain with something ancient and terrible. The castle is not empty. Something hunts the corridors.

Amnesia: The Dark Descent does not give the player a weapon. There is no combat. The only options when a monster is encountered are to hide, run, or die. The game's design is built around dread, darkness, and the deliberate denial of safety.

### Core Systems and Survival Tips

#### The Sanity System
Sanity is the central mechanical concept of Amnesia: The Dark Descent. Daniel's sanity is represented by a bar (visible in the inventory screen) that decreases under specific conditions and must be actively managed.

**Sanity Decreases When**:
- Daniel stands in darkness for extended periods
- Daniel witnesses a monster, a death, or a disturbing event
- Daniel looks directly at a monster
- Daniel stands in areas with disturbing environmental details (torture implements, remains, etc.)

**Sanity Effects by Stage**:
- **Mild loss**: Subtle visual distortion at screen edges, occasional auditory hallucinations
- **Moderate loss**: Screen tilts and blurs, footstep sounds from empty corridors, objects fall over on their own
- **Severe loss**: Strong visual distortion, Daniel breathes heavily, heartbeat is audible
- **Critical loss**: Daniel becomes temporarily incapacitated — he will stumble, fall, and be vulnerable. Recovery is slow.

**Restoring Sanity**:
- Standing in a well-lit area for sustained periods
- Completing objectives and progressing the story (minor sanity restoration)
- Reading certain notes and diary pages (small restoration)
- Laudanum does **not** restore sanity — it only restores health

**Managing Sanity**:
- Keep the lantern on when moving through dark areas to slow sanity loss
- Light candles and torches using tinderboxes — illuminated areas do not drain sanity
- When a monster is nearby, look away from it. Looking directly at a monster causes rapid sanity loss even if you are hidden.
- Do not linger in dark rooms longer than necessary.

---

#### The Lantern
Daniel carries an oil lantern that is his primary light source throughout the game.
- The lantern consumes oil over time when active. Monitor oil levels in the inventory.
- Oil is found throughout the castle in containers — some visible in the environment, others in drawers, cabinets, and chests.
- The lantern cannot be refueled mid-use. Collect oil whenever found, even when the lantern is full — excess oil is stored.
- **Tinderboxes** are used to light static light sources (candles, wall torches, fireplaces). These do not consume oil. A well-lit room can be navigated with the lantern off, preserving oil.
- Prioritize lighting candles in rooms where you will spend significant time solving puzzles.

---

#### Tinderboxes
Tinderboxes are small matchbox-like items found throughout the castle. They are used to ignite candles, wall torches, and fireplaces. Each tinderbox is consumed on use.
- Lit candles provide permanent light in a room — they do not go out after use.
- Lighting candles also provides small sanity benefits in the surrounding area.
- Tinderboxes are collectibles tracked for the **Illuminatus** achievement (PlayStation) — a specific number must be collected in a single playthrough. Do not ignore them.
- Tinderboxes are often found in drawers, on shelves, and on tables. Interact with every container in each room.

---

#### Hiding from Monsters
Amnesia's monsters cannot be fought. Attempting to engage them results in death. The only viable responses are hiding or running.

**Hiding Mechanics**:
- Closets and alcoves with doors are the most reliable hiding spots. Enter the closet and close the door behind you.
- Once hidden in a closet, **do not peek out** — opening the door or looking through a crack while the monster is nearby causes sanity loss and may trigger detection.
- Monsters detect Daniel through sound (running, knocking over objects) and sight (direct line of vision in lit areas).
- Crouch-walking significantly reduces noise. Always crouch when a monster is nearby.
- Moving objects, opening/closing doors, and dropping items all make noise. Be still while hiding.
- Monsters will patrol and search an area before eventually leaving. Wait for silence and the ambient music to return to normal before emerging.

**Running**:
- If a monster has detected Daniel and is actively chasing, sprint for the nearest door and put as many doors between Daniel and the monster as possible.
- Monsters cannot open doors that Daniel has closed behind him while fleeing, but they can break through some doors given time.
- Run toward unexplored areas where the monster has not been — it may stop pursuit at area transitions.

---

#### Inventory Management
- Oil, tinderboxes, and laudanum are stored in the inventory (Tab).
- There is no weight limit — collect everything.
- Laudanum restores health when consumed directly from the inventory. Save it for sustained damage situations.
- Notes, diary pages, and story documents are automatically filed in the Notes section of the inventory. Reading them provides sanity-neutral or slight sanity-positive effects.

## Part 2: Walkthrough — Act I

## Act I: Rainy Hall

### Overview
The Rainy Hall is Daniel's starting location — a large, rain-soaked entrance chamber in Brennenburg Castle. It is intact but dark, with rain visible through broken skylights. No monsters are present in the Rainy Hall.

### Objective: Follow the Trail

#### Waking Up
Daniel wakes on the floor. The room is dark. A lantern sits nearby.
- Interact with the lantern to pick it up. It is now equipped and can be toggled with F / Triangle.
- A **Note** is on the floor near Daniel's starting position. Interact to read it. Written in Daniel's own hand, it describes his decision to erase his own memory and instructs him to descend to the inner sanctum and kill Alexander of Brennenburg.
- **Tinderbox**: On the windowsill to the right of the main entrance doors, on the narrow stone ledge.
- Follow the blood trail on the floor — it leads further into the hall.

#### The Hall Interior
The Rainy Hall's main corridor leads past collapsed sections and a broken fountain.
- **Tinderbox**: On the table in the small anteroom off the right side of the main corridor. The anteroom door is ajar.
- **Oil Container**: On the shelf in the anteroom.
- **Diary Entry — Daniel's Journal, Early Entry**: On the writing desk in the anteroom. Reading it provides background on Daniel's journey to Prussia and his initial contact with Alexander.
- The blood trail leads to a large wooden door at the hall's far end. Interact to open it.

#### The Note on the Wall
Before exiting the Rainy Hall, a torn piece of paper is pinned to the wall beside the exit door.
- Interact to read. It is a warning from Daniel to himself — he describes something pursuing him through the castle and urges his amnesiac future self not to trust his own earlier reasoning.
- Exit through the large wooden door. The Rainy Hall section ends.

## Act I: Old Archives

### Overview
The Old Archives is a series of storage rooms and reading rooms filled with books, shelves, and scattered documents. It is largely intact and presents no immediate monster threat. This section teaches inventory management and environmental exploration.

### Objective: Find a Way to the Entrance Hall

#### The First Reading Room
Daniel enters a room lined with bookshelves. Several candles are unlit.
- **Tinderbox**: In the left bookshelf's bottom drawer. Interact with the drawer to open it.
- Use a tinderbox to light the candles on the central reading table. The room brightens — sanity stabilizes.
- **Note — Archive Index Fragment**: On the reading table. References classified lower archive sections under Alexander's personal lock.
- **Oil Container**: Behind the reading table on the floor, partially hidden under the table's edge.

#### The Collapsed Section
A section of the archives has partially collapsed — shelving units have fallen and block the direct path forward.
- Navigate through the collapse by climbing over the fallen shelving (jump, Space/Cross).
- **Tinderbox**: Wedged between two fallen shelf units in the center of the collapse.
- **Laudanum**: In a small wooden box on the floor past the collapse, to the right of the path.

#### The Locked Gate
A locked iron gate blocks the route toward the Entrance Hall.
- The key is in the archive supervisor's room — a small room off the left side of the corridor before the gate.
- The supervisor's room door is unlocked. Enter.
- **Key — Archive Gate Key**: On the supervisor's desk. Pick up.
- **Note — Archive Supervisor's Personal Notes**: On the same desk. Describes the supervisor's growing unease with Alexander's restricted sections and strange noises from below.
- Return to the iron gate and use the key (interact while holding it in inventory). The gate opens.
- Continue forward into the Entrance Hall.

## Act I: Entrance Hall

### Overview
The Entrance Hall is a large, multi-story hub connecting several wings of Brennenburg Castle. Multiple doors lead to different areas. Several are locked and require items or progress from other sections. The Entrance Hall is revisited multiple times during Act I.

### Objective: Explore the Castle Wings

#### The Main Hall
Daniel enters the Entrance Hall from the Old Archives corridor.
- The hall is large with a grand staircase leading up to a mezzanine level. Several doors are visible.
- **Tinderbox**: On the mantelpiece of the fireplace on the hall's ground floor, right side.
- Light the fireplace using a tinderbox. The ground floor brightens significantly.
- **Note — Alexander's Welcome**: On the table in the hall's center. A formal but cold note, apparently left for Daniel by Alexander upon his original arrival.

#### The Mezzanine Level
Climb the grand staircase to the mezzanine.
- **Oil Container**: On the mezzanine's side table, left of the staircase landing.
- **Tinderbox**: In the drawer of the small writing desk on the mezzanine.
- **Diary Entry — Daniel's Journal, Arrival Entry**: On the writing desk. Describes Daniel's first impressions of the castle and Alexander's hospitality.
- Three doors are accessible from the mezzanine. Two are locked. The third leads to the Archives.

#### The Archives Door
The unlocked mezzanine door leads toward the Archives section.
- Before proceeding, thoroughly explore the Entrance Hall's accessible areas for tinderboxes and oil.
- **Note — Servant's Complaint**: On the floor near the locked east mezzanine door. A servant describes instructions to avoid specific castle sections after dark.
- Enter the Archives door to proceed.

## Act I: Archives

### Overview
The Archives is a larger and more complex section than the Old Archives — a multi-room complex of storage vaults, reading chambers, and document repositories. The first hints of genuine danger appear here.

### Objective: Find the Passageway to the Wine Cellar

#### The Document Vault
The Archives' primary room is a large vault with rows of document cabinets.
- **Tinderbox**: On top of the third document cabinet from the left on the vault's north wall (the tall cabinets). Requires climbing or jumping to reach the top.
- **Tinderbox**: In the bottom drawer of the desk in the vault's southwest corner.
- **Oil Container**: In the opened cabinet near the vault's entrance, on the middle shelf.
- **Note — Classified Research Abstract**: In the document cabinet marked with a red wax seal (on the vault's east wall). Describes Alexander's research into a substance called "Vitae" — the life energy extracted from suffering. This is a significant story document.
- A large painting on the vault's north wall depicts Alexander. Interacting with it causes a brief disturbance — a shadow crosses the painting. Sanity loss is minor but unavoidable.

#### The Reading Chambers
Two reading chambers adjoin the main vault.
- **Reading Chamber A** (left door): Contains a **Laudanum** on the armchair's side table.
- **Reading Chamber B** (right door): Contains a **Tinderbox** on the bookshelf's second shelf, and a **Note — Cipher Fragment** on the reading stand. The cipher fragment is part of a larger document referenced later.
- Both chamber doors are unlocked.

#### The Hidden Passage
The passage to the Wine Cellar is concealed behind a bookshelf in the document vault's southeast corner.
- Approach the southeast bookshelf. Look for a subtle gap along the wall — the shelf unit does not sit flush with the stone.
- Interact with the left edge of the bookshelf to push it aside (right-click hold and drag, or interact prompt on PlayStation). A narrow passage is revealed.
- **Tinderbox**: On a small natural stone ledge just inside the passage entrance.
- Follow the passage down a stone staircase. It leads to the Wine Cellar.

## Act I: Wine Cellar

### Overview
The Wine Cellar is a damp, underground section beneath the castle proper. Flooded sections, wine barrels, and storage racks define the environment. The Wine Cellar introduces a water management puzzle and the first environmental hazard: the dark water.

### The Dark Water Hazard
Certain pools of standing water in Amnesia are marked by an unnatural darkness — a black, inky quality distinguishable from normal puddles.
- **Do not enter dark water**. Contact with dark water causes rapid health loss and sanity loss simultaneously. It is instantly lethal if Daniel remains submerged.
- Normal water (clear puddles, shallow flooding) is harmless.
- Dark water sections must be bypassed using elevated surfaces, planks, barrels, or alternative routes.

### Objective: Drain the Wine Cellar Flooding

#### The Flooded Lower Cellar
Daniel descends into the Wine Cellar to find the lower section flooded with water.
- The water itself is clear — it is not the dark water hazard. However, it is deep enough to slow movement significantly.
- **Tinderbox**: On the wine rack shelf above the waterline on the flooded section's east wall — visible from the entry stairs. Climb the rack to collect.
- **Oil Container**: Floating in a wooden crate near the entry stairs. Interact to take it from the crate.

#### The Water Pump
A hand-operated water pump is located in the northwest corner of the lower cellar, on an elevated stone platform accessible via steps.
- The pump requires activation — interact with the pump handle and hold to operate.
- Operating the pump slowly drains the flooded section. The pump must be operated for approximately 30 seconds of continuous interaction.
- **Note — Cellar Worker's Log**: On the pump platform's work bench. Describes flooding incidents and a worker's fear of working alone in the cellar after sunset.
- After draining, the floor is revealed. Continue through the now-accessible south door.

#### The Dark Water Passage
The path through the drained cellar leads to a connecting passage that contains dark water.
- A wooden plank spans the dark water pool. It is narrow — walk carefully across it. Do not fall to either side.
- **Tinderbox**: On the stone ledge on the far side of the dark water plank crossing, before the passage exit.
- **Laudanum**: In the alcove on the right side of the passage, past the dark water crossing.
- Exit the passage into the Laboratory approach corridor.

## Act I: Laboratory

### Overview
The Laboratory is the first major puzzle area in Amnesia: The Dark Descent. It requires Daniel to create an acid solution capable of dissolving a corroded lock. Three chemical components must be located, and the puzzle involves mixing them in the correct vessel. The Laboratory also contains the game's first potential monster encounter.

### Objective: Create an Acid Solution

#### The Laboratory Main Room
A large stone laboratory with workbenches, equipment, and shelving.
- **Tinderbox**: On the workbench near the entrance, beside a glass beaker.
- **Tinderbox**: In the cabinet on the laboratory's south wall, bottom shelf.
- **Oil Container**: On the upper shelf of the ingredient cabinet on the east wall.
- **Note — Research Notes, Acid Formula**: On the main workbench. Describes the three components required for the acid solution: Oryx Tusk (base compound), Tar (binding agent), and Spirit of Clarity (catalyst). The notes include a warning that the mixture must be done in order and that all three components must be present before mixing begins.
- The **mixing vessel** is the large glass apparatus on the main workbench. Components must be placed into it in sequence.

#### Component 1: Oryx Tusk
- Located in the laboratory's specimen cabinet on the north wall. The cabinet is unlocked.
- The Oryx Tusk is a large curved horn-like object. Pick it up and add it to the inventory.

#### Component 2: Tar
- Located in a sealed container in the laboratory's storage room — accessible through the door on the west wall.
- The storage room is dark. Light a candle using a tinderbox before entering to maintain sanity.
- **Tinderbox**: On the storage room shelf, right side.
- The **Tar container** (a black sealed jar) is on the storage room's center shelf.
- **Note — Storage Inventory**: On the storage room's floor, partially under a shelf. A mundane inventory list — but circled in red is a notation about a restricted component taken from storage by Alexander personally.
- Return to the main laboratory with the Tar container.

#### Component 3: Spirit of Clarity
- This component is not immediately accessible. A locked cabinet in the laboratory's upper alcove contains it.
- The upper alcove is reached via a wooden stepladder on the laboratory's north side. Climb it.
- The cabinet lock is frozen with age — it requires the **small iron key** found in the drawer of the workbench beside the specimen cabinet (ground floor). Collect the key first.
- Return up the ladder. Use the key on the cabinet. The **Spirit of Clarity** (a glass flask of clear liquid) is inside.
- **Diary Entry — Laboratory Visit**: Also in the locked cabinet, beside the Spirit of Clarity. Daniel's own notes describe his first observation of Alexander's experiments and his initial fascination mixed with growing unease.

#### Mixing the Acid
- Return to the main workbench. Add components to the mixing vessel in order: Oryx Tusk first, then Tar, then Spirit of Clarity.
- Each component is added by interacting with the vessel while the component is in inventory.
- After all three are added, a reaction begins — the mixture produces the acid solution.
- The **Acid Solution** now appears in inventory as a filled glass flask.

---

### Objective: Dissolve the Corroded Lock

#### The Monster Encounter
The corroded lock is on the door at the laboratory's far end. However, before Daniel can reach it, an audio cue signals danger — a guttural sound from the corridor beyond the laboratory's west door.
- **First Monster Warning**: Stop moving immediately. Extinguish the lantern (F / Triangle). Move to the storage room and close the door.
- The monster — a Grunt — will enter the laboratory from the west door, move through the room, and exit through the east corridor after a short search.
- Do not peek out during the search. Do not move. Wait for the ambient music to return to normal and all footsteps to cease.
- **Critical**: Do not attempt to run past the Grunt. In the laboratory's confined space, it will catch Daniel.

#### Applying the Acid
After the Grunt leaves:
- Retrieve the Acid Solution from inventory.
- Approach the corroded lock on the far door. Interact to pour the acid solution on the lock.
- The lock dissolves over several seconds. The door opens.
- Exit through the door. Act I ends as Daniel descends further into Brennenburg Castle.

## Part 3: Walkthrough — Act II

## Act II: Refinery

### Overview
The Refinery is a large industrial section of Brennenburg Castle — a working facility that Daniel recognizes, in fragmented flashes, from his earlier time with Alexander. Stone furnaces, metal vats, and mechanical equipment dominate. The Refinery is actively dangerous: a Grunt patrols its main floor.

### The Grunt: Enemy Reference
Grunts are the most common enemy in Amnesia: The Dark Descent. They appear as malformed humanoid figures with exposed flesh and a shambling gait.
- **Detection**: Grunts detect Daniel through sight (line of vision, especially in lit areas) and sound (running, falling objects, door movement).
- **Response to Detection**: A Grunt will charge on sight. It is faster than Daniel in a sprint and lethal on contact.
- **Behavior When Searching**: After losing Daniel, a Grunt will search the last known location and nearby rooms before de-alerting. It may stand still for several seconds in a suspected hiding area — this is a search behavior, not a patrol pause.
- **Strategy**: Always hide before the Grunt enters a room. Crouch-walk, extinguish the lantern, and enter a closet or behind a large object. Do not move until you hear the Grunt's footsteps fully recede and the atmospheric music normalizes.

### Objective: Reach the Lower Refinery

#### The Upper Refinery
Daniel enters the Refinery from the Laboratory exit passage, emerging onto a raised walkway above the main floor.
- **Tinderbox**: On the walkway railing support bracket, near the walkway's midpoint.
- **Oil Container**: On the small supply table at the walkway's south end.
- **Note — Refinery Foreman's Log**: On the supply table beside the oil container. Describes production targets for Vitae and the foreman's refusal to ask questions about the source of the raw material.
- The walkway leads to a staircase descending to the main floor. A Grunt is patrolling the main floor below — observe from the walkway before descending.

#### Navigating the Grunt's Patrol
The Grunt on the main floor follows a consistent route between the east furnaces and the west storage alcove.
- **Patrol Route**: East furnace bank → center floor → west alcove → pause → return.
- The patrol cycle takes approximately 45 seconds.
- **Strategy**: Descend when the Grunt is at the east furnace bank — it faces away from the staircase. Crouch immediately on reaching the floor and move to the large wooden storage cabinet on the floor's west side. The cabinet can be used as a hiding position.
- **Tinderbox**: On the floor beside the center vat, visible from the walkway. Collect only when the Grunt is at the east furnace bank.
- **Oil Container**: Inside the west alcove, on the shelf. Collectible only when the Grunt has moved to the east section — approach and retreat quickly.

#### The Lower Refinery Access
The lower refinery is accessible via a heavy iron door in the floor's south wall — a set of stone steps descend from it.
- The door requires a **Brass Key** found in the foreman's office — a small room on the east side of the main floor, accessed via an unlocked door.
- **Strategy**: Enter the foreman's office only when the Grunt is at the west alcove. The office door is in the Grunt's sightline from the center floor — do not enter if the Grunt is facing the office.
- **Key — Brass Refinery Key**: On the foreman's desk.
- **Tinderbox**: In the foreman's desk drawer. Open the drawer with a right-click hold (grab/pull).
- **Diary Entry — Daniel's Journal, Refinery Memory**: On the foreman's desk. A disturbing memory — Daniel recalls assisting Alexander in the refinery and witnessing the process by which Vitae is extracted.
- Retrieve the key. Return to the iron floor door. Use the key. Descend into the lower refinery.

#### The Lower Refinery
A more confined section with narrow passages between machinery.
- **Tinderbox**: On the pipe housing along the lower refinery's north wall.
- **Laudanum**: In a wooden box tucked under the machinery platform, east side.
- **Note — Technical Specification**: On the machinery platform. Describes the mechanical process of Vitae extraction in clinical terms — its detachment from the human suffering it requires is the document's most disturbing quality.
- Follow the lower refinery passage to its exit. It leads to the Cellar Archives.

## Act II: Cellar Archives

### Overview
The Cellar Archives are the underground repository of Alexander's most sensitive research documents. They are partially flooded and structurally compromised. The area contains important story revelations about the Shadow — the entity that pursues Daniel throughout the game.

### The Shadow: Story Context
The Shadow is not a standard monster. It manifests as a creeping black mass that pursues Daniel through the castle, leaving organic growths and flesh-like protrusions in its wake.
- **Origin**: The Shadow was drawn to Daniel through his contact with an ancient artifact — one of the Orbs that Alexander has been collecting.
- **Function**: The Shadow is consuming Daniel slowly. The flesh growths it leaves behind mark areas where the Shadow has recently passed.
- **Avoidance**: The Shadow cannot be interacted with, hidden from, or fought. When it manifests directly, it is accompanied by a distinctive audio cue and screen distortion. Move away from its manifestation point.

### Objective: Find Alexander's Private Records

#### The Flooded Reading Chamber
Daniel enters the Cellar Archives to find the main reading chamber partially flooded with clear water.
- The water is ankle-deep — movement is slowed but the water is not the dark water hazard.
- **Tinderbox**: On the reading chamber's east bookshelf, second shelf from the top. The shelf is above the waterline.
- **Oil Container**: Floating in an overturned wooden box in the chamber's center.
- **Note — Research Record, Shadow Contact**: On a raised reading lectern (above the waterline). This document is a critical story item — it describes Alexander's theory that the Shadow follows whoever holds an activated Orb, and that the Shadow can be transferred to another person by deceiving them into activating an Orb themselves. This is the basis of Daniel's situation.

#### The Restricted Vault
A locked iron vault door on the chamber's south wall leads to Alexander's private records.
- The vault requires a combination — a three-digit code found in a note in the adjacent anteroom.
- **Anteroom**: Accessible via the unlocked west door. No monsters currently in the anteroom.
- **Tinderbox**: On the anteroom's writing desk.
- **Note — Vault Access Code**: On the anteroom's wall, pinned beneath a small candle sconce. The code is written in Alexander's precise handwriting.
- Return to the vault door. Enter the code using the three rotating dials on the vault face (interact with each dial and rotate to the correct number).
- The vault opens. Enter.

#### Alexander's Private Records
The vault interior is small — a stone alcove with shelves of documents.
- **Note — Alexander's Research Summary**: The primary document. Alexander writes candidly about his origin — he is not from Daniel's world but from a realm accessible only through the Orbs. He has been stranded for centuries and is using Vitae to power the portal that will return him home. The document reveals that Daniel volunteered to help Alexander in exchange for protection from the Shadow — and that Daniel subsequently helped Alexander capture victims for Vitae extraction before losing his nerve.
- **Tinderbox**: On the vault shelf, left side.
- **Laudanum**: In the vault's storage box on the floor.
- Exit the vault. The Cellar Archives section ends.

## Act II: Archive Tunnels

### Overview
The Archive Tunnels are a network of natural stone tunnels and constructed passages beneath the Cellar Archives. They are the most confined area encountered so far and contain the first encounter with the Water Lurker — a monster that inhabits flooded passages.

### The Water Lurker: Enemy Reference
The Water Lurker is an aquatic monster that inhabits dark water sections. It is invisible — it cannot be seen directly.
- **Detection**: It detects Daniel by proximity in dark water. If Daniel enters dark water, the Water Lurker will attack within seconds.
- **Behavior**: The Water Lurker will track Daniel's movement through the dark water. Entering dark water for any reason is fatal.
- **Avoidance**: Never enter dark water sections with Water Lurker presence. Use elevated surfaces, planks, barrels, and elevated passages to cross.
- **Sound Cues**: A distinctive rippling and breathing sound indicates Water Lurker proximity. If you hear it while on a surface above dark water, the Lurker is below — do not fall in.

### Objective: Navigate the Tunnel Network

#### The First Tunnel Section
The Archive Tunnels begin as a relatively wide natural passage with stone reinforcement.
- **Tinderbox**: On a stone ledge on the right side of the first tunnel section, approximately 15 meters from the entry point.
- **Oil Container**: In a wooden supply box at the tunnel's first junction.
- At the junction, the left passage is partially collapsed — only a crawl space remains. The right passage is clear but leads to a dark water section.

#### The Dark Water Crossing
The right passage opens into a larger cavern with a dark water pool occupying the floor.
- **Water Lurker Warning**: Rippling sounds are immediately audible. The Water Lurker is in this pool.
- A series of raised stepping stones and natural ledges crosses the pool. Each stone is separated by a short gap — jump carefully.
- **Strategy**: Move slowly between stepping stones. Do not sprint — misjudging a jump into the dark water is fatal. Plan each jump before taking it.
- **Tinderbox**: On the central stepping stone — a flat natural rock in the pool's center. Collect while crossing.
- Reach the far side of the pool. A tunnel continues forward.

#### The Collapsed Crawl Route
Return to the first junction. The crawl space in the left passage is the alternative route (useful if the stepping stones are difficult).
- Crawl through by crouching and moving forward. The passage is narrow and low — Daniel cannot stand upright.
- **Tinderbox**: Halfway through the crawl passage, on the left wall in a natural stone alcove.
- The crawl passage reconnects with the main tunnel past the dark water pool.

#### The Tunnel Exit
Both routes converge in a final tunnel section leading to a wooden door.
- **Note — Carved Wall Text**: On the tunnel wall near the exit door. Ancient text carved into the stone — Daniel cannot fully translate it, but fragmentary meaning comes through: a warning about a price to be paid.
- **Laudanum**: On the floor beside the exit door.
- Exit through the door into the Back Hall.

## Act II: Back Hall

### Overview
The Back Hall is a transitional area — a wide stone corridor connecting the tunnel network to the upper castle sections. It is partially damaged with rubble sections and a Grunt patrol.

### Objective: Find the Route to the Storage

#### The Back Hall Corridor
A long stone corridor with several side alcoves and a collapsed section roughly at its midpoint.
- **Tinderbox**: In the second alcove on the left side of the corridor (alcoves are numbered from the entry end).
- **Oil Container**: In the collapsed section's debris, visible as a glinting container partially under fallen stonework. Interact to retrieve.
- A Grunt patrols the corridor's second half — from the collapse to the far end.

#### The Grunt Patrol
The Back Hall Grunt patrols a shorter route than the Refinery Grunt — it moves from the corridor's midpoint to a side door and back.
- The side door is a wooden door the Grunt cannot open — it walks to the door, pauses, and returns. Use this pause to move past.
- **Strategy**: Wait for the Grunt to move toward the side door. When it faces the door, sprint past the midpoint and into the first side alcove past the collapse. Crouch and wait for the Grunt to pass on its return. Continue forward when it moves back toward the side door.
- **Tinderbox**: In the third alcove past the collapse, right side.
- **Note — Guard's Personal Note**: On the floor near the far end of the corridor, partially kicked under a broken piece of furniture. A castle guard's private note — he has decided to flee the castle. His body is not found; it is unclear if he escaped.
- The Back Hall exits through a door at the far end into the Storage area.

## Act II: Storage

### Overview
Storage is a large warehouse-like section of the castle — stacked crates, supply shelves, and equipment racks fill a vaulted stone room. It is a significant collectible area with multiple tinderboxes and oil containers distributed throughout. A Grunt patrols the main storage floor.

### Objective: Find the Route to the Guest Room

#### The Storage Main Floor
A large room with crate stacks and shelf units creating partial cover and visual obstacles.
- **Tinderbox**: On the supply shelf directly right of the entrance door, second shelf.
- **Tinderbox**: On top of the central crate stack — the tallest crate formation in the room's center. Climb the lower crates to reach the top.
- **Tinderbox**: In the drawer of the wooden workbench at the storage floor's far end.
- **Oil Container**: On the shelf behind the workbench at the far end.
- **Oil Container**: Inside a supply crate that has been partially opened — the crate is on the floor in the room's northwest corner. The lid can be lifted with a grab-and-pull interaction.
- **Laudanum**: On the top shelf of the tall storage rack on the room's east wall. Requires climbing the lower rack shelves to reach.
- **Note — Supply Manifest**: On the workbench. A standard castle supply list — but handwritten additions at the bottom, in a different hand, list "subjects" by number alongside standard provisions.

#### The Grunt's Patrol
The Storage Grunt has a longer patrol route than previous Grunts — it covers the full main floor in a figure-eight pattern around the central crate stack.
- The figure-eight means the Grunt has no consistent blind spot that persists for more than a few seconds. Timing is required for every collectible retrieval.
- **Strategy**: From the entrance, identify the Grunt's current position and direction. Collect items only when the Grunt's route takes it to the opposite side of the crate stack. Use the crate stack as persistent cover between movements.
- A second, smaller storage room is accessible via the door on the east wall. The door is unlocked.

#### The Side Storage Room
A smaller room off the main floor.
- No Grunt in this room.
- **Tinderbox**: On the side storage room's shelving unit, left side, second shelf.
- **Diary Entry — Daniel's Journal, Moral Crisis**: On the side storage room's table. Daniel writes about his growing horror at what Alexander's work requires and his first impulse to refuse — but Alexander convinced him to continue by emphasizing the Shadow's threat. This entry begins establishing Daniel's moral collapse.
- The Guest Room is accessible through the side storage room's far door.

## Act II: Guest Room

### Overview
The Guest Room is Daniel's original quarters during his stay at Brennenburg Castle — a furnished bedroom and sitting area that feels incongruously normal after the castle's industrial sections. Memory fragments play here. No monster is initially present.

### Objective: Recover the Key to Alexander's Study

#### The Sitting Room
Daniel enters the Guest Room's sitting area — a furnished room with armchairs, a writing desk, and a fireplace.
- **Tinderbox**: On the fireplace mantelpiece. Light the fireplace for significant sanity restoration in this room.
- **Oil Container**: On the writing desk.
- **Note — Alexander's Personal Invitation**: On the writing desk. The original letter Alexander sent to Daniel in London — courteous, intellectual, and inviting. Reading it now, with knowledge of what Alexander is, gives it a sinister quality.
- A **memory fragment** triggers when Daniel approaches the armchair — a brief visual and audio flashback to a conversation between Daniel and Alexander about the nature of the Orbs and the barrier between worlds.
- The bedroom is through the far door.

#### The Bedroom
Daniel's original sleeping quarters.
- **Tinderbox**: On the bedside table, right of the bed.
- **Laudanum**: In the bedside table's drawer. Open with a grab-pull interaction.
- **Diary Entry — Daniel's Journal, First Night**: On the writing desk in the bedroom's corner. Daniel's first night in the castle — his excitement about Alexander's research and his complete trust in his host.
- **Key — Study Key**: Hidden under the mattress of the bed. Interact with the mattress corner to lift it. The key is beneath.
- A second **memory fragment** triggers when Daniel picks up the Study Key — he briefly remembers hiding the key here deliberately, and why: he had intended to go to the Study to confront Alexander and needed the key, but lost his nerve at the last moment. He hid the key so his amnesiac self would find it.

## Act II: Study

### Overview
Alexander's personal study — a large, well-appointed room filled with books, correspondence, and research materials. The Study contains the game's most significant mid-game story revelations. No monsters are present.

### Objective: Read Alexander's Research

#### The Study Entry
The Study is accessed via the Guest Room corridor. Use the Study Key on the study door.
- The study is large — a central research table, wall-to-wall bookshelves, a globe, and several locked document cases.
- **Tinderbox**: On the research table's corner.
- **Tinderbox**: On the bookshelf's middle level, right side, beside a set of green-bound volumes.
- **Oil Container**: On the small side table near the window.

#### The Central Research Table
The most important documents in the game are on this table.
- **Note — Alexander's Letter to Daniel (Unsent)**: A letter Alexander wrote but never sent. In it, Alexander explains his true history honestly — his origin in another realm, his isolation, his centuries of work to build the portal. He writes that he chose Daniel because of Daniel's contact with an Orb in Africa and his resulting Shadow vulnerability — Daniel was already marked. The letter ends with an expression of what reads as genuine regret. Whether Alexander is sincere is left to the player.
- **Note — Orb Research Summary**: Describes the three Orbs Alexander has collected and the one remaining piece needed to complete the portal mechanism — the Orb in the Inner Sanctum.
- **Diary Entry — Daniel's Journal, The Bargain**: Daniel's account of the deal he struck with Alexander — Shadow protection in exchange for assistance with the Vitae process. Daniel writes that he told himself the victims were criminals, that they deserved it, that there was no other choice.

#### The Locked Document Case
A locked wooden document case on the study's east wall requires a small key found in a drawer of the research table.
- **Key — Document Case Key**: In the leftmost research table drawer.
- Open the document case.
- **Note — Vitae Formula (Complete)**: The complete technical document for the Vitae extraction process. Clinical and precise. The formula requires a living human subject to be subjected to extreme suffering — the suffering generates Vitae as a byproduct of the nervous system's response. Death of the subject ends Vitae production.
- **Note — Prisoner Roster**: A numbered list of individuals held in the castle's prison section, annotated with their Vitae yield and processing status. Daniel's name does not appear — he was a partner, not a prisoner. The distinction is cold comfort given what the roster describes.
- Reading all documents in the Study provides a full sanity restoration — the game treats understanding the truth as psychologically grounding, however horrific that truth is.
- Exit the Study. The Machine Room is the next destination.

## Act II: Machine Room

### Overview
The Machine Room is the largest and most mechanically complex area in Act II. It houses the primary machinery that powers Brennenburg Castle's lower sections. The puzzle requires getting a non-functional machine operational. The Machine Room is dark, hazardous, and heavily patrolled by a Grunt.

### Objective: Restore Power to the Lower Castle

#### The Machine Room Upper Level
Daniel enters the Machine Room on an upper platform overlooking the main machinery floor below.
- The room is large. The main machinery is a complex of gears, pistons, and a central drive mechanism, all non-functional.
- **Tinderbox**: On the upper platform's equipment rack, immediately right of the entry point.
- **Oil Container**: On the platform's far end, beside the staircase descending to the lower level.
- The lower level is visible from above — a Grunt is present on the main floor.

#### The Grunt on the Lower Floor
The Machine Room Grunt has a predictable but wide patrol that covers most of the floor.
- Its patrol passes the staircase landing on a regular interval — approximately every 30 seconds.
- **Strategy**: Descend the stairs only when the Grunt is at the floor's far end (west side). Move immediately to the large machine housing on the floor's east side — it provides full cover.

#### The Machine Components
The machine requires three components to be reattached or activated before it will run:

**Component 1 — Drive Belt**:
- The drive belt is detached and lying on the machine floor beside the main drive wheel. It is large and requires a grab-hold to move into position.
- Place the belt over the drive wheel pegs using a right-click grab and careful manipulation. The belt must sit flush on all peg points.
- **Tinderbox**: Under the machine floor grating near the drive wheel access point. Lift the grating with a grab-pull.

**Component 2 — Pressure Valve**:
- The pressure valve is a metal wheel-handle on the machine's north side. It has been turned to the closed position.
- Interact with the valve handle and rotate it to the open position (right-click hold and rotate in the direction indicated by the arrow on the valve housing).
- **Note — Machine Operator's Notes**: On a clipboard hung on the machine housing near the valve. Lists operational steps for the machine and notes that the emergency shutoff was triggered three weeks ago — with no further explanation.
- **Laudanum**: In the tool cabinet beside the valve, bottom shelf.

**Component 3 — Oil Reservoir**:
- The machine's oil reservoir is empty. It requires oil to be filled — not lantern oil, but a specific lubricant oil in a large metal can.
- The lubricant oil can is in the storage alcove on the machine floor's south side.
- **Tinderbox**: In the storage alcove, on the shelf above the lubricant cans.
- Carry the lubricant can to the machine's oil reservoir (a large funnel fitting on the machine's east face). Interact to pour.

#### Starting the Machine
With all three components addressed:
- Interact with the main activation lever on the machine's south side. Pull it down.
- The machine starts — a sequence of audio and visual cues signals successful activation.
- Power is restored to the lower castle sections. The gate to Act III areas is now unlocked.
- **Warning**: Starting the machine is loud. The Grunt on the floor is alerted and will search the immediate area for approximately 60 seconds. Hide in the storage alcove (close the door) and wait.
- After the Grunt's search subsides, exit the Machine Room through the newly unlocked gate on the floor's far end. Act II ends.

## Part 4: Walkthrough — Act III

## Act III: Prison

### Overview
The Prison is the darkest section of Brennenburg Castle — a functional detention facility beneath the machine levels. Daniel's flashback memories here are more sustained and more specific than any encountered previously. The Prison reveals what he actually did. Two sections must be traversed: Prison North and Prison South. A new, more dangerous enemy — the Brute — is introduced.

### The Brute: Enemy Reference
The Brute is a larger, stronger variant of the Grunt. It is slower but more durable and more alert.
- **Detection**: The Brute has a wider field of vision than the Grunt and is sensitive to any light source. Extinguish the lantern earlier than usual when a Brute is in the area.
- **Behavior When Searching**: The Brute searches more thoroughly than the Grunt — it will check closets by standing at the door for an extended period. If it stands at a closet door, hold still and do not breathe (hold the crouch position — do not release it).
- **Strategy**: The same hiding principles apply as with Grunts, but with greater discipline required. Do not move until the Brute has fully left the room and its footsteps have been silent for at least 10 seconds.

### Objective: Pass Through the Prison

#### Prison North — Entry Corridor
Daniel enters Prison North through the gate from the Machine Room exit passage.
- The corridor is a stone hallway lined with iron-barred cell doors on both sides.
- **Sanity Note**: The Prison is the game's most sanity-hostile environment. The darkness is deeper, the ambient sounds are more disturbing, and the environment details (chains, restraints, dried blood) are more explicit. Prioritize lighting candles whenever possible.
- **Tinderbox**: On the floor of the third cell on the left side. The cell door is open. Enter to collect.
- **Oil Container**: On the guard desk at the corridor's midpoint — a small station with a stool and a lantern mount.

#### Prison North — The Flashback
A sustained memory sequence triggers when Daniel reaches the guard desk.
- Daniel relives a specific event — he escorted a prisoner to Alexander for processing. He remembers the prisoner's face and name.
- **Story Beat**: This is the most explicit confirmation of Daniel's direct participation in Alexander's crimes. The Shadow was real, the threat was real, but so was Daniel's agency.
- The flashback ends. A Brute has entered the corridor from the far end during the flashback.

#### Navigating the Brute — Prison North
The Brute patrols from the far end of Prison North toward the guard desk.
- **Strategy**: Immediately after the flashback, enter the third open cell (the same cell where the tinderbox was) and close the cell door. Crouch. The Brute will walk past the closed cell door and proceed to the guard desk area before returning.
- Wait for the Brute to pass the cell door on its return trip and move back toward the far end. Exit the cell when it is at maximum distance and sprint for the south gate.
- **Tinderbox**: On the wall bracket beside the south gate. Collect while sprinting if possible.
- **Key — Prison South Gate Key**: On the hook beside the south gate — visible from the corridor. Interact to take. Use immediately on the gate.

#### Prison South — The Cell Block
Prison South is a wider cell block with a two-story structure — cells on a ground floor and a raised walkway with additional cells above.
- **Tinderbox**: On the walkway's second-level railing support. Access the walkway via the wooden stairs at the cell block's east end.
- **Tinderbox**: In a ground floor cell in the cell block's northwest corner. Door is ajar.
- **Laudanum**: On the ground floor in the central open area, on a small supply table.
- **Note — Prisoner's Scratched Text**: Found scratched into the stone wall of one of the ground-floor cells. A prisoner's final words — they describe what they heard and eventually understood about what was being done to them.
- A Brute patrols Prison South's lower floor.

#### Prison South — The Warden's Office
A warden's office in Prison South's east wing is accessible via the walkway.
- The office requires the warden's keycard — located on the ground floor in a guard cabinet beside the supply table. The cabinet is locked but can be opened by interacting with the lock mechanism (a puzzle: align the lock pins using right-click holds).
- **Warden's Office**: Contains the **Prison Master Key** needed for the exit gate. Also contains:
  - **Diary Entry — Daniel's Journal, Silence**: A short entry. Daniel writes only one sentence: "I told myself I didn't hear them."
  - **Tinderbox**: On the warden's desk.
  - **Oil Container**: On the office shelf.
- Retrieve the Prison Master Key. Exit Prison South through the main gate at the west end of the cell block using the Master Key. The Cistern is ahead.

## Act III: Cistern

### Overview
The Cistern is a large underground water reservoir — a vaulted stone chamber partially filled with water. The central water area is partially dark water. Platforms, walkways, and a mechanical water management system allow traversal. This is the most spatially complex area in Act III.

### Objective: Drain the Cistern's Dark Water

#### The Cistern Entry Platform
Daniel enters on an elevated stone platform above the cistern's water level.
- The water below is a mix: clear water near the entry platform, dark water in the cistern's center and far sections.
- **Tinderbox**: On the entry platform's left side, on the stone railing.
- **Oil Container**: In a niche in the platform's stone wall, right side.
- The Water Lurker is present in the cistern's water. Any dark water contact is fatal.

#### The Drainage Mechanism
The cistern's dark water can be partially drained using a mechanical valve system — two large wheel valves in different sections of the cistern.

**Valve 1 — North Platform**:
- Accessible via the wooden bridge on the entry platform's right side. The bridge is intact.
- **Tinderbox**: On the north platform's railing, midpoint.
- Interact with the north valve wheel and rotate fully. Water level in the north section drops, revealing a stone walkway previously submerged.

**Valve 2 — West Platform**:
- The west platform is accessible only via the newly revealed stone walkway after operating Valve 1.
- The walkway has a gap near its center — the gap is over dark water. Jump across carefully.
- **Strategy**: The jump is short but the penalty for missing is death. Take the jump from a full running start and aim for the right side of the walkway on the far side (the right edge is slightly wider).
- **Tinderbox**: On the west platform's equipment stand.
- **Laudanum**: In the west platform's supply locker.
- Interact with the west valve wheel and rotate fully. Water level drops in the west section. The cistern's dark water is now at a level low enough to traverse the center section on a series of stone pillars.

#### Crossing the Cistern Center
The center section now has a series of stone pillars visible above the reduced dark water level.
- Jump between pillars to cross. Each pillar is stable. Precision is required — falling between pillars is fatal.
- **Tinderbox**: On the third center pillar. Collect mid-crossing.
- Reach the far side. A door leads to the Control Room.

## Act III: Control Room

### Overview
The Control Room is a small but mechanically critical area — a stone chamber containing the control mechanisms for the Prison and Cistern sections. It is compact, well-lit relative to surrounding areas, and contains no monsters.

### Objective: Unlock the Path to the Morgue

#### The Control Panel
A large stone panel with levers and rotating mechanism controls.
- The panel is complex — multiple mechanisms, not all of which need to be operated.
- **Note — Control Room Instructions**: On the wall beside the panel. Written by an engineer — provides exact instructions for the correct lever sequence to open the gate toward the Morgue.
- **Lever Sequence**: Pull the left lever first, then the center lever, then the right lever. Wait 3 seconds between each pull for the mechanism to register.
- The gate to the Morgue section unlocks after the correct sequence.
- **Tinderbox**: On the control panel's left side, on the stone ledge beside the lever housing.
- **Oil Container**: On the floor behind the control panel, accessed by walking around the panel's right side.
- **Diary Entry — Daniel's Journal, Why I Did It**: On the writing desk in the control room's small anteroom. Daniel's most self-analytical diary entry — he examines his own motivations and finds them worse than he had told himself. He concludes that he helped Alexander not just from fear but from intellectual fascination. He acknowledges this and does not excuse it.
- Exit through the newly unlocked gate.

## Act III: Morgue

### Overview
The Morgue is the most disturbing single area in Amnesia: The Dark Descent. It is where Alexander's processed subjects were stored. The environment is severe — explicit and difficult. Sanity loss in the Morgue is continuous regardless of light level due to the disturbing environmental details. A Brute patrols the main morgue floor.

### Objective: Pass Through the Morgue

#### The Morgue Approach
A short passage leads from the Control Room exit to the Morgue's entry door.
- **Laudanum**: On the floor in the approach passage, beside the Morgue's entry door. Consume if health is low before entering.
- **Tinderbox**: On the wall bracket in the approach passage.
- Enter the Morgue. The ambient audio changes — sustained, low, and deeply unsettling.

#### The Morgue Main Chamber
A large stone room with stone examination tables, wall storage units, and chains suspended from the ceiling.
- **Sanity Note**: Sanity drains continuously here regardless of lighting. Light candles to slow (not stop) the drain.
- **Tinderbox**: On the first examination table immediately inside the entry door. Collect without delay.
- Light the candles on the examination tables using tinderboxes.
- **Tinderbox**: On the wall storage unit's middle shelf, east wall.
- **Oil Container**: On the floor in the northwest corner, behind the storage unit.
- **Note — Processing Record, Final Entry**: In the wall storage unit. The final entry in a clinical record of Alexander's processing operations. The record ends mid-sentence. Whatever happened at that final session was significant enough to stop the record-keeper from completing their note.
- The Brute is in the morgue's south chamber — a connected room accessible via the south arch.

#### Navigating the Brute — Morgue
The Morgue Brute does not patrol — it is stationary, positioned in the south chamber, apparently examining one of the tables.
- To exit the Morgue, Daniel must pass through the south chamber.
- The Brute is facing away from the south chamber's entry arch.
- **Strategy**: Extinguish the lantern completely. Crouch. Move through the entry arch when the Brute's head is down (it periodically looks up — wait for the down position). Move along the south chamber's east wall — the wall furthest from the Brute. Reach the exit door on the south chamber's far end without passing through the Brute's field of vision.
- Do not touch any objects on the south chamber's tables — any interaction causes sound that will alert the Brute.
- Exit through the south door. The Sewer entrance is ahead.

## Act III: Sewer

### Overview
The Sewer is the final section of Act III — a network of underground water channels and stone passages that lead toward the castle's deeper sanctum. The Sewer is dark, water-filled, and contains both Water Lurker hazards and a Grunt patrol. It is the transition point between the castle's working sections and its ancient inner areas.

### Objective: Reach the Nave

#### The Upper Sewer Walkway
Daniel enters on a narrow stone walkway above the sewer channels. The channels below contain dark water.
- **Tinderbox**: On the walkway's first branching point, left branch, on the stone railing.
- **Oil Container**: On the walkway's right branch at the branching point, on the wall mount.
- The walkway continues forward — do not descend into the channels.
- A Grunt enters from the far end of the upper walkway after Daniel has walked approximately 30 meters.

#### The Grunt on the Walkway
The Sewer Grunt patrols the upper walkway's second half — from the midpoint to the far gate.
- The walkway is narrow. There is no wide cover available. The only hiding option is a recessed alcove in the walkway's right wall approximately 10 meters before the midpoint.
- **Strategy**: Wait in the alcove for the Grunt to pass on its patrol toward the entry end. When it passes the alcove, remain still until it moves out of sight. Then move forward — the Grunt will be behind you. Reach the far gate before it returns.
- **Tinderbox**: On the walkway's far end, beside the gate controls.
- The gate requires a lever to open — interact with the lever beside the gate.

#### The Lower Sewer Passages
Through the gate, Daniel descends to the lower sewer level — narrow passages between the water channels.
- The lower passages are above the dark water level but very close to it. Do not fall.
- **Water Lurker Warning**: A rippling sound is audible from the channels below. The Water Lurker is active in the lower sewer channels.
- **Tinderbox**: In a stone alcove in the lower passage's right side, approximately halfway through.
- **Laudanum**: In a wooden supply box at the lower passage's junction with the ascending staircase.
- **Note — Ancient Carved Seal**: On the wall near the staircase. A carved stone relief of an unfamiliar symbol — the same symbol that appeared in the Archive Tunnels' carved wall text. No in-game translation is provided, but the symbol is associated with the Shadow in later documents.

#### The Sewer Exit Staircase
An ascending stone staircase at the lower passage's far end leads upward out of the Sewer section.
- **Tinderbox**: On the staircase's landing, midway up.
- At the top of the staircase, a large wooden door opens into the Nave. Act III ends.

## Part 5: Walkthrough — Act IV

## Act IV: Nave

### Overview
The Nave is the great central hall of Brennenburg Castle's ancient chapel — an enormous vaulted chamber that predates the castle's more recent construction by centuries. The architecture is older, more worn, and more alien in quality than anything Daniel has seen. The Nave marks the beginning of the end. The Shadow's presence is stronger here — organic growths and flesh-like protrusions mark the walls in greater density than in any previous area.

### Story Context: The Final Descent
At this point in the game, Daniel knows the full truth. He helped Alexander. He participated in the Vitae extraction. He erased his own memory as a moral escape from what he had done, leaving himself a note to finish what he could not complete consciously: kill Alexander and destroy the ritual. The remaining acts are the execution of that intention.

### Objective: Reach the Transept

#### The Nave Entry
The large wooden door from the Sewer staircase opens directly into the Nave's south end.
- The Nave is vast. Torches on the walls are unlit — the lantern is essential.
- **Tinderbox**: On a stone pillar base at the Nave's entry, left side. Light the pillar's torch mount if desired.
- **Oil Container**: In a stone alcove on the Nave's west wall, first alcove from the south end.
- The Shadow's flesh growths cover the east wall in the Nave's center section. Sanity loss occurs when standing within close range of these growths — treat them as environmental hazard zones.
- **Note — Daniel's Final Memo to Himself**: On a small stone pedestal near the Nave's center. This is the last note Daniel left himself — a sparse, determined message. It contains only one instruction: "Finish it."

#### The Nave Monsters
The Nave contains both a Grunt and a Brute, operating in different sections.
- **Grunt**: Patrols the Nave's south half — from the entry to the center.
- **Brute**: Stationary in the Nave's north alcove, near the transept doors.
- Both can be avoided with careful timing and lantern management.
- **Strategy for the Grunt**: The Nave's large central pillars provide full-body cover. Move pillar-to-pillar when the Grunt faces away.
- **Strategy for the Brute**: The north alcove is offset from the main path. If you move directly toward the transept doors along the Nave's center aisle, you stay outside the Brute's standard field of vision.

#### The Transept Doors
At the Nave's north end, a pair of large stone doors leads to the Transept.
- **Tinderbox**: In the stone niche immediately left of the transept doors.
- **Laudanum**: On the floor at the base of the right transept door.
- The doors are unlocked. Push them open (right-click hold, drag forward). Enter.

## Act IV: Transept

### Overview
The Transept is the lateral arm of the chapel — a wide, cross-shaped chamber intersecting the Nave at a right angle. It is partially flooded and contains a significant puzzle involving a broken water system and a submerged passage.

### Objective: Restore Access to the Choir

#### The Flooded Floor
The Transept's lower section is flooded with clear water — not dark water. It is shallow and harmless but slows movement.
- **Tinderbox**: On a stone ledge above the flood level on the Transept's east wall, accessible by climbing the stone steps at the room's corner.
- **Oil Container**: Floating in a wooden crate in the Transept's center.
- The water source is a broken aqueduct valve on the Transept's north wall — water pours from a damaged stone channel.

#### The Aqueduct Valve Puzzle
The valve can be closed to stop the inflow, but the existing flood must be drained separately.
- **Step 1 — Close the Inflow Valve**: Interact with the aqueduct valve wheel on the north wall. Rotate clockwise to close. The water stops flowing in.
- **Step 2 — Open the Drainage Sluice**: A drainage sluice gate is on the Transept's south wall, partially submerged. Crouch and wade to it. Interact with the sluice handle (it requires two right-click interactions in sequence — pull, then push up). The sluice opens and water begins to drain.
- Draining takes approximately 45 seconds. Wait.
- **Tinderbox**: On the Transept floor, revealed after draining, near the sluice gate.
- **Note — Ancient Chapel Record**: On a stone shelf on the east wall, revealed after draining. In an older form of German — Daniel can read it partially. It describes the chapel's construction and references a sealed chamber beneath the chancel that predates the chapel itself by an unknown period.

#### The Submerged Passage
After draining, a passage visible through an iron grate in the Transept floor becomes accessible.
- Interact with the grate to open it. A stone ladder descends.
- The passage below is not flooded — only the Transept above was.
- The passage leads under the chapel to the Choir entry.
- **Tinderbox**: In the passage below the Transept floor, on the left wall.
- Follow the passage to the Choir access door.

## Act IV: Choir

### Overview
The Choir is the section of the chapel where choristers would have sung — a raised section behind the main altar with wooden choir stalls and a large pipe organ. It is now silent and badly deteriorated. A puzzle involving the organ pipe mechanism must be solved to unlock the passage to the Cells.

### Objective: Open the Passage to the Cells

#### The Choir Entry
Daniel climbs from the Transept passage into the Choir's lower level.
- The Choir is dimly lit by moonlight through damaged rose windows. No monsters are immediately present.
- **Oil Container**: On the choir master's stand at the Choir's entry level.
- **Tinderbox**: On the end of the front choir stall row, right side.

#### The Pipe Organ Puzzle
The Choir's large pipe organ is connected to a mechanism that operates the passage gate to the Cells. The organ is non-functional — its bellows system has failed.
- **Identifying the Problem**: Interact with the organ keyboard. No sound is produced. A note on the organ's music stand explains the issue.
- **Note — Choir Master's Technical Notes**: On the organ music stand. Describes the bellows mechanism and identifies that the bellows feed pipe, located in the organ loft above, is disconnected.

**Reaching the Organ Loft**:
- The organ loft is accessible via a wooden staircase at the Choir's east side.
- **Tinderbox**: At the top of the loft staircase landing.
- **Laudanum**: On the loft's wooden floor, near the bellows assembly.
- The bellows feed pipe is a large flexible leather tube — one end is detached from the organ's air manifold.

**Reattaching the Bellows**:
- The detached end of the bellows pipe must be reconnected to a fitting on the manifold's upper surface.
- Use right-click grab to pick up the pipe end and guide it to the manifold fitting. It requires manual positioning — move carefully to align the pipe end with the fitting connector and release.
- Once attached, the bellows seal with an audible click.

**Playing the Mechanism Sequence**:
- Descend to the organ keyboard.
- The organ now functions. Interact with the keyboard and play the three-key sequence indicated by the marked keys (circled in chalk on the keys themselves): Low C, Middle A, High F.
- The passage gate mechanism activates. A grinding sound signals the gate opening.
- **Note**: If the sequence is entered incorrectly, a discordant tone plays and the sequence resets. Try again.
- Exit the Choir through the now-open passage gate on the Choir's west wall.

## Act IV: Cells

### Overview
The Cells are a series of monastic living quarters — small stone rooms where the chapel's monks would have resided. Several cells are still furnished with rotted beds and personal effects. The Cells also contain the game's final story documents and the encounter with Agrippa — a crucial character for one of the game's three endings.

### Meeting Agrippa

#### Who Is Agrippa?
Herbert Agrippa is a historical scholar who, according to the game's lore, encountered Alexander centuries ago and entered into a bargain with him — similar to Daniel's. He has been kept alive by Alexander's magic for hundreds of years, though not healthily — he is a barely-living remnant, imprisoned in the Cells.
- Agrippa is not hostile. He speaks to Daniel and provides key context.
- **Story Beat**: Agrippa reveals that his apprentice, Johann Weyer, is on the other side of the barrier that Alexander's portal opens — in the realm Alexander came from. If Daniel can find a way to send something to Weyer, Weyer could pull Daniel to safety through the portal rather than leaving Daniel to be consumed by the Shadow.
- **Agrippa's Request**: He asks Daniel to prepare a tonic from ingredients in the Inner Sanctum and bring it to him. Drinking the tonic will free Agrippa — and he will then provide Daniel with a piece of himself (his teeth/mouthpiece) to throw through the portal to signal Weyer.
- **This triggers the Agrippa Ending path**. Completing Agrippa's request is optional — you can proceed without it and take one of the other two endings.

#### The Cells Area
- **Tinderbox**: In the second cell from the entry, on the rotted bed frame.
- **Tinderbox**: In the fifth cell, on a small shelf above a rotted writing desk.
- **Oil Container**: On the floor in the cells corridor, midway through.
- **Laudanum**: In a wooden box in the cells' end room (the room where Agrippa is).
- **Diary Entry — Daniel's Journal, I Remember**: In the cells corridor, on the floor near Agrippa's room. Daniel writes that his memories are returning in full — not just fragments. He describes what it feels like to remember everything.
- **Note — Agrippa's Account**: In Agrippa's room, on the floor beside him. His own written account of his centuries of imprisonment — composed with remarkable intellectual calm given its content.
- Exit the Cells through the door at the far end. The Chancel is ahead.

## Act IV: Chancel

### Overview
The Chancel is the sanctuary section of the chapel — the area immediately before the altar and the Inner Sanctum entrance. It is the most monster-dense area in Act IV and requires careful navigation. The Chancel is also where the Castle's structural collapse has progressed furthest — sections of the ceiling have fallen and fires burn in isolated areas.

### Objective: Reach the Inner Sanctum

#### The Chancel Entry
The door from the Cells opens into the Chancel's outer section.
- The Chancel is partially collapsed. Several ceiling sections have fallen, creating rubble obstacles and opening new sight lines for monsters.
- **Sanity Warning**: Fire in the Chancel creates bright light — standing near fires slows sanity loss. However, the fires also create noise and visual disruption. Monsters are not affected by fire.
- **Tinderbox**: On a fallen ceiling section (now a ramp of stone debris) on the Chancel's west side, at the ramp's midpoint.
- **Oil Container**: In the Chancel's far right corner, behind a fallen pillar.
- Two Grunts are in the Chancel — one in the outer section, one in the inner section near the altar.

#### Navigating the Chancel Grunts
The two Grunts have overlapping patrol routes in the Chancel's center.
- **Outer Grunt**: Moves between the Chancel's entry side and the center rubble pile.
- **Inner Grunt**: Moves between the center rubble and the altar area.
- Both are active simultaneously.
- **Strategy**: Use the rubble piles as cover. Move only when both Grunts are moving away from your position. If one Grunt is moving toward you while the other is moving away, wait — both must be facing away before advancing.
- **Tinderbox**: On the altar table in the Chancel's inner section. Collect only when both Grunts are at the outer and center positions simultaneously.

#### The Inner Sanctum Door
The door to the Inner Sanctum is at the Chancel's far end, behind the altar.
- The door is locked — it requires the **Chancel Key** hanging on the wall beside the altar.
- **Key — Chancel Key**: On a hook on the wall beside the altar's left side. Interact to take.
- **Laudanum**: On the altar surface. Collect while retrieving the key.
- Use the Chancel Key on the Inner Sanctum door. The door opens. Enter.

## Act IV: Inner Sanctum

### Overview
The Inner Sanctum is Alexander's ritual chamber — the heart of Brennenburg Castle. It is an ancient, pre-castle chamber carved from the bedrock beneath the chapel. Alchemical symbols and Vitae channels are carved into every surface. The Orb is here. This is the point of no return — once Daniel proceeds past the Inner Sanctum's threshold, the game moves directly toward one of its three endings.

### The Agrippa Tonic (Agrippa Ending Prerequisite)
Before interacting with the Orb, if pursuing the Agrippa ending:
- Two tonic ingredients are in the Inner Sanctum:
  - **Ingredient 1 — Agrippa's Tonic Base**: On a stone shelf on the Inner Sanctum's west wall.
  - **Ingredient 2 — Binding Catalyst**: In a sealed vial on the ritual table at the Sanctum's center.
- Combine them in the mixing bowl on the ritual table. The tonic is automatically prepared.
- Return to the Cells and give the tonic to Agrippa. He drinks it, thanks Daniel, and gives him the mouthpiece. Carry the mouthpiece in inventory.
- Return to the Inner Sanctum.

### Collectibles — Inner Sanctum
- **Tinderbox**: On the ritual table's surface, beside the mixing bowl.
- **Tinderbox**: In the stone niche on the Inner Sanctum's east wall.
- **Oil Container**: In the supply alcove off the Inner Sanctum's north side.
- **Laudanum**: On the floor at the base of the Orb pedestal.
- **Note — Alexander's Final Instructions**: On the ritual table. A technical document — Alexander's precise instructions for the final activation sequence. Reading it, Daniel understands exactly what Alexander has been building toward and how close it is to completion.
- **Diary Entry — Daniel's Last Entry**: On the floor near the entry door. A single-line diary entry: "I know what I have to do."

### Approaching the Orb
The Orb sits on a stone pedestal at the Inner Sanctum's center. It is the same type of artifact Daniel originally encountered in Africa.
- When Daniel approaches the Orb, the Shadow manifests strongly — screen distortion and audio disturbance are at maximum intensity.
- **Decision Point**: Interacting with the Orb triggers the endgame sequence. Ensure all desired collectibles are retrieved before interacting.
- Interact with the Orb. A cutscene triggers. Alexander appears — not physically, but as a projected presence. He knows Daniel is there. He tells Daniel that the ritual is nearly complete and that Daniel cannot stop it now.
- Daniel destroys the Orb. The ritual chamber begins to destabilize.
- Exit through the passage the Orb's destruction opens — it leads to the Orb Chamber.

## Act IV: Orb Chamber

### Overview
The Orb Chamber is the game's final area — a deep, primordial chamber beneath the Inner Sanctum. Alexander has been performing the final stage of his portal ritual here. The chamber is massive, ancient, and beyond the architecture of any human construction. The portal is nearly complete.

### The Three Endings

#### Ending 1 — Redemption (Main Ending)
Daniel confronts Alexander in the Orb Chamber as the portal completes.
- **Story**: Daniel calls out to the Shadow — the entity that has been pursuing him through the castle. He acknowledges what he did, accepts responsibility, and offers himself to the Shadow in exchange for Alexander.
- **Outcome**: The Shadow turns from Daniel and attacks Alexander. Alexander is consumed. The castle begins to collapse. Daniel escapes through the crumbling passages back to the surface. He emerges into daylight — the first light seen in the game — with his memories fully restored. He walks forward. The game ends.
- **Tone**: This ending frames Daniel's survival as a result of accountability — he did not escape justice but faced it and was, in some interpretation, granted mercy.

#### Ending 2 — Agrippa's Ending (Secret Ending)
This ending is available only if Daniel completed the Agrippa tonic quest and carries Agrippa's mouthpiece.
- **Story**: As Alexander opens the portal, Daniel throws Agrippa's mouthpiece through the portal opening. On the other side, Weyer — Agrippa's apprentice — receives it and recognizes what it means. He reaches through the portal and pulls Daniel to safety in his realm.
- **Outcome**: Daniel does not return to the surface. He is taken to Weyer's realm. What happens to Alexander is not shown. Whether Daniel faces any consequence for his actions is unresolved.
- **Tone**: This is the most morally ambiguous ending — Daniel escapes the Shadow's judgment by a technicality. It is also the most cinematically hopeful, as Daniel is actively saved rather than merely surviving.
- **Mechanical Requirement**: Agrippa's mouthpiece must be in inventory. When the portal opens, an interaction prompt appears: throw the mouthpiece through the portal. Do not miss the prompt — it is time-limited.

#### Ending 3 — Damnation (Bad Ending)
This ending triggers if Daniel does not destroy the Orb and allows Alexander to complete the ritual.
- **Story**: Alexander completes the portal and steps through it, escaping to his home realm. He thanks Daniel — sincerely or ironically — as the portal closes behind him.
- **Outcome**: With the ritual complete and Alexander gone, the Shadow has no one to divert it. It catches Daniel. Daniel is consumed. The game ends.
- **Tone**: The bad ending is brief and final. There is no cutscene of Daniel's fate — only the Shadow's arrival and darkness.

### Completion Notes

> **Hard Mode**: Available from the main menu before starting a new game. Hard Mode does not change enemy behavior or puzzle difficulty. It reduces the amount of oil and tinderboxes found in the environment, making resource management significantly more challenging. Sanity loss may also be accelerated. Hard Mode is the recommended second playthrough mode.

> **Custom Story Mode**: Amnesia: The Dark Descent includes a built-in custom story editor and community story support. From the main menu, select Custom Story to install and play community-created content. The Steam Workshop hosts hundreds of custom stories of varying quality and length.

> **Achievement — Illuminatus (PlayStation)**: Collect all tinderboxes in a single playthrough. Tinderboxes are consumed on use — they must be collected before being used to light static sources. See the Collectibles Guide for area-by-area tinderbox locations.

> **Achievement — Master Archivist (PlayStation)**: Collect all notes in a single playthrough. Notes auto-save to the Notes inventory tab on collection — they do not need to be read at a save station. See the Collectibles Guide for all note locations.

> **Achievement — Agrippa's Friend**: Complete the Agrippa ending. Requires: completing the tonic quest in the Cells, returning to give Agrippa the tonic, carrying the mouthpiece to the Orb Chamber, and using it when the portal opens.

> **New Game+ Note**: Amnesia: The Dark Descent does not have a formal New Game+ mode. To collect all endings, replay the game from the beginning or use chapter select (if available). The Agrippa ending and the Redemption ending can both be pursued in a single playthrough — pursue Agrippa's quest first, then use the portal prompt for the Agrippa ending; alternatively, ignore the mouthpiece prompt to get the Redemption ending.
