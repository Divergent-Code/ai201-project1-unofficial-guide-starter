## 1.1 Welcome

        
  Hello and welcome to my guide to Darkwood, by Acid Wizard Studios, released in
2017. This guide includes a FAQ section, a full walkthrough of the campaign, and
various other details to help you out with all of the most important stuff.

  This game is surprisingly complicated in some ways, and so this guide does not
include every possible detail about every single thing. I tend to focus on what
I think is an optimal playstyle, based on a great many of my own playthroughs
and testing. Therefore I suggest certain items and strategies over others, and
leave the rest up to you to explore if you want. However, if you do want a ton
of information which is not necessarily in this guide (including stats), I
recommend you check out the excellent Darkwood wiki, found here:

  https://darkwood.fandom.com/wiki/Darkwood_Wiki

  I made use of this site many times when writing this guide and it helped me
out a lot, so this document couldn't exist without the wiki authors' help.
Thank-you very much to all of those contributors! Having said that, much of the
information in the wiki is outdated and wrong, largely because the game took so
long to come out from 2014 to 2017, and details kept changing, with new things
being added and old things being removed. I have done my best to tidy up all of
the errors found there, and since the game is no longer being updated I believe
this guide will remain the most current guide to Darkwood. However, if you do
find any errors, please contact me and I may correct the guide accordingly.

                          2. GAME INFORMATION & TIPS
        

  This reference section of the guide is for bits and pieces which don't quite
fit into the Walkthrough section, but which you might want to know anyway.

### Saves

        
  Darkwood does not allow you to save whenever you like, but it does save on its
own each time you interact with an Oven or an NPC, whenever you leave one zone
to travel to a separate one, any time you die and respawn at the Hideout, and
any time you make it all through the night and the new morning begins. However,
each time it saves it overwrites the previous file, meaning mistakes are
permanent unless you can find some other way to restore your game from an
earlier save. You have to do this outside of the game, by finding the game's
save files in this location:

C:\Users\[username]\AppData\LocalLow\Acid Wizard Studio\Darkwood\1_4Save

  (Note that [username] is replaced by whatever your Windows user folder is
called.) 

  This root folder holds five other folders, corresponding to the five possible
profile slots you see when starting a new game or reloading an existing one.
This means that any time the game saves you can close it and then make a backup
of your profile folder, renaming it to something you recognise. If something
goes badly wrong you can then delete the main profile folder and replace it with
your backup, making sure you change the folder name back again.

  Consider whether or not you want to overwrite your save before you talk to
anyone or move between zones; and of course if you desperately need to save and
are nowhere near an Oven, talking to someone will serve.

# 2.1 Interface

  The controls in Darkwood can often feel like the weakest part of the game.
It's important to be able to get the Protagonist to do what you want him to do
as quickly and efficiently as possible, as opening and closing the inventory
takes ages and does not pause the game.

  The Options allow you to enable Relative Controls. This means that the left /
right keys will behave according to the orientation of the Protagonist, rather
than being fixed to the screen layout.

### Keyboard Controls

        
  W A S D: These keys move the Protagonist around at walking pace, but cannot be
used to turn him around.
  Q: This brings up the active Skill menu and closes it again. It can be used to
slow the game way down if you need a second to think.
  E: This allows you to cycle among multiple containers and other interactable
elements of the environment in situations where they are placed on top of one
another. If you hover your cursor over an interactable and see small arrow
symbols that indicates that you have multiple things to choose from by pressing
E.
  R: Firearms can be reloaded from ammo in your inventory by selecting the
weapon from your hotbar, holding down the right mouse button, then pressing R to
reload. This same key also allows you to reload flashlights with batteries.
  J: This brings up the journal and pauses the game.
  M: This brings up the map and pauses the game.
  1 - 7: These are the hotbar hotkeys, allowing you to select the hotbar item
you want immediately, rather than left-clicking or wheel cycling.
  TAB: This opens the inventory.
  SPACE: This allows the Protagonist to hop over some low obstacles, such as
open windows and low fences. He does not have to be facing the obstacle,
although the already long animation will take even longer when he has to turn to
face it first. Climbing in this way takes a long time and leaves the Protagonist
vulnerable to attack, but he will have fully recovered his stamina by the time
the animation is over.
  Left SHIFT: This key is used to sprint to the limits of the Protagonist's
endurance / stamina bar. Just pressing it and then releasing it pauses the
stamina bar's recovery, so only press and hold it when you mean to use it. Its
other function is to allow you to transfer the entire contents of one inventory
slot to another.
  Left CTRL: This key triggers a backdash, allowing the Protagonist to dodge
trouble on reaction. Melée attacks can be cancelled into it at any point,
potentially conserving stamina and allowing the Protagonist to attack and
retreat at the same time. Another use for the backdash is that it allows the
Protagonist to traverse dangerous terrain more quickly than a sprint (although
he cannot use it to dodge traps or mushrooms). Its other function is to allow
you to transfer single units from one inventory stack to another.

### Mouse Controls

        
  Left: This is used all the time to interact with the UI, the NPCs and the
world, as well as to trigger charged weapon and item functions. The mouse cursor
also appears on the screen as a dynamic dot, controlled by the left mouse
button. It shows you exactly where the Protagonist is looking, and dictates the
direction in which he is facing; it also gives you information about any
interactables on top of which it is placed, so long as the Protagonist is not
sprinting at the time. The mouse is how you turn him around. Knowing where to
point the cursor is especially important because the Protagonist's field of view
does not necessarily show you everything within it: overhanging trees will
obscure anything within the field of view unless the cursor is specifically
aiming underneath them. Try this out while under a canopy of trees and you
should see that the Protagonist can now only see the small circle around the
cursor. Constantly monitoring the position of the cursor will allow him to see
enemies and traps while under the trees; don't just rely on the field of vision
cone. When moving under trees keep the cursor hovering about ten yards directly
in front of him.

  Right: This is exclusively for operating weapons and other items which you
have selected from the hotbar. Hold it down and the Protagonist will perform the
relevant action if one is available. Press it to turn selected flashlights on
and off.

  Middle: When pressed it activates the quick attack of your selected melée
weapon. The wheel function allows you to cycle among your hotbar items.
        

### Miscellaneous

                
  The map (M) is dynamic in its way. Hover the mouse cursor over discovered
Locations to see their names. A Location lit in red indicates your rough
position, allowing you to better navigate from there. Most Locations must be
discovered by visiting them, although some will be marked on the map upon
speaking to certain NPCs or using certain key items, such as maps. A red dot
indicates any inventory you dropped when you died. A red cross is produced on
the spot when you use your Navigator Skill. Note that you can click and drag on
the map to move it most of the way off the screen, allowing you to assess your
surroundings: this is useful for triggering the map when you get ambushed in
order to pause the game, especially at night.

  The journal allows you to keep track of your character and his progress. The
Me tab shows all the Skills you have selected when levelling up; the Journal tab
is the Protagonist's diary, mainly useful for story and lore; the Items and Keys
tabs show any key items you have found and also allows you to click on them to
open a description, very important for remembering keycodes. Click on any empty
space to turn the page back or forward.

  Your health bar is shown in red at the top left of the screen. You can gain an
extra half-bar of permanent health by choosing the late game Vitality Skill.
        
  The endurance / stamina bar is underneath the health bar, marked in white.
This is depleted every time your character performs any physically stressful
action beyond simply walking; although climbing over an obstacle will replenish
it, as will walking or standing still. When it is emptied completely the
Protagonist is unable to run or fight, meaning you have to carefully watch this
bar and choose your actions carefully in combat to prevent it from ever running
out.

  Pressing TAB opens the inventory. This will take about twice as long if the
Protagonist is holding a weapon at the time, since he has to put it away first
and then open the inventory. This argues for keeping one hotbar slot free, then
selecting it each time before opening the inventory.

  You can drop items from the inventory and hotbar into the ground by
left-clicking on them and then clicking somewhere outside the inventory display.
This will drop the items on the ground until you pick them up again.

# 2.2 Skills

  New Skills are acquired by spending essence at any Oven. Essence comes in the
form of hallucinogens: rooms, embryos, odd meat, insects, rats, fish and chicken
eggs. Once the fourth and final Skill level has been reached hallucinogens
cannot be spent at the Oven.

  Gaining more essence - and therefore more Skills - is generally a good thing
to do and a matter of some priority, especially since most essence-related items
will rot if unused. However, each new Skill level reached will also demand that
the Protagonist chooses a deleterious debuff along with his new Skill, meaning
one disadvantage for every two advantages.

  Your active Skill menu is accessed by pressing Q at any time, which also has
the effect of greatly slowing down the game, buying you a little time to think.
Active Skills can only be used once per day (the period starting each new
morning and ending with each following night), and another Skill cannot be
triggered while the effects of the previous one are still active.

### 2.2.1 Level 1

                
    Positive:   

  Eagle Eye: Your range of vision increases, allowing you to hover the mouse
cursor over any spot on screen and see what's there from maximum distance. This
is particularly strong in the Swamp, where you cannot afford to be running into
areas full of poison mushrooms, human spiders, etc. The scouting Eagle Eye
offers is a permanent buff, never not useful, and often the difference between
life and death. This is probably the best Skill in the game and should be picked
at the first opportunity.

  Moth: This is mainly for tricky night encounters, allowing you to trigger a 45
second burst of healing which endures while standing next to any lit
electrically powered light (including the wall lights in the Old Woods Hideout).
This skill is useless outside of this situation, and bandages and other slow
curatives are better alternatives since they work no matter where you are.
Besides, your well is right outside anyway and serves the same purpose. The
healing effect only works during this period if you are standing next to an
electric light source, making it mostly useful for moving around Hideouts with
multiple lit lamps, leapfrogging from heal to heal. If your Generator runs out
of fuel this Skill becomes worthless; and of course while it is active you
cannot trigger any other Skills. It's not bad to have more healing, but other
Skills are just better.

  Navigator: This places a red marker on the map, telling you exactly where you
are. Most Locations serve the same purpose and can be 'used' infinitely, and
it's rare that you will ever be this lost outside the Swamp. What it doesn't
tell you is that the Protagonist drops a part of his body where he stands, which
then acts as a lure for carnivores (like with meat). This therefore allows you
to use Navigator in the same way, such as by triggering it and then placing a
trap on the same spot, or setting up a shotgun ambush. This Skill is obviously
only useful if you actually use it, making it suitable only for certain
playstyles.

  Mushroom Healing: Mushrooms are generally better spent on gaining essence, but
any extra healing is always welcome in a pinch. Eating a mushroom is basically
the same as using a bandage, meaning if you want to set out without bandages you
can still rely on finding mushrooms to take care of any healing you might need,
especially in the Swamp. This is probably the second best level 1 skill after
Eagle Eye, especially because mushrooms stack to 6 and require no materials for
crafting; although it's worth pointing out that mushrooms will rot over time and
become useless as curatives.

    Negative:
                
  Shadows: You are forced to pick this debuff as the game's way of making you
stay close to lit lamps during the Shadows night event, and in fact choosing
this Skill actually unlocks that night event for the first time. Once you leave
the Dry Meadow Hideout and relocate somewhere else, the Shadows night event will
happen whether you picked this Skill or not.
        

### 2.2.2 Level 2

                
    Positive:

  Acid Blood: This perk can occasionally save you by finishing off an enemy who
has first managed to hit you. You generally don't want to get hit, and if you
die along with the enemy it may just respawn anyway. Also, enemies whose health
bars are short enough for this ability to make a difference are easy enough to
kill off as it is, whereas tougher enemies are not going to care about Acid
Blood. The exception is the banshee babies, each of whom will surround you, hit
you for very little damage, and die to the Acid Blood.

  Third Eye: The ability to see all around you is great, even if it only lasts
60 seconds per day. In fact this is far better at night, which lasts only 4.5
minutes, allowing you to spend nearly a quarter of it with perfect 360 degree
vision. Third Eye is particularly strong against human spiders, as they cannot
teleport while being seen.

  Runner: This is another top tier ability, even if the stamina pills do almost
the same thing. Use it to escape from enemies and move more quickly through the
water in the Swamp. It's useful in combat because it greatly slows your stamina
reduction for a while, no matter how you use your stamina; and it's great for
outrunning floor gore for those rare occasions where you find yourself out in
the wild too late at night. Even though it doesn't make you any faster, and even
though enemies (other than elks) don't have stamina, it's still a great
emergency ripcord to get you the hell out of there.

    Negative:
        
  Vulnerable To Poison: Avoid mushrooms (particularly easy in the Swamp if you
find the Rubber Boots) and this debuff need never bother you. The vulnerability
will still not kill you from full (2 bars) health if you stood in one poison
mushroom, so it doesn't matter much; and it is later offset by the Vitality
Skill, too.

  Fearful: This is bad, you don't ever want your already limited sight getting
worse, especially in combat which is when you are most likely to take damage.
It's particularly bad at nighttime when your vision is already massively
impaired even with the Generator running. Avoid this; although it's worth noting
that it does not apply to damage such as traps, poison, pain glares, etc.
        

### 2.2.3 Level 3

                
    Positive:

  Careful Step: Since you can just mash out of traps and then heal, this ability
is next to useless if you are paying attention. It does allow you to set traps
at night and then deliberately run over one to bait an enemy into following you
through a chokepoint, but that's its only real use. Note that it treats
mushrooms as traps, allowing you step on one per day without being poisoned;
this is useless once you find and equip the Rubber Boots.

  Appetite: This is another occasionally nice nighttime Skill, since you will
likely be carrying planks around then anyway in order to quickly repair melée
weapons or fix barricades. It doesn't let you eat logs, and so it will only ever
be useful out in the wild and during the day if you either find planks (which
rarely happens) or take them with you (which you likely won't do if you have any
other healing items - remember you can make bandages from rags no matter where
you are). There's nothing wrong with this Skill, but certainly don't pick it
over Scream.

  Scream: Other than Eagle Eye, this is probably the best Skill you've been
offered up to this point, an instant get-off-me against nearly all enemies which
allows you to escape and reposition. Try to save it for nighttime, but if you
need it at any other time it's there. Only banshees are not affected, and you
hardly ever meet them during the day outside of the Radio Tower; everything else
runs away for a bit and then recovers, so make the most of Scream.

    Negative:           

  Weak Lungs: Obviously this does not apply at all unless your stamina
absolutely runs out, in which case you are penalised by a doubly longer wait
until it begins refilling. By the time you get this you will be using ranged
weapons at least as often as melée, and ranged weapons cost no stamina (they
can't even be fired or reloaded while running). Also, by the time you can choose
this Skill you will likely have developed a habit of never allowing your stamina
to reach zero, in which case it's irrelevant.

  Weakness: You deal less damage at low stamina, but not no damage. Besides, by
the time you have the option to pick this you will again likely be using ranged
weapons a lot anyway, in which case this debuff does not apply. An upgraded axe
more than counteracts this debuff, and you will be used to managing your stamina
in melée combat by now anyway, especially since common practice is to swimg your
melée weapon and then back away while you wait for your stamina to fully recover
before the next swing. This is probably slightly better than Weak Lungs, but
there's very little in it.

### 2.2.4 Level 4

                
    Positive:

  Adrenaline: Dealing double damage is nice, but not when you're close to death.
It only applies to melée damage, meaning you need first to be about to die and
second to get close enough to whatever put you in that situation, making death
all the more likely. You're not looking to get into melée range at all against
enemies who can actually kill you; and in such a situation what's likely giving
you so much trouble is being attacked by multiple enemies at once, in which case
killing just one of them helps very little, so in the end there is nothing good
about this Skill and you should not waste your upgrade on it.

  Vitality: This gives you an extra half a bar of health, which is far better
than nothing. It also mitigates your choice of Weak Regeneration as your debuff
and makes you more survivable in all ways, including to the Vulnerable To Poison
debuff you were likely forced to pick earlier. Choose this at the first
opportunity.

  Chameleon: Temporary invisibility is not useless, but it doesn't actually
remove the enemies giving you problems, or indeed move them in any way at all.
They will just hang around and wait for it to wear off, since the power is lost
if you attempt to move. It does give you time to rummage in your inventory and
equip something to your hotbar which might help solve the problem, but that's
about it. It's better than Adrenaline, but Scream does a better job at an
earlier level.

    Negative:
        
  Weak Regeneration: This debuff is meaningless if you use bandages, wood and
mushrooms as your curatives, since their effects last long enough to override
it. You will notice the well and other fast acting curatives do not supply as
much health, but it's no big deal.

  Shaky Hands: This is not a debuff you want if you are relying on rifles and
pistols as your ranged weapons, since it means you have to wait so much longer
for the zone of fire to narrow and it's still wide enough for you to miss even
at medium range. On the other hand, if you use mainly shotguns (which you
should) then you'll never notice the debuff, as you want to be nearly at point
blank range against everything in that situation anyway. This one just depends
on playstyle.

# 2.3 Status Effects

  Status effects are indicated by either a white or red icon at the top left of
the screen, and are incurred by interacting with environmental elements,
consumables and Skills.

### Positive

                
  Armour: This is applied by wearing light armour (from bug shells) in your
hotbar. It will operate at 100% efficiency and decrease damage taken from enemy
attacks while the armour is placed in any hotbar slot, but will disappear with
the destruction of the armour. It does not protect against other elements, such
as fire and poison.

  Endurance: This can be applied by consuming various items, including pills,
bread and chicken eggs. It slows your stamina consumption significantly while
active, and lasts 60 seconds. The Runner Skill applies almost the same effect:
your stamina will not decrease at all but the effect lasts only 20 seconds.

  Healing: Consume pills, alcohol or bandages to heal back any damage; you can
also consume mushrooms and wood with the appropriate Skills. Pills are a poor
option for healing as their effect lasts very little time, although the heal is
far more immediate than with that of a bandage, the preferred option.
        
  Night Protection: This takes effect as long as you are within the protective
catchment area of your Hideout at night, with the Oven lit. The Generator does
not affect this status, on or off. Night Protection merely prevents the floor
gore from invading the building. If you have not lit the Oven in the Hideout in
which you are spending the night, you will not see this status effect and you
will die within seconds.

  Poison Protection: This can be applied by consuming antidotes or by wearing
the Rubber Boots. Putting the Boots on will not cure you of any current poison
effects, but antidotes will cure and then protect you for 30 seconds.

  Sight: This status effect is induced either by consuming a red egg or by
triggering the Third Eye Skill. It simply removes the sight restriction which
forces the Protagonist to look in front of him to see things.

  Thick Skin: This buff is applied when you consume odd meat, farmed from
various higher level enemies. Very similarly to the Armour effect, it reduces
damage taken from attack. However, unlike with Armour, this status effect is
unaffected by any damage and is purely time based, meaning you always get the
maximum 150 seconds of protection out of it; whereas Armour can be stripped
inside a few seconds. Since odd meat is far more plentiful than bug shells,
thick skin is far more valuable than Armour; although it's worth noting that
their effects stack with each other.

  Time Freeze: This is only a positive effect insofar as it pauses things like
Generator fuel consumption and the decay of certain consumable; although it will
not halt poison or fire damage. Time Freeze kicks in every morning at the
Hideout and will not go away until you roam too far from the building, at which
point it disappears and cannot be brought back by returning to the Hideout. It
also operates in certain special Locations, such as the Village, the Doctor's
House and underground. Time Freeze at the Hideout effectively protects you from
attack, since the game despawns all nearby enemies each morning; but it will not
similarly protect you in any other areas, although all enemy encounters in such
places are scripted.

### Negative

                
 Burning: Inflicted by fire, causing damage reduction over time. Cannot be
healed directly, must be counteracted by healing over time, ideally with
bandages. The first thing to do is to move out of the fire, but the Burning
effect will remain beyond that and is compounded by the damage taken by standing
in the fire.

  Gas: Similar to fire, counteracted in the same way. The best defense is just
to move out of the gas cloud, at which point the negative effect will disappear,
unlike with fire.
        
  Immobilised: You are caught in a bear trap. Mash your movement keys to escape
more quickly and take less of the damage over time. Note that bear traps take
off about half a bar of health with the initial snap and then hold you in place,
meaning you cannot heal or access your inventory, weapons or hotbar while
trapped, so getting out is the priority.

  Pain: This mild damage is caused by looking directly at a 'pain' glare,
usually found in Chapter 2.

  Poisoned: Caused by walking over mushrooms or becoming trapped by vines
without first putting on the Rubber Boots or consuming an antidote. Antidote
removes it again; and bandages can be used to heal past its slow effect.

  Sight: This reduces your vision to almost nothing, leaving you very vulnerable
for up to 30 seconds. Don't drink alcohol or interact with twitching corpses and
this will never happen unless you step in mushrooms.

  Speed: Your movement is reduced significantly, stopping you from sprinting.
Backdashing can quickly get you out of this effect, typically caused by worms or
some other surfaces. Note that drinking alcohol also inflicts this debuff.

  Strength: Your Strength can be lowered by triggering the Weakness Skill at
very low health, causing you to do less damage until the effect is removed by
healing.

# 2.4 Weapons, Equipment & Inventory

### 2.4.1 Weapons

        
  These are all the weapons and other things which can be used to damage and
kill enemies in the game. This list does not include scripted items such as the
table leg and the flamethrower.
        

#### 2.4.1.1 Melée

                
  With one exception, all melée weapons can be crafted at the Workbench with
basic materials. They obviously do not require ammunition, but the downside is
that they do need to be repaired when their durability drops to zero. 

  Melée weapons will be your main focus until you reach the Swamp, where they
start to drop off badly in utility. It is recommended to have two melée weapons
equipped in Chapter 1, preferably a fast one and a slower, heavy hitting one.
Since you cannot craft the axe until Chapter 2, the weapons of choice are mainly
down to player taste and style.

### Torch

                
  Craftable: Yes
  Repairable: No
                
  Easily crafted from really common materials, the torch is both weapon and
light source while held in the hand. Using it as a light reduces its utility as
a weapon and vice versa, since its durability is reduced over time and per hit,
making it a particularly weak weapon; although its fire effect will seemingly do
additional damage to dogs.

  Torches cannot be refuelled or repaired, and since each successful strike with
a torch damages it for 25% of its total durability, it's impossible to rely on
the torch as a weapon. It should be used a light source first and as a weapon
only in emergencies (although it can be used to burn away worms). Even if you
have a torch equipped in the hotbar, it's best only to bring it out when you
need to be able to see or clear away worms; otherwise keep it equipped but
unselected, since it will not burn down while not being held.

  Torches are far less useful once lanterns are available, since the lantern's
light works without having to be held in the hand. Torches are actually at their
most useful when being sold to the traders, since they can be built from really
cheap materials and sold in handfuls for a very nice return.

  Note that dropped torches will not burn out, meaning you can drop them all
over the place in spots you think you will revisit, allowing you to pick them up
and use them later as local light sources.

### Board With Nails

                
  Craftable: Yes
  Repairable: Yes
  Upgradeable: Yes
                
  This is the first proper weapon you will likely craft, and it will serve you
well in the Dry Meadow and Silent Forest, especially when upgraded. Even though
it can take three upgrade levels, you cannot upgrade all three in Chapter 1; and
by the time you reach Chapter 2 you will not want to be using this weapon at
all.

  Its damage is always low, its range is laughable, its quick attack is not
nearly quick enough; and so the only reasons you will use it are for its
relative speed in comparison to all the other non-torch melée weapons, and for
its ability to interrupt enemy attacks (admittedly a very helpful quality). Once
you get used to the slower alternatives the board with nails should be
discarded, particularly when you find yourself up against groups of enemies. The
game hands you a free shovel head in the Old Woods as a hint that it's time to
ditch the board with nails. Even upgraded, its low damage output means that each
enemy requires more strikes to kill, which in turn causes the weapon to break
earlier than you want it to.

### Staff

                
  Craftable: No
  Repairable: No
  Upgradeable: No
        
  Dropped by Savages, the staff is a melée weapon with decent range until you
get something better. The only reason to keep one around is so you can craft the
shovel later; but otherwise the staff's mixture of speed and poor damage makes
this weapon like having a board with nails with longer range.

### Knife

                
  Craftable: No
  Repairable: No
  Upgradeable: No
        
  These can't really be relied upon in melée because you cannot craft, repair or
upgrade them. This means that when you find them out in the world you generally
use them to spare the durability of your real weapons, knowing knives are always
disposable.

  As well as its three-hit secondary attack, the knife can also be thrown from
range by charging up its primary attack. Unlike with the guns, a miss with the
knife means you lose the weapon, and it will either be found on the body of the
dead enemy or dropped on the floor if it misses.

  The knife is always just a stop-gap weapon, not worth any real consideration
as anything more than a consumable capable of doing damage. If you are going out
exploring with the knife equipped as a weapon, make sure you have another,
stronger weapon equipped alongside it so you are not left fumbling around in
your inventory when the knife misses or breaks.

### Sickle

                
  Craftable: Yes
  Repairable: Yes
  Upgradeable: No
        
  As the tooltip says, the sickle cannot interrupt enemy attacks. This is a big
deal and will probably be the thing which drives you away from using it at all.
It hits quickly and twice when charged, but this is very rarely enough to kill
an enemy in one attack; and since its primary attack also moves you towards the
enemy it ends up putting you in harm's way more often than any other weapon. You
don't want to move towards enemies in a canned animation: backing away from them
and punishing them is far better. 

  However, the sickle actually allows for this, thanks to the fact that attacks
in this game are cancellable into a backdash. Charge up the primary attack,
release it at an enemy within range, then immediately cancel into your backdash
(Left CTRL) on hit, allowing you to hit once and dodge out of danger in the same
motion, with no stamina cost to the backdash because you cancelled into it. This
helps make up for the fact that the sickle can't interrupt enemy attacks, since
you don't need to interrupt them if you are now out of range of them. Practise
this until you get comfortable with it, it's the best way to use the sickle.

  The quick attack is also interesting, a fast strike-and-backdash which can be
spammed five times before you run out of stamina, making it great for when your
back is literally against the wall. This is especially good against chompers out
in the open because you can time it as they rush at you, move slightly aside,
then quick attack them to strike them while dodging out of the way of an attack
which would have almost certainly hit you without the sickle's backdash
component - all without having to charge up your attacks.

  However, the sickle can't be upgraded, and by the time you get around to being
able to craft it you are also able to craft the shovel and can find pitchforks
in the area. The sickle is an awkward weapon, only recommended if you
particularly like its style.

### Pitchfork

                
  Craftable: No
  Repairable: Yes
  Upgradeable: Yes
        
  This is a very underrated melée weapon, mainly because of a couple of 
drawbacks. These would be the fact that it takes very high stamina output for
mediocre damage (pre-upgrades), and the fact that the weapon can get stuck in
walls if your attack impacts them, leaving you extremely vulnerable.

  However, once upgraded with the Sharpened quality, and when not used in
Hideouts during the night, the pitchfork really shines out in the wild. Its main
selling point is the fantastic reach of the admittedly slow primary attack,
capable of striking and interrupting everything well before it gets anywhere
near you, and with an easily learned animation which does not have nearly the
windup of the incredibly slow shovel. Just by backing away and poking you
conserve the stamina you need, and the phantom hitbox lets you hit targets even
when it looks like you missed. Used correctly, it's hard for enemies to get
close to you without rushing you down in numbers or ambushing you, making this a
sweet spot of a melée weapon.

  Its secondary attack is very good because you can spam it while backing away,
although it does not interrupt enemy attacks. As with the primary attack, it
does have a nice hitbox.

  You cannot craft the pitchfork, however, so you will have to forage for it at
the Pig Shed, which I suggest you do upon first arriving in the Silent Forest. 

### Shovel

                
  Craftable: Yes
  Repairable: Yes
  Upgradeable: Yes
        
  This weapon is built up throughout Chapter 1 as a game-changer for when you
finally get to build it by either buying or finding the shovel head. However, it
has massive liabilities which argue for avoiding it entirely as a weapon and
using it only for digging purposes.

  The bottom line is that it is absurdly slow, forcing you to wait forever for
its primary attack to charge up, then forever again for the swing to come out.
Combat with the shovel requires perfect timing on choosing when to charge and
then choosing when to release - get either wrong and you will be punished, then
punished again while you try to recover from having been interrupted.

  It's true it does very good damage for a melée weapon at the point in the game
where you are likely to first encounter it (the Silent Forest), but you never
actually need it. By that point you can craft the pistol and buy ammo for it;
and the fact that the shovel cannot hit multiple targets, despite its lateral
swings, only makes its attractiveness decrease even further. One point in its
favour is that the primary attack uses surprisingly little stamina for how long
it takes; while its secondary attack is useless, too slow and costing more
stamina than the primary attack.

  Other than that, this weapon is liable to get you in big trouble. Unless
everything works in your favour, it fails more than it succeeds, and I recommend
you never use it in combat if you have a pitchfork, axe, pistol, shotgun or even
board with nails!

### Axe

                
  Craftable: Yes
  Repairable: Yes
  Upgradeable: Yes
                
  This is the big daddy of the melée weapons, the strongest one in the game, and
even more so when upgraded. By the time you can craft the axe you can also
upgrade the Workbench to accommodate all its extras, and if you insist on using
primary weapons in Chapter 2 (which I recommend you don't) then the axe is the
way to go.

  The big overhead chop can one-shot any enemy in the game except Wolfman, once
the axe has been upgraded. It's slightly faster on startup than the horrible
shovel swing, another plus point for the other one of only two heavy hitting
melée weapons. Its secondary is pointless, since you got and upgraded the axe to
do maximum melée damage per swing, which means you're using primary all the way.

  Take it into the Swamp anyway, equipped in your hotbar, for when you run out
of shotgun ammo.
        

#### 2.4.1.2 Ranged

                
  Ranged weapons cannot be upgraded. They do not need to be repaired; instead
they must be reloaded with the appropriate ammunition when empty. This is done
by holding the right mouse button to aim the weapon, then pressing R to reload,
assuming you have ammo in your inventory. Note that reloading from a magazine
will consume the entire magazine, even if you had only spent one round from the
weapon you are reloading, so only reload from magazines once the gun is empty.

  You might think that you can just find or craft one firearm and stick with
that for the rest of the game. This is almost true for the pistol, but not for
any other gun. Ammunition is the problem: you can find it in several places, but
otherwise you have to buy it each morning in very limited doses, assuming you
have the money. One shotgun shell per day is a terrible offer, so shotgunning
everything in the game is impossible unless you are willing to grind for weeks.
Try to have at least two types of firearm whenever possible, and buy and hoard
all the ammo you possibly can; otherwise you may have to fall back on other
weapons when you don't want to.

  Each time you craft any firearm, it appears in your inventory with full ammo.
So, by crafting any gun you are also crafting a full clip of ammunition for
free. Consider this when there are no more ammo rounds available from the
traders but you do have the materials to make a new gun (although ammo loaded
into a gun cannot be taken out again).

### Pellet Gun

                
  Craftable: No
        
  Only found in the Wolfman's locked crates in Chapter 1 (at his camp), this
pathetic gun fires the pellets you might find in one or two places. Its damage
is low enough to make it clear that this is a joke weapon.

### Submachine Gun

                
  Craftable: No
        
  Also only found in Wolfman's stash, this uses the same ammo as the pistol.
There are not many automatic weapons in this game, so this is not bad, but you
will have to guess Wolf's codes to find it.
        

### Single-shot Pistol

        
  Craftable: Yes
        
  There should never be any need to make this, although you can find one or two
around the place in Chapter 1 to help with particularly tricky encounters you
might expect to have. The tooltip isn't lying, this Clint Eastwood gun will
one-shot a huge dog if you want it to, but the fact that it's then totally
useless even for selling makes it totally unappealing otherwise. It's a gun you
find, not one you craft.

### Pistol

                
  Craftable: Yes
        
  This 8-round handgun is the best ranged weapon you can have until you craft
the single-shot shotgun at the level 5 Workbench. It doesn't do much damage per
shot, but its great range and accuracy, coupled with its ability to interrupt
whatever the enemy is doing, all contribute to its being arguably the best
all-round Chapter 1 weapon. Ammo is quite cheap and found for free in several
places, making it definitely a weapon of choice at the earliest opportunity.

  The pistol falls off in Chapter 2, being replaced by far more powerful guns:
the pistol's mediocre damage per shot becomes quite noticeable. Note that you
can get a free pistol by giving the ELECTRONIC GAME to Wolfman.

### Homemade Shotgun

                
  Craftable: Yes
        
  As with the single-shot pistol, this gun becomes useless once its two rounds
have been spent (each round is fired separately, rather than from both barrels
at once). In this sense it is just a more expensive and powerful version of the
single-shot pistol, worth two insta-kills instead of one. And again, there is no
need ever to craft this, so if you find one out in the wild make sure you equip
it and use it against the next dangerous enemy you see, then discard it.

  NOTE: This shotgun is very cheap to make if you keep buying pipes at the
traders, meaning you can build one every two days, which will pay off greatly
when defending your Old Woods Hideout.

### Single-shot Shotgun

                
  Craftable: Yes
        
  Unlike the single-shot pistol, this similarly named shotgun can be reloaded
after each shot. It's by far the best weapon you are able to craft when it turns
up, and you should buy or find the components immediately. This is such a good
(if overpowered) Chapter 1 firearm that it argues for buying a shotgun shell
from the Trader every single morning, starting on Day 2, in order to have a
decent stock by the time you craft the gun itself. Note that shotgun shells are
otherwise hard to find in Chapter 1, so if you want to wait until Chapter 2
where this gun's power is justified then that's a decent plan, too. At any rate,
this is a top tier weapon and will change the way you play.

### Double-barrel Shotgun

                
  Craftable: Yes
        
  The only thing which distinguishes this shotgun from the single-shot version
is that it can hold two rounds at once, allowing you to fire twice before having
to reload - you can't fire both at once, however. Since reload time is quite
significant, which one you craft is a matter of preference; although the
single-shot version is available sooner than the double-barrel version, meaning
by the time you can craft this gun you probably don't want or need to.

### Pump-action Shotgun

                
  Craftable: Yes
        
  Pretty certainly the game's best weapon of any type, this shotgun holds 8
rounds at once, making reloads rare. Since shotgun shells stack to 10, you
should have no problem running around with at least 20 shots available, and
since just one shot is enough to take down nearly any enemy at point blank range
there is little need to use anything else while ammo is available. Since nearly
all enemies in Chapter 2 are ones you want to shoot instead of melée, and since
this gun is only available in Chapter 2 anyway, it should be your first choice
for any and every situation. Ammo will likely be a problem preventing you from
killing absolutely everything with it - especially if you die and cause some
enemies to respawn - but the fact that the shotgun's range and spread also allow
it to hit multiple enemies at once, both beside and behind each other, you can
conserve ammo by maneouvering your enemies into the spread field and unloading
to hit several at once. One additional benefit which the shotgun (of any type)
has over the pistol and assault rifle is that its shells are stored in stacks,
rather than in magazines, meaning you only ever spend exactly as many rounds as
you want to when reloading, with none wasted. Craft it as soon as you see it and
never let it out of your sight, whether during day or night!

### Hunting Rifle

                
  Craftable: Yes
        
  This is the game's sniper rifle, with fantastic range and accuracy. It's
extremely powerful, too, enough to one-shot just about anything at max range,
which is its unique selling point. However, it does have some problems. 
  As with the shotgun, its rounds can be stacked so you can reload as
efficiently as you would like. However, bullets are hard to come by, and can
only be bought once per day from the traders, making it a weapon you keep
meaning to use but never quite get around to it because you're worried about the
ammo. It cannot hit multiple enemies at once, unlike the shotguns; and any enemy
you can shoot at the hunting rifle's max range can also be avoided entirely,
with no need to engage it. And in literally all circumstances where you might
want to use the hunting rifle to shoot an enemy at long or mid range you really
might as well move closer and open fire with the shotgun, knowing that if you do
you can also hit whatever is behind that enemy for additional damage.

  It's terrible at night because you need to be accurate in the dark and in
close quarters, usually while things are running at you at point blank range;
and it also needs to be reloaded each and every time you fire it, which is far
too dangerous in a fight. If this weapon's accuracy suits your playstyle then
it's the best one for the job, but having played this game a million times I was
unable to come up with any examples where this gun is worth crafting. Note that
the hunting rifle can be used for recon in playthroughs where you didn't pick
Eagle Eye - but why would you not pick Eagle Eye?!

### Assault Rifle

                
  Craftable: No
        
  You have three chances to find this weapon in the game, and only two of those
occasions allow you to actually use the gun in combat. Along with the
pump-action shotgun, the assault rifle is the other top tier firearm in
Darkwood, well worth the wait as long as you have the ammo for it.

  If you manage to grab the assault rifle from Wolfman in Chapter 1 or find it
in the Wreckage Location in Chapter 2, you will find it to be a very powerful
gun, capable of killing anything in two very fast rounds (which is the same as
one shot, since it fires two rounds at a time). It's powerful enough to stop any
enemy in its tracks, further increasing your defense in cases where your first
shot was unable to kill (such as against black substance). It's deadly accurate
at close to mid range, and decent even at long range if you give it enough time
to zone in.

  Ammunition is once again the biggest problem, arguably more so than with any
other gun. You are lucky if you find five medium caliber magazines in a complete
playthrough, and any you find in Chapter 1 will have to be freighted to Chapter
2 since you almost certainly will not be using them in Chapter 1. Each time you
reload the assault rifle it swallows an entire magazine, so if you're not
careful you can waste several bullets because you reloaded before the gun was
empty. This is exacerbated by the fact that magazines cannot be bought or
crafted (same as the gun itself).

  As powerful as it is, this weapon is not one you take out on its own. You
equip it as a backup gun for situations where it is preferred over a shotgun,
but you tend to save it for emergencies or groups of enemies which you do not
want to get too close to. I find that I finish the game having never used this
gun, either because I didn't bother picking it up or because I just didn't need
it, thanks to the pistol and shotgun. It's a magic potion gun: you keep it back
for a time when you really need it... and then you never need it after all.

  NOTE: The first 'chance' to get the assault rifle is basically all of Chapter
1, since you always have the option of killing Wolfman and taking it from his
corpse. Find a medium caliber magazine in Chapter 1 (which you can often do as
early as the Dry Meadow) and you can be running around Chapter 1 with the
assault rifle, although this is kind of overkill!

#### 2.4.1.3 Miscellaneous

                
  Some of these items allow for stacked damage: for example, two molotovs will
inflict twice the damage, etc.

### Bear Trap

                
  Craftable: Yes
        
  Very cheap to make, deploy it to trap and possibly kill any enemy who walks
into it. The bear trap holds them for a long time, allowing you to finish off
any held enemies with your cheapest weapon. Just don't walk into your own trap;
but if you do, make sure you mash the four movement buttons quickly to speed up
your escape.

  Bear traps work on all living things in the game, including rabbits and bugs.
        

### Bottle

                
  Craftable: No
                
  This glass bottle will break against an enemy for minor damage, but is better
used to create a broken glass surface for a caltrops-type effect: toss it on the
ground and any enemy walking over it will take big damage, unlike the
Protagonist. Still far more useful for making better weapons.

### Chain Trap

                
  Craftable: Yes
        
  This trap inflicts no damage on the Protagonist or anything else, but it
tethers its prey to its leash. If you get caught run away from the centre and
strain the chain until it breaks.
                

### Exploding Barrel

                
  Craftable: Yes
        
  Attack this barrel with fire or gunfire to cause it to instantly explode,
similarly to the gas tank. Torches will not set it off, but all other fires
will. It works as a nice trap at the end of a trail of gasoline.
                

### Gas Bottle

                
  Craftable: Yes
                
  When thrown this bursts and creates a large zone of gas which will stay there
for about 30 seconds, slowly damaging anything caught in the area. This is a
good trick to use on enemies: toss a gas bottle at your own feet just after
popping an alcohol bandage, then just run around while the gas kills everything
chasing you without you having to swing a weapon or fire a shot. Another good
trick is to let an enemy chase you towards a door, slam the door behind you to
force them to batter it down, then chuck a gas bottle at the door - the gas will
seep through and kill the enemies. Great against banshee babies and all groups
of enemies.

  Note that you can also set the gas cloud on fire for a big explosion. Throw a
match or a torch at it, but stand well back. This works really well with the
door trick, although it will also destroy the door! However, a gas cloud
explosion will not clear worms out of the way, despite the fire.  

### Gasoline

                
  Craftable: No
        
  Gasoline can be equipped in the hotbar and then poured on the floor to create
trails of flammable fuel. This allows you to paint the floors of your Hideouts
with gasoline, particularly next to chokepoints like doors and windows, then
throw a match to light the whole thing, burning it for 30 seconds, along with
any enemies trying to break down your barricades. They will burn to death, not
knowing how to escape the fire. Alternatively, you can set a trail from your
position to an explosive barrel for a bomb trap.

### Gas Tank

                
  Craftable: Yes
        
  This item can be found or crafted in Chapter 2. Pick it up and equip it in the
hotbar, then place it on the ground like laying a trap. Once it's down you can
push it to rotate it. This affects the direction it will travel when hit with a
melée weapon.

  NOTE: Hit the end with the small nozzle to shoot the tank in the opposite
direction, where it will explode after a second. This is only useful to fire it
at a spot where you have previously placed an explosive barrel or pool of
gasoline. If you stand pressed against the thicker end and then hit it, the tank
will shoot towards you, carrying you with it - then killing you in the
explosion!

  Once placed, it's better to shoot at the gas tank to cause an explosion on
that spot, capable of killing multiple enemies. This is best set up by first
placing meat down to attract dogs, or by closing a door on multiple enemies and
then placing the gas tank behind it while they break it down.
                

### Molotov Cocktail

                
  Craftable: Yes
                
  This creates a small fire on the ground which burns for 30 seconds, damaging
anything still standing in it or passing through it. Best used in bottlenecks
where enemies must pass - just don't throw it at your own escape route. The
molotov is also good for clearing away worms when you don't have a torch.
Enemies will continue to burn for 10 seconds after being caught by the molotov's
fire, making it a good starter against elks.

### Stone

                
  Craftable: No
                
  An exceptionally weak weapon to be thrown at an enemy, worthwhile only to
irritate them. Save them for crafting purposes.
        

### 2.4.2 Equipment

        
  These are some details about certain important inventory items: by no means an
exhaustive list, just the ones you most need to pay attention to.

#### 2.4.2.1 Consumables

  Odd-looking mushroom: These are picked from the ground and can be cooked at
any lit Oven to gain essence. Allowed to rot over time they will become rotten
mushrooms, as with most other hallucinogens. 

  Odd meat: At first this seems like just another decent way to gather some
essence at the Oven; but when you eat one it gives you 150 seconds of the Thick
Skin debuff. Farm these and use them before they rot entirely. Note that the
degree of rot does not affect the duration of the buff: even an almost entirely
rotten odd meat will still give you the 150 seconds.

  Embryo: A rare item, always found next to bug nests, this can be cooked for a
huge boost of essence. Alternatively, you can consume it for healing and even an
extra life (on Hard mode). 

  Chicken egg: You can find several of these in the Village, especially the
Chicken Lady's house and barn. They are useful for endurance / stamina and come
in very handy in speedruns.

  Red egg: This can be found in the Chicken Lady's barn and when eaten it will
give you the Sight and Endurance buffs.

#### 2.4.2.2 Miscellaneous

        
  Axe blade: This item is essential for crafting the axe, the game's best melée
weapon. However, this weapon cannot be crafted in Chapter 1, even though the axe
blade itself can be bought from the Chapter 1 traders and also found in the
Village. If you do buy or collect it in Chapter 1 you are then forced to spend
an inventory slot on hauling it into Chapter 2, when that slot could have been
better used for something else. Meanwhile, you can actually find a fully crafted
axe at the Doctor's House, making the axe blade only useful for upgrading later.
So, I recommend you ignore all Chapter 1 axe blades and wait until Chapter 2 -
there is no point in doing otherwise.

  Bike Bell: This unique item is only found in the Silent Forest Hideout
Workbench and can be equipped to the hotbar. Use it from there to summon Bike
Man, who will appear outside your Hideout when you are not in the vicinity of
the nearby Saw, Workbench or Oven. Talk to him and give him alcohol to make use
of his freight services. Keep the Bike Bell safe until you are ready to leave
Chapter 1: store it in a container and remember where you left it, or else you
will be forced to haul all your rubbish from one Hideout to the next.
        
  Fabric: The first six of these should be tracked down as a priority if you are
interested in upgrading your hotbar and inventory. Once those are maxed out the
Fabric is much less valuable and can be ignored.

  Flare: The flare can be held in your hand via the hotbar, dropped at your feet
with a right-click, or thrown by holding the right mouse button and then
left-clicking. They place a light source on the floor which burns for 80
seconds, and since they have been dropped you can then equip a weapon and defend
yourself within the circle of light, precluding the need for a torch to be held
in the hand. Best used at night, especially because you won't be leaving the
vicinity and because the flare can fend off the Shadows night event, the main
argument for keeping at least one aside in your Workbench in preparation for
each night. This is particularly important because the Shadows night event will
disable any lanterns you are holding.
        
  Gasoline: This versatile item is used to fuel the Generator and Saw, and to
create torches and lanterns. It can even be poured on the floor to create trails
to be set on fire. Don't spend it on a Generator you are about to leave behind
for another Hideout; and don't spend it on a Saw unless you have the logs to
justify it.

  Lantern: This is the best light source available to any Protagonist; it's
arguably even better than daylight, because the lantern is capable of stripping
black substance off of protected enemies! I recommend you wear one of these on
your hip at all times from the Silent Forest onwards, as it will save your life.
Note that lanterns can be combined with other lanterns to refill the first one
from the second one, much like gasoline cans. Lanterns are also better than
flashlights or torches because you can have a lantern equipped while you are
carrying a weapon. Unfortunately, the lantern is temporarily disabled by the
Shadows night event.

  Light armour: Usually just called armour in this guide, this piece of clothing
is crafted from bug shells and will decrease any damage you take from attacks
while wearing it, and while it endures. Once it's completely damaged it
disappears and you will have to make another one.
        
  Lockpick: Never leave the Hideout without a stack of these. Each one can be
crafted from two pieces of wire, but they repay that cost many times over by
allowing you to unlock various random and non-random crates and doors.

  Medium caliber magazine: These are totally useless until you find an assault
rifle, but they're so rare that any you come across should be kept and carried
into Chapter 2, unless you don't intend to use the assault rifle.

  Pliers: Necessary for Workbench upgrades in Chapter 2, it's possible to find
five of these in the Swamp, although you only need two.
        
  Rags: Very useful all throughout the game if you like healing and light, which
you definitely will. Always by all rags from any trader; and don't use any until
you have to, since they have several uses.
                
  Rope: This might seem like a very mundane item, but you should grab any you
can find in Chapter 1. This is because they are essential for upgrading your
inventory and hotbar, along with the equally important Fabric. Look in destroyed
wells particularly.

  Rotten mushroom: This is created by picking any mushroom and allowing it to
rot over time, rather than cooking it. A rotten mushroom can then be used to
create the gas bottle.

  Rubber Boots: Found in the house at the centre of the Great Lake in Chapter 2,
when equipped to your hotbar these protect you from all poison damage. Avoid
stepping in worms or acid while wearing them, as they will be damaged and cannot
be repaired, crafted, bought or replaced.

  Shell: Farmed from bugs at nests, each shell can be used to craft one light
armour. Since shells stack to three and armour does not stack, don't craft
armour until you are ready to use it, as you are wasting inventory space
otherwise. Remember to carry all Chapter 1 shells into Chapter 2, otherwise they
will be lost; note that these items are finite and cannot be bought from anyone;
the exception is when the Bug Infestation night event comes around, offering a
further four shells to be farmed - but this cannot be planned for.

  Shovel blade: This expensive item is out of reach for the first day or two,
but once you upgrade your Workbench you can craft the shovel, the strongest
Chapter 1 weapon in terms of damage (not counting the possibly inaccessible
axe). The shovel is not just a powerful weapon, it can also be used for digging,
and in Chapter 1 this allows you to grab certain otherwise unavailable weapon
components. The earliest you can find one is in the Church Ruins, in the Old
Woods, but consider buying one before then if you have the money and are
comfortable with the shovel's slowness in combat.

  Toolbox: You need two of these to upgrade your Workbench early on, so buy them
from the Wolfman or Piotrek, or find them in a few different Chapter 1
Locations.

  Watch: If you don't want to buy a watch from a trader you can find a free one
in various places, most obviously in the locked crate at the back of the Silent
Forest Hideout's Generator building - this crate can be opened with the Key
found at the Cottage. Just having a watch in your inventory is enough to allow
you to see the time of day on screen; equipping it to the hotbar does nothing
except prevent it from being dropped should you die.
        
  Weapon Parts: These are used to craft all the strongest firearms in the game,
often requiring multiples for just one single gun. They stack in pairs in your
inventory and are some of the most valuable items you can sell to any traders.
Stockpile them and take them into Chapter 2, then either sell them in bulk for a
huge cash injection or keep them for crafting great guns later on.

  Welder: An item needed in Chapter 1 for a single Workbench upgrade. Find it as
a quest reward from Piotrek, or in the Hideout or Church Ruins in the Old Woods;
you can also find more in Chapter 2.

# 2.5 Enemies & Hazards

        

### 2.5.1 Enemies

                
  Darkwood does not have a huge variety of enemies, and most of them can be
dealt with by the same means, arguing for a basic, all-purpose loadout no matter
where you are in the game. This changes at night, when the game sends multiple
groups of enemies and types at you, on top of the usual night events. Still,
once you learn how to combat each enemy type you will be far better prepared for
every area of the Darkwood.

  Here are the various enemies, arranged roughly in order of encounter; and how
to deal with them.

### Dog

        
  You will always be sent a single dog on your first night in the Dry Meadow
Hideout, and since they are otherwise common to the Dry Meadow and the Silent
Forest it's likely that this is the first enemy type you will meet even if you
skip the Prologue. Initially they will stand there and bark in warning, but if
you don't move away or if you move closer they will attack in slow rushes and
easily dodgeable bites - you can run up to them while they bark and get in a
couple of free hits. They also turn like cruise liners, giving you plenty of
time to reposition and load up a melée strike. Release your weapon just as they
reach you while simultaneously moving aside to interrupt their attacks and they
should die in a few strikes of a torch or a board with nails. A bear trap will
kill them immediately; and a dog will run away at the sound of gunfire. Torches
and other fire damage weapons appear to do slightly more damage to dogs than to
other enemies.

  Dogs can turn up at night and can leap over fences and windows.
        

### Elk

                
  These huge creatures are passive enough until their territory is invaded by
anything else, at which point they will growl and then quickly attack by
lowering their antlers and rushing at their antagonist. This rush can be
sidestepped, and should be as it does big damage on connection.

  Elk are vulnerable to molotovs tossed from a distance, and will take damage
from the initial burst, followed by additional DoT damage from the Burning
status effect. This softens them up for your followup attacks, which should be
effected from point blank range: sprint towards them and hug their side while
you swipe, turning as they turn in order to keep out of the way of their
antlers. Several hits later they should fall, allowing you to salvage their
antlers for trading.

  Since you know where elk will be once you find them, you can return later to
the same spot with a bear trap. Plop it down and bait the elk into it, and it
will remain damaged and trapped for ages, unable to fight back, allowing for a
cheap, easy kill.

  Try baiting nearby enemies to any elks in the vicinity and let them kill each
other.
        

### Savage

        
  These grunting humans will come at you with staffs and stones initially; and
later variants are wielding sickles. The staff guys have slow windup on their
attacks, so despite their quick advances they can be baited into an easily
punishable swing or overhead; just watch out for the double swing which can hit
you if you don't predict it. The stone throwers stay back and line up accurate
throws which interrupt your attacks but do very little damage, and without the
support of the melée savages they are very vulnerable. The sickle savages get to
use the sickle's double swing and lunge attack. Just keep backing away from the
melée guys until they commit to an attack; and once they're down rush in on the
stone throwers. Note that if you can get a melée savage in between you and a
stone thrower the stones will actually hit and interrupt the melée savage.

  Savages killed in the Hideout should always be looted for useful items before
their bodies despawn, the best argument for trying to kill them rather than
avoiding them.

### Huge Dog

                
  First found in the Dry Meadow's Crop Field, the huge dog is a tougher and more
aggressive version of the dog. It will not flinch at gunfire and does more
damage upon each hit. It is no faster or smarter than an ordinary dog, however,
and will fall for the same tactics. Despite the increased danger, huge dogs are
worth killing for the odd meat they always drop. If encountered at night, try
just running away from the huge dog and hoping it runs into another enemy who
has also turned up: the huge dog should attack whatever else it finds.

### Shadows

                
  These are special enemies, only ever encountered during scripted night events.
See section 2.6 for more information.

### Mushroom Man

                
  Your first meeting with one of these delights may be in a Mushroom Field in
the Silent Forest, where you will hear a tearing noise and a man covered in
mushrooms will emerge from the poisoned forest floor and rush you down. This is
your cue to sprint away to the limits of your endurance, since after about ten
strides the mushroom man will pause and then explode on the spot, doing damage
heavy enough to one-shot you if you are too close.

  Mushroom men are always scripted in this way: they will either explode next to
you if they reach you, or run for a while and explode anyway. Turning around and
attacking them will make them pause and explode prematurely, but this is only
necessary if you have run out of stamina. Since you always know exactly what is
going to happen when a mushroom man appears you can use him to your advantage:
run towards any other enemies in the area, stop in place to make him pause, then
move aside as the other enemies reach you. With luck they will all be caught in
the blast.

  Mushroom men are designed to make you panic, to sprint into nearby mushrooms
and become poisoned, potentially in big trouble because you cannot afford to
stop and heal or use an antidote. Don't panic: poison will not kill you over
time unless your health was already very low. Avoiding the explosion is what
matters, then deal with any healing afterwards.

### Red Chomper

                
  This horror is one of the worst things in the game. You will likely first meet
it at the Wedding in the Dry Meadow, as a unique encounter; but they later turn
up as common enemies, both in the day and at night.

  Red chompers are always dangerous, no matter at what stage of the game. They
are super-aggressive and are the fastest moving enemies in the game. They will
chase you for a long time, hear and spot you from a reasonable distance, and
will stick with you long enough to wear out your endurance, forcing you to turn
and fight. They are also tough enough to weather several strikes with the weaker
melée weapons, and a single pistol bullet is not enough to kill them.

  If you do engage them in melée combat you are almost certainly going to get
hit at some point unless you master the timing involved in dodging their rush
attacks. This is done by charging up your melée weapon, letting them rush at
you, then sidestepping them at an angle which moves towards them and past either
side of them - if done correctly this means you pass them during the startup
frames of their bite and the actual attack whiffs past you. This is tricky but
can be nailed with some practise.

  Alternatively you are entitled to use all manner of cheap tricks to take down
chompers, whether at scripted Locations or encountered in the wild. The bear
trap is the cheapest of all: carry one or two with you, especially when you know
you will be meeting a chomper, then just place it down before the chomper spots
you, before baiting it towards you and into the trap for an easy kill. The
pitchfork is also a good option if you are fighting out in the open, as it
allows you to attack and interrupt them way before they get close enough to you;
and it also stops their rushes while having a very nice hitbox. One downside to
this is that the developers saw fit to give all chompers a backdash, one of the
cheapest things in the entire game given how the Protagonist's stamina and melée
swings work. This therefore recommends the much more powerful and reliable
ranged weapons for fighting chompers; and in fact the prevalence of red chompers
in the Old Woods and Swamp is the best reason to be using guns by the time you
reach the Old Woods.

  Some night events (Silent Forest on) send red chompers; and there are other
situations where you may have to face off against more than one at once. Try to
avoid this if you can, as it's one of the most likely ways you will die in
Darkwood. Running away is often the best policy outside of night encounters.

  Still, red chompers are worth fighting for the odd meat they drop. Try not to
fight more than one at a time if you can possibly avoid it; but if you do then
bring out the shotgun.

  Avoid all twitching corpses and dormant bodies from the Silent Forest onwards:
these will become red chompers if stepped on or otherwise bothered (as events at
the Bridge are supposed to teach you).

### Black Chomper

                
  Encounters with black chompers are always scripted and are very rare, and in
most cases dying to them changes some small thing about the game but does not
otherwise trouble your progress in any way. The worst penalty for dying to a
black chomper happens in the Grave Dream, where if you die you don't get a free
shovel head.

  Anyway, they behave the same way as red chompers and have basically the same
stats. The only difference is that you will always face them without your
preferred gear, making these pretty much scripted losses unless you manage to
pull out a lucky victory. This is possible but generally not even worth
bothering with: run away if you can, fight if you want, but don't worry if you
die.

### Villager

                
  First encountered in the Silent Forest, these behave similarly to Savages when
it comes to combat. Some Villagers are armed with pitchforks, but otherwise the
tactics are the same.

### Banshee

                
  The first time you will meet this enemy will likely be during a night event in
the Silent Forest, although they become more frequent during nights in the Old
Woods and can be found at certain scripted Locations in Chapter 2 (there is also
a small chance of seeing a banshee at night in the Dry Meadow). This crow-like
creature's arrival is preceded by a unique scream and a flock of crows crossing
the screen. Once the banshee itself turns up it will begin slowly stalking
around the Hideout, looking for the Protagonist. If it is able to see you for
long enough it will scream, temporarily knocking out all the lights in the area,
including your lantern, after which the banshee will explode and produce five
banshee babies which will then begin waddling insistently in the Protagonist's
direction. If they manage to reach him they will peck at him for low damage, but
because there are five at once that damage can add up quickly.

  Escaping and avoiding the banshee altogether is the best tactic, because if it
is not attacked and does not see you it cannot explode. Meanwhile, banshees are
basically immune to traps and don't care about your Scream ability. On the other
hand, since they are unable to attack, they cannot deal with barricades, so if
you manage to barricade yourself into a specific room (such as the Generator
room or hallway nearest the Saw (both in the Old Woods Hideout), you are
entirely safe unless some other enemy smashes the barricade down for the
banshee. Alternatively, you can lock a banshee into a specific room by letting
it go inside a room whose other entrances are already barricaded, following it
inside and letting it see you (holding it in place for a second or two), then
retreating and barricading the door behind you before it can explode. Banshees
can open doors even if they cannot attack them, but closing a door will break
line of sight. They are so slow moving that they are easy to avoid just by
walking to some other spot; and they tend to get stuck easily in doorways and
corners when they know the Protagonist is on the other side of a wall but can't
get to him. They also cannot attack furniture and so they can become stuck in
some circumstances: for example, place a lamp and the nearby bowl next to each
other in the gap between the very back room of the Silent Forest Hideout and the
next one in, and a banshee cannot push them out of the way to reach the
Protagonist.

  Anyway, the babies are the main problem, since they are what kills you. They
do very little damage, but the problem is the stunlock and the fact that they
can surround and trap you. If you are stunned you cannot swing or fire a weapon,
so you have to back away from them and take them down. A shotgun is best with
its ability to hit multiple targets; and gas and molotovs are also great if you
can find the space and time, thanks to the fact that the babies typically group
up in tight clumps and move slowly. Like the mother banshee, the babies have
very little health.

  An excellent strategy against the banshee and its babies is to choose the Acid
Blood Skill, remove any armour you are wearing, and pop an alcohol bandage. Then
just let the banshee find you, scream and die, whereupon the babies appear and
attack you and all die to the Acid Blood while your health regenerates.

  Meanwhile, the banshee babies are able to destroy barricades (pathetically),
but are not able to negotiate windows or fences. Another excellent escape tactic
is to bait them towards an open window and then just vault over it, leaving them
stuck on the other side.

### Human Spider

        
  These terrifying enemies appear in Chapter 2, roaming the Swamp, but ugly 
though they are they're mostly all bark and no bite. They can take a reasonable
amount of damage, although an upgraded axe swing or a point blank shotgun blast
will one-shot them. The shotgun is best against them as keeping them away is
crucial. They mostly move towards you slowly, but they can teleport to you
whenever you are not looking at them, making them very dangerous if they appear
with other enemies. They have their own way of making that happen, since the
first spider can toss its torso at you and spawn another, weaker version of
itself, giving you two targets which you then need to keep locked in your field
of vision to avoid the teleports. Keeping all spiders in view slows them way
down and makes their movements very easy to track, although you have to watch
out for the irritating sidestep they have in order to counter your ranged
weapons.

  Either the spider or its duplicate can hit you with ranged limb attacks
(although a spider who throws a projectile while too close to you will actually
throw it harmlessly over your head). They inflict little damage, their danger
lies in their rushdown, so take one out as soon as you see it to minimise the
likelihood of encountering any more. Just keep backing away non-stop at walking
pace, waiting for a chance to line up a shot or a swing. Remember they are
terrible in water, being unable to teleport there.

  A human spider will die to a bear trap, and since their hurtbox is so wide
they generally cannot avoid the traps, making these a great defensive mechanism
at night. Additionally, always be ready to trigger your Third Eye Skill when
having trouble against human spiders, as it effectively removes their teleport
ability while active.

### Swamper

                
  These things usually lurk underwater in the Swamp, where they are immune to
damage; they are still detectable by moving ripples. They typically open by very
quickly vomiting acidic bile at you from long range which captures you like a
chain trap while you stand in it and stays around for a few seconds. They can
reuse this ability every ten seconds or so, so when they do it once you know
that the next thing they are likely to do is rush at you for a fistfight. Their
rush ability is only available to them from a submerged position, so once they
disappear underwater expect a pounce. (If you hover your mouse cursor over the
bile pool you might spot a tooltip which says "chain trap"!)

  Once engaged in melée they are just standard enemies, a lot like human
spiders. If they fail to lock you down with the bile trap then you are just
backing away and loading up swings or shots, the usual stuff. While swampers
never turn up at night, they are not nerfable by fighting in water, making it
preferable to fight them on land where you have plenty of room to move aside to
avoid the bile shots. Any time you see a corpse, a crate or a mushroom in the
middle of a body of Swamp water, expect a swamper to be nearby and have your
shotgun accordingly ready.

### Centipede

  Probably the most dangerous of the Swamp-specific enemies, centipedes have
several ways to discommode the Protagonist. They can charge at you from mid
range, burrow up from the ground underneath you, and swipe at you with their
pincers. They like to hide in holes, particularly dangerous because some holes
are loot caches and others are centipede lairs, and unless you take care to spot
the little insects crawling around the inhabited holes it's impossible to know
which is which until you are close enough to activate the centipede. 

  One easy way to deal with them is to let them burrow and appear under your
feet, during which time you can walk calmly away to reposition. This will almost
always incite the centipede to burrow again, and you can use that time to load
up an axe swing. When they reappear underneath you they take about a second to
fully emerge and turn towards you - this is plenty of time to execute them, and
because you are continually moving you are never where the centipede burrows to
by the time they emerge.

  Otherwise they are made to be taken down with guns, ideally in one close range
shot. Weapons like gas and fire are very poor because of their mobility and
their constant burrowing, so keep it simple and just shoot them when they get
close.

### Burning Villager

                
  Depending on the route you take through the game, these may well be the last
enemies you ever encounter. You will only meet them at night, and only then on
the night following your decision to kill the Talking Tree. See section 2.6 for
more details.

### 2.5.2 Hazards

        
  These are environmental effects which typically cannot be tackled with weapons
and must be negotiated in other ways. They do not benefit the Protagonist in any
way, so anticipation is a big part of defense, which usually means having the
right inventory prepared.

### Black Substance

                
  This is unique to Chapter 2, a black coating around various enemies found in
the Swamp, almost always in the Great Lake (although some black substance
enemies can turn up during the night, too). Once the black substance has been
removed by exposure to light (not including daylight), the surrounded creature's
armour is gone and it can be fought as normal. Attacking it while the black
substance remains is pointless, as it will take no damage. Have a lantern or
flashlight ready to remove the black substance from protected enemies, and stay
away from them until it's gone. The substance itself is not capable of hurting
you, it's just the enemies' version of armour.

### Corpses

                
  From the Silent Forest onwards, all corpses on the floor should be treated
with suspicion. I refer to 'twitching corpses' a few times in the guide: these
are technically living people but they might as well be dead, and as far as you
are concerned they should be avoided along with all actual corpses. The
exception is where a corpse / body is lying perfectly still and either glued to
the ground or abandoned by crows when you approach. Such bodies are safe to loot
for minor supplies; all others will either burst and poison you when you step on
them or try to open them, or - even worse - transform into red chompers.

### Darkness

                
  It's the Darkwood, and darkness is everywhere. You can't fight what you can't
see, so when you find yourself without light because you didn't plan properly or
you ran out of materials you are at your most vulnerable, often to the point
where you can't even escape the area because you are so badly lost. At this
point you are unlikely to make it back to the Hideout before the floor gore gets
you, assuming you are above ground and outwith the Hideout's environs, so you
should use your map and your Navigator ability to give yourself a chance.
Alternatively, if you deliberately allow yourself to be killed before the clock
hits 19:30 you will respawn at your Hideout and can hope to recover your dropped
items the next day - the point of this is to give yourself a chance to survive
the night in the Hideout, which will at least give you some money.

  You may also find yourself in the darkness while underground, but at least
there you can always find light sources have been provided to help you navigate.

  Note that the Old Woods is noticeably darker than previous biomes (or even the
Swamp) which makes navigation there harder, suggesting that you should not
explore that biome without some sort of light source at the ready.
                

### Floor Gore

                
  This scary occurrence will chase you around whenever you attempt to leave the
safe confines of an Oven-protected Hideout at night (from 20:00 to 8:00). It is
slightly slower than the Protagonist's sprint, but it will catch and damage you
once your stamina runs out, making the Runner Skill and other endurance boosting
consumables critical for outrunning it until you can make it back to the
Hideout; slow acting curatives are also useful.

  It cannot be exploited in order to make it damage enemies; but at the same
time it cannot possibly reach you while you are within the Hideout's catchment
area. Experiment with the outer walls of each Hideout to learn their limits; and
note that the Swamp Hideout is especially mercurial when it comes to the floor
gore's access. Floor gore is kept at bay by the Oven's gas, which cannot be
disabled at night (since you cannot interact with a lit Oven during the night);
while the Generator is unrelated to the floor gore, meaning if the fuel runs
out, along with the light, you are no more vulnerable to the floor gore than you
otherwise would be.

### Mist

                
  This will mainly appear in the Swamp, tied to specific and apparently randomly
chosen areas outside of Major Locations, although it also features heavily in
the Mushroom Glade, the Swamp Cottage and the Old Shed. It's just another
version of darkness, although you can at least see well enough to navigate,
thanks to the wider circle of vision.

### Pain

                
  These glare zones show up in Chapter 2. See section 2.3 for more information.
        

### Poison

                
  Some areas can be considered poison surfaces, as they are so heavily covered
in poison mushrooms. You can carefully walk around all the mushrooms, but this
usually requires light and careful positioning with the mouse cursor. Moving
among poison mushrooms will not affect you with the Poisoned status effect
unless you actually step on at least one mushroom, with subsequent ruined
mushrooms stacking the DoT damage. This can all be avoided with the antidote or
the Rubber Boots. Some enemies can also step on mushrooms and receive mild
poison damage.

### Rainstorms

                
  These don't damage you exactly, but they are a nuisance because they cut down
on visual and audio cues, making it harder to get around safely. The only remedy
is to wait them out.

### Slow Surfaces

                
  Some few surfaces in the game act like water, slowing you down and preventing
you from sprinting. The ? Minor Location in the Old Woods behaves in this way:
just stay off the affected area.

### Traps

                
  All the biomes are littered with bear traps, the worst being the Old Woods.
There are very few places where chain traps may be encountered.

### Trees

                
  Referring to the overhead branches and foliage, the trees are a problem
because they obscure areas of the ground underneath them, even when the
Protagonist is already looking in that direction. To solve this problem you have
to move the mouse cursor over the patch of ground directly under the trees to
actually see what's there, which may include enemies, traps or poison which
would have otherwise remained invisible until it was too late.

### Vines

                
  Vines appear at the base of some trees in the Swamp, warning you of their
presence with a loud twisting, snapping sound. If you get too close and the
vines choose to attack, they will grab and hold you, dealing light poison
damage, until they eventually wither away and you can escape. They will usually
not even try to trap you, no matter how stubbornly you stand on them, unless
there is another enemy around who can take advantage of the trap: therefore, by
killing everything in the Swamp you render the vines harmless.

### Worms

                
  This floor coating can spawn in various places throughout the game, mostly in
the Swamp. It can be crossed at the cost of a little damage, although it also
slows down the Protagonist's movement: the quickest way to cross it is therefore
not with the disabled sprint but with several backdashes. It can be cleared away
with a molotov or by hitting it with a torch; although throwing a gas bottle and
then setting the gas on fire will somehow not clear it.

  If you encounter the Bug Infestation night event it's important to kill all
four bugs quickly, otherwise your Hideout will be coated in worms the next
morning.

# 2.6 Hideouts & Night Events

  About two thirds of Darkwood involves foraging in the forest during the day;
but at night the game changes and your strategies have to do likewise. This
section is for anyone struggling to know how to manage their Hideouts and
therefore keep dying at night. Each biome's Hideout has its own section in the
Walkthrough part of the guide, so these are some more general tips and
strategies.
        

### 2.6.1 Hideouts

        
  Any Hideout can be used as a base of operations to explore any biome, with all
sorts of permanent storage and an invisible zone around it which prevents
enemies from approaching it unless they are specifically chasing the
Protagonist. Enemies are not unduly attracted by light or sound, but they will
respond to spotting the Protagonist in the light or hearing him moving around,
changing their behaviour from random exploration to homing in and attacking.
Stay away from windows and doors, even barricaded ones; and try to get all your
furniture, lamp and trap placements done before nightfall.

  Each Hideout has five very important tools: the Oven, the Saw, the Workbench,
the Generator and the lights. Each also has its own unique layout which never
changes game by game (although its orientation might), which in turn dictates
how they might be defended using barricades.

### OVEN

  The Oven is the most critical because without its protective gas you will not
be able to survive the night, chiefly because the floor gore can then get in and
kill you, and you have nowhere to run. With the Oven lit the floor gore cannot
reach you within the walls of any of the buildings, and also just outside the
walls (although the limits there must be tested and they are particularly
unclear in the Swamp). When you light any Oven all other Ovens in the game are
turned off, so you can only have one active Hideout at a time. If you find
yourself in a Hideout at night where the Oven has not been lit the game will let
you interact with the Oven at any time, day or night, so you can light it and
protect yourself. A lit Oven cannot be interacted with between 20:00 and 8:00 to
prevent you from save-scumming. A lit Oven therefore determines your current
Hideout.

  The Oven is also used to spend essence from hallucinogens, including
mushrooms, meat and embryos, among other things. Each successive Oven's
efficiency is quadruple that of the previous one: 1 > 4 > 16 > 64. This means
that the Old Woods Hideout is by far the best place in Chapter 1 to spend
hallucinogens, as you are getting 16 times as much essence from the same items.
Since the Old Woods is the most lucrative biome in terms of essence, it might be
used as your Hideout from Day 1 until you leave the Swamp. This is a fun
challenge and you should level up far more quickly.

### Saw

                
  The Saw allows you to convert finite logs (most often found in bunches at
logging camps) into planks, at the cost of gasoline. It can also be used for 8
units of storage. Try not to fill it with more gasoline than you need it for, as
that fuel cannot be recovered and serves no other function.

### Workbench

        
   The Workbench allows you to craft many items and weapons in the game; and
also provides you with a lot of storage space. You generally want to store all
your crafting materials in the Workbench and find somewhere else for
non-crafting items, otherwise you will not be able to see all the things which
are currently available for crafting. The Workbench refers to its own storage
and your inventory when checking what items are available.

### Generator

                
  Unlike the Oven, the Generator is not essential for night survival, and in
fact you can ignore it altogether if you want a more exciting time at night or
if you really need to conserve gasoline. All it ever does for you is provide
limited amounts of light, depending on where you choose to place your lamps
(although the Old Woods Hideout also features several immovable lights which
cannot be turned off except via the Generator).

  Being able to see at night is obviously extremely helpful, but this comes at
the cost of gasoline, although more can be bought from the traders. The
Generator produces sound and light, but it should be pointed out that neither of
these actually draw more enemies (as per the NOTE found in the Dry Meadow
Generator room) - enemies are indeed "coming either way". Therefore it's better
to have it on than off if you can afford it.

  When active the Generator not only provides light to any lamps, it also turns
on a light next to itself to keep it visible, and causes the Workbench to light
up slightly, allowing you to use them for navigation. The Workbench's weaker
light cannot be used for the Moth Skill's healing.

  Time freezes each morning and this pauses the fuel consumption of the
Generator, so there is no urgent need to turn it off. You should however turn it
off before leaving your Hideout each morning, or else Time Freeze will disappear
and the Generator will continue burning fuel.

### Lights

                
  Powered by gasoline from the Generator, your Hideouts always contain some
electric lights which cannot be destroyed. You can turn them all off by turning
off the Generator or allowing it to run out of fuel; banshees can turn them all
off temporarily by screaming; and the Shadows night event can turn all but one
of them off temporarily.

  The floor lamps can be moved by pushing or dragging them, allowing you to
position them according to your own taste (typically illuminating multiple
access points, traps you have set, or the Workbench). You can turn them on or
off manually by clicking on them (note that the Generator will burn the same
amount of fuel no matter how many lamps are on, including if NONE are on, so you
might as well have them all on).

  Floor lamps also act as useful barriers for enemies. Banshees are particularly
bad at pushing past them and can become permamently stuck against a floor lamp
placed at a chokepoint. In fact all enemies struggle to bypass lamps, since they
are so heavy and have wide collison. You can therefore place one or two behind a
door, so that even if an enemy batters down the the door they then have to push
back the lamps, without the sense to try a different route.

  Floor lamps are tied to parent Generators: the Dry Meadow lamps only work when
the Dry Meadow Generator is turned on, etc. One interesting feature of the floor
lamps is that they work no matter how far away they are from their parent
Generator, meaning you could theoretically drag the Dry Meadow and Silent Forest
lamps all the way into the Old Woods Hideout and then light all three Generators
to have lights everywhere! Of course, this would take a lot of time and a lot of
gasoline, but it is possible.

  The Old Woods Hideout features several wall lights. They all turn on and off
with the Generator and cannot be interacted with; although they can be used with
the Moth Skill. They do not cause the Old Woods Generator to burn more fuel than
would be used by the floor lamps in other Hideouts.

### Access Points & Barricades

                
  Defending your Hideout is helped by the fact that you can barricade most
access points, using planks and nails. This will not keep invaders out
indefinitely, but it does give the Protagonist a huge advantage. Firstly it
wastes a lot of the invaders' time, forcing often weak melée enemies to break
down a strong obstruction, eating up most of the night. Secondly, any enemies
committed to attacking a barricade can be attacked from behind and often will
not react, making for an easy kill. Tracking them down and killing such enemies
is an important priority, because you reduce the night enemies by one, take no
risk (at least against that single enemy) and save a barricade which would
otherwise have to be rebuilt.

  Doors and windows can be barricaded, but choosing which is better if you only
have limited resources depends on which enemies you think you are likely to
meet. Some enemies cannot climb through windows; whereas others cannot open
doors and must instead break them down. I suggest you barricade all windows but
only some doors - doors are far faster for your escapes and do not require that
you spend any time waiting for animations to end, during which you can be hit
and even killed. Once you commit to climbing through a window all sorts of
things can happen, whereas you can change your mind at any point with a door.
For the same reason, try to place traps only under windows. Note that enemies
will not select their access points based on how easy they are to use, they just
pick one and either use it or attack it.

  As for traps: they are too expensive in metal scrap to be placed everywhere.
They are very useful when triggered by an enemy, and utterly useless when
avoided. Generally, keep one or two traps in your inventory each night, but
don't place them behind doors or windows until you hear something attempting to
break through, then you know they will not be wasted. Traps placed in doorways
are especially helpful because any trapped enemy will then block all other
enemies (although be careful they are not blocking your only escape route).

  With all that said, no Hideout is completely impregnable, otherwise the game
would be very boring. Once you realise, especially later in the game, that
barricades and elaborate trap setups are a waste of resources given the
potential rewards for surviving, you should realise that evasion and running out
the clock are nine tenths of the law. If you find yourself running low on planks
and nails in the Silent Forest it's likely because you are building barricades
you didn't need to build. Unless you think dogs and savages are so terrifying
that they must be kept out at all costs, just run away instead, knowing that
morning will kill them all.
        

### 2.6.2 Night Events

        
  Once the clock hits 19:30 you are in danger of wasting a night, and therefore
some valuable reputation points, if you die between now and 8:00. Once the clock
hits 20:00 night is considered to be upon you, along with whatever the game
chooses to spawn into it to come and get you. Night lasts 4 and a half minutes
of game time; however, note that despite the fact that night technically begins
at 20:00, the likelihood of meeting any enemies or other night events before
21:30 at the earliest is almost zero, meaning you really have about 4 minutes at
the most. Keep a watch in your inventory throughout the night, and if you have
the music turned on listen for the slow swell indicating the arrival of morning
(the light will turn red anyway).

  Here are all the known night events, with names taken from the Darkwood wiki
to avoid confusion. The descriptions and strategies are mine.

### Beneficial

                
  A Strange Voice: A voice appears in text form, leading the player from various
points of origin to a shiny stone.

  Mysterious Benefactor: This event very rarely happens in the latest update, in
any Hideout. You'll know it when you hear it, as a knock on the door will become
louder and ever more insistent. Investigate to find some supplies dropped on the
floor.

  Poisonous Mushrooms: An odd-looking, glowing mushroom slowly spawns, with a
glow drawing the player's notice. Once it spawns you have a short amount of time
in which to harvest it, although it may draw you into danger.

  Shiny Stone: A shiny stone will spawn outside the Hideout walls, in the
darkness, usually outside the catchment zone of the protective gas, meaning you
have to make a dash for it. The trick with this event is that it resembles two
detrimental events, and you generally don't know which has happened until you
are close enough to be in danger.

  Wedding Invitation: See section 3.3.1.3 (Wedding) for more information.

### Neutral

                
  Bedfellow: A man (the Protagonist from the past) appears on the bed and talks
sorrowfully about his predicament. Once you move away he will disappear for
good. Don't attack him, you're attacking yourself and will take damage.

  Doors: Closed doors may randomly open by an invisible hand. This is meant to
draw you out of hiding to investigate in case it was an enemy.

  Flare: Looking identical to the beneficial Shiny Stone event from within the
Hideout, the Villagers throw a flare on the ground to draw you away from the
Hideout.

  Heartbeat: I have only ever seen this happen in the Old Woods, and only once.
The ground outside the Hideout boils with blood and guts (although it is not a
harmful surface to stand on), while a heartbeat noise can be heard.

  Infestation (visual): This is supposed to be rare, but it happens to me all
the time. Little bugs start crawling around the floor in the hope of making you
explore the Hideout in search of the larger bugs in order to kill them before
they can spread worms, but in the case of this type of Infestation event there
are no larger bugs, you're being toyed with.

  Infestation (worms): This is another common one, where little bugs appear to
warn you of four larger bugs who will pop out of bug holes which appear in the
floor of the Hideout. These can be killed for shells (loot them before morning
arrives), and should be because if left unkilled they will wander off and pop
themselves, creating carpets of worms once the morning comes around. You will
then have to clear the worms with fire.

  Nightly Sacrifice: Kill enough Savages in the Silent Forest and this event may
happen, a ring of fire surrounding a sacrifice. It's not clear whether this is
some sign of submission or worship, or if it's meant as a warning. Either way
it's insignificant and can be ignored.

  Villager: A Villager approaches the Hideout and then runs away. Don't bother
pursuing, the loot is not worth the danger.

### Detrimental

                
  Full Moon: This just announces the arrival of dogs with a persistent howling
noise which will remain even after you kill the two dogs who turn up. This may
actually be beneficial, as huge dogs are worth odd meat, plus they will attack
any other enemies they find before reaching you.

  Red (pain) Glare: Pain centres spawn under the Protagonist's feet, forcing him
to move away. After a while they relocate to wherever the Protagonist is
standing. The damage is mild, but its danger comes from forcing you to move, so
try moving to somewhere you don't need to be in order to place the pain centre
somewhere meaningless.

  Molotov: Someone chucks a molly at your Hideout, likely setting some part of
it on fire. This will not destroy walls, obviously, but it does prevent you from
moving through the burning area without taking damage. This is an extremely rare
event.

  Poltergeist: One of the most annoying events, all the more so because it's so
common. Expect all your furniture, your lamps, and even the Protagonist to be
moved around the Hideout until the event stops. Traps remain where they are and
enemies are seemingly not affected.

  Black Fog: Another very rare event, a black mass forms where the Protagonist
is standing, forcing him to move away. It will cause damage while you stand in
it, but it will not move to chase you.

  Earthquake: Similar to the Poltergeist but a bit more powerful. The final
tremor temporarily knocks out all the lights except one randomly chosen one,
similar to the Shadows event.

  Shadows: Most people's least favourite, the Shadows event will happen on any
night after you have chosen the Shadows Skill after levelling up. Shadows appear
and chase you around the Hideout, occasionally hitting you a for a little
damage. You have to stand by your one working lamp until the event abates; and
if you can't get there try popping a flare instead. Shadows also knocks out your
lantern, and a torch won't help, so stay near a lit lamp and pop a bandage while
you wait it out. A really bad event to get in conjunction with banshees.

  Wind: A long lasting gust of wind sweeps through the Hideout, coating
everything in patches of darkness. This hardly ever happens, but it can be
really bad when it does, since you can't see traps or enemies even if you have
lights at the ready. Pop a bandage just in case, and camp in a corner near a
couple of escape routes.

#### 2.6.2.1 Inferno

  If in Chapter 2 you choose to kill the Talking Tree, the following night will
impose a unique night event known as the Inferno. First of all the radio talks
to you for a while, then once it's finished it sets the Hideout's Oven room on
fire, all your lights go out, and you are swarmed from midnight on by endless
Burning Villagers (don't bother trying to fight them, just equip some armour,
some alcohol bandages and a lantern and keep walking away to avoid them as they
are very slow; use Runner and Chameleon; brilliantly, they can be killed with
fire!).

  This night is special in another way, being that the Burning Villagers do not
appear until the radio fire has spread far enough, and up until that point the
usual floor gore which patrols all non-Hideout areas is also paused. This means
that you can be a long way away from your Hideout when night kicks in at 20:00
and still make it back without even seeing the floor gore.

# 2.7 Speedruns

  I thought it might be fun to put together a possible strategy for getting
through Darkwood as quickly as possible (playing on Nightmare difficulty and
skipping the Prologue). I laid it out and tested it, and found that I could
complete Darkwood on Day 1, in a time of around 34 minutes (from waking up in
the Dry Meadow to the end of the Transcript). This is a very fun challenge,
although once you manage it the horror of this game is significantly reduced!

  (I do know that speedruns typically involve various skips and glitches, none
of which are listed below. I have not looked into the existence of any possible
Darkwood speedrun community, but if you want to take this part of the game
seriously I suggest you have a look for yourself.)
        
  Obviously, any lightning fast playthrough would eliminate levelling up via
essence or upgrading the Workbench. There are no nights involved, and Hideouts
are only visited for minor materials (the Dry Meadow Hideout crate is not used).
All possible story is skipped and no non-essential Locations are visited. All
combat is avoided and so there is no need for weapons (although at least one
torch is required for light and for killing the Chicken Lady). Since the
speedrun time can be reduced further through use of endurance consumables, any
such items are collected and used regularly. Locations where time is frozen
(Village, Underground Entrance, Radio Tower Bunker) are exploited for inventory
management in order to conserve daylight. The daylight problem is ameliorated
further by the fact that arrival in the Swamp begins a new morning, giving the
player that whole day to reach the Radio Tower Bunker. Certain Locations have to
be identified by recognising certain characteristics of the maps' edges; and it
is possible to be unlucky with Location spawns (such as the Road To The Doctor's
House spawning at the opposite end of the Old Woods from the Burned House, for
example). Finally, the speedrun requires that you leave the Village via the
Radio Tower Bunker and achieve the Bliss Ending.
        
  Anyway, here are my step-by-step strats for what I think is the fastest
possible speedrun route, using only guaranteed materials from key Locations in
order to utilise at least two torches, alongside plentiful curatives and
endurance boosters. 

  Part 1 (Dry Meadow):

    Spawn in the Dry Meadow Hideout.
    Collect rags x2, alcohol x2 and torch from the wardrobes and corpse 
      (all guaranteed).
    Run to the Entrance To Silent Forest.
    Collect any rags / matches / gasoline from the generator (some 
      guaranteed).
    Open the first DOOR with the KEYCODE (3-3-3-3).
    Collect any rags / matches / alcohol from the wardrobe (some 
      guaranteed).
    Open the second DOOR with the KEYCODE (3-3-3-3).
                
  Part 2 (Silent Forest):
        
    Run to the Hideout (run straight ahead); collect the potato from the box in
      the Generator room and the alcohol from the outdoor wardrobe (both
      guaranteed).
    Use the potato for endurance.
    Identify the Road To The Village on the Silent Forest map.
    Run to the Road To The Village.
        
  Part 3 (Village):
        
    Run to the Chicken Lady's house.
    Collect the bread, chicken eggs, planks and alcohol from her house and
      shed (all guaranteed).
    Use the torch to kill the Chicken Lady; unequip the torch to 
      conserve light.
    Collect the DOCTOR'S INSTRUCTIONS #3 and chicken eggs from her corpse
      (both guaranteed).
    Equip all chicken eggs in the second hotbar slot.
    Use the bread for endurance.
    Craft at least one more torch, using the gasoline instead of the alcohol; 
      use the remaining materials to craft bandages / bandages with alcohol; 
      rearrange the hotbar.
    Leave the Village via the holes in the ground.
                
  Part 4 (Silent Forest):
        
    Reappear outside the Silent Forest Hideout.
    Run to the Bridge.
    Identify the Road To The Doctor's House on the Old Woods map.
                
  Part 5 (Old Woods):
        
    Use a chicken egg for endurance to run past the chomper(s).
    Equip the torch for light.
    Run to the Road To The Doctor's House, using chicken eggs for stamina.
                
  Part 6 (Doctor's House):
        
    Unequip the torch to save fuel.
    Unlock the SAFE with the KEYCODE (8-4-5-9).
    Collect the MAP and the CODE.
    Equip the torch for light.
    Return to the Old Woods.
                
  Part 7 (Old Woods):
        
    Run to the Train Wreck, using chicken eggs for stamina.
    Open the Train Wreck with the CODE.
    Collect alcohol x2 from the bed (guaranteed).
    Spare the Doctor.
    Run to the Burned House, using chicken eggs for endurance.
    Collect the KEY and the pill; open the DOOR to reach the Dry Meadow; 
      collect the rags x2 from the outdoor wardrobe (all guaranteed).
                
  Part 8 (Dry Meadow):
        
      Unequip the torch to save fuel.
      Run to the Underground Entrance.
      Loot the containers outside for any curative materials (some guaranteed).
      Go underground; equip the torch for light.
      Get the Doctor to OPEN THE FIRST ARMORED DOOR.
      Talk to the MAN to OPEN THE SECOND ARMORED DOOR.
      Rearrange the hotbar to include curatives and endurance boosters, leaving 
        the third slot free.
      Exit to the Swamp.
                
  Part 9 (Swamp & Radio Tower):
        
    Find the lantern in the Workbench and equip it in the empty hotbar slot.
    Identify the Radio Tower on the Swamp map.
    Run to the Radio Tower, using endurance boosters (keep at least one).
    Talk to the Doctor to save the game (optional).
    With the lantern equipped, run past the banshees to reach the window.
    Run past the remaining banshees to reach the METAL DOOR.
    UNLOCK THE METAL DOOR with the CODE (8-6-8-4).
    Enter the Radio Tower Bunker.
                
  Part 10 (Radio Tower Bunker & Final Dream):
        
    Reach the METAL LEVER and ATTACK THE FUNGUS with a melée weapon.
    Survive the Final Dream.
    Exit to the Road Home via the east exit.
                
  Part 11 (Road Home & Epilogue):
        
    Run to the Epilogue as quickly as possible from the Road Home.
    Run to your apartment, TAKE OFF YOUR COAT, collect the KEY, LIE DOWN.

# 2.8 Frequently Asked Questions

  Q: What happens if I skip the Prologue or turn off the Indiegogo content?
  A: Skipping the Prologue has no effect on anything. You miss no items at all,
and once you've played it for the story there is no point in ever playing it
again. It's just there to set up the rest of the game in terms of lore and of
learning the controls. Disabling the Indiegogo content just turns off a totally
irrelevant Dream sequence which breaks the fourth wall.

  Q: What am I seeing on screen? Why can I see things but the Protagonist can't?
  A: The Protagonist can only see in detail things which fall within his vision
cone, directly in front of him. This is controlled by moving the mouse cursor.
All other things outside of this cone are treated as his memories of what he has
seen in the area. Thus he can remember the positions of walls, trees, boulders,
water, etc. because their positions are static; whereas he cannot remember or
trust the positions of dynamic elements, such as enemies, traps, poison
mushrooms. This is why the darkness surrounding the Hideout at night is swirling
with horrors - he is imagining all sorts of things which might be lurking there;
similarly the Hideout is crawling with worms and tentacles each morning until he
checks and finds that he is imagining it.
        
  Q: I only have six hotbar slots. How can I get the seventh?
  A: Gossip with the Wolfman at his camp in the Silent Forest until he tells you
to "silence the squealing", kill the Sow in the Pig Shed, then gossip with
Wolfman again once you have crafted the first six hotbar slots with fabric and
rope. He will reward you with a seventh hotbar slot which you then must equip
and use from the hotbar.

  Q: The Options > Controls menu indicates there are nine hotbar hotkeys, but I
can only use seven. Why is this?
  A: That menu is a legacy artifact which they never got around to changing,
having created it before creating the rest of the game. It also has other
defunct options.

  Q: What's the best Chapter 1 Hideout?
  A: If you are asking that question you are likely quite new or inexperienced,
and so you want the easiest nights for the best money return in order to get
better. The Silent Forest is your best bet. Other than the very occasional
single banshee there is little to worry about at night, and the area is rich in
resources. You also level up faster here than anywhere else, whether in terms of
Skills or inventory. For all of these reasons it's recommended that you spend as
little time as possible in the Dry Meadow and Old Woods Hideouts, and make the
Silent Forest your main focus in Chapter 1.
        
  Q: How do I open this locked crate / door?
  A: All locked crates and doors are detailed in section 3. WALKTHROUGH. There
are two crates at Wolfman's camp whose keycodes are randomly generated and must
be guessed.
        
  Q: How do I open this door at the Wedding?
  A: See section 3.3.1.3 (Wedding).
        
  Q: How do I deal with these cables at the Pig Shed?
  A: You need to buy a length of Cable from Piotrek or kill Hanuska in the
Village and take hers. See section 3.3.2.2.4.

  Q: I really can't be bothered with all the Village crap. Can I just skip it?
  A: Yes, nearly all of it, thankfully. Buy a Chain from Piotrek or the Wolfman,
repair the Well, run to Janek's house and grab the Key, then leave, never to
return. Other than some high level weapon components and a whole bunch of lore,
the only other thing of worth in here (not counting the Musician) is the
Doctor's Instructions #3, and you don't need that if you collected the Key. You
can similarly skip the Pig Shed.

  Q: Should I give the Key to the Musician or the Wolfman?
  A: The Wolfman is by far the better choice. You get access to much better
equipment, you save the Musician's life (sort of), and you prevent the Wolfman
antagonising you in Chapter 2. There seems to be little to no benefit in giving
it to the Musician, it's all downside.
        
  Q: I know I need to get into this Train Wreck but I have no idea how. What do
I do here?
  A: This is what Chapter 1 has been leading to and can be pretty complicated.
Start at section 3.3.3.2 and read on.
        
  Q: I moved to Chapter 2 and forgot to bring the Bike Bell. How do I get Bike
Man to bring over my stuff?
  A: The Bike Bell cannot be used to transfer items between the Chapters, so it
was useless at that point anyway. You have to carry anything you want to keep.
        
  Q: In terms of equipment, what's the best way to increase my defense and
survivability?
  A: The best form of defense is avoidance: don't pick fights you don't have to.
With the exception of Wolfman, no enemies ever drop anything valuable enough to
justify the fight. Farming for odd meat is the best argument I can come up with
for unnecessary combat, and even then only in the Swamp. Odd meat counts towards
defense, as each time you consume one it gives you the thick skin status buff
for a massive 150 seconds. This can complement the effects of light armour which
provides its own separate buff; and with both in effect you are very hard to
kill (although neither does anything against poison, gas, fire, worms). Stack
both these positives with the prolonged curative effects of a bandage with
alcohol and the extra half-bar of health from the Vitality Skill and you are as
close to invincible as you can get. Add some more Skills on top (Moth, Acid
Blood, Scream, Chameleon) and nothing is touching you. Of course, it's rare that
all of these things will come together perfectly, so it's a good thing that you
will hardly ever need them to. Optimisation and min-maxing among all these
options is the key to survival. The other side of survival is the offensive
side, making sure you are using the best weapons.

  Q: There's a lot of weapons and stuff here. What are the best ones, which
should I be focusing on?
  A: Player style is a part of it, but if you ask me I would recommend the
following weapons, in order of Workbench level and biome: board with nails >
pitchfork > pistol > single-shot shotgun > axe > pump-action shotgun; ignore the
rest or use them as secondarys. Always upgrade your chosen melée weapons where
possible, and always buy and forage for the ammo for your chosen ranged weapons.

  Q: I can't hit anything with [melée weapon], I just keep getting stunlocked!
How do you fight anything in this game?
  A: See section 2.5.1 for more information.

  Q: I keep dying in the Old Woods / Swamp. Why is this so hard?
  A: Almost certainly because you haven't upgraded your inventory to cope. Each
time you upgrade the Workbench you get new options, mainly for weaponry. The
fact that the Workbench can be upgraded to level 5 in Chapter 1 should suggest
what weapons you should be using, at a minimum (although even stronger ones can
be found in Chapter 1, if not crafted). If you insist on spending any time in
the Old Woods, you should not be leaving your Hideout without an upgraded melée
weapon (ideally a pitchfork or shovel), a ranged weapon (pistol or single-shot
shotgun; and plenty of ammo), a lantern, a stack of curatives (ideally bandages
with alcohol), and some armour would be nice. Don't sprint everywhere, move
slowly and carefully, scout ahead, listen for sounds of nearby enemies and avoid
them if at all possible. If you must fight something always try to do so in the
open and in the light, with plenty of space between you and it. Avoid mushrooms
and watch out for traps; and don't forget to use your active Skills, they can
save you. As for the Swamp, it's the hardest and last biome in the game, but it
offers plenty of advantages if you know where to find them. The first one is a
maximally upgraded Workbench, capable of crafting the pump-action shotgun, the
hunting rifle and the fully upgraded axe. The second is water: it slows down all
enemies except swampers, buying you all the time you need to load up a swing or
reload your weapon, always while backing away. The third is that everything
drops odd meat, allowing you to constantly apply the thick skin status effect to
yourself. The fourth is open air: there are far fewer trees which means it's far
easier to see. And the fifth is resources: they are everywhere. If you can
optimise all of these factors you will unavoidably become far better at the
game, and Nightmare mode will be pretty much indistinguishable from Normal.

  Q: Yeah, what are the differences between the difficulty levels?
  A: None, outside of number of lives. In Normal there is no permadeath, you can
play infinitely. In Hard you get a handful of lives, although each embryo you
find can give you an extra one. And Nightmare is Ironman mode: if you die once
it's game over (not counting dying in Dreams). Once you get good enough at the
game to 'speedrun' it, you realise that there are very few places in the game
where you are likely to die (not counting night events). I would nominate the
following as potential banana skins in Nightmare mode: Big Ruin (3), Great Lake,
Sawmill (outside of Wolfman's Path), Radio Tower.

  Q: I killed [NPC] or lost [item]. Can I still complete the game?
  A: Yes, don't worry about it. There are no mission-critical NPCs in this game,
and the extremely few key items are either impossible to lose or are not really
key anyway.
        
  Q: OK, so what are these key items?
  A: There are only five key items in the game, whose collection is
(technically) critical to completing Darkwood. The first is the Key Covered In
Chicken Feces, found in Janek's house, in the Village. This must be given to
either the Musician or the Wolfman in order to continue. If you somehow cannot
find it or you killed both those NPCs, an alternative is to find the Doctor's
Instructions #3 by killing the Chicken Lady in the Village and taking it from
her body. This item will remain in place until you pick it up, whereupon the
Road To The Doctor's House will be opened for you in the Old Woods. The Key and
the DI#3 are therefore interchangeable, you only need one of them and they're
both in the same location and are imperishable, being key items. Note that the
Road To The Doctor's House is closed off at the start of the game, and the only
ways to open it are by collecting the DI#3 or asking the Wolfman to take you to
the Doctor's House. The Big, Metal Key is the third key item and it is held by
the Doctor. You have to meet him in the Train Wreck and take it from him either
after killing him or allowing him to live, at which point you no longer need it
anyway, as he will open both Armored Doors for you. The fourth key item is the
Compressor Parts, found in both the Junkyard and the Radio Tower Locations in
the Swamp in Chapter 2. Once either has been found and used to repair the
Compressor at the Hideout, the other is irrelevant. Finally, the Oxygen Tank is
the last key item you need, and it can be found in the Swamp Cottage and the Old
Shed. Fill it at the Compressor and you can then traverse the Underwater
Corridor, therefore allowing you to kill the Talking Tree. So, in summary you
need the following: EITHER the Key Covered In Chicken Feces OR the Doctor's
Instructions #3; and always the Big, Metal Key. Neither the Compressor Parts nor
the Oxygen Tank(s) are essential because you can also escape the Swamp and
complete the game by leaving via the Radio Tower Bunker.

                                3. WALKTHROUGH
        

  This is the meat of the guide, designed to take you through the entire game in
the most optimal and efficient way possible. Darkwood is a game in which there
is no point in delaying its completion. Unless you are trying to explore
everywhere and find everything for the sake of adventure or completionism, the
sooner you escape from Chapter 2 the better, especially on Nightmare mode where
it's essential that you spend a minimal amount of time in the Darkwood.

  Therefore, I recommend you use the information below to help you build your
own optimal approach, according to your own playstyle. Best case scenario is
that you will be able to complete the game before Day 1 is over; otherwise try
to reach the Swamp by about Day 8, and keep in mind that if the objective is to
escape from the Darkwood, and the Radio Tower Bunker offers you a way to do so
virtually immediately, the only reasons not to do so are either about challenge
or about experiencing the story.

  Finally, the only difference between Normal mode and Nightmare mode is the
number of lives: infinite in Normal, one in Nightmare. Once you complete the
game on Normal, Nightmare should become your default choice and cannot fail to
improve your skill at the game.

# 3.1 Generic Minor Locations

        
  This is a brief list of the various Minor Locations from which the game
randomly chooses when generating each biome. They all have a chance of appearing
in any biome upon starting a new game, scattered around the various maps to fill
space and provide loot and dangers. Even if there is no loot to be found, they
are always useful as map markers for navigation.

  Note that each time you start a new Chapter the map will be randomly spawned.
The overall shape is taken from a pool of about four or five possibilities, and
the positions of all of the Major Locations which are always found at the
forest's edge within each one will remain consistent. This means that you can
tell at a glance where certain Locations will be, just by recognising the shape
of the map and of the wiggly line representing its edges. This helps a lot when
it comes to finding Locations like the Silent Forest Cottage, the Road To The
Village, the Road To The Doctor's House, the Train Wreck, etc. All other
Locations will be randomised in position to a certain degree, although some
rules remain in place (such as the Silent Forest Hideout always being found by
running in a straight line after emerging from the Entrance To Silent Forest).
        

### Bug Nest

                
  This is a really nice place at which to farm shells for armour, so it's great
when it spawns near your Silent Forest / Old Woods / Swamp Hideout. Each time
you approach the bugs they will scatter into the large holes and hide for a long
time, so if you can run to one of the holes and swing at the nearest bug as it
tries to duck inside you can get a cheap kill. You can also hit them with
projectiles (e.g. stones) or AoE attacks (e.g. molotovs). Make sure to return
here at least once per day for a chance at another shell or two.

  You will also find an egg in the centre of each nest when you first visit it.
Attack and destroy it for another embryo. Embryos do not rot over time, so keep
them until you reach the Old Woods Hideout at the earliest to get the maximum XP
boost from that Oven, or carry them into the Swamp at some inventory space cost;
alternatively, each embryo can be used as a very expensive curative, or to gain
an extra life in Hard mode.

  Remember that you can place bear traps over the several holes at each bug
nest, then just come back a short while later to find the bugs have reappeared.
Approach them to scatter them and any holes covered by bear traps will catch you
the shells you need.
        

### Logging Area

        
  A few felled trees surrounded by five caches of logs, nails and stones. They
are marked on the map as three logs. These sites are your best source of logs
and nails early on. The Swamp versions have the five caches placed around a
large body of water, usually with a crate or mushroom in the centre of the
water.

### Mushroom Field

        
  Marked as a single toadstool on the map, this is a pocket of white, poisonous
mushrooms. In the centre of the cluster, at the base of the trees, you should
find three harvestable mushrooms. Later Mushroom Fields will be guarded by
mushroom men.

### Rocks

        
  This generic Location is a cluster of rocks, usually guarded by savages, dogs
or a chomper. Sometimes there is a crate in there with random, minor loot. The
Silent Forest version has a corpse containing some reasonable loot; however,
interacting with it springs a trap where several savages spawn in and attack.

### Shrine

                
  These are lore-related markers; but try shining a light in the vicinity and
you should usually find a shiny stone. Savages praying at shrines should run
away upon the Protagonist's approach. There will never be more than one Shrine
spawned per biome.
        

### Treebound Corpse

        
  Some "unlucky bastard" tied to a tree and left for dead.
        

### Wooden Thicket

                
  Similar to the Mushroom Field Location, this is a very dense pocket of black
trees, requiring you to inch through tight corridors to reach the middle. In
there you can find three more odd-looking mushrooms and a pot with some meat in
it.

  The Swamp has its own version, just a circle of writhing trees almost entirely
surrounding a dense thicket. It is sometimes guarded by a pain centre, but
exploring around this can yield a few mushrooms. There are some traps on the
floor and a chomper hiding among the trees; but you can also find a backpack
with some reasonable gear.

# 3.2 Prologue (the Doctor's House)

  This is the optional tutorial section of the game, definitely worth playing if
it's your first time in Darkwood. It will teach you almost all of the basic
mechanics and drop a little lore, but there is nearly nothing in the Prologue
which affects events beyond it, so if you have already seen it there's nothing
to be lost by skipping it, especially since nothing you find in here will carry
over into Chapter 1.

  With one exception: the PLASTIC CHICK. This is a minor story item and so if
you do skip the Prologue and just begin in Chapter 1 you will find it already
collected among your key items. The PLASTIC CHICK can later be given to the
Chicken Lady, revealing that Janek (the man the Protagonist kills in the
Prologue) is / was her brother.

### 3.2.1 The Doctor

  By examining various things in the house you can learn that your current
character is a doctor, albeit one who has given up on helping the people who
were once his patients; now he is more focused on survival and escape.

  Explore the bedroom and the Doctor will note that his Generator is nearly out
of fuel. You find the KEY in the wardrobe: this will unlock the FRONT DOOR of
the house and allow you outside. 

  NOTE: Once you pick it up you will see that it is not in your inventory - this
is because key items (such as actual keys) are stored permanently and when it's
time to use them the game will let you do so automatically. You will also see
that even though you can collect items and add them to your inventory you cannot
yet craft anything from it.

  There are two doors in your house which you cannot yet open, so instead use
the KEY to go outside. Among other interactables you will find THE PEOPLE'S
TRIBUNE in your car, not far from your suffering dog. There is currently nothing
you can do for him, so head farther east and hop the fence. You should find an
AXE lying on ground, near a fallen TREE which blocks the path. Equip the AXE in
one of your hotbar slots, then select it. Bash the TREE out of the way with it
and proceed.

  NOTE: At this point you can also go back and put the dog out of its misery
with the Axe. If you do it will prevent a certain enemy from spawning much later
in the story, but this decision is of very little consequence either way.

  The game will present you with a dormant bear trap; then encourage you to open
and learn how to use your map; and then farther on it will finally allow you to
craft a torch, which you will need if you want to see more clearly in the
gathering dark. 

  NOTE: Time is effectively paused in the Prologue, but even though there is no
day-night cycle here there are still varying degrees of darkness. Also note that
you cannot equip your Axe and your torch at the same time, so there may be
occasions when your torch will have to serve as a crude weapon. Make one and
equip it before continuing.

  The abandoned house has a few minor items and interactables dotted around, but
farther east is where you want to go. Here you should find your goal: an
UNCONSCIOUS MAN lying at the end of a trail of blood (weirdly, you can't kill
him with the Axe - plot armour). Search him to find the BIG, METAL KEY: "If
there is an exit out of these woods, this key will surely open it."

  With the BIG, METAL KEY (note its value!) collected you will be treated to a
cutscene where the Doctor interrogates the Unconscious Man with no apparent
success, and once this is over you will switch control over to this new
character, with all the items you collected as the Doctor now gone.

### 3.2.2 The Unconscious Man

        
  This is the Protagonist of the game, and your first job is to get him out of
his new cell. Search the CLOTHES on the floor, then open your inventory to craft
a BANDAGE and a LOCKPICK. Equip the BANDAGES to heal yourself, then use the
LOCKPICK on the CONTAINER to find a SHOVEL and a flashlight. Equip the SHOVEL
and use it to bash down the BARRICADED DOOR (this will break the Shovel).

  As you rush out to confront your captor the lights go out and he vanishes. The
flashlight won't help you find him, but you can now see that night has fallen,
and the game will not let you leave the house. Interact with the MEDICAL TOOLS
to find the SYRINGE, then attempt to open the METAL DOOR nearby. You can hear
another captive beyond it, but you currently do not have the keycode. Go into
the bedroom, push the wardrobe out of the way and open the now unlocked WOODEN
DOOR behind it. In here you will find a GENERATOR, but it will not function
until it has GASOLINE, which you can find in the CRATE nearby, along with a
TABLE LEG (swap out your broken Shovel for the TABLE LEG in your hotbar). Equip
the GASOLINE and use it to fuel the GENERATOR, then TURN IT ON. 

  Back in the previous room you will find that the radio has been replaced with
a twitching corpse connected to the now working Generator by cables (if the
Generator is off the corpse will moan at you to turn it on). Examine the corpse
and then leave the room to the north, at which point the corpse should tell you
the code to open the METAL DOOR: 

    4-8-9-2

  Get your Table Leg ready and open the METAL DOOR, using the TABLE LEG to bash
it all the way open. Follow the voice which leads you closer. You will be
attacked, but your assailant is easy to defeat with a few swings (you can't lose
this fight). Search the corpse for the PLASTIC CHICK, then leave the room so
that you can't see his body. From here just await your inevitable doom...

# 3.3 Chapter 1

                       
  This very long chapter takes you from the Dry Meadow, through the Silent
Forest, into the Old Woods and finally beyond, back to the Underground Entrance,
never to return. While there are plenty of story beats in here, it should be
treated as one long levelling up expedition in preparation for the difficulty
spike represented by the Swamp. It is possible - and recommended - to spend
roughly one to two weeks in Chapter 1 collecting and building everything you
will need for later so that you enter the Swamp in the strongest possible
position. This means fully upgrading your inventory, upgrading your Workbench to
level 5, reaching level 3 on the Skills progression tree, and arming yourself
with the best available melée weapon (upgraded shovel / upgraded axe) and one or
two ranged weapons (pistol / single-shot shotgun / hunting rifle / assault
rifle).

  Each of the Chapter 1 biomes has at least two major locations (crucial either
for story or development), and a large assortment of minor locations (useful
mainly for loot alone). The major locations are covered first in each biome's
section, then the minor ones are assigned to their own section so that you can
decide whether or not you want to bother going there. Finding the major
locations sometimes means exploring for yourself so you can tag them on the map,
so when you find one treat it as a priority. I have included a TLDR section with
each biome so that you can do and find all the important stuff to optimise and
progress as quickly as possible, ignoring everything incidental.

  Finally, although I do detail the Musician's path if you choose to do that, I
recommend the Wolfman's path instead, as the rewards are far greater and the
risks are lower.
        

# 3.3.1 Dry Meadow

        
  The Dry Meadow is the first and definitely easiest of the biomes in Darkwood.
Once you know what you are doing it is actually quite difficult to die in here,
and you should be aiming to make 100 reputation points per day to get the most
out of it while you are here. The most efficient playthrough should allow you to
kill and plunder everything in the Dry Meadow (not including the Underground
Entrance weapon components) and still relocate to the Silent Forest by Day 3.
None of the Points Of Interest are all that far away from your Hideout, which
makes exploring easy. It really just exists to provide you with the gear and
training you will need to deal with the Silent Forest and beyond (you can even
have built a handgun by Day 3).

  Apart from a couple of scripted chompers and a huge dog, the most dangerous
creatures found in the Dry Meadow during the day are savages and regular dogs
(although the elk might disagree). Watch out for white poisonous mushrooms and
bear traps as you run around, and use the long tracks as much as possible to
conserve stamina. Make the most of the elk while you are here (you can find as
many as three; kill them for their valuable antlers, worth 100 reputation each
at any trader).
        

## Tldr

  
    Collect / buy some gasoline.
    Collect logs and nails from logging camps, use the Saw to convert the logs 
      into planks, build and upgrade a board with nails, upgrade the Workbench 
      to level 2.
    Collect / buy rope x4 from destroyed wells or the Trader.
    Collect FABRIC from the Dry Meadow Rocks / Burned Houses / Hideout crate.
    Collect the Invitation from the Hideout, collect FABRIC from the Wedding.
    Upgrade inventory x3 & hotbar x1.
    Buy two TOOLBOXES from the Wolfman, upgrade the Workbench to level 4.
    Relocate to the Silent Forest within the week (ideally before the end of
      Day 3.

#### 3.3.1.1 Miscellaneous

                

##### 3.3.1.1.1 The First Day

        
  You appear in your first Hideout, next to the all important Oven, already lit.
Interact with it and cycle through all the options, ending with COOK, to save
your game and to learn about how it works: forcefield generator, save point, and
level up point.

  There are also two wardrobes in your new Hideout: among other minor items, one
contains the MUSHROOM BOOK (which is just reminding you of the importance of
collecting, cooking and eating any mushrooms you find - you will find some
behind your front door and more by the Generator); and the other contains the
PHOTO OF A ROAD, a key item indicating your overarching objective. Push the
larger wardrobe so that it blocks the hole in the wall (you cannot ever properly
block this hole, so that wardrobe is currently the best way to deal with that
entry point).

  You will also find two lamps in your Hideout, so try dragging them around to
where you want them to be. I recommend that you place one so that it is
equidistant between the large wardrobe and the Workbench: this will let you see
if anything pushes the wardrobe aside, and it will also let you find the
Workbench if you need to use it during the night. The other lamp should stay in
the bedroom, which is where you should likely retreat to in emergencies - there
is a free bear trap already primed in there, and ideally you want to channel
your enemies towards traps, so drag the lamp to the centre of the room so that
you can see all four entry points and the trap.

  The Workbench is found in the front room. For now you cannot build much with
it, but it is a very useful storage basket for all the random crap you will be
picking up. Dump everything you don't currently want in here, and note that the
Workbench can be upgraded - this is a matter of priority. You can see that the
first tier of upgrade requires 6 boards and 10 nails; meanwhile your earliest
weapon, the board with nails (assuming you don't want to be swinging a torch
around) requires 2 boards and 10 nails. Finding these items is therefore crucial
at this stage.

  Leave via the front door and do a tour of the outside of the property. You
should find a Saw near the front door. It lets you convert logs into the boards
you will be wanting, but although it currently has some gasoline fuel it does
not have any logs. 

  NOTE: Don't spend gasoline on the Saw until you have logs to cut up, as any
extra fuel left in the Saw is wasted and cannot be recovered. Each bar on the
fuel meter represents 1 log.

  The Well is also nearby, but it requires yet more boards before it can
function as a once-per-day heal. Behind the Hideout is the Generator room. When
you enter the room your character notes that there is enough fuel in the
Generator for one more night, but more gasoline can be found at the Underground
Entrance (already marked on your randomly generated map), so for now just loot
the corpse on the floor for some more items, including a very useful torch. You
can also find the ominous GENERATOR NOTE on the floor, as well as another
mushroom.

  NOTE: Even though there seems to be a lot of fuel in the Generator when you
first arrive, the game will swallow nearly all of it on the first night if you
turn it on, irrespective of whether or not you even use your lamps, so finding
more gasoline actually is really important.

  NOTE: The torch is the first weapon you get beyond the Prologue. It is good
against dogs but little else, and because it acts as a light source it is
constantly burning down while you hold it, depleting it to the point where it
burns out and becomes useless. Using it as a weapon also depletes it. Having it
equipped in your hotbar will not deplete it, since you actually have to select
it, too. Bear in mind that shiny stones are really valuable at the start of the
game, but they will only show up when illuminated by a light source, which right
now is your torch. Run around with it selected as much as possible to get the
most value out of it, but look to replace it early as both weapon and light
source.

  Finally, you should also find a locked crate against the side wall of the
Hideout. This contains a FABRIC and two ropes, among other things, allowing you
an early inventory upgrade on Day 1. The code to this container is:

    2-9-1-9
        
  It was originally added to the game as an exclusive to Indiegogo backers, but
was finally added to the base game. It's up to you whether or not you want to
open it, but I assume you won't. It will still appear in games where you chose
not to see Indiegogo content.

  Once all of this is taken care of you should venture out into the surrounding
woods, poking around for pieces of material you can haul back to your Hideout.
You are especially looking for nails, logs and rags: the first two will let you
build a board with nails, Workbench upgrades and building repairs; and the rags
are for bandages and torches. Build a torch as a matter of priority (or find and
loot the Crop Field for the lantern), allowing you to explore with a light
source and find shiny stones - even one shiny stone can allow you a big cash
injection on Day 2 and afford you your first Toolbox from the Wolfman.

  There are certain places which are very beneficial to visit as early as you
can, although you will have to find them first. I recommend you tag some or all
of the following:
        
    Underground Entrance (loot cache)
    Burned Houses (loot cache)
    Dry Meadow Rocks (loot cache)
    Entrance To Silent Forest (loot cache)
    Wedding (allows Invitation night event to spawn)
                
  NOTE: Despite having only just arrived in the Dry Meadow, you should be
looking to leave very soon, only returning later when you have to. Because of
the way trading and levelling up work in this game, and because of the scarcity
and price of certain items, it is far better to move house to the Silent Forest
as early as you can, with its abundance of important resources, 50% more
valuable Hideout and 300% more valuable Oven. In other words, each night spent
in the Dry Meadow is an opportunity missed to spend a night in the Silent
Forest, which is a bit of a sweet spot in risk-reward terms. Therefore, plunder
the Meadow for everything you care about, then leave it behind so that you can
start making better money. This process should take a week at most in game
terms, so by Day 7 at the very latest you should have relocated to the Silent
Forest.

##### 3.3.1.1.2 The First Night

                
  At this point in the game you have no way to tell the exact time, but you will
know nightfall is approaching when the light around you starts yellowing. This
is your early warning that it's time to get back home and start preparing; and
in fact the game will literally warn you to get back home when 18:30 hits and
you're still out in the wild. As a general rule, you want to be home by 18:00,
as this gives you plenty of time to set up for the night. Once the game clock
hits 20:00 the Oven becomes inaccessible for the rest of the night, so make sure
you activate it while you still can to create that save point. 

  Don't forget to run to the Generator and turn it on, then head back into the
Hideout and take up position by the bedroom lamp (turn the other one off) as
night hits. Assuming you currently have no better weapon, have your torch
equipped in your hotbar, but don't actually select it unless you need to use it,
as it will burn down while you are holding it and eventually go out. You may
also consider keeping the Generator off entirely during this first night in
order to conserve fuel.

  The game should send you a single dog on the first night, this being the start
of the game, but if you keep the lights off and stay still you likely won't even
meet it. Remember that regular dogs can't open doors, so by running through and
slamming them behind you you force the dog to find another route. Note that dogs
can smash down barricades and jump through unbarricaded windows, or push the
wardrobe aside; also note that they can smell you through walls, so by standing
in the middle of the floor, by the lamp, you avoid this. As in all Hideouts,
tossing a piece of meat down at nightfall effectively nullifies any dogs that
find it, so dump a piece outside and avoid it from then on. The game will also
spawn a mushroom outside, near the Generator and the dog, to entice you to go
outside.

  Listen for the slow swell of music to know when dawn is arriving, at which
point it is pretty certainly too late for anything to damage you quickly enough
to kill you before you are safe once more.

  NOTE: You might consider dying to the dog or to the floor gore (the red horror
which tracks you if you try to move too far from the Hideout). The reason is
that this is the only night for which you gain no Trader credit for surviving,
and therefore there is no point in waiting for the morning, as the only downside
is that some of the extremely few enemies you may have already killed in the Dry
Meadow have a small chance of respawning. The exception to this is if you
already met and spoke to the Wolfman in the Dry Meadow, in which case you do
stand to earn Trader credit for surviving your first night.

##### 3.3.1.1.3 The Second Day

                
  The first thing that will happen when you wake up is that you will meet the
Wolfman in your Oven room (assuming you did not yet find his Camp in the Dry
Meadow and talk to him there; if you did then the Trader will turn up instead
and you will earn your first 100 reputation points then), a key quest giver and
trader. Talk to him while he's here and exhaust his dialogue, and check out what
he has for sale - you start with 50 reputation in credit, which means you can
sell him a shiny stone, a torch and a board with nails (for example) in order to
afford the TOOLBOX he is selling for 200, the earliest possible chance you have
of getting a TOOLBOX without going into the Silent Forest (again assuming you
didn't yet find his Camp). He will then mark both his Dry Meadow Camp and his
Silent Forest Camp on your map, and you can always find him there if you have
any spare weapons, weapon parts or ammunition to sell (he replenishes his stock
each time he moves, meaning he will have another TOOLBOX for sale when you go to
his Dry Meadow Camp - this is the only way to get both the TOOLBOXES you need
for Workbench upgrades without leaving the Dry Meadow). Other than the TOOLBOX,
the most significant items he sells are the watch (tells you the time while you
have it in your inventory, and the CHAIN (a key item later on) - you can find
both of these items elsewhere, however. Buy the TOOLBOX immediately if you can
afford to; and I recommend you buy a metal pipe from him or the Trader at some
early point, allowing you to buy the 'hardened' upgrade for your board with
nails.

  Speaking of which, you will want to upgrade your Workbench to level 2 whenever
you can. The first upgrade costs only boards and nails, while building your
first proper weapon, the board with nails, requires the same materials, so do
both of these once you have the materials. You can then build bear traps and
hotbar / inventory upgrades, among other things.

  NOTE: Upgrading the Workbench to level 3 allows you to create molotovs and
lanterns, both excellent pieces of equipment. However, as stated above, you will
need two TOOLBOXES for the upgrades, and the only way to get two in the Dry
Meadow is to buy one from the Wolf on Day 2, then buy another one from him at
his Camp. This is well worth spending 400 rep points on, especially if you have
been building torches and finding shiny stones.

  You will have noticed that, even if you took any damage during the night, you
woke up in the morning with full health. This always happens. If you died during
the night you will lose nothing (normally you miss out on trader credit, but
this does not apply to the first night), but some inventory items you were
carrying (not including hotbar items) may have been dropped on the spot where
you died, so make sure you recover them.

  You will also have noted that the Time Freeze mechanic has kicked in (unlike
on Day 1) and will stay in place until you leave the vicinity of the Hideout.
This pause freezes the fuel consumption of the Generator and allows you time to
repair, heal, refuel, etc. (although status effects such as poison or healing
are not frozen, nor are Skills). Once you roam too far from your Hideout it will
be removed and the day will begin properly. The Generator and lamps do not
continue to consume fuel while Time Freeze is in place, but turn them off anyway
before you leave. Save the game at the Oven, craft any items you want to take on
your next scavenge, then leave the Hideout.

  Now that you have a good grasp on what's going on, check out section 3.3.1.3
below for the various Minor Locations to be found in the Dry Meadow. Try to
visit as many of them as you can in a single day, and know that once you have
visited each one once there is hardly ever any reason to return, since
Location-specific loot and enemies do not respawn. Your main goals at this point
are to find the important upgrade materials (see TLDR above). Now equipped with
a decent weapon, a torch, and ideally a lockpick or two, exploring the Dry
Meadow on the second day becomes much easier, so take advantage by grabbing as
much loot as you can during the daytime.

##### 3.3.1.1.4 The Second Night

                
  This time you should be much better prepared, ideally with a reasonably
barricaded Hideout, a decent amount of fuel in the Generator, an upgraded board
with nails, a torch and a bandage. That should be all you need: just stay by a
lamp and act accordingly (especially if you unlocked the Shadows debuff when
levelling up). Even if it goes wrong and you die, you only missed out on 100
trader reputation points, which will become more important in the morning.

  If you managed to level up enough to have been forced to choose the Shadows
debuff from the Skill menu the game will hit you with the Shadows night event,
in which the Shadows will temporarily disable one of your lamps, forcing you to
move to the other one to avoid attack. After a while they will go away and both
your lamps will work again.

  If you have found more scrap metal before the second night don't use it to
make more traps, just save it instead. The Dry Meadow Hideout is not dangerous
enough to require loads of traps around the place, and there is nothing here
that can't be dealt with by just swinging a basic weapon at it. You will
definitely be wanting a lot of scrap metal later in the game, so the more you
save now the better. Also, at this very early stage of the game it is worth
looting the bodies of any savages or huge dogs you manage to kill in order to
salvage whatever they were carrying, especially since their bodies will despawn
with the arrival of morning, along with all the contents.
                
  Try to make use of your single trap if you need to do damage or just buy
yourself some time; stay near the lights so that you can see what's happening;
and if you do need to run away remember you can go outside and run around the
walls, using your torch (if you have one) for a light source. And listen out for
knocking if you are interested in visiting The Wedding, as this night is the
first and most likely moment for this event to spawn (assuming you already
visited the Wedding and found the Door locked) - if you do hear the knock on the
front door, run to it to find the Invitation on the ground in front of you. Grab
it, it's worth another piece of FABRIC!

##### 3.3.1.1.5 The Third Day

                
  Where you met the Wolfman the previous morning, this time you meet the Trader,
the person who rescued you from the Doctor's house (which explains how you got
out in the Prologue). He will appear every morning from now on in Chapter 1,
ready to trade, and he buys nearly anything and everything. He especially likes
torches, so before you trade with him make sure you fill your inventory from
whatever stashes you have in your Hideout, then see how much money you can get
from him. This early in the game you don't have much of value, but then you
don't need much of what you have found anyway. At this point the best things to
sell him are probably pills, flares and torches (note that he sells matchsticks,
which means you can buy a few of them really cheaply, use them to make a few
torches, then sell the torches back to him for profit).

  As for what he sells, there's a lot of good stuff in here, but unless you have
somehow managed to create lots of torches it's all pretty certainly out of your
reach for now, especially since your Workbench is not upgraded enough to build
the good stuff yet, so I recommend you instead buy all the scrap, rags and wire
he has each and every time you see him, and sell him everything you don't want,
with an emphasis on the higher value items, such as weapon components and shiny
stones.

  One more point: the shotgun is the best weapon type in the game, and all four
shotgun variants require shotgun shells for ammo. These can be found as loot in
both Chapters, but they can also be bought from both the daily traders, one at a
time. Buy one shotgun shell per morning and hoard them, they will pay off
massively later on, and they stack to 10.

  From here you are just trying to investigate the Dry Meadow as thoroughly as
possible, so work your way through the Locations below and tick them off one by
one. There are two you should leave until the others are done: the Underground
Entrance and the Entrance To Silent Forest.

        

#### 3.3.1.2 Major Locations

        

##### 3.3.1.2.1 Underground Entrance

                
  NOTE: This location is marked on your map from the beginning of Chapter 1 as a
large X. While visiting it is essential, it's not urgent that you do it right
now, you can leave it until much later in the game. However, since it does give
you some loot you may as well do the section above ground when you first
encounter it.
        
  Be careful exploring the building, as there are bear traps both inside and
outside, as well as large mushrooms. Loot the sack, backpack and clothes (on top
of each other) and the two wood logs outside for various items. Loot the
wardrobe for the PACK OF CIGARETTES, then move it out of the way to reveal the
door behind it. Another large mushroom in here, as well as the ENTRANCE. Equip a
light source and go inside.

  Time is frozen in here and the game autosaves. 
        
  NOTE: To the west is a blockage which requires a fully repaired shovel to
clear. This path disappears entirely after you leave the Underground Entrance at
your first visit (assuming you fully interacted with the DOOR), so make sure you
clear the two blockages with the shovel and loot the corpse behind it for its
valuable loot (long barrel, tubular magazine, weapon parts and shotgun shells,
all items for building the pump-action shotgun which is laughably far off)
before you try to mess with the DOOR, arguing for leaving this location until
later. Also, since it's impossible to build the shovel until your Workbench is
upgraded to level 4, coming back much later is recommended.

  Either way, explore to the east to find a backpack containing the MOTORCYCLE
MAGAZINE. Follow the passage the only way it goes and you should find a corpse
near a horrible blockage which moves when you shine your light on it and yields
some odd meat. You should find two more mushrooms before coming to a DOOR (the
same one from the Forest Dream). Interact with it and choose 'Look through the
keyhole' to see your exit point, maddeningly out of reach.

  Upon leaving the DOOR you must rush to the exit before a chomper can spawn in
and kill you. Killing the chomper is quite hard in the dark and with only a
basic weapon, and it only offers odd meat, so it's best to just run for the
exit. The next time you enter the tunnel the chomper should be gone, making this
a one-time encounter.

  Only one essential thing left to do before you can ditch the Dry Meadow
altogether.

##### 3.3.1.2.2 Entrance To Silent Forest

        
  This is currently the only way out of the Dry Meadow, so come here once you've
tied off everything else in the area. Before you make the trip be sure you have
plenty of inventory space, as well as the following supplies:

    Weapon
    Torches
    Curatives
    Alcohol

  Make sure to place all other supplies in your Dry Meadow Hideout into the
Workbench. Try to start your journey in the morning to give you plenty of time.

  Push your way through the tightly packed trees and you should come to a wall.
Follow it around until you find the hallway leading to a generator opposite a
crate which contains at least a molotov, useful for what's coming. The generator
still has some fuel, so turning it on should give you some light inside the
building. This will be your first encounter with bugs, so bring out your torch
or weapon and try to swipe one of them (one is scripted not to run away) to get
a shell, which can later be used to create armour at a level 5 Workbench. As
with all other bug locations, you can come back here later when the bugs have
respawned and kill more, up to a set limit of 4 per location.

  You should see a lot of horrible fungus (known as worms) all over the floor,
blocking the path in two areas. It will damage you slightly if you try to walk
over it, but you can remove it by attacking it with fire, which is why you
brought a couple of torches (note that exploding gas does not burn this stuff
away). Ignore the first locked door, loot the various containers around the
place, and follow the hallways until you find a second DOOR set in the outer
wall - it wants a code you do not yet have. 

  NOTE: You can actually unlock both doors here before ever finding the code,
since it is always the same.
        
  Continue exploring to find a large egg on the floor - you can attack this to
get an embryo, which can be cooked at the Oven (save it until you reach the Old
Woods Hideout). Next is another locked door which also needs a code, although
this is just a shortcut door which opens back into the Dry Meadow.

  Go around to the back rooms, and when you go towards the darkness in the last
room a savage will attack you with a staff. Chuck the molotov at him, or kite
him into the room lit by the lamp and finish him off, then loot the containers
and the shiny stone from the room he was hiding in.

  NOTE: Grab the savage's staff, as it is a decent replacement for your board
with nails, and it can also be used to make better weapons (including the shovel
if you have already bought the shovel head or are planning to).

  Explore the large wardrobe to find the NOTE telling you the KEYCODE for the
first outer DOOR you found:

    3-3-3-3
        
  It also mentions a Pig Shed, which sounds a bit ominous (remember the pig
sounds coming from inside the Burned House?).

  Use the KEYCODE on the outer DOOR to open it and gain access to the Silent
Forest. The same KEYCODE works on the other locked outer door. Loot the Sleeper
lying on the ground immediately outside to receive his MAP OF THE FOREST and
some shotgun shells. The MAP marks two locations on your map: the Silent Forest
Hideout and Piotrek's House. Once you walk away from the Sleeper he talks to
you, telling you to look for an exit beneath the floor of the last Hideout (he
means the apartment in the Epilogue: you're nowhere near that, but remember it
anyway). Since you want to reach your next Hideout as quickly as possible, head
for it on your map.

#### 3.3.1.3 Minor Locations

### Broken Tractor

                
  Search the truck for a VEHICLE PART and some gasoline, among other things.
        

### Broken Tree

                
  Look for a corpse with some minor gear beside it.

### Brook

        
  Follow the water to a narrow tunnel among the undergrowth. Follow it to find
some furniture, including a wardrobe containing some minor items. Note that
there is a chance you can find a medium caliber magazine in this wardrobe, an
exceptionally rare and valuable item which cannot be bought or manufactured -
hold onto it until you get a chance to use it!

  If you drag the wardrobe out of the way you can reach a generator. Turn this
on to reveal a corpse trapped beside a lamp, neither of which can be reached,
although turning on the generator will spawn a dog back at the entrance which
will ambush you when you try to leave.

### Burned House

        
  This House blocks the path through to the Old Woods. Approach the locked metal
door to overhear an unseen cutscene and some piggy sounds; but other than some
minor gear in the wardrobe and some mushrooms there is currently nothing else to
see here.

  This same location will become key later on in the game, in the Old Woods.
Once you open it up it serves two purposes: one is to make it easier to reach
the Underground Entrance from the Old Woods, which you will be doing later; and
the other is to allow you to explore the Old Woods while using the Dry Meadow
Hideout as your base, meaning you avoid all the nighttime nastiness in the Old
Woods.

### Burned Houses

        
  You can find these buildings by following the path which leads from your
Hideout. There are dogs roaming around, so distract them with meat while you
explore the various caches of minor items. Check the tractor outside for some
gasoline and a VEHICLE PART; and explore the destroyed well for a useful length
of rope. 

  In the corner room you can find the NOTE FROM THE BURNED HOUSE; and inside, in
the last room, you can move the sofa from above a container, inside which you
can find the WHITE DRESS, as well as a free bolt of FABRIC. There are also three
mushrooms to be found among these buildings.

  This is a very useful location to visit early, since it offers both the FABRIC
and the rope, both of the items needed for a hotbar or inventory upgrade.

### Camp (dogs)

        
  This is a campsite taken over by dogs. Try baiting one of them towards you,
kill it, then use its meat to distract the others while you grab any minor gear
you can find.

### Crop Field

        
  You'll know this when you see it, several rows of hedges which make up a maze.
There is a disassembled tractor at one outer corner, which can be looted for
minor treasure. You can also explore the maze, collecting as many as three
mushrooms along the way (listen for them, they are placed as traps around blind
corners); and when you get to the far side of the maze you should find a crate
in a clearing, inside which you can find a free lantern, a really nice piece of
early gear. 

  Unfortunately, reaching the crate spawns a huge dog, a pretty deadly opponent
in such tight confines, so make sure you go into the maze with a bandage and a
weapon, as well as plenty of inventory space. You might also try taking a bear
trap with you and setting it in the last tunnel just before the crate, luring
the huge dog into it for a one-shot kill.

### Dry Meadow Rocks

                
  This is a collection of large boulders, guarded by three dogs. Among the rocks
you should find a large chest which can be opened without a lockpick, and inside
should be a bolt of FABRIC and a couple of other things. This should be a
priority find for the opportunity to upgrade your inventory.

### Markings

        
  One or two savages are drawing patterns in the clay, and they run off when you
approach. Stand in the middle of the designs and look at the area of screen
behind your character. A shiny stone should spawn in the centre if you're close
enough; you don't need a light source to make it appear.

### Wedding

                
  This Location should be found by Day 2 and explored as far as possible - this
is because if you don't visit it at all then the related night event will never
happen and you will be unable to open the Metal Door. By visiting the Wedding on
Day 1 or 2 you allow the night event to spawn as early as it possibly can (the
second night).

  The Wedding Location resembles the Crop Field, but it's easy to reach the far
side by following the flower petals on the ground. Here you will find a dog
guarding an area set up for a wedding party, curiously abandoned. You can
explore the well for a piece of rope, and approach the first locked METAL DOOR,
only to find you can go no further.

  To get inside you will need to encounter a certain nighttime visit, indicated
by a knock on the front door during the second night at the earliest. Answer the
door as the knocks continue, then look on the ground at your feet to find the
INVITATION. This should give you the randomly generated KEYCODE required to
access the rest of the Wedding location.

  NOTE: This night event can happen at any point during the night while in the
Dry Meadow, Silent Forest or Old Woods, so if you are still waiting for it it
may just have not happened yet - remember that you have to have visited the
Wedding already and tried to open the Metal Door. It also has a chance of never
happening despite the fact that you have visited the Wedding Location. It
requires that you keep the door designated as the front door closed during the
night: the knocking can still happen if the front door is open, but since you
cannot open an already open door the Invitation will not spawn and the chance
will be lost - this is a bug. The knocking will never happen if you are too
close to the Hideout's front door at the time, or can see it. You don't have to
actually open the front door, you can run around outside and find the
Invitation.

  Before setting off to the unlocked Wedding, make sure you are properly
equipped for the most dangerous fight in the Dry Meadow biome. I recommend at
least a board with nails, a bear trap, a lantern (see Crop Field), and a bandage
or some other healing item. Otherwise, clear some inventory space for lots of
materials and a key item.
        
  Open the METAL DOOR with the keycode from the INVITATION (you can also see a
spectral bride in the courtyard if you have first collected the INVITATION).

  You will find various containers, including a shelf holding a flashlight; and
a pig presumably fattening for a feast. Come to another metal door and go
outside to find a tractor containing gasoline and a VEHICLE PART. Turn on the
generator, then jump through the open window to the banquet room.

  The banging is coming from the groom, smashing his head on the table and
chanting "We've been waiting for you". Explore the room to the north-west for
another chance at some gear, then go into the area to the north of the groom to
find some pirouetting women imploring you to dance with them. This causes the
groom to vanish, and then you return to the banquet room you find the lights
off, the metal door slammed and the window barricaded. The CORPSE BRIDE (a red
chomper) then bursts through the other locked metal door and attacks.

  NOTE: This is likely your first encounter with a red chomper. At this stage
they are tough, since you do so little damage with your basic weapons. However,
there are things you can do to prepare for this fight before it occurs. One is
to place the bear trap you brought against the metal door when you first pass
the groom, and then bait the CORPSE BRIDE into it, buying you plenty of time to
hack it to death before it can release itself. Another is to pour some gasoline
on the floor at a chokepoint (e.g. the door) and set it on fire with a torch or
a match when the CHOMPER walks over it; alternatively you can chuck your molly
at it while it's caught in the trap. If you do die during this encounter you can
always come back and finish off the CHOMPER, since any damage you do to it will
remain.

  Alternatively you can just run past the CHOMPER and back outside, slamming the
metal doors behind you, since she broke the door open for you. Killing the
CORPSE BRIDE gives you a free piece of FABRIC and a shiny stone from her...
corpse.

### Wolf's Camp

                
  Here you will find the Wolfman. Sell him any weaponry and ammo you're not
using, and make use of him as a save point.
        
  If you do find him before your first night in the Dry Meadow, make sure you
know how you want to proceed before you talk to him. If you want him to turn up
on Day 2 and sell you the Toolbox then don't talk to him in his Camp before that
happens; if you want the extra 100 reputation points with the Trader then do
talk to him in Camp before Day 2.

# 3.3.2 Silent Forest

  This is a significant step up from the Dry Meadow in terms of both size and
difficulty. The Silent Forest is certainly where you will be spending most of
your time in Chapter 1, since it acts as a sort of hub area, connecting to the
Dry Meadow, the Old Woods, and an area not shown on the map, called the Village.
Each successful night in the Silent Forest wins you 150 reputation points with
the Trader, and in terms of the relationship between difficulty and reward this
is the best deal you will get. 

  You can technically skip the Silent Forest Hideout and go directly to the Old
Woods Hideout instead, although you're pretty unlikely to get away with it,
since you just don't have the gear or supplies yet to cope with that difficulty
spike. The benefits are the XP boosts and reputation, but that second one only
applies if you actually manage to survive the night in the Old Woods, which you
almost certainly won't.

  NOTE: It is possible to relocate to the Silent Forest on Day 3, having
completed everything in the Dry Meadow, including visiting and looting all
Locations, killing all enemies (and elk), upgrading your inventory / hotbar x4,
upgrading the Workbench x3, crafted the shovel (and upgrades x2), earned your
first Skill at the Oven, and even acquired the weapon components from the corpse
under the Underground Entrance, all before the third night begins. This is the
most efficient way possible of entering the Silent Forest.

    Use alcohol and the Bike Bell to transport your loot from the Dry Meadow to 
      the Silent Forest.
    Visit Piotrek's house and collect the FABRIC, upgrade hotbar x1; upgrade 
      the Workbench to level 4 with Piotrek's TOOLBOX if you didn't in the Dry 
      Meadow.
    Complete Piotrek's quest by giving him all four VEHICLE PARTS, receive the
      WELDER, upgrade the Workbench to level 5 (Chapter 1 maximum).
    Build the single-shot shotgun, begin buying shotgun shells from the Trader
      if you haven't already.
    Collect FABRIC and the CHEST KEY from the Cottage, use the CHEST KEY to
      collect the WATCH from the Hideout.
    Collect the CHAIN and the CABLE from the Village, repair the WELL, collect 
      the KEY COVERED IN CHICKEN FECES from Janek's House, use the KEY to 
      collect the FABRIC from the Pretty Lady.
    Use the CABLE to KILL THE SOW.
    Upgrade hotbar x2.
    Give the KEY COVERED IN CHICKEN FECES to the Wolfman, tell him you killed 
      the Sow, receive the seventh hotbar upgrade (maximum).
    Relocate to the Old Woods within a week (ideally before the end of Day 7.
  
  

#### 3.3.2.1 Miscellaneous

##### 3.3.2.1.1 The First Day

  Make sure to light the Oven as soon as you arrive in order to flag this as
your current Hideout, replacing the old one. As well as several mushrooms, you
can also find the RATION STAMPS and the BIKE BELL. This second item is very
useful, as you can equip it and ring it to summon Bike Man to your new Hideout
(check outside). Talk to him and give him alcohol (you should find some in the
large wardrobe) and he will helpfully transfer all the items from the Dry Meadow
Workbench to your new Hideout, saving you the trouble. He should return not long
after leaving - you don't have to wait until the next day, just listen for the
ring of his bell and look for the package he drops by your Oven. This package
operates as an additional storage bank, so as long as you never empty it
completely you can leave it where it is (although note that anything you take
out of it cannot be put back in, and you cannot move items around within it or
add new items to it).

  With the supplies you did bring, start barricading windows and repairing
doors, along with the Well outside. Place your traps where you want them and
dump everything else into your new Workbench, including the various materials
from the several storage spots dotted around the Hideout. Make sure to check the
tractor in the yard, as you should find another VEHICLE PART in there. There is
also a locked chest in the back of the Generator room, and it needs a Key; you
can find the CHEST KEY which opens it in the Cottage (see Minor Locations), and
inside you can find a free watch, among several other items; unlocking this
chest also provides you with 8 more storage compartments in your Hideout.

##### 3.3.2.1.2 The First Night

        
  The Silent Forest Hideout comprises two buildings and a bunch of access points
for both. Barricading all of this is going to take a lot of nails and planks, so
you should think carefully about whether or not you want to bother, given that
there is rarely anything scarier in the Silent Forest nights than savages and
huge dogs. Both of these are obviously slowed down by barricades, but because
the three rooms at the back are all open plan, they can all be accessed via any
single entry point, making those barricades pretty poor. The same thing is true
for bear traps: you can't really afford to have a trap at every door and every
window every night.

  Because of the size of the house and the number of access points, it's better
just to leave them open and use them to escape. Running away from enemies is a
very good strategy here because they have to waste time chasing you, and since
savages and huge dogs are the two most likely antagonists, moving them around
can cause them to run into each other, increasing the chances that the huge dogs
will attack the savages instead. The best argument for attacking and killing at
least the savages is that you can salvage their equipment, an important
consideration since you will likely be levelling up your Workbench to build more
and better equipment, so supplementing your resources at night really helps out
- your decision depends on whether or not you need minor supplies

  There is now a much greater chance that a banshee will show up during the
night, and this represents a new challenge while you are stuck in your Hideout,
as you can't just run away. What you can do is use the many access points to
avoid the banshee entirely, hopping through windows and hiding in corners to
escape its scream. This is far easier and safer than allowing it to spawn its
offspring, but if they do turn up there are so many windows in this Hideout that
all you have to do is bait them towards one and then just hop over it, leaving
them clamouring ineffectually.

  The bathroom is an interesting option, similar to the room behind the front
door of the Dry Meadow Hideout.
        
  Note that you cannot access the holes after nightfall.
        
  After your first night in the Silent Forest Hideout the Trader will relocate
to you, and he will give you a free can of gasoline. You are about to get a lot
of valuable loot from the Silent Forest, most of which you will not need. Now is
a good time to start buying shotgun shells if you haven't already; also consider
buying weapon parts every morning whenever you have the money - this will pay
off later.

##### 3.2.2.1.3 Upgrading Your Weapon

  One of your first priorities in this area is to upgrade your weapon, since a
board with nails is no longer going to cut it, particularly against much more
common groups of enemies, and at night when you can't really run away so easily.
You can also use staffs which are now dropped by the savages in this area, but
there are even better weapons than that which can be found or created now that
you have reached the Silent Forest. The most appealing melée alternative might
be the shovel, which you will have to make from a level 4 Workbench. The various
traders sell the shovel's head for 450, so keep that in mind when you're
thinking about how to spend your money; alternatively you could make a run at
the free one in the Church Ruins in the Old Woods, at some risk. The heavy
hitting shovel can be repaired and upgraded, and its lateral swipes allow it to
hit multiple enemies with the right timing, so it goes straight to the top of
the Silent Forest tier list in all respects.

  If you only got one Toolbox from the Dry Meadow you only need one more before
they become useless (except for selling). You can buy one from the Wolfman in
the Silent Forest (you already know where his Camp is); or see Piotrek's House,
the Pig Shed and the Broken Truck / Cottage (Minor Locations) for how to get the
free Toolboxes early, allowing you to tackle the Silent Forest with a shovel and
lanterns, which is highly recommended.

  Once your Silent Forest Workbench reaches level 4 (the sooner the better), you
will then want to upgrade it to level 5 very quickly. The great benefit in these
upgrades is that they allow you to create the single-shot shotgun, an amazing
weapon for this stage in the game; the armour, greatly increasing your
survivability; and the pistol. You will need to find the WELDER, which is most
readily available from Piotrek in the Silent Forest. However, you will also need
the crafting materials to build the shotgun, and if you don't want to buy them
they can be found behind two piles of rubble at the Underground Entrance, in the
Dry Meadow. And in order to clear that rubble you will need - you guessed it - a
shovel.

  If you do want to bolt to the Old Woods Church Ruins for the shovel head (and
possibly the WELDER), rather than buying one, wait until you have bought and
used the MAP OF THE OLD WOODS from Piotrek. When you're ready, empty everything
from your inventory and equip only a couple of hotbar items (lantern, bandages).
Run to the Church Ruins (stamina pills and the Runner Skill help) and loot the
containers inside the back rooms for the special items you're looking for, equip
them in your hotbar, then kill yourself to respawn back at the Silent Forest
Hideout, having lost nothing (although each death causes roaming enemies to
respawn). You can then do the same with the Underground Entrance in order to
grab the parts for the single-shot shotgun - just make sure your shovel is fully
repaired and that you do not use it for anything else along the way.

  Finally, there is a way to get an axe very soon after reaching the Silent
Forest. It involves killing the Chicken Lady and accessing the Doctor's House
very early. If you are interested in trying this, check section 3.3.2.2.3.2.

        

#### 3.3.2.2 Major Locations

        
  There are several things you will likely want to have done before proceeding
to the Old Woods, so I'll go through them in what I think is the best order.
Note that neither the Pig Shed nor Piotrek's House are technically Major
Locations: depending on the decisions you intend to make you can actually skip
both of them, though I recommend you don't because of the benefits they offer.

  Also, you will probably start to run into storage problems in your Hideout
while exploring the Silent Forest, as there is a lot of stuff to collect. Even
if you do keep the Bike Man's package in place you might struggle to contain
everything, so make use of all the various containers sprinkled throughout the
Hideout; and remember that anything you drop on the floor will not despawn with
time.

  NOTE: There are several points in the Silent Forest where you will have to
make a decision which will have repercussions for the remainder of your
adventure. I will note these as they occur, as well as the risks and rewards for
each one.

##### 3.3.2.2.1 Piotrek's House

        
  If you spoke to the Sleeper earlier this location should already be marked on
your map, and the tracks leading from your Hideout should take you there. Bring
a torch, a weapon and some lockpicks; and you have almost certainly found
several VEHICLE PARTS by now, so take along one of each (ideally four).
Piotrek's House is well worth visiting immediately, as it's marked on your map
anyway (upon collecting the MAP from the Sleeper), and here you can get the two
TOOLBOXES you need for Workbench upgrades if you haven't already got them
elsewhere, as well as lots of other nice loot for your new Silent Forest Hideout
(including a WELDER and some FABRIC).

  There are two buildings: a house and a garage. Explore the yard first and you
can find some meat in a pot, which might come in handy; and some rope in the
destroyed well. Look at the back of the house to find a locked door: unlock it
with a lockpick to find a generator you can turn on, as well as another crate.
Inside this crate should be a bolt of FABRIC and a couple of molotovs. 

  Go into the garage and loot the tractor and the crate in the corner, as well
as the mushrooms. There are more wriggling worms blocking the path to a door,
behind which is a DOG - you need to kill that DOG before Piotrek will talk to
you, so get rid of the worms with your torch or by using the molotovs. Next open
the door, toss the meat and kill the DOG while it's distracted. In the last room
you will find more mushrooms and a crate, inside which is the BLUEPRINT and a
free TOOLBOX.
        
  Now go inside the house and inside another crate you can find the POSTCARD
WITH A PORTRAIT OF A MAN (Yuri Gagarin). Piotrek is in the Workbench room, and
now that the dog is dead he will talk to you. 

  NOTE: As with other Workbenches in the game, Piotrek's Workbench levels up
with yours. 

  Show him your various key items and you will learn that he wants to build a
rocket and escape the Darkwood, but he needs various pieces of gear to make that
happen.

  There are lots of things you can sell to Piotrek in order to afford what he's
selling, but he will also give you 100 credit for every VEHICLE PART you bring
him (to a maximum of four). These are key items which you can find in tractors
and some other vehicles, marked with 0 value:
        
    Battery
    Exhaust Pipe
    Fan Belt
    Rearview Mirror
    Steering Wheel
    Tire
        
  NOTE: Don't try to sell him the VEHICLE PARTS via the Trade option, you have
to choose Show Item to hand those over and earn the credit. If you are trying to
pass him the Vehicle Parts and they are coloured red, this is why.
        
  The best things Piotrek sells are the CABLE, the MAP OF THE OLD WOODS, the
handgun frame and the TOOLBOX (don't buy his weapon parts). You only need two
Toolboxes for Workbench upgrades, and if you didn't already buy one from Wolfman
you can find both for free in the Silent Forest (you already found one in
Piotrek's garage); there is another one at the Cottage, another at the Pig Shed,
and a fourth in the Village. As mentioned above, the CABLE is a key item, used
in a later quest, although you can also find one of these for free in the
Village (if you are willing to kill an NPC). The MAP will ping two key locations
on your map - the Old Woods Hideout and the Church Ruins - so that you don't
have to run around looking for them later, but since they are both large and
fairly central it's easy to figure out roughly where they will be, and so this
is scarcely worth 100 reputation.
        

###### 3.3.2.2.1.1 Piotrek's Quest

        
  Once you give Piotrek all four PARTS (any four will do, so long as none are
repeated) and leave, he will disappear from the area (having built his rocket),
removing him as a trader. Make sure you buy everything you want from him before
he goes, especially the CABLE if you intend to kill the Sow later. As a parting
gift he gives you some weapon parts and the WELDER, a key item needed to upgrade
your Workbench (note that you only need one Welder in the entire game, so you
can keep Piotrek around but still get a free WELDER from the Village, the Old
Woods Hideout or the Church Ruins if you are willing to wait a bit longer).
There are more Welders to be found in Chapter 2, in the Swamp.

  There are three ways to give Piotrek his PARTS.
        
  The first and best option is to simply give him all four, whereupon he leaves
as described above. The effect this has is that he will later be found dead in
the Junkyard (a location in the Swamp). The implication is that his rocket
crash-landed and killed everything in the area, including himself. This is
beneficial for the Protagonist, as it means the Junkyard is far less dangerous
to explore; and it also means you will find an extremely valuable pair of PLIERS
on his body.

  The second option is to give any VEHICLE PART to Piotrek and then take any
other one to the Wolfman. He will turn it into a bomb, so that if you then
return to Piotrek and give him the three remaining PARTS he needs (including the
one the Wolfman tinkered with) he will still disappear from the Silent Forest.
The Junkyard will also be cleared of hostiles later on, but neither Piotrek nor
his rocket are anywhere to be found. This is of no benefit to you, as you miss
out on the loot, and especially the Pliers.

  The third option is to give him three of the four VEHICLE PARTS (for maximum
profit), then leave him alone so that you can continue to trade with him. This
is of little benefit once you've bought what you want from him, since he does
not sell ammo or other consumables, and leaving Piotrek where he is will mean
the Junkyard will later be found full of enemies and empty of Pliers.

##### 3.3.2.2.2 Wolf's Camp

                
  You can once again meet and trade with the Wolfman, but this time he has a
quest in mind for you (gossip with him). He wants you to go to the Village, and
he'll give you directions to find it. He warns you of a "hag who reeks of
chickens", points out the locked door in her house, and tells you he wants the
KEY to that door. Bring it to him and he will give you the KEY you really want.

  He also wants you to silence the squealing of the pigs in the area, which will
involve going to the Pig Shed, another of the Silent Forest's Major Locations.
        
  NOTE: It's easy to confuse these two quests, but while they are related they
can be completed independently of each other. The first offers you one way out
of Chapter 1; whereas the other merely wins you a free hotbar upgrade.

  NOTE: There are two locked crates at the Wolfman's camp, neither of which you
have the codes for. To unlock them you need to literally guess the combinations,
and these are randomly generated. There is nothing in them that cannot be found
or made elsewhere.

  With all of this done it's time to visit the Village.
        

##### 3.3.2.2.3 The Village

                
  This is a large section of the game, a biome in its own right. Time is frozen
while you are in here in order to make it easier to get back home when your
inventory is full; and there is no map. There are no hostiles in the area (at
least above ground), but that doesn't mean you can afford to turn up unequipped.

  NOTE: Even though the Time Freeze status is in effect, you still can't cheese
the game by visiting the Village at night via the road. The Village will also be
dark at night, and the floor gore will still chase and kill you in here. You can
also no longer use the holes at the Hideout to travel here at night.

  The first time you visit you will be scouting around and figuring out what's
going on and where you need to go. You should also collect a fair bit of loot
and you should return home with it before undertaking the second stage of the
Village, which involves exploring underground. Make sure you have lots of
inventory space on the first visit, you don't really need to take anything else
(although a lockpick or two couldn't hurt).

  If you want the Sow part of the story to make more sense, consider visiting
the Village and obtaining the KEY COVERED IN CHICKEN FECES before making a
decision about the Sow, allowing you to return after you kill her if that's what
you choose to do, and thereby experience the full ramifications of your
decision.

###### 3.3.2.2.3.1 The First Visit

                        
  There's not much of interest to be found on the near bank of the river: a
Villager in a crop field who warns you to stay away, a shrine with a shiny stone
in it (there are no ramifications for taking it;. although if you try to collect
it while your inventory is full it will be lost forever), and a locked house off
in one corner. You may also find a lone mushroom somewhere, but you have to
cross any of the bridges to reach the main village.

  If you cross the nearest bridge and follow the tracks leading off to the
character's right you should come to a couple of buildings close together. The
five room house (the mayor's house) should have a man banging on the wall and
shouting at someone inside; and it has a burning generator against the outside
corner wall, at the back. There are two locked doors inside, and when you try to
get through a hidden voice (the mayor) shouts about how the Sow is starving, and
to go away or he will let Antek loose! Remember this building if you kill the
Sow, as you will want to return here later. Search the well outside for some
rope.

  The barn behind the Mayor's House contains a locked crate which requires a
Keycode. This CODE is found by combining the two pairs of numbers at the edges
of the pages of the MADMAN'S SCRIBBLINGS #1 (found on a NOTE beside the nearby
bed) and MADMAN'S SCRIBBLINGS #2 (found on a corpse in the Pig Shed); and in the
crate is some reasonably valuable loot and the PICTURE OF THE SOW. The bed can
be searched to the find the PIG SHED MAP, which will mark that location on your
map if you haven't already found it.

  Past the Mayor's House and sticking near the outer edge of the area, you can
find another seated Villager muttering about the Doctor; and in the nearby
bonfire you can find the BURNED DOCTOR'S BAG. Inside the building behind the
muttering Villager (hop through the window) you can find the THERMOMETER and the
DOCTOR'S INSTRUCTIONS #2, as well as three patients in some pain, one of whom is
talking in his sleep about trees. Don't miss the weapon parts on the shelf!

  On your way to the building next door you should pass two more seated
Villagers, and they are talking about someone who has barricaded himself into
his own house. This house is the one in the back corner, with all three of its
windows barricaded; and when you try to get in via the back door you find the
way blocked by some furniture. Once you make it to the back room you will notice
everything going dark outside, and when you approach the cross on the floor a
shiny stone should appear if you have a light source equipped. There is an
infected Villager in here and when you get too close the floor of the room
becomes riddled with lines and symbols. You can attack her and kill her with one
weapon strike and loot the various containers in here - there are no
ramifications.

  The next large building along has nothing inside but the SIPHON, although
there is an unlocked crate in the corner behind the building.

  Amidst all these buildings you should also see a WELL, and it requires that
you rebuild it with a Chain before you can descend. You can find one later in
the Village if you haven't previously bought one.
  Follow the tracks along and you should come to several holes in the ground,
with someone yelling at you to stay away. By now your inventory is probably full
or close to it, so you can save yourself a lot of time by examining the small
hole to learn that a man is down underground, digging tunnels. Examine the large
hole and descend into it: this opens a shortcut back to your Hideout! Drop off
all the things you have collected, equip yourself with weapons, two lockpicks
and a light source (and some armour if possible), and then use the hole to
return to the Village.

###### 3.3.2.2.3.2 The Second Visit

                        
  NOTE: Despite the fact that you will likely return home at least two more
times, I'm just treating this whole section as one visit, with the shortcut
opened up.
                        
  Upon your return you next want to visit the crematorium, the largest compound
building in the Village. You'll know it by its obvious size, its proximity to
the shortcut hole, and the fact that it is connected to a big yard containing a
couple of bread ovens. You can jump through the window of the house to bypass
its locked front door, and inside you can find the MEAT GRINDER in a wardrobe,
as well as some pipes and tape (useful upgrade materials); the Villager here
tells you to take whatever you like, just don't tell anyone what you found. You
can also pick the lock of the long barn to reach the yard, then pick the lock on
the back door of the house to find lots of unburied corpses, each lootable for
minor items, including the very useful rope.

  Nearby you should find a small, ruined house tucked into a corner of the
Village, with a single Villager outside - he is insisting to someone or
something on the other side of the woods that they will not succeed in squeezing
between the trees. Behind this house should be another mushroom, and against
another of its walls is a wardrobe.

  NOTE: Later on you can return to this man and kill him to recover the axe
blade he is carrying, your earliest opportunity to find this item without buying
it. Note that you still will not be able to actually build the axe until you
reach Chapter 2.

  Continue exploring and you should find the Chicken Lady's House, marked by
assorted chickens having something of a cluck; this House is also easily reached
via the first bridge by following the tracks from the main t-junction, past a
completely ruined shell of a building where a dead-eyed woman sits, guarding a
crate containing a damaged DOLL. This woman is Hanuska, the one who cut the
Cables at the Pig Shed (she exhorts you to "kill the pig"), and if you kill her
you will find an incriminating CABLE and knife on her body. This is how to get
the CABLE you need for the Pig Shed if you previously let Piotrek leave before
buying it.

  Go inside the Chicken Lady's House and loot the shelf for the MAP which marks
the Pig Shed. Talk to her, show her the PLASTIC CHICK and she will have a
disturbed reaction which ends by her giving you the PHOTO OF THE DOCTOR. Gossip
with her a couple more times and, depending on whether or not you killed the
Sow, you will either learn that the Villagers know of her death and are worried
about starving or that there's still a problem because the Pig Farmer is dead
anyway and there is no-one to look after the Sow. Either way the conversation
should culminate with the appearance and sound of the MUSICIAN outside.

  NOTE: If for whatever reason you choose to kill the Chicken Lady before
talking to her for the first time, the Musician will not spawn into the game.
        
  NOTE: If you kill the Chicken Lady or give her the BLOODSTAINED SHAWL (later
found in the Barn Ruins in the Old Woods if you give the KEY COVERED IN CHICKEN
FECES to the Wolfman), she will give you the DOCTOR'S INSTRUCTIONS #3, which
opens the Road To The Doctor's House (although you will still have to find it
according to the directions on the DOCTOR'S INSTRUCTIONS #3) and therefore
allows you to access it without giving away the Key (although you can still
choose to do that, too). This is a way of skipping the Silent Forest and Old
Woods almost entirely and reaching the Swamp several days earlier than you
otherwise would. I will proceed as though you chose not to do this, but if you
do you should skip to 3.3.3.2.3.

  Search the nearby shelves for the same PIG SHED MAP you found earlier; and in
some wardrobes in the room beyond you can find another free TOOLBOX and another
CROSS. There is more loot to be found in the smaller buildings behind her House,
including a VEHICLE PART, some gasoline, some chicken eggs, a red egg and a
mushroom (all cookable for essence); there is also a crate tucked into the
corner in which you can find a shotgun shell. Note the locked door in the back
of the main House - this can later be opened with the KEY COVERED IN CHICKEN
FECES for a free Fabric.

  Follow the sound of the violin (to the east of the House) to find the
MUSICIAN; whereupon he runs away and hides under the TRACTOR (directly opposite
the window he was playing at). Interact with the Tractor to talk to him and
learn that he wants to marry the Pretty Lady locked in the Chicken Lady's House.
Keep nodding to him and he will tell you that the KEY is in Mr. Jan's possession
and that he was last seen near the Well. Exhaust his dialogue and he will also
tell you that he saw the Villagers carting away the corpse of the Sow (if you
killed it). Ultimately he wants the KEY you are going to collect from Mr. Jan -
the same one that the Wolfman wants. This will present a dilemma later on, but
for now you need to actually get into the WELL at the other end of the Village.
Meanwhile, the Musician will move to the Silo in the Silent Forest, where you
can find him until you give away the Key.
        
  Near the Chicken Lady's House is another, smaller house in the corner. In here
you can find a sleeping Villager on a bed, moaning in her sleep about how the
doctor told her it "needed to be done". Nearby is the DOCTOR'S INSTRUCTIONS #1.
In the back room you can find a bound and charred corpse, lootable for a single
free CHAIN, the key item you need to repair and access the WELL. 

  With everything else in the Village tied off for now, go back to the WELL at
the other end of town, use the CHAIN on it to repair it, then go down inside.

###### 3.3.2.2.3.3 Under The Village

                
  Time is still frozen down here, and there is still no map. You should find a
torch and two flares upon entering, but I suggest you bring out your lantern if
you brought one.

  Note the childish drawings on the floor. This is a maze but not much of one,
since there is only one exit and a few mushrooms (careful you don't blunder into
one and poison yourself). The area is also guarded by a small chomper who will
jump you at some point, but a few swings or a molotov should finish it off
easily, and you can loot its corpse for another DOLL (this one in much better
condition than the one you found in the Village). Apparently you just killed
Hanuska's child.

  Anyway, by following the swell of sound you should know when you are near the
EXIT, marked by two mushrooms at a chokepoint, so go up to find yourself in
Janek's House.

###### 3.3.2.2.3.4 Janek's House

  Harvest a couple of mushrooms and open the only door. In the wardrobes are the
PLASTIC CHICKENS and the DOGTAG; and in the next room you can interact with the
BLOCKADE to remove it. The nearby shelf holds the KEY COVERED IN CHICKEN FECES,
the object of your search - don't leave without it!

  Return to the Chicken Lady's House and use your new KEY to open the METAL DOOR
in the back. You can find a military flashlight in the wardrobe and a wedding
ring among the sheets. You can also search the twitching bed for some FABRIC,
although the Pretty Lady underneath begs you to cover her by putting it back -
this is up to you as just a moral choice.

  NOTE: Visit this room before giving away the Key, otherwise you will not be
able to get in here and will miss out on the loot. Assuming you have used all
the other Fabric you have found up to this point on inventory and hotbar
upgrades (and assuming you have found all five of the previous ones), this is
the last bolt of FABRIC you can find for free before reaching the Old Woods
(meaning without having to buy more from traders). You should also have five
hotbar slots at this point, and collecting this FABRIC allows you to reach your
sixth and final hotbar upgrade. There is a way to get a seventh, however (see
below).

  You can now leave the Village in peace, as you likely have not yet decided
what to do about the Sow (if anything). Leave via the holes you dug, grab the
CABLE (either bought from Piotrek or collected from the Village), as well as a
weapon and a light source, and set out for the Pig Shed.

##### 3.3.2.2.4 Pig Shed

                
  You can find the Pig Shed by following the tracks in a different direction
than the one leading to Piotrek's House.

  There are two distinct parts to this location. Start by visiting the single
building guarded only by a picket fence. Other than the cooking pot and the
three wood piles against the walls, you are looking for the CUT CABLES on the
ground outside. Interact with the missing parts and the game will show you an
icon of what you need to fix them: the CABLE. Use the CABLE (either from Piotrek
or Hanuska in the Village) on the CUT CABLES to REPAIR them and allow power to
reach the Sow.

  Go inside the building and watch out for mushrooms. In the first room is a
LEVER you can interact with, but only once the power is turned on. To do this go
into the next room and fight off a Villager, and loot the nearby shelf to find
the FLYER. Into the last room and you can find another mushroom and the
necessary GENERATOR, which you need not turn on if you have not yet fixed the
CABLES, because doing so will just waste power and you may have to bring more
fuel back here to refill it. You can also use the nearby mirror to see your own
reflection and save the game.

  Now that the Generator is on you can EXAMINE THE LEVER. Long story short, you
need to REMOVE THE LOCK, set the LEVER to the BOTTOM, and then PULL it to
deliver a fatal jolt to the Sow. (As far as I know, pulling the Lever when it's
in the middle does nothing useful.)

  The larger part of the Pig Shed location is accessed through a gap in the
spiky fence which will lead you into the smaller of two buildings. In here you
can find a wood pile and a crate, the latter of which contains a TOOLBOX. There
are also two mushrooms to be found in this building.

  There is a tractor in the yard which should yield one of Piotrek's VEHICLE
PARTS. You can also find two Villagers out here, one of whom is armed with a
pitchfork, likely your first encounter with this weapon. Kill it and take it
from him, as it's a great weapon with long reach and the ability to make human
enemies run off after being hit once. Check the corner behind where the two
Villagers were sitting for another mushroom.

  As well as a second pitchfork wielding Villager, more mushrooms and a few more
loot caches (one of which contains a flashlight) in the larger building, if you
make your way to the back corner you can find the giant mother SOW twitching and
wriggling on the floor, unless you killed her, in which case you can pass her
safely to find a corpse with the MADMAN'S SCRIBBLINGS #1 and a single-shot
pistol, and another mushroom in the other corner.

###### 3.3.2.2.4.1 The Sow

                
  Killing the Sow is optional, although it may be considered an act of mercy,
since she is clearly the captive of the Villagers, who are relying on her for
food. However, her death does have several ramifications for the player.

  The Villagers will react to the death of the Sow. First of all they will
bemoan the death of their beloved food source. Next they will transport her dead
body back to the Village and begin praying to it. Soon afterwards they will
learn that the Protagonist is responsible and will visit the Silent Forest
Hideout secretly, leaving a circle of bloody pig heads on the floor by the Oven.
When the Protagonist returns to the centre of the Village he will be told to
visit the mayor's House, with a nasty surprise ambush waiting in the Village
Cellar under the Mayor's House. Finally, the villagers will all attempt to
abandon the Village and head for the Swamp, where they will regroup in the
Quarry.

  Return to the Wolfman and gossip with him to tell him the Sow is dead and he
will reward you with a free hotbar upgrade (equip it as a hotbar item and use it
to gain an extra hotbar slot). This is probably the best argument for killing
the Sow, as you will otherwise be stuck with a maximum of six hotbar slots for
the rest of the game; make sure you don't use this item until you upgrade the
hotbar to six slots first, otherwise it will be effectively wasted.

  However, you may choose to leave her alive, in which case none of the above
happens. Choosing this option means that you can't access the Village Cellar
under the mayor's house and therefore cannot collect the WELDER and CHAIN down
there; and you also cannot open the locked crate in the barn behind the mayor's
house, because you do not have the Code from the MADMAN'S SCRIBBLINGS #1 found
under the Sow's corpse (although you can guess one half of the Code). On the
other hand the Villagers have no reason to leave the Village while the Sow
remains alive, and so they will not appear in the Swamp Quarry, making certain
things there a bit safer. If you are determined to leave Piotrek where he is and
also leave the Sow alive, the only other way to get a WELDER for the Workbench
is to find it in the Old Woods (Hideout or Church Ruins).
        
  If the Sow is dead go back to the Village (ideally after passing another
night, giving the Villagers time to move the Sow to the Village) and to the
mayor's house (a seated Villager should let you know when you're there and
invite you inside), and open the previously locked door inside the house. Here
you can take the Cellar stairs down and find yourself in a fight, first with
Antek (a red chomper on the end of a chain which will quickly break) and then
two maddened Villagers. Search the crate in the back room once they're dead to
find some nice loot, including another CHAIN, a WELDER, and the CELLAR KEY you
will need in order to leave.

  Also, since you should now have both the MADMAN'S SCRIBBLINGS you should know
the Code for the crate in the Village barn.

  At which point you are likely done with the Village forever, and it's time to
return to your Hideout and drop off all the latest loot.
        

##### 3.3.2.2.5 The Key Covered In Chicken Feces

                
  This is one of the most important quest items in the game, although it can
technically be ignored (see section 3.3.2.2.3.2). Otherwise you must give it to
either the Wolfman or the Musician in order to meet the Doctor, who is
ultimately your way out of Chapter 1.

###### 3.3.2.2.5.1 The Wolfman's Path

                
  If you choose to give the KEY to the Wolfman he tells you that the Doctor's
House can be found in the Old Woods (but gleefully doesn't tell you how to get
there: you will need to find a way to make an appointment). He also gives you a
Chicken Head to show to the Chicken Lady if you are so minded, then he vanishes
to the Old Woods, leaving his camp deserted. He will later kill the Pretty Lady,
the girl locked in the back room of the Chicken Lady's house, and her corpse can
be found in his Barn Ruins hideout in the Old Woods. You can loot the corpse for
the BLOODSTAINED SHAWL and take that back to the Chicken Lady, whereupon she
will give you the DOCTOR'S INSTRUCTIONS #3 (which you will no longer need, since
the Wolfman will have offered to take you to the Doctor's House anyway. Once he
does this he has no further relevance, and he will remain in the Barn Ruins.

  Meanwhile, the Musician's path will remain closed, as will the Creepy House in
the Old Woods (his parents' home), and you will lose out on the loot inside. He
moves to the Silo, as usual, and remains there until Chapter 2, where he later
appears in the Swamp Hideout as a sort of pet (if you killed the Sow). 

  Following the Wolfman's Path can win you a free hotbar upgrade (increasing the
normal maximum of 6 to 7), a hunting rifle, an assault rifle (if you kill him),
an axe (near the Doctor's House), a shiny stone (via the Strange Box from the
Church Dream), and further opportunities to trade with him before leaving
Chapter 1. It also precludes having to meet him in a hard boss fight in Chapter
2.

###### 3.3.2.2.5.2 The Musician's Path

  The Musician moves to the Silo after you first encounter him in the Village,
so if you want to give him the Key you have to find him camped in the very back
of the Silo. Giving him the KEY, he responds by giving you a DRAWING to give to
his mother in the Creepy House, in the Old Woods; and having done this he then
moves to the Church Ruins (you can also gossip with him again before he leaves
to receive the CHESTNUT DOLL). He wants you to retrieve his VIOLIN from the
Creepy House (see section 3.3.3.2.1), but if you try to take it without having
first given his MOTHER the DRAWING (by placing it in her inventory after first
examining her), both she and her husband will transform into red chompers and
attack you. 

  If you kill them and then give the Musician the VIOLIN he will magically
unlock the TRAIN WRECK and then move to the Creepy House and stand outside,
knowing his parents are dead and refusing to have any more dealings with the
Protagonist. If you give his MOTHER the DRAWING and then take the VIOLIN, or if
you don't leave the DRAWING and instead just steal the VIOLIN and run away,
leaving the transformed chompers inside the Creepy House, the Musician will soon
move back to his parents' home, where he will be killed by them; although you
can loot his body for the VIOLIN to sell it to the Trader.

  Whatever happens, returning the VIOLIN to him and getting the appointment is
the point, and the Protagonist's relationship with the Musician is doomed no
matter what.
        
  Following the Musician's Path merely wins you a military flashlight and a
watch; plus you do not get to visit the Doctor's House, thereby missing out on
the axe (although you can still cheese this by going back and killing the
Chicken Lady for the DOCTOR'S INSTRUCTIONS #3). His Path also results in the
Wolfman becoming an antagonist and tracking the player to the Swamp, where he
will later steal several items from the Workbench after a few days, leaving the
NOTE FROM THE WOLF in their place, inviting you to a showdown at the Sawmill.
This Path is by far the weaker of the choices, and the only reasons to do it
involve the story and achievements (with neither of which this guide is
concerned).
        
                
  Either way, it's time to leave the Silent Forest behind and head for the Old
Woods, once you've grabbed any last elk antlers or bug shells you're still
interested in. If you fully exploit the Silent Forest for all its gear, quests
and upgrades and still make it to the Old Woods Hideout before the end of Day 7
you are doing exceptionally well.

        

#### 3.3.2.3 Minor Locations

                

### Bridge

                
  You will definitely be coming back here later, but it's not really a key
location in itself. Avoid crossing just yet, and instead look for the broken
truck off to one side. Walk onto the back of it and search the container to find
the NOTE FROM THE TRUCK, a VEHICLE PART and some ammo.
        

### Broken Truck

                
  You can loot the truck's engine for gear. When you enter the back of the truck
the corpse on the floor comes to life and attacks you (a red chomper). Kill it
and loot its body for some odd meat and a VEHICLE PART; then explore inside the
truck for a crate containing a TOOLBOX, some weapon parts, some gasoline and
another VEHICLE PART.

### Camp (savages)

                
  As in the Dry Meadow, a deserted campsite full of loot containers, guarded
this time by some savages. Take a molotov to make this easier. You may be able
to find some valuable weapon components on one of the corpses.

### Cottage

                
  This location is always found halfway down one of the two outer edges of the
Silent Forest. At first it might seem as if there's not much here, since there
is nothing to find at the front except a couple of storage caches. However,
follow the forest's edge around to the side and get close enough to a couple of
twisting branches and they should disappear to reveal a hidden path. Follow this
to reach the back door.
                
  Loot the corpse inside to find the CHEST KEY, which can be used to open the
chest at the back of the Silent Forest Hideout's Generator building; there is
also a shelf against the back wall which can be searched - either or both of
these caches may contain a small caliber magazine. The locked door in this room
should open on its own when you interact with the corpse, and behind it lurks a
Villager who calls you a "f*cking thief!" before coming at you with a sickle -
take her down and loot her body. In her room is a crate in which you can find
another TOOLBOX; and a picture on the wall which conceals some FABRIC.
        

### Giant Hole

                
  This is just a crescent-shaped hole in the ground; you can't fall in it. There
is nothing here of interest, except a glimpse of a certain giant Tree you may
meet at the end of the game.
        

### Markings

                
  This has the same icon as the Dry Meadow Rocks, but this time it's a bit more
significant. There is a hidden corpse in the centre which you can loot for some
reasonable gear; and you should also find a shiny stone nearby. However,
collecting this spawns in some savages, so be ready to run or fight.

### Road To The Village

                
  This is the only way to reach the Village to begin with. Just follow it to
walk off the edge of the Silent Forest map.

### Savage Lair

                
  This place has the same symbol as the Ritual Clearing / Signs from the Dry
Meadow. You should find a few savages in here, one burying himself and another
burying someone else, but they should not attack you as you wander in. There are
some clothes lying around, and the ones at the base of the fallen tree in the
centre should yield the CROSS. You may also find a free shiny stone somewhere
around the centre.
        

### Silo

                
  This can be a key location of sorts if you follow the Musician's Path, but you
can visit there anyway for three mushrooms and some minor treasures in a crate
at the back. Here is where you will find the Musician once he leaves the
Village.

### Tank Wreck (a)

  A WW2 tank guarded by two or three savages. Around it you can find corpses,
one of which has a piece of FABRIC, a VEHICLE PART, a knife and some gasoline.
        

### Tank Wreck (b)

                
  This WW2 tank is roughly as valuable as the other one, as the tank and the
corpse hold a single-shot pistol, various pieces of ammunition (including a
medium caliber magazine) and some gasoline. You'll know when you've found Tank
Wreck (B) because there's no Fabric here.

# 3.3.3 Old Woods

  Despite the size of this zone, this is where you should be aiming to spend the
least amount of time - five nights at the absolute most. If your character has
the maximum upgrades and best weapons to this point then the Old Woods is
virtually useless to you. There are only three things in it which are otherwise
inaccessible in Chapter 1, and all three of them can be found by following the
Wolfman's path. Outside of those items (the axe, the hunting rifle and the
assault rifle), spending any more time in the Old Woods than is necessary for
story reasons is detrimental to your playthrough.

## Tldr

  
    Use alcohol and the Bike Bell to transport your loot from the Silent Forest 
      to the Old Woods.
    Depending on which Path you took, either find the Musician in the Church
      Ruins or the Wolfman in the Barn Ruins, and talk to them to proceed.
    Obtain the hunting rifle / assault rifle from the Wolfman if you want to.
    Retrieve the VIOLIN from the Creepy House and return it to him for access
      to the Train Wreck (Musician's Path only).
    Get the KEYCODE from the Doctor's House (Wolfman's Path only).
    Access the Train Wreck and confront the Doctor.
    Retrieve the BIG, METAL KEY if you killed the Doctor.
    Return to the Hideout and sell all unwanted equipment to the Trader, using
      the reputation to buy the last of his weapon parts.
    Fill your inventory and hotbar with all your most valuable items.
    Return to the Dry Meadow (ideally via the Burned House).
    Visit the Underground Entrance and pass through both ARMORED DOORS to reach
      Chapter 2.
  
  

#### 3.3.3.1 Miscellaneous

##### 3.3.3.1.1 The First Day

  Your next Hideout is an interesting building, but it's not in a great state of
repair. Make sure you haul the following items with you when you're ready to
leave:

    Bike Bell
    Alcohol (all!)
    Gasoline (at least 2)
    Planks (plenty)
    Nails (plenty)
    Weapons (at least 2, including one firearm)
    Armour (equipped)
    Curatives
    Lantern

  The alcohol and Bike Bell are especially important for saving you a dangerous
few trips, since you can light the Old Woods Hideout Oven and then ring the Bike
Bell to summon Bike Man (remember to light the Oven first!). Bringing all the
alcohol is important because if you leave some in the Silent Forest Hideout and
then employ Bike Man to bring all your stuff over he will apparently drink all
the alcohol he finds there.

  Anyway, you can get to the Old Woods Hideout by crossing the Bridge. Do some
exploring once you get there and you should find three mushrooms, a WELDER, the
"GENTLEMAN" MAGAZINE and the DESERTION NOTE #1; make sure you use the WELDER to
upgrade the Workbench to level 5 if you did not already get a WELDER from
Piotrek or the Village. You should also find two Generators, one of which is
completely destroyed but which can still be used as a container. The other
Generator is tucked into the corner, and should be refuelled before nightfall.
Refuel the Saw, too, and start making use of your boards and nails to repair and
barricade, along with fixing your new Well.

##### 3.3.3.1.2 The First Night

  If you turn on the Generator you will notice that you now have only one lamp,
and that some of the rooms and hallways are lit by wall lamps which cannot be
moved, or turned off while the Generator is on. You might be worried that all
those lights will devour your fuel, but they don't: in fact there is no
difference in fuel consumption compared to the other Hideouts. The downside is
that you cannot turn off the lights without turning off the Generator entirely;
and you are also more vulnerable to Shadows, since that event turns off all but
one of your lights, including both wall lamps and your single room lamp, forcing
you to heal your way through the darkness if you are also under attack. I
suggest you drag your one lamp into the room with the destroyed generator and
allow its light to pour into both adjacent corridors and some way into the
Workbench room, and stand beside the lamp each night, giving you plenty of early
warnings and multiple escape routes. This room also contains a section of broken
wall which you can use to run rings around any hostiles. 

  There are a lot of barricades you can build, but while I suggest you barricade
all windows (huge dogs, villagers and chompers can all jump through windows),
you should leave all doors repaired but open. The idea is to allow enemies to
walk into your Hideout at night and waste time trying to look for you, then once
they find you you just sprint in another direction and slam the outer door
behind you, forcing them to smash it down from the inside, wasting much more
time and allowing you to reposition to the opposite corner of the Hideout. The
best thing to do when you're under assault during the night in the Old Woods is
to run outside and keep touring the outer walls of the building while you run
out the clock. Wait for whatever it is to catch up and then just sprint away
again with full stamina. If something does turn up which requires you to be near
a light you can just keep bouncing from wall light to wall light, either inside
or outside; but if the game is throwing dogs and chompers at you just time them
out and save your weapons' durability, traps, etc.

  Try equipping your lantern in your hotbar and keeping your hands empty as you
run around - this will remove the animation of having to put a held weapon away
before the character can climb through a window, saving a lot of time and
health.

  There are several metal crates in the Barn Ruins location, each of which can
be dragged back to your Hideout to let you hide behind them, unreachable by
enemies. This takes a lot of time and is not worth doing, given how easy it is
to survive by just moving around, but if you feel like doing it it's an option.

  It's worth pointing out that if you are following my strategy for levelling up
and trading, surviving nights in the Old Woods is virtually a waste of time, you
might as well just allow yourself to be killed by the first enemy which comes
along, skipping ahead to the morning. The reason is that getting credit with the
Trader at this point is not as valuable as you might think, since you will lose
it all when you move to the Swamp; plus you ideally already have all the
weapons, upgrades and materials you will want to take to that biome anyway,
which reduces your need to buy them from the Trader. Instead of making money by
surviving nights, you should aim to make money by selling any valuable Old Woods
crap to the Trader, buying back even more valuable items (such as weapon parts),
and then selling those to the Swamp traders later. Long story short, if you can
get away with spending just one or two nights in the Old Woods before moving to
the Swamp then you should do so. Obviously the dying thing does not apply in
Hard or Nightmare mode, but the getting out of the Old Woods thing does.
        

##### 3.3.3.1.3 Exploration Tips

                
  There is very little of importance in the Old Woods, despite its size. There
are only five key locations, including the Hideout, and all the rest is just
lore and loot (nearly all of which you will likely already have or can afford to
buy), so if you know where everything is beforehand you can just skip all the
crap you don't need to visit. Unless you are farming bug shells and grinding
mushrooms, concentrate on the Barn Ruins / Creepy House, then the Doctor's House
(Wolfman path only) and Train Wreck - nothing else matters at all (except the
Burned House).
        
  Empty any non-essentials from your inventory before setting out from the Old
Woods Hideout. You are much more likely to die in here than ever before, but by
only having hotbar items equipped you lose nothing when you die. Obviously you
will be collecting stuff as you move around, so err on the side of caution when
your inventory starts filling up, and sprint back home to drop it off before you
lose it.

  Take at least two weapons. The shovel is great against certain enemies but
it's terrible against chompers because of their speed. Consider taking a torch
for its fire effect, as large dogs are a common occurrence. Firearms are
excellent by this point in the game, and you should have created at least one
and have been buying ammo for it. The single-shot shotgun is fantastic, since it
can one-shot chompers and therefore everything else; it can also hit multiple
enemies within its field of fire, one behind the other.

  Attempt to run away from chompers which are not tied to any specific Major or
Minor Location. Fighting them is not worth the effort or the loot.
        
  Never interact with any corpse which is twitching. It will almost certainly
explode into a chomper and that chomper will then become a permanently
patrolling creature which may respawn the next day if killed. Interacting with
corpses in the Old Woods is effectively the same as adding a lot of extra
chompers, but if you ignore all the corpses you will only see the original
chompers, of which there are far fewer. This makes an enormous difference to the
difficulty of this biome. Don't run over the corpses either!
        
  Use a light whenever you think you need it, ideally a lantern. The darkness
here is heavier and the ground is a lot more dangerous than in any previous
biome, and since you will be running from chompers a lot you will likely be
running headlong into trouble which you need to be able to see coming. Consider
having a torch equipped as your default item, since you are not looking to get
into fights in the Old Woods - it's much more important to be able to see and to
run.

  Mushrooms and other XP boosts are now much more valuable, with the Oven at 16
Efficiency, so harvest any you find (300% more valuable than in the Silent
Forest).

  While there are bugs in the Old Woods, there are no elk and therefore no
antlers. If you're really low on money consider a trip back into the Silent
Forest for some farming.

  Don't forget to use your Skills to escape danger. Runner, Navigator and Scream
are particularly helpful.
        
        
  With all of that said, the Wolfman / Musician sent you to the Doctor, and so
this is your main priority. I will therefore focus on the Locations you must
visit in pursuit of this objective.

        

#### 3.3.3.2 Major Locations

##### 3.3.3.2.1 Creepy House (musician's Path Only)

        
  Note that the Creepy House will be locked forever if you chose to give the KEY
to the Wolfman. If you did give the Musician the KEY COVERED IN CHICKEN FECES
the Creepy House will be unlocked when you get there. Inside, past the
generator, you can find and examine a half-dead man (the Musician's father).
Search farther inside the house for the CHESTNUT MAN and a WATCH, then move the
wardrobe aside to find the VIOLIN. Before you collect it make sure to EXAMINE
and SEARCH the HALF-DEAD WOMAN in the bath (the Musician's MOTHER). PLACE THE
DRAWING IN HER INVENTORY and she will allow you to grab the VIOLIN (and a shiny
stone) from the PICTURE ON THE WALL behind the wardrobe.

  NOTE: If you take the VIOLIN without placing the DRAWING like you're supposed
to, both parents will turn into chompers and attack you; this will also happen
if you attack them first.
        
  With the VIOLIN recovered, return it to the Musician, who should now be
waiting in the grounds of the Church Ruins in the Old Woods. In return he will
tell you he has arranged an appointment with the Doctor at the Train Wreck
(which you will still have to find) and your business with him is concluded;
although he does invite you to visit him back at the Creepy House. You can now
go directly to the Train Wreck and begin making preparations to leave Chapter 1
(skip to section 3.3.3.2.4.1).

##### 3.3.3.2.2 Barn Ruins (wolfman's Path Only)

        
  Off at the back edge of the Old Woods biome is a large Barn surrounded by
fences and barking dogs. This is the Wolfman's Hideout, and is where to go if
you gave him the KEY COVERED IN CHICKEN FECES. He is hiding all the way at the
back, behind various rooms which make up something of a maze. Note that this
location can still be explored if you chose to follow the Musician's Path, but
the Wolfman will not be there and you will therefore not be able to enlist his
help in reaching the Doctor's House; you will also not be able to find an
assault rifle before Chapter 2.

  Before coming here make sure you bring some meat, a couple of heavy hitting
weapons, some healing, a light source and a couple of lockpicks. Armour wouldn't
hurt either. Otherwise try to leave aside a lot of free inventory slots, as
there are plenty of mushrooms to be found at the Barn Ruins. Set off first thing
in the morning to give yourself plenty of time.

  When you first arrive you will find a gap in the fence at the corner, and an
animal corpse with some meat on it. Go inside and you can either unlock a door
or push various crates out of the way to go the long way round and jump a picket
fence, although this involves getting past some dogs, which is why you brought
the meat. Go into the building and you can again unlock another door, move
another crate and go the longer, safer way; or take a more dangerous route past
more dogs, bypassing the locked door. Keep an eye out for several mushrooms in
this area.

  Having pushed a crate aside to reveal a wooden door in the north, you should
come to a generator at the back of the building; you will know you are in the
right place if you find some stuffed corpses in the hallways beyond. Find the
door next to the generator and open it to meet the Wolfman once again. In a
nearby wardrobe you can find several items, including the KEY TO WOLF'S HIDEOUT
which opens the nearby locked metal door leading back to the Old Woods by a
safer route.

  Talk to the Wolfman and choose the "Do Nothing" option to keep him friendly.
Having gotten his Pretty Lady back, thanks to the KEY COVERED IN CHICKEN FECES
you gave him, he will agree to take you to the Doctor's House, and this will
become a dialogue option in the main conversation screen. He will also give you
a nice hunting rifle if you chose to "Do Nothing", your only chance to get this
item in Chapter 1. If you are not yet ready to go there then you can always come
back later.

  NOTE: In the back room you can find the remains of the Pretty Lady and loot
her corpse for the BLOODSTAINED SHAWL. Take this back to the Chicken Lady to
receive the DOCTOR'S INSTRUCTIONS #3 which allows you to access the Doctor's
House by another route. This allows you the option of killing the Wolfman (see
below).

  When you are ready to progress with the game, come back to the Wolfman and
have him escort you to the Doctor's House (note that you can use him to fast
travel to the Doctor's House from the Barn Ruins unlimited times). After this
the Wolfman has no further relevance to the plot, and he will remain in the Barn
Ruins from now on. You can still trade with him until you leave for the Swamp,
you can still choose to go back and kill the Sow in order to get his hotbar
upgrade, and best of all you can kill him with a shotgun or some other heavy
hitting weapon to gain the unloaded assault rifle and a wedding ring. If you do
plan on killing him, make sure he takes you to the Doctor's House first, since
this opens the location. You can then return and kill him, entering his
sanctuary via the safe back route with his KEY.

  NOTE: The assault rifle is one of the game's best weapons. You can find one in
the Swamp (Wreckage) or you can murder Wolfman at any point and take it from him
(ideally after he has given you the hotbar upgrade and the hunting rifle), but
the problem is ammo. The medium caliber magazine is a rare and random loot drop,
meaning you cannot buy it from any trader. You will likely find several
magazines in the Swamp, but before then your best chance of finding one is
probably in the wardrobe in the Brook (Dry Meadow) or one of the Tank Wrecks in
the Silent Forest. If you do acquire this gun in Chapter 1 make sure you carry
it and any ammo you have found to the Swamp.
                

##### 3.3.3.2.3 The Doctor's House

                
  This location can only be accessed by either having the Wolfman take you there
or by killing the Chicken Lady for the DOCTOR'S INSTRUCTIONS #3, thereby opening
the location for access from the Old Woods biome; if you try to find it
otherwise by just walking along the edge of the Old Woods you will find it
blocked by boulders and it will not be flagged on your map. 

  As with the Village, time is frozen in this area. Bring plenty of inventory
space and a weapon, that's all you need.
                
  Once you allow the Wolfman to escort you to the Doctor's House you will
transport to an area off the main part of the Old Woods, and your map will no
longer work. You can return to the Old Woods just by turning around and
following the road south, at which point the Doctor's House can be accessed from
the Old Woods without the Wolf's help, and will appear on the map.

  Follow the road until you find a ruined building with a locked door. Farther
on is the House itself, with a bear trap waiting for you on the ground. Go
inside and find the suffering madman in a corner of the second bedroom. Put him
out of his misery and loot his body for the RUSTY KEY, which you can then take
back to the previous building and use to unlock the bathroom; in there you will
find Chapter 1's only axe and an odd stone.
                
  If you played the Prologue you will recognise the third and final building.
Turn on the generator outside to help you see, then hop through the window. Loot
the containers for various items, including another PHOTO; and note the familiar
corpse in the back room.

  Go outside and do some more looting. If as the Doctor you declined to
euthanise the dog in the Prologue you will find a huge dog in the yard. Standing
in the yard, turn away from the House but look at the floor inside. You will see
that it is showing the House as it used to be, but if you turn and look inside
you see it as it currently is.

  By looking at the centre of the main room you can see that the rug is pulled
back in the present, and a FLOOR SAFE has been revealed. It requires a KEYCODE,
and the necessary numbers are scrawled into the floor (sometimes the Protagonist
has to look at them to see them; sometimes they only show up when he is looking
away). There is a line drawn from the FLOOR SAFE to the bedroom, and this line
bounces around the walls as it meets all four NUMBERS:

  #1: Immediately next to the SAFE.
  #2: In the south-west corner of the bedroom.
  #3: Under the wardrobe in the bedroom (drag the wardrobe away).
  #4: Under the bedroom window.
        
  If you are not interested in trying to 'solve' it yourself, the FLOOR SAFE
code is:

    8-4-5-9
        
  Use this CODE on the SAFE to open it and reveal the MAP (this shows the Train
Wreck's location in the Old Woods), the CODE (the Doctor tells you how to open
the TRAIN WRECK'S METAL DOOR; this code is randomly chosen from a small pool of
possible codes) and a TOY.

  Run all the way back down the road and exit this area via the south to return
to the edge of the Old Woods. The Doctor's House location can be accessed from
now on just by walking along the open path.

##### 3.3.3.2.4 Train Wreck

        
  Loot three mushrooms from the area, and look for the large boulder on the
other side of one of the fallen trees from the cars. This boulder can be shoved
aside to reveal a corpse holding a homemade shotgun (there are several such
moveable boulders in the Swamp) and a pill. Approach the end of the TRAIN WRECK
to find a locked METAL DOOR.

###### 3.3.3.2.4.1 Musician's Path

  Having been passed an invitation by the Musician, open the TRAIN WRECK'S METAL
DOOR and meet the Doctor. He tricks and gases the Protagonist and then runs off,
whereupon you must play through the Train Wreck Dream sequence (see section
3.6.6).

  If you survive in the Dream you reawaken as the Protagonist and you get to
confront the Doctor in the Train Wreck, just as you would if you had followed
the Wolfman's Path; whereas if you die the Doctor will be gone from the Train
Wreck and you must go to the Underground Entrance alone, finding the First
Armored Door open when you get there. Also, death in this Dream causes the
Doctor to set up a camp in the Radio Tower in Chapter 2, whereas you would
otherwise merely find him sitting alone at the entrance.

###### 3.3.3.2.4.2 Wolfman's Path

                
  Having gotten the CODE from the Doctor's House, you can OPEN THE TRAIN WRECK
to find the Doctor inside. Loot his bedding for the PHOTO, confront and talk to
him, then either choose to spare him (in which case he agrees to meet you at the
Underground Entrance and opens the FIRST ARMORED DOOR for you with the BIG,
METAL KEY) or kill him (and retrieve the BIG, METAL KEY from his body, and open
the FIRST ARMORED DOOR yourself). If you spare him he can be found in various
locations in the Swamp, slowly going insane; and each such meeting is worth a
valuable item.
        

##### 3.3.3.2.5 Leaving Chapter 1

        
 Whatever you choose to do with the Doctor, the next step is to go to the
Underground Entrance in the Dry Meadow. This means leaving all three of these
biomes behind for good, along with all the equipment and materials you don't
carry with you. The events described above should take you about a day
(especially because time freezes in the Doctor's House, and all the locations
involved are often quite close together, so return to your Hideout for one last
night and take advantage of the time freeze to do some inventory sorting in the
morning, selling everything you don't want to take with you to the Trader.

  Make sure you fill your inventory with all the most valuable things before you
leave, because you're about to lose everything else, including all the credit
you have built up with the Trader. I recommend you carry your best ranged
weapons (and ammo, especially any medium caliber magazines) and your upgraded
axe (or shovel if you didn't visit the Doctor's House; make sure this is fully
repaired if you have not yet visited the Underground Entrance). Spend whatever
spare inventory space you have on a lantern, a stack of lockpicks, stacks of bug
shells, and whatever other high value items you still have, especially the
weapon parts you should have been buying from the Trader since the Old Woods or
Silent Forest. Any spare embryos you have not yet cooked should go along, too.
        
  NOTE: The Bike Bell is now useless, the Bike Man can't follow you into the
Swamp.
        
  Assuming you start out in the morning, you have lots of time before the next
night because time also freezes underground and you begin Chapter 2 at the start
of a new day. If you can make it out of Chapter 1 in optimal condition before
the end of Day 8 then this is ideal.

###### 3.3.3.2.5.1 Underground Entrance (dry Meadow)

        
 Go into the tunnel via the TRAPDOOR and follow it along to find the DOOR. (Note
that if you have a lantern equipped it will not run out down here, since time is
frozen). If you did agree to meet the Doctor he will be here waiting for you;
otherwise he will have opened the DOOR. If he's there with you, examine the DOOR
and choose the "Ask the doctor to use the key" option to proceed. Go down the
stairs and into the next area (you can still go back up the stairs and all the
way back out to the Dry Meadow from here).

  Follow the tunnel until you find the SECOND ARMORED DOOR up to the north,
currently locked. The Doctor draws your attention to a MAN entangled in some
roots: talk to him to receive the WEIRD CROSS. After some monologue you will
also receive the BULLET, the Man will die, and the Doctor will have fled if he
came with you, opening the SECOND ARMORED DOOR in the process, then looting the
corpse behind it for the RUSTED IRON COWBELL and a torch. Head north (the moving
things can't hurt you) and explore to the north-west to find a locked crate
containing various curatives, a lantern and a 9V battery (you actually can die
down here by stepping on the mushrooms, hence the curatives).

  Pass more tangled bodies as you explore this flooded area to the east. Follow
the tunnels the only way they go, and you should come to a corpse past some
growths in the floor.
        
  NOTE: You will encounter a few more growths in the swamp. The way they
generally work is that they appear in pairs, and if one is open the other is
closed (blocking your path). To make the closed one open you have to make eye
contact with the other one, causing it to close and the other one to open in
response, allowing you past. Try closing the one blocking the tunnel in this
way, and walk over it backwards to avoid looking at it.

  Examine the shiny stone on top of the corpse to receive another CROSS. The
EXIT is just at the end of this last tunnel - a point of no return - whereupon
you will emerge in the Swamp and Chapter 2 will begin.
        
        

#### 3.3.3.3 Minor Locations

### ?

                
  This appears to be the carcass of some giant animal or tree; but other than
three harvestable mushrooms there is nothing here of interest. Note that the
surface acts like water (which you have not yet encountered outside of the
Forest Dream), slowing down both you and enemies (you more than them). This
place will always be guarded by a single huge dog, making it a good place to
visit if you want some odd meat armour.

  This could almost count as a Generic Location, as it's possible for it to
spawn more than once in the Old Woods.

### Anti-tank Gun

                
  This might just look like another piece of environmental design, like the
Shrines; but if you look nearby you should see a boulder similar to the one at
the Train Wreck. Move it aside to find a crate underneath.

### Broken Tree

                
  A large, fallen tree with a single savage sleeping next to it: he will run
away if you disturb him, and offers various minor loot if killed.

### Burned House

        
  The House should already be marked on your map if you found it while in the
Dry Meadow, except this time you are approaching it from the other side. A short
track marked by mushrooms should lead into its yard; and you may notice that
your vision cone reveals the House as it is now, whereas anything you are not
looking at will appear as it did in the past.

  Other than some mushrooms in the vicinity, you can find the KEY TO THE BURNED
HOUSE and the "FLICKER" MAGAZINE in a wardrobe inside. The KEY opens the locked
door, allowing you to pass back through to the Dry Meadow if you so desire,
which you will later to reach the Underground Entrance.
        

### Corpses

                
  A few corpses lying around on the ground, next to arrangements of twigs made
to look like corpses. Usually patrolled by chompers, who either presumably
killed them or are waiting for them to become more chompers. Given the low level
gear on offer, this place is not worth visiting.
        

### Church Ruins

                
  This is the same Church from the Dream (see 3.6.4). Check the perimeter and
the generator room for some mushrooms. Assuming you have already experienced the
related Dream, you can find a similar MEDALLION to the one from the Dream on a
chomper corpse in the main room, near the altar, with the same TRAPDOOR KEYCODE
on it; alternatively, you can find a Madman chanting the code (after you
interact with the Trapdoor) if you chose to escape from the Dream instead. If
you previously opened the TRAPDOOR in the Dream then it will currently be open;
if not you can open it now.

  NOTE: If you visit this Location before having the Church Dream, the Trapdoor
will be closed and you will not yet have learned the Keycode. Also, neither the
chomper corpse nor the Madman will be here yet, although the gear from the back
room will be.

  In the wardrobe in the back room (behind the door) you can find a WELDER and
the ELECTRONIC GAME; and there is a free shovel blade on a shelf.

  As annoying as the Church Dream is, you still want to have it before leaving
the Old Woods and going to the Swamp, otherwise you will not be able to access
the Church's cellar (which is admittedly only worth a shiny stone). Down here
you will find either dormant chompers (don't turn on the radio!) or active huge
dogs, depending on how you dealt with the events in the Dream. Find the wardrobe
or loot the body of the single huge dog in the back room to find the TWISTED
KEY, which can be taken to Wolfman along with the STRANGE BOX (found only in the
Church Dream). He will open it to reveal the DRAWINGS and a shiny stone.
        

### Hunter's House

# 3.4 Chapter 2 (the Swamp)

  This is by far the toughest biome you have yet encountered, especially since
you get to bring very little of your equipment with you. Even if you brought all
your best gear, you begin with low material resources for Hideout maintenance
and finding more is pretty dangerous in the Swamp.

  Chapter 2 takes place entirely in the Swamp, with two ways out of it to reach
the Epilogue, the end of the game: 
        
  The first option involves simply finding the Radio Tower's location and making
your way through it to the Radio Tower Bunker, then on to the Road Home and the
Epilogue. No key items are required for this, meaning you can attempt it as soon
as you arrive in the Swamp; and if you brought even half decent gear from
Chapter 1 you don't even need to upgrade the Workbench or build anything else,
meaning you can literally arrive in just about any condition, run directly to
the Radio Tower (assuming you can find it) and leave. This is the Chapter 2 part
of the speedrun strategy. To take this route please skip to 3.4.2.1.

  The second, much more convoluted option requires finding the Compressor Parts
either at the Junkyard or the Radio Tower, then finding an Oxygen Tank at either
the Swamp Cottage or the Old Shed, then killing the Tree in the Village. This
takes far longer, covers about half of the Swamp in exploration tasks, and is
unarguably far more difficult, as it requires a lot more travelling through
dangerous locations and a lot more equipment. To take this route please skip to
section 3.4.2.2 (although you can still explore the Radio Tower location even if
you don't choose to access the Road Home that way).

  This guide covers both of these options, meaning you can see and do everything
of importance for either approach in the Swamp, then leave yourself the choice
of which route you want to take when it comes time to leave.

## Tldr (radio Tower / Fast Version)

  
    Find the Radio Tower (via the Holes or the MAP from the Village)
    Enter the Radio Tower Bunker.
    Survive the Final Dream.
    Leave the Swamp via the Radio Tower Bunker to reach the Road Home.
        

## Tldr (village / Slow Version)

  
    Collect the PLIERS x3 from the Junkyard, Village, Holes, Mushroom Glade or
      Big Ruin 2, upgrade the Workbench to level 8.
    Collect the COMPRESSOR PARTS from the Junkyard or Radio Tower.
    Refill the Swamp Cottage GENERATOR with fuel, talk to the ELEPHANTS. 
    Free Marcinek from the Mushroom Glade, collect the EMPTY OXYGEN TANK from 
      the Swamp Cottage / Old Shed.
    Repair the COMPRESSOR and refill the OXYGEN TANK.
    Use the OXYGEN TANK to reach the VILLAGE BASEMENT, kill the TALKING TREE 
      with a TORCH.
    Leave the Swamp via the Village to reach the Road Home.
 
  

### 3.4.1 Miscellaneous

#### 3.4.1.1 The First Day

  Whatever time it was when you entered and exited the Underground, you should
find that it is a new day in the Swamp (although time is not frozen on this
first morning), giving you plenty of time to figure out what's happening. 

  You will see a new COMPRESSOR in your Hideout, next to the trapdoor from the
Underground Entrance. It is currently broken and requires the Compressor Parts
which can be located in the Junkyard and the Radio Tower. There is a crate
covered in the black substance, with another NOTE about it on the floor: this
stuff is impervious to damage but is destroyed by exposure to light, so turn on
the Generator and move a lit lamp next to it, or shine any torch, flashlight or
lantern at it to watch the black substance's health bar deplete, allowing you to
access the crate for some gasoline, a flare and a gas bottle. In the same room
is a backpack with the TOBACCO.

  In the next room is the radio, inoperative for now. Search the wardrobe in the
room adjacent to the Oven room, then drag it aside to find the container
underneath, holding the NOTE WITH A PHOTO OF SIGNS. There is another destroyed
wardrobe in the Oven room (already lit). In the Workbench building you will find
another smashed wardrobe and a locked metal door (which will be unlocked later
if the Musician survived Chapter 1).

  You can find a corpse near the Saw, with a NOTE which when examined will mark
the Village and the Cottage Near The Junkyard on your map. Moving in the
direction opposite your Saw's position, you should find a place on the ground
which looks at lot like the holes you opened in the Silent Forest Hideout,
connecting it with the Village - this will become relevant later on (see 3.4.3,
Holes). Nearby should be another corpse with some minor materials. You should
also find two explosive barrels at one corner of your Hideout: these can be
collected as items and then deployed like traps by equipping them in your
hotbar. Once placed they will explode if shot or exposed to fire. Next to these
you can find a corpse with the BURNED BOOK.

  Meanwhile, your new Hideout is in a terrible state, and fortifying it
effectively is pretty much impossible: even if you had tons of boards and nails
and traps, there are still too many points of entry, and the new night events
and creatures you will experience in the Swamp often don't care about your
defenses, they'll reach you anyway. The argument for barricades is that, as in
the Old Woods, the Swamp nights frequently feature pairs of banshees which
cannot negotiate barricaded doors or windows, making them easily trapped: I
recommend barricading a few windows, but leave one or two others free so that
you have some quick exit and entry points - this really pays off in this
Hideout. Get your bearings and do a few high speed tours of the environment, so
you know how to get around in a hurry. You will also spot some fences which can
be repaired, but I recommend you leave them alone, as they are more of a
hindrance to you than anything else when you need to get away, especially since
nearly everything which will come after you can hop over them more quickly than
you can, and will probably hit you while you climb. 

  However much of a gutpunch it feels to be stripped of all the stuff you've
been collecting, know that there is plenty more to find here. You will quite
quickly begin to run out of storage space, and the game is generous with
providing you with nearly all the things you think you need. Scrap, rags and
gasoline are low for now, though, so be sure to prioritise those things both
when scavenging and trading. Your first day will likely be spent on all this
work, so try to grab anything from the immediate surroundings and don't stray
too far into the swamps around you.

  NOTE: At some point, a few days after you arrived, assuming you killed the Sow
and the Musician survived Chapter 1, you will find weird tracks leading to and
beyond the locked metal door in your Hideout. Sometime afterwards, during the
night, you will hear the metal door unlock and open, and you will find the
Musician inside. Speak to him and you can then spend that night camped behind
the indestructible metal door (although nothing else should even happen during
that same night), but after that you will lose health in that room and cannot
stay there, thanks to the Musician's deterioration. Do not wear your Rubber
Boots in here, as the acid on the floor will damage and ultimately destroy them.

  NOTE: If, on the other hand, you followed the Musician's Path and declined to
pass the Key to the Wolfman, a few days after you arrive in the Swamp you will
find some items missing from your Workbench, and the NOTE FROM THE WOLF will
invite you to the Sawmill - see 3.4.3 (Sawmill) for more details.

#### 3.4.1.2 The First Night

                
  NOTE: You should see that the radio in the middle of the Oven room starts
trying to communicate with you on the first night, before any enemies show up.
This is a little bit of lore. Don't bother turning on the Generator until after
its last message goes away.
        
  The nights here tend to play out similarly to those in the Old Woods, albeit
the danger rating has been ramped up. You will be hit with many of the old
classics, as well as some delightful new ones. In most places it's harder to get
away with camping in a corner, the game generally won't let you, but that's
actually fine because, like the Old Woods Hideout, the Swamp Hideout supports an
evasive strategy over a confrontational one. This is why it's important to be
familiar with the layout of the buildings, especially where all the doors and
windows are. You don't want to fight here, you want to run and hide, wait until
you're found, then run and hide again. Your weapons and supplies will thank you
for it. Keep in mind this is not a game which favours combat, and since the
Swamp nights will typically throw several enemies of different types at you each
night, your chances of survival are almost nil if you try to take them on. Delay
them with barricades and the occasional trap, and just focus on being where they
are not.

  This requires light, and so it's important to keep the Generator topped up as
much as possible in order to illuminate the place reasonably well. Make sure you
have placed your three lamps in places the wall lights can't reach, ideally
shining on multiple routes and entry points at once to aid your emergency
navigation (outside between the Generator and the Saw is a good place). I tend
not to even use weapons, I instead keep hold of a torch and keep it unselected
until I need to move; I then use it to light my path to some other hidey-hole,
and turn it off once I'm there.

  I recommend you don't bother with armour at night, it's better to keep it for
daytime exploring rather than throw it away on trying to survive a nighttime
gank. If you're close enough to be hit so many times that you need armour to
save you, you're doing something very wrong.

  The new Swamp enemies (human spiders, centipedes and swampers) can all attack
with ranged abilities, meaning you can no longer get away with camping behind a
barricade made of furniture and picking off night invaders with your pistol
(besides, you don't have the furniture). You won't meet swampers at night, but
the other two will turn up in pairs and represent a serious headache.
        
  There are certain spots you can stand where nine times out of ten nothing will
find you during the night, provided you are standing there before the clock hits
20:00. One such spot is against the long side wall, just next to the window.
Here there is a container attached to the wall, and a small gap between the
container and the tree trunk. Stand in the corner with your back to the wall and
you will likely avoid everything. The exceptions are those night events which
force you to move, such as Gas, Shadows and Centipedes, but otherwise you
usually get a quiet night.

  There is a growth by the well which emits a bright light at night, meaning
even if you run out of fuel you still have a light source which cannot be
disabled by enemies. Sticking by this spot also means being close to the Well
for its healing.

  Once you have upgraded your melée weapons fully, nearly all metal scrap you
find and buy can be spent on traps (save some for weapon repairs). Place several
around key positions (but not chokepoints) in your Hideout, and make sure you
always place them in the same spots and memorise them, so that you will not
wander into them by accident.

  After the first night in the Swamp you can find a body lying near your
Hideout. Explore the corpse to find a SEVERED HEAD, the KEY TO THE UNDERGROUND
(useless), and the WEIRD BOOK.

#### 3.4.1.3 Death & Trading In The Swamp

                
  If you do happen to make it through the night you get 250 reputation credit
with your new trading partners, The Three. They will turn up somewhere near your
Saw after the night has been survived, and you can trade with them as you did
with the Trader. Get rid of all your weapon parts and other high-end items which
you brought from the Old Woods in order to receive a massive cash infusion which
will stand in good stead for many nights to come (I was able to make ~2000
reputation points in one trade on Day 7 this way; and ~3000 in playthroughs when
arriving here around Day 13). And start buying all the ammo you can find for
your guns of preference every morning, especially the shotgun ammo which will
carry you through this biome.

  Note that if you do offload all your top tier items in this way you have
basically solved all your nighttime Hideout problems. You will no longer need to
make any money with the Three once you have this much credit, so dying during
the night and losing 250 reputation means nothing, especially given how much
amazing loot you will be able to find and sell to them by plundering the Swamp.
On top of this, if you explore the Swamp systematically (meaning fully exploring
and looting each area on your first visit and then never returning), and
prioritise running away from enemies over killing them, the only other downside
to death is also neutralised, since even if the few enemies you killed do
respawn you will likely never meet them again since you are not retreading old
ground. (Remember that enemies scripted to appear in Location catchment areas
never respawn when killed.) The bottom line is that death in the Swamp means
nothing unless you lose some good items way out in the wilderness, so don't
worry about it unless you are playing on harder difficulty levels.

  Keep buying all the mundane stuff you didn't bring with you, especially wire,
rags and metal scrap - the scrap is necessary for repairing and upgrading your
axe if you are using it, so don't forget about it if you decide not to bother
building traps. Rags might seem like a really boring thing to be buying, but
you're going to want plenty of them for armour, torches and lanterns. Lots of
the cheapest stuff is used in Workbench and weapon upgrades, as well as Hideout
maintenance, so keep track of what you need and make the most of the Three while
they are around. Don't forget that upgrading the Workbench to level 7 takes 3x
wire, so try not to spend the first three you find on lockpicks.

  Finally, you will need to find three pairs of PLIERS somewhere in the Swamp in
order to upgrade your Workbench to Level 8 and get access to all the best
firearms. The hunting rifle and pump action shotgun are fantastically powerful,
but they're useless without ammo, so make sure not a meeting with the Three
passes where you don't buy their best ammo for your guns of choice so that you
have a nice stock of rounds for when those weapons do finally come around. If
you got the hunting rifle from the Wolfman and brought it with you, all the more
reason to be buying one measly bullet per day; the assault rifle is a bit
different because you can't buy medium caliber magazines, but you can find a few
in the Swamp if you are willing to explore. As for PLIERS: there are at least
four to find in the Swamp, and you can even make that five if you gave Piotrek
all four VEHICLE PARTS in the Silent Forest (and declined to let Wolfman turn
one of them into a bomb).

#### 3.4.1.4 Exploration Tips

  Death is quite common in the Swamp if you don't know what you're doing, so
when you think your inventory is full enough to justify a trip back home to drop
it off you are usually correct. Play it safe, the Swamp isn't going anywhere.

  Always leave your Hideout each morning with a fully repaired shovel, as well
as some other strong weapon (ideally an axe). This is important because the
Swamp has several places and blockages which require a shovel to deal with, and
interacting with these things inflicts damage on the shovel, even as it rewards
you with progress or loot. You might find that your shovel gets too damaged to
use and that you need to fall back on another weapon, which is why you bring at
least two.

  The new enemies include swampers, human spiders and centipedes (see section
2.5.1).
        
  You will see several 'pain' centres in the Swamp, large, glaring zones with a
bright red light in the centre. These are not as dangerous as they might seem,
so don't worry too much about getting close if you have plenty of health - as
long as your FOV does not meet the shining centre they can't hurt you. They can
move around to different locations periodically, including while you are near
enough to see them.

  The Swamp is full of water which slows you down and massively saps your
stamina when you are trying to run, which is particularly dangerous if you chose
the Weakness debuff. However, the water is actually your friend. It slows you
down, of course, but it does the same thing to all enemies other than the
swampers (which are not very common). Try to fight everything in the water.

  There are lots more bear traps lying around than ever before, so either grab
them for scrap or lure your many enemies into them.
        
  Stay away from all twitching corpses and piles of human spider corpses.

  Listen carefully when exploring heavily wooded areas with lots of twisting
trees lying around. You should occasionally hear a loud grinding noise of wood
writhing. Look on the floor at the base of certain trees and you will see
tendrils of wood, vines, reaching along the floor, looking to trap you. This is
very dangerous when there are other hostiles around, as the trap lasts quite a
long time and can inflict poison. Avoid them when you see them.

  If you allowed the Doctor to come to the Swamp with you you have a chance of
meeting him at three randomly chosen Locations in the Swamp, including some
Major Locations, assuming he made it here alive. Each time you meet him he will
give you (via trade) a free item, as well as dropping some lore gossip on you.
By gossiping with him he can tell you the rough locations of both the Underwater
Corridors (to the Wreckage and the Burned Cottage), and also the Quarry. He is
effectively otherwise just a trader and lore dump until he disappears for good,
the poor man. 

  

### 3.4.2 Major Locations

        

#### 3.4.2.1 Radio Tower

                
  This is a very important Location, and if you are trying to finish the game as
quickly as possible upon arriving in the Swamp then you should find and explore
the Radio Tower as a priority. On the other hand, if you are interested in
exploring the Swamp and / or using the Village to complete Chapter 2, this
Location can and should be left until much later (if you even dare to visit it
at all), as it can represent a major difficulty spike. The whole place has been
taken over by banshees, so you will want to come here with armour (especially if
you didn't pick the Acid Blood Skill), plenty of melée and ranged weaponry, a
handful of molotovs, and a few healing items, as well as at least one lantern.
Set out in the morning to give yourself the time to cover all the important
stuff in one visit.

  NOTE: The Radio Tower is accessed via the edge of the map. When you reach the
corridor leading to the Location, look to either side, just 50 yards away in
either direction, also along the edge of the forest, in order to find the
Doctor's abandoned camp (if you died in the Train Wreck Dream). There you should
find a campfire and a bed, and among the items you can find the CAMERA, the
BURNED PAGE WITH A MAP (showing the Compressor and an Underwater Corridor) and
the TORN PAGE FROM A JOURNAL #1.
                
  The Radio Tower can be quite hard to find, even if you've already marked it by
finding the MAP in the Mushroom Glade. It is often easily found by first
locating the Holes Location, as the Radio Tower is often right next to it: the
Holes can be used as a very convenient shortcut to the otherwise hard to reach
Radio Tower, so consider visiting the Village early if only to find the MAP
there which marks the Holes POI (this MAP is very close to the Village
entrance). Hug the edge of the trees and look for a narrow corridor near a
corpse on the grass - this route should lead you inside.

  NOTE: Depending on whether or not you freed the Snail from the Cottage Near
The Junkyard, the entrance to the Radio Tower may look quite different. Normally
it's a short, twisting corridor through the trees, but if the Snail has left his
Cottage the trees will have been battered down and you can follow a straight,
slimy path to the Radio Tower. Not far in, just before the part where you have
to squeeze between some rocks and a building wall, you should find its giant
corpse in which banshees have made a home. If you are willing to clear them out
you can find a shiny stone at the deepest part.

  Ignore the area behind the first fence unless you are interested in the corpse
with the flare and flashlight, and pass through the gap between the two sections
of the falling tower. You should see a couple of growths here, one of which will
open when you look at the other one, allowing you to pass. Don't fall for it -
as the game warns you about making noise - the growth will close behind you and
you will be caught in a dangerous area full of sleeping banshee babies and two
banshees. Instead, move along the nearby path, silently killing any babies you
meet with a melée weapon. Pick a mushroom on a narrow cliff edge, then peek
ahead until you see a patrolling banshee move towards the place you first
entered, out of your way. Creep past her, hugging the edge, and pass more
nesting babies to reach a place where you can squeeze between a wall and some
rocks. Go around to find an open window and a corpse with a molotov.

  Jump through the window and begin chopping any more babies in their nests;
around the back exit you should find another banshee, so let her scream to bring
more babies at you, at which point you can jump back through the window and
torch them all with the molly; do the same with any other banshees and babies
outside at the other end of the building unless you want to risk sprinting past
them. In the back corner of this building you should find a backpack with the
JOURNAL FROM THE RADIO TOWER #2, a crate with the OLD CASSETTE, and a wardrobe
with some minor loot.

  NOTE: The babies are only a problem if they are awakened by noise: either
yours or the adult banshees'. The adult banshees will only wake them up with
their final screams, not with their warning screams. The warning screams can be
aborted by breaking line of sight, so if you spot an adult and she is trying to
scream at you, reposition quickly to put a tree or some other barrier between
you and her. If you know where you are going and are quick enough you can just
run past all of them in this way, jinking and jiving, and reach first the open
window and secondly the Metal Door without ever waking any of the babies - and
for this you need no equipment other than maybe a light source and a boost of
stamina via a consumable.

  Move under the collapsed tower, squeeze through another gap, and explore this
next area full of corpses. You will come to a METAL DOOR under attack by a lone
savage and requiring a Keycode, so attempt to open it and then move away,
whereupon the Protagonist should remember the CODE:

    8-6-8-4
        
  Before unlocking the Metal Door and going inside, consider exploring the rest
of this area. There is a second growth in the corner which you can look at to
make the one by the crate open, at which point you can drag the crate out of the
way to provide a shortcut back out. Farther around in the other direction are
two more places of interest, including a back corner where you can find a hard
to see backpack on the ground near the edge - it contains FABRIC, gasoline and a
second lot of COMPRESSOR PARTS (since the Junkyard Compressor Parts are much
easier to reach, these ones only exist in case you somehow lose the first ones
before repairing the Compressor). Nearby are some corpses by some markings on
the ground; and you can pass under the broken metal to find more markings beside
three graves and a couple of sacks, one with the IRON COWBELL and the other with
the WEIRD DRAWING.

  In the only direction not yet explored you will come to more banshee babies
and another banshee (she may be sleeping if all the other banshees and babies in
the area are dead). Look at the growth tucked into the corner to make the other
one light up, allowing you to pass through and arrive back at the window of the
building.

  Go back to the METAL DOOR, UNLOCK it and go inside to the hatch down into the
Radio Tower Bunker.
        

##### 3.4.2.1.1 Radio Tower Bunker

        
  Time is once more frozen. Equip your armour and lantern, and have your weapons
and curatives ready, too.
        
  Once down there you will find a military flashlight and the JOURNAL FROM THE
RADIO TOWER #1. Explore to the right and you will come to two METAL DOORS: close
the first one behind you to unlock the second one.

  NOTE: You can double-click on the first DOOR from the inside to cause it to
slam shut and then open again before it has time to lock. This is a bug which
allows you to leave whenever you want.

  Explore the side passage to find a shovel and a horrible mouth which tells you
you have only one chance... Look for the METAL LEVER and attack the FUNGUS with
any melée weapon to fall unconscious and trigger the Dream (see section 3.6.8).
        
  If you survive the Dream and open the way to the east, please skip to The Road
Home (section 3.5.1). If you died in the Dream or if you want to return to the
Swamp and continue exploring, please continue reading from this point.

#### 3.4.2.2 Junkyard

                
  This is a very good location to visit early on, as it offers a huge cache of
useful materials for all sorts of purposes if you have just arrived at the Swamp
with very little. Take at least one fully repaired shovel for digging, plus a
weapon or two and some curatives; otherwise leave your inventory as empty as you
can. Consider visiting the Cottage Near The Junkyard before coming here, as
there you can find the NOTE showing a map of the Junkyard and the contents of
its dig sites, which is helpful both in finding where the sites are placed and
in being able to tell which one contains the COMPRESSOR, the main reason for
coming here.

  The sites can be found as piles of grey rubble spread around the Junkyard, and
each one can be dug out with about a third of the health of a shovel. You may be
able to find a spare shovel lying on the ground between sites D and F, which can
save you a trip. Site E can be hard to see, so scan the ground with the cursor
to make it show.

  The contents of the Junkyard are always basically the same, and arranged in
the same order:
        
    A: Bear trap x2, nails, planks
    B: Long barrel, wire, scrap metal
    C: Tubular magazine, weapon parts, scrap metal
    D: Antlers, tape, rope, scrap metal
    E: WELDER, TOOLBOX, shovel blade, scrap metal
    F: COMPRESSOR PARTS, rags, scrap metal
    G: Axe blade, bottles, scrap metal
    H: FABRIC x2, rags, scrap metal
        
  You mainly want the COMPRESSOR PARTS, as once you find them you will be able
to repair the COMPRESSOR at your Hideout and use it to fill the empty OXYGEN
TANKS (see Swamp Cottage and Old Shed), allowing you to continue the main quest.
Since you (technically) can't access the Old Shed without the code learned from
Marcinek, you may as well go to the Swamp Cottage next.

  NOTE: The Junkyard is the biggest treasure trove in the game, but its contents
are so many and varied that collecting them all will not only take a lot of time
but is also probably pointless. Prioritise the stuff you want the most from the
list above, and consider leaving the rest unless you are really struggling for
supplies.

  NOTE: The Compressor Parts are one of the most important items in the game,
and if you drop them and lose them you cannot complete the game until you find
them again. As soon as you collect them the first time make sure you equip them
in your hotbar and take them back to the Hideout as soon as possible. If you
somehow do lose them you can find more in the Radio Tower.

  NOTE: If you gave Piotrek all his rocket parts in Chapter 1 you can find the
ROCKET WRECKAGE here, with his corpse inside. Explore the WRECKAGE to find some
valuable PLIERS, among lots of other things, possibly including another WELDER.
If Piotrek died in Chapter 1 or if you did not finish his quest his rocket will
not be here and the Junkyard will be populated with enemies, making exploration
a lot harder.

#### 3.4.2.3 Swamp Cottage

        
  The Swamp Cottage is almost totally surrounded by a barricade of trees and
other things. Walk around the perimeter until you find a wardrobe you can push
aside. Make your way around the building and collect all the bear traps hidden
under the grass, then open the front door to start a little cutscene wherein you
learn that you cannot speak to the Elephants without first REPAIRING THE
GENERATOR outside. Use the SCRAP METAL from the traps to REPAIR THE GENERATOR,
then turn it on (you don't have to). The Elephant should now invite you inside
and the first METAL DOOR will unlock. Go in and SPEAK TO THE ELEPHANTS, who turn
out to be a MOTHER and her two sons in gas masks. Exhaust the dialogue to learn
that the MOTHER wants you to find MARCINEK, her other son. She may also tell you
about an explosion in the Junkyard (Piotrek's rocket), and tell you its location
(without actually marking it on the map), and you should be given the ROOM KEY,
allowing you to unlock the other METAL DOOR.

  Fold up the OLD DOORMAT to find the PRIMITIVE MAP, and use it to get a rough
location of Marcinek. You can also collect his DRAWINGS which are heavy on
mushrooms, a clue.
        
  If you look at your map after opening the PRIMITIVE MAP, you will see that a
large area is circled. Whether or not you have already found the Mushroom Glade,
the circle will include that location, and that is where you will want to go to
find Marcinek.

  NOTE: The Swamp Cottage is also one of the locations where the Doctor might
randomly be found.

#### 3.4.2.4 Mushroom Glade

                
  This location is not as big as the map would have you believe, and its centre
is easy enough to find. You may notice some footprints leading inwards from the
perimeter from one corner, away from a corpse holding the BLOODSTAINED ROCK,
evidence of a murder (this only appears if the Villagers survived Chapter 1 and
the Sow didn't); alternatively, if you follow the footsteps from the other
corner you could find a backpack among some trees, holding the CIGARETTE PACK
with a picture of the Quarry (again assuming the Villagers survived Chapter 1
and the Sow didn't): either or both of these items can be shown to Mushroom
Granny to learn the location of the Quarry. Be very careful on the ground here
as there are a great many poisonous mushrooms, so collecting the Rubber Boots
from the Great Lake first is encouraged. Take along plenty of curatives; and the
Mushroom Healing perk helps a lot here, too.

  Ignore the various people sitting around outside, they are dormant. Avoiding
the mushrooms, follow either set of footprints and enter either through the
front door or by jumping through the window. Make your way to the back of the
building, past a shelf which contains some PLIERS and the KEY ON A KEYRING
(which opens a metal door in the Village), to find the MUSHROOM GRANNY. 

  If you talk to MUSHROOM GRANNY and show her the DRAWINGS you found in the
Swamp Cottage, she will then send you to kill all the healthy Villagers in the
Quarry (assuming you killed the Sow and not the Villagers in Chapter 1). If you
then return to her, having either killed the Villagers or DESTROYED THE WOODEN
BEAMS to make the large BOULDER collapse (thus trapping the Villagers), she will
give you the MUSHROOM GRANNY KEY which opens the nearby METAL DOOR. (If you
already killed all the Villagers or knocked over the Boulder you don't have to
revisit the Quarry.) Note that while she sleeps you can pick two high value
glowing mushrooms from her head for free, but a third will kill her, at which
point you can loot her body for the MUSHROOM GRANNY KEY anyway. The nearby crate
is locked (see section 3.4.2.6).

  Unlock and open the METAL DOOR to reveal MARCINEK, the son of the ELEPHANT
WOMAN. Talk to him and choose to take him back to his family after you collect
his NOTE from the floor. You will note that he is chanting a rhyme, and this
rhyme contains the CODE to unlock the Old Shed which should be nearby:

    1-2-4-5
        
  Once you choose to take him back to his Mother, you will be transported all
the way back to the Swamp Cottage and the ELEPHANT WOMAN will give you a free
EMPTY OXYGEN TANK, a key item.
        
  NOTE: In order to finish the game via the Village, you only need one Oxygen
Tank, not both. A Filled Oxygen Tank can be used indefinitely. Always keep
Oxygen Tanks in your hotbar, rather than your inventory, so that you don't drop
them when you die. Always move them back to your inventory before attempting to
refill them at the Compressor, as there is a bug which can cause them to
disappear from the game entirely if filled from the hotbar.
        
  Before you leave make sure you go back outside and explore the passage to the
side of the building, which leads to a barn where much of the floor is covered
in worms, and the door is blocked by a growth inside. Shatter the door with a
weapon, then unlock the crate inside for some minor loot. Beyond is a hidden
passage outside, although a mushroom man will try to ambush you and the
explosion will kill you if your health is too low (just run back to the wooden
door and close it once inside). At the back of this secret passage is a
mushroom-encrusted crate containing some better loot. Note that you can also
access this location from the Swamp itself, although the exit is easier to find
than the entrance.

  NOTE: This location is not essential if you already got the OXYGEN TANK from
the Old Shed (although that does require knowing the Keycode beforehand).
        
  NOTE: The Swamp Cottage is also one of the locations where the Doctor might
randomly be found.
        

#### 3.4.2.5 Old Shed

                
  This location should be very close to the Swamp Cottage, with the trail of
petals leading to you to it from there, past a broken tractor (you should be
able to find the gasoline you need for the Swamp Cottage here). Off to one side
you can find a barrow, and next to that a grave which requires half a shovel's
worth of health to dig up. Inside you can find the UNDERGROUND KEY (a useless
item) and some minor loot. Inside the Shed is a shelf where you may also be able
to find gasoline. The METAL DOOR itself is unlocked by the CODE:

    1-2-4-5 (see section 3.4.2.4)

  Inside is an unlocked crate containing high level weapons gear, as well as the
MAP marking the location of the Radio Tower. The OLD JOURNAL in the last room
also relays the location of the Radio Tower, and there is a corpse of a boy, the
other son the Elephant Mother hinted at when you gossiped with her - loot it to
recover an empty OXYGEN TANK.

  Since you now have either or both OXYGEN TANKS, you can move to leave the
Swamp by visiting the Village.

  NOTE: This location is not essential if you already got the OXYGEN TANK from
the Swamp Cottage.

#### 3.4.2.6 Village

                
  NOTE: Despite leaving it 'til the end, I recommend you visit this Location
upon first arriving at the Swamp, if only to find the Map which shows the Holes
Location - this will help you get around quicker.
        
  This is a reasonably large area which may take two or three days to explore
thoroughly, and it is quite dangerous because of several enemies, poison and
fighting in tight areas. I suggest you take a couple of weapons, a fully
repaired shovel, and a lantern or two (you don't really need lockpicks). Some
armour and healing items would help, too; and set off first thing in the
morning. Luckily it usually spawns very close to the Hideout. If you have found
either of the OXYGEN TANKS before coming here, make sure you have also repaired
the COMPRESSOR, filled your OXYGEN TANK and brought it with you in your hotbar
in order to potentially save yourself a trip.
        
  Deal with some human spiders at the entrance and then note the flooded area to
the Protagonist's left: this is patrolled by a single swamper, aided by some
vines at the base of a tree. There is nothing to find or see around the
perimeter of this water area (other than a single room with the word "thieves"
written on the floor), so focus on the two more central buildings on the other
side of the swamper's pool, near the tractor. Find the doormat with the flower
petals on it and go inside: the metal door inside can be unlocked with the KEY
ON A KEYRING you can find in the Mushroom Glade. In the back room you can find
the PHOTO OF A FAMILY on a shelf; and you can push the table aside to access the
container underneath, holding the PAINTINGS (as well as a lantern and a shiny
stone). You don't need any of the PAINTINGS, but the only useful one is the
PAINTING FROM 1975, with the year representing the keycode to Mushroom Granny's
locked crate:

    1-9-7-5

  Go back outside. The building next to this first one should be visited next by
going around, past the mushrooms, and finding the DESTROYED PAINTING in a room
with a corpse. Next to that room is a twisted tree with two blazing braziers
next to it, lighting the way. There is a blockage of rubble here, so clear it
out of the way with your shovel to access the area beyond (and explore the hole
underneath). There are two ways to go in the dark, watery area you have just
opened up, but it is guarded by a swamper and a mushroom man, so be careful.
There is a corpse in the middle of the water with a homemade shotgun and a
knife; and there is a shelf against a nearby wall with some gasoline. The
barricaded door cannot be destroyed, but you can sneak around the back of this
building to jump through a window and into another "thieves" room: this is worth
doing because the crate contains the MAP which will mark the location of the
Burned Cottage on your map if you haven't already found it.

  Make your way back to where you removed the rubble blockage and explore the
rooms next door, where you should find the Cripple, sitting behind a door which
was blocked from the other side by a blockade on the Cripple's side. Ignore the
Cripple for now and continue exploring by going through the now open metal door
and heading to the Protagonist's left. Be ready for another mushroom man ambush,
then follow the only corridor until you reach and remove another blockage,
allowing you to meet a chomper beyond the metal door. This guy is protected by
black substance, so make sure your lantern is equipped or chuck a flare down
before dealing with him. Move through the room and look to the Protagonist's
right, going past the fallen tree: you should see a large cart which has been
tied in place by creeping vines. This spot marks the exit you will use later, so
ignore it for now and find the nearby generator - you should fill it with the
gasoline you found and turn it on to explore this area (you might also want to
make a run to this if you need help versus the chomper). Watch out for the huge
dog which will try to jumpscare you as you approach the kennel; otherwise you
are rummaging among the various rooms. You may find a couple of corpses, and
there is a locked wooden door near the huge dog, beyond which is the "thief"
room, where there is a wardrobe and a shelf containing the PHOTO OF 3 BOYS.

  In the other corner of this side area, near the two growths (one of them lit
up) are some more rooms. Open the wooden door to find a corpse on the floor,
carrying PLIERS and a sickle; and there is a wardrobe in the corner with FABRIC
and the LOTTERY TICKET - you'll know you are in the right room when the
Protagonist remarks on a large Tree visible through the low window. Open the
other wooden door and go into a new room next to the lit growth. In a back
corner, near the open window, you can find a locked crate. This can be opened
with the KEYCODE you will have just spotted on the LOTTERY TICKET (the four
numbers crossed out in the four columns which start with 1:

    2-5-3-1
         
  This crate contains two long barrels, a pipe and some shotgun shells, making
it a fantastic find if you favour the shotgun. Jump through the window, past
another lit brazier, and you should find yourself back in the Cripple's
building, at which point you might as well talk to him and show him the various
PHOTOS you've been collecting from the Village.

  NOTE: This is a good point at which to deal with the Talking Tree, so if you
want to undertake those events now, please skip below to 3.4.2.6.1 so that you
can leave the Village by a different route and avoid retreading old ground, then
return here and read the following (which I will now describe anyway).

  From the generator, move in the direction of your Hideout and explore the back
corner, where you should find a door leading into a house. You should see voices
speaking behind the wall, but the nearby door is locked; if you don't want to
use a lockpick you can go back outside and around the corner to smash down a
barricaded window. There appears to be little of interest in here, but if you
look away from the wardrobe you can spot a hidden container underneath, so drag
the wardrobe aside to find a shiny stone in a floor safe. Directly across from
the window you jumped through is another barricaded window, so smash it down to
find a crate with a flashlight, a bear trap and some gasoline - all useful items
in the Village, or would be if you weren't leaving, never to return.

  Keep heading away from the generator area to reach the shrine part of the
Village, where you should find a backpack on the ground, containing some FABRIC,
bandages with alcohol, and the MAP which pinpoints the Holes Location.

  Head out of the Village once and for all (assuming you made up your mind about
the Talking Tree), then await the coming night, where you will experience a
unique event.
        
  NOTE: The Village is also one of the locations where the Doctor might randomly
be found, near the shrine.

##### 3.4.2.6.1 The Talking Tree

  The Cripple hates the Tree and wants you to find Jasiek's Basement near its
base in order to access the roots, from where you can destroy the Tree for good.
Move through the back of his building, across the water, to where you find the
TALKING TREE against the back of the area. Talk to it and exhaust the dialogue.
You currently can do nothing with it (although one of the faces mentions a
radio). Explore to the right of the Tree to find a metal door, behind which is a
shovel head on the floor and a wardrobe containing the FOOD INVENTORY (which can
also be taken back to the Cripple). The Basement Hatch here is blocked.

  At the opposite end of the Tree area is a dark room with a corpse on which you
can find another sickle, and at the back of this room is a second BASEMENT
HATCH, this one accessible. Go down there to find a pain glare semi-blocking the
way (there is a corpse with a pistol and a small caliber magazine next to it),
and an UNDERWATER CORRIDOR you can only explore with a FILLED OXYGEN TANK. Do so
to find yourself in the Cellar Dream sequence (see 3.6.7).

  Once you come out of the Cellar Dream, explore the backpack on the floor to
find the TORCH you will need. You have to SET FIRE TO THE TALKING TREE, and you
do this by interacting with the TANK VALVE and choosing OPEN THE VALVE to fill
the corridors with gas. At this point, make sure you equip one or two healing
items to your hotbar, then equip the TORCH to there as well. Walk to the gap
where you can see the TREE, then THROW THE TORCH at the TREE. This will light
the trail of gas which leads from the TANK to the TREE, but it may also cause
you to catch fire, so hit the healing items. While the TREE burns, go back to
the UNDERWATER CORRIDOR to re-emerge at the Village, where the TALKING TREE is
now on fire. Talk to the Cripple as you pass, then return to the area of the
Village where you found the generator earlier.

  On the night following the death of the Talking Tree you will experience a
unique night event (see section 2.6.2.1 for more information).

  Whether or not you survive, you will wake in the morning to find everything
back to normal, except the Three are gone, never to reappear (in spite of the
fact you will still earn another 250 credit per morning with them).

  Return to the Village to find the Cripple gone, along with the Tree itself.
You can now pass beyond the Tree's corpse to reach the Road Home. Alternatively,
you might consider reaching the Road Home via the Radio Tower, assuming you
either have not experienced the Final Dream or did experience it and also
survived it.

  Either way, the game is effectively over, and you can skip to section 3.5.1 to
proceed.

  

### 3.4.3 Minor Locations

        

### Big Ruin (1)

                
  As with the two other Big Ruins, this one is marked with a large house icon on
the map. This dark building is guarded by two chompers, but its containers offer
a watch, some FABRIC and a shovel. Shoot a gun outside to get the chompers'
attention so that they smash the windows open for you.

### Big Ruin (2)

                
  This Ruin is also marked with a house icon. There is a growth blocking the
entrance, but it can be closed by looking at it. There is a wardrobe inside, and
the choice of a locked metal door or another growth. If you cause this second
growth to close by looking at the other one, walk over it, then accidentally
look at it again, it may open and trap you, meaning you will have to go through
the house and open the door from the inside to get out. Its metal door is
pickable from either side with a lockpick. Inside is a banshee to be dealt with,
easy enough by baiting it outside and fighting its babies there. The four
containers can be looted for lots of stuff, including the very valuable PLIERS,
as well as a military flashlight. The madman in the corner can be killed for no
loot or consequences.

### Big Ruin (3)

                
  Push open the front door (partly blocked by a wardrobe) of this small building
and you will find several people on the floor, just waiting to become chompers.
Get rid of them with molotovs, gunfire, explosive barrels or whatever, then
explore the second room and you will find another door which has to be blasted
open with violence. In the crate in the third room you can find antlers, an axe
blade, a FABRIC, and one other item; however, interacting with it will also
alert two huge dogs who will then break through the door behind the wardrobe.

  This is one of the most dangerous places in the Swamp because of the numbers
of fast, strong enemies you may encounter at once, and that's even if you don't
happen to alert any monsters in the vicinity of the building. Bait all the
enemies outside and fight them in the water, nerfing the chompers' speed. If you
are really worried about how to deal with six chompers at once, an easy pass is
to take along one bear trap and one gas bottle. Place the bear trap in the front
doorway and then chuck the gas bottle past it, into the room. The first chomper
will hit the trap and block the others from passing.
                

### Bonfire

                
  This location is often guarded by at least one savage, and maybe some human
spiders. The bonfire in the middle is permanently ablaze, not even put out by
the rain. You can only just reach the three sacks in the centre, one of which
holds the WELDER if you somehow don't already have one.

### Burned Cottage

  This ruined building is at the edge of the map, somewhere near the Village; it
will be marked by three crosses if you have found and opened the MAP from the
Village. Take a lantern and a good weapon, plus a FILLED OXYGEN TANK. At first
there is nothing to see but a couple of broken walls and a cart; however, if you
have a filled Oxygen Tank you can use it to access the Underwater Corridor in a
pool next to the building to access the Burned Cottage area. 

  You'll arrive on the other side to find that the three crosses are facing the
building, so move away from them, past some skeletons and onto dry land. Once
inside the building, collect minor loot from the corpse and the first wardrobe.
When you collect the PHOTO from the inner wardrobe (as well as some traps) a
human spider spawns in the main room and attacks, having first moved the first
wardrobe against the door so you can't just run outside. A previously locked
door nearby is now open, and in a third wardrobe beyond it you can find a second
PHOTO. The human spider once killed drops the KEY FROM THE BURNED COTTAGE, which
can be used to unlock the metal door leading back outside. This metal door helps
you deal with three more human spiders who have now spawned between you and the
Underwater Corridor.

  NOTE: You can reach the Burned Cottage without the MAP from the Village, it's
just harder to find.
        
  NOTE: This is one of the Locations where the Doctor might randomly be found;
he will be outside, near the broken walls and cart, by a long fallen tree.

  NOTE: If the Doctor made it to the Swamp by stealing the Key in the Train
Wreck you can find some of his things behind the open window of the ruined
building, before using the Underwater Corridor. In the sack is the NOTEPAD and
PHOTOGRAPHIC FILM, and in the campfire you can find his MEDICAL TOOLS.

### Cottage Near The Junkyard

        
  The only non-story reasons to visit this location are to find the NOTE for the
Junkyard, the MAP locating the Radio Tower, and the FABRIC if somehow you have
not yet upgraded your inventory.
        
  Follow the path past some trapped unfortunates and you will arrive at the
Cottage, with a giant SNAIL blocking the way. Explore the surroundings to find a
free shovel and some other things, including a wardrobe behind some broken walls
and a growth - loot the wardrobe for the NOTE - this pinpoints the locations of
various loot caches in the Junkyard. You might as well leave now unless you are
interested in lore.       

  Find the cracked area of the SNAIL SHELL and use a weapon to smash a hole in
it. Go inside and collect the SHED KEY from the HAND; then talk to the SNAIL. He
wants some gumboots, but more than that he wants to be set free. (You can come
back here later and show him the Rubber Boots before he has moved, although he
will not take them from you.)

  If you choose to free the SNAIL, as it asked, you have to use the SHED KEY on
the nearby METAL DOOR outside. This allows you to maintain eye contact with the
GROWTH closest to the DOOR (next to the wardrobe). With that GROWTH closed from
the eye contact, the other one in the corner will open, allowing you past. In
the clearing beyond you will find another GROWTH which looks like a fallen tree.
Attack it to free the Snail, then return to him again and talk to him to make
him move. Wait a day or two and return to the Cottage and you will find the
Snail gone, allowing you to access the Cottage. Alternatively, examine his BELLY
when you first meet him and you will find that you can smash it to open the
passage into the Cottage underneath, although this will kill the Snail. 

  Once inside you can find the NOTEBOOK which mentions a Tape Recorder, the
Junkyard and the Radio Tower. A nearby wardrobe contains some FABRIC and a MAP
which will pinpoint the Radio Tower. Approach the corpse in the back and then
try to leave, and the radio on the floor will talk to you before blowing out.

### Earth Mover

                
  This ruined piece of machinery at the edge of the map represents a place where
someone has tried and failed to break through the trees. There are a couple of
corpses nearby.

### Great Lake

        
  This is a large zone predominated by water, as you would expect. At first it
seems like a big, empty area with a few enemies, but if you make your way to the
centre you will find yourself in a permanent windstorm and surrounded by
darkness, illuminated by the odd lightning flash (these are random, they are not
showing you the correct way, but you can use them to briefly see around you).
Take a lantern to provide a bit of light, as well as to remove the black
substance from the several chompers and twitching bodies (which will become
chompers if interacted with). Also take plenty of ammo and healing, etc.

  There is a large growth at the centre, near a ruined house. The wind and
darkness are coming from this growth, so by attacking it you force it to close
and the area returns to the same weather conditions as the rest of the Swamp.
The problem is that attacking the growth also causes all the bodies in the
vicinity to rise as chompers and attack at once. Since fighting in the darkness
is incredibly hard, especially against multiple enemies, and since gunfire will
attract the nearby chompers and swampers to you anyway, there are two options
for this area. 

 The first is to move very carefully and slowly, using only melée and trying to
attract one enemy at a time. This is still very hard because you can't see and
there are so many enemies that you will likely face more than one at a time;
plus they have black substance armour which takes time and lantern light to
deplete, giving them plenty of time to kill you; and anyway your melée weapons
will degrade too quickly facing so many enemies. If you take this approach be
sure to loot every body you create for the valuable odd meat, giving you extra
protection.

  The second option is to get to the centre bit by bit, attack the growth to
remove the darkness, then trigger Runner to get out of the area as quickly as
you, with Scream as backup if you do get surrounded. Get away from the enemies
and then turn around and engage them on your own terms, in daylight and one at a
time. Note that daylight does not remove the black substance from the protected
chompers, reducing the attractiveness of this approach.

  However you deal with the enemies, at the centre of the area, marked by a
tractor and a ruined well near a garden, and very near the growth, you will find
a building with at least one more twitching body in there; look for the closed
metal door near the tractor. Loot the containers for some FABRIC and the
fantastic Rubber Boots. You can then remove the blockade to pass through to the
other side, where there are more corpses in the water for additional loot.

  NOTE: Equip the Rubber Boots immediately and keep them on in the Swamp, this
is one of the best items in the game. They allow you to step on mushrooms and
corpses without being poisoned (although you still take some little damage from
the initial burst). They also allow you to step on worms with no damage taken,
although the Boots themselves will be damaged and there is no way to repair
them, so take great care of them.

  The Great Lake is by far the most dangerous area in the entire game, with far
too many enemies and every other hazard waiting to kill you. If you die before
you can kill the growth you are then faced with trying to relocate your dropped
backpack, in the dark and in the water, while dealing with all the enemies you
didn't kill. When heading in, always take a lantern, a shotgun with a lot of
ammo, armour, and bandages with alcohol - anything less will get you killed
quickly. Finally, the Great Lake usually does not spawn very close to your
Hideout, and since there are no story or quest reasons to go there it may well
be one of the last locations you visit, unless you are making an early run
specifically for the Rubber Boots. If you encounter this area late in your Swamp
adventures then you might consider avoiding it entirely, since the Rubber Boots
are really only beneficial in the Mushroom Glade and the Village. They are
non-essential and the risk in getting them is very high, plus there is nothing
else in the Great Swamp worth finding.

  NOTE: Don't take any Oxygen Tanks or Compressor Parts in here in case you drop
them; and if you do take them make sure they are in your hotbar slots first.
        
  NOTE: If you do encounter any dormant chompers lying on the ground, move close
to them and let your lantern light remove the black substance before you
activate them: this way they cannot fight you while protected.
        

### Holes

                
  This location contains a crate with PLIERS inside. Interacting with the
largest hole opens a two-way shortcut to and from your Swamp Hideout, so finding
and using this location first for both these reasons is highly recommended. Its
main purpose is to provide you with a quick way of reaching the Radio Tower,
which always spawns far away from your Hideout; the Holes and the Radio Tower
can always be found next to each other, on the same edge of the map.

  NOTE: This is one of the locations where the Doctor might randomly be found.
        

### Markings (corpse)

                
  This location has a corpse placed on some markings on the ground. You can't
loot the body, just examine it for a description. Check for a shiny stone
nearby.
        

### Markings (trees)

                
  This location at first seems to offer nothing but another savage guard and
another shiny stone, but if you can find the large boulder you can shove it
aside to reveal a corpse holding an axe blade, a sickle and a knife. Be careful
of at least one tree root waiting to trap you.
                

### Markings

                
  Another group of rocks guarded by three savages, hiding a couple of corpses
with some random loot. Look for a shiny stone, it shouldn't require a light
source.
        

### Quarry

                
  This is mainly a quest location relating to the Mushroom Granny, lootable for
some minor supplies. If you killed the Sow in Chapter 1 the Quarry will be
inhabited by more Villagers, most of them hostile, the rest dying. There is a
giant boulder supported by beams which can be broken with melée weapons in order
to drop the boulder, trapping anyone inside the Quarry, including you (you can
kill the Villagers just by dropping this boulder, allowing you to avoid a fight
against several armed enemies). Make sure you attack the inner beam first, then
the other two second, so the boulder falls with you on the outside of the
Quarry.

### Ruin (1)

                
  This is just a ruined house, guarded by a huge dog on a broken chain, with a
chest behind it.
        

### Ruin (2)

                
  This small house contains a corpse, a wardrobe and a chest, but when you try
to loot any of them a savage with a staff will ambush you from behind the door.
You should at least find a shiny stone in here.

### Ruin (3)

                
  This is very similar to Ruin (4) except it's much more dilapidated. You'll
know it when you find it because it is marked only with an unnamed dot on the
map. The floor is again covered in worms which will need to be cleared to reach
the chest, although there is no gas this time (see Ruin (4)). The door offers a
closer route to the unlocked chest, but it requires a lockpick; or you might
want to risk climbing through the window and stepping on the worms.

### Ruin (4)

                
  This tiny building has some worms growing on the floor, but if you try to set
it on fire to clear it the gasoline in the room will also ignite, possibly
damaging you. Having looted the three explosive barrels first, throw in a match
from distance to approach the chest, which contains a handgun frame and some
minor loot.

### Ruin (5)

                
  Another building marked only with an unnamed dot. The locked door can be
smashed down, and there are a couple of gas tanks against the outer wall.

### Ruin (6)

                
  This small house is likely guarded by a couple of human spiders patrolling
outside, and has two gas tanks by the outer wall. The metal door cannot be
opened or destroyed, and there is a wardrobe locking it from the inside. You can
place an explosive barrel in the middle of the floor by looking through the gap
in the far wall, then blow up the barrel to destroy the wardrobe; or just chuck
a molotov at the wardrobe, allowing you to open the metal door and go inside.
You will find two wardrobes (including the destroyed one) and a crate, this last
containing a tubular magazine and some weapon parts.

### Sawmill

                
  The outdoor entrance area is lightly guarded and contains everything you might
expect to find at a standard logging camp Location.

  Inside are lots of logs, some of them apparently moving, leading to a locked
door which requires a key. This door will only ever open if you followed the
Musician's Path in Chapter 1, rather than the Wolfman's. If you did help the
Wolf then this door will remain locked.

#### The Wolfman Fight

                        
  After a few days in the Swamp, assuming you followed the Musician's Path, you
will want to return to the Wolfman to recover some things he stole (see 3.4.1.1
for more details). Before doing so, make sure you are fully healed and that
ideally you have not used any of your active Skills on this same day. Approach
the otherwise locked METAL DOOR within the Sawmill and open it to find his
ASSISTANT. Open the second METAL DOOR and let him talk to you through the
radios, then go back and dump your entire inventory into the ASSISTANT'S
INVENTORY. Wolfman will allow nothing into the fight, meaning you have no
weapons, curatives or any other tools to help you, other than what he provides.
There is a way to cheese this a bit by popping some odd meat just before you go
into the fight; and of course you still have your active Skills.

  Anyway, loot the backpack in the middle of the room and equip all three items.
You will first be ganked by two huge dogs, so be ready with Scream and smash
them with the table leg. Once they're dead the Wolfman comes in, so loot the
corpses for more odd meat and keep it active.

  When he appears he will open fire with his assault rifle, so just camp behind
any pillar and wait until he spends all his bullets. After that it's a melée
fight against an aggressive, chomper-like enemy who takes a lot of damage. This
is one of the hardest fights in the game, especially given your limited
equipment, so any edge you can get is essential, including Runner, Chameleon and
odd meat for its thick skin buff. Make sure to attack as quickly and often as
you can after using either of the pills in order to keep its regeneration going.

  If you win the fight you can open a third METAL DOOR to find your lost items
and a NOTE; if you lose your items are lost forever and all you get is the
charming CONSOLATION PRIZE.

### Wooden Thicket

                
  This is worth mentioning as a unique Swamp location, given how different it is
to the Chapter 1 Wooden Thickets. 
        
  The first variety has two or three large, fallen trees make a circular
barricade around the standing trees in the middle. There is usually a pain glare
in effect in here, and the area should be guarded by a chomper. Look for three
mushrooms and two sacks on the ground. The location appears as a circle on the
map.

  The other kind is also guarded by a chomper, but this time is represented by a
few trees on the map. There is a backpack here with some loot, but also two
chain traps waiting for you.
        

### Wreckage

                
  This zone is off the map and can only be accessed via an Underwater Corridor
in one of the pools nearby, which can be quite hard to see. You will also need a
FULL OXYGEN TANK. Once through at the Wreckage you can find the pilot's corpse
with an assault rifle, a medium caliber magazine and a military flashlight.
Inside the Wreckage you can find two more corpses: one has a ruined parachute
and the other is carrying the DESTROYED PHOTO.

  The assault rifle is not damaged, you just need to reload it with a medium
caliber magazine. This is the very best weapon in the game, and it takes a fully
upgraded Workbench to build one, assuming you have or can afford the parts. The
alternative to finding three Pliers and everything else is to come to the
Wreckage and retrieve the assault rifle, and since this requires the Compressor
Parts and an Oxygen Tank recovering those should be an early priority upon
arrival in the Swamp if you want the weapon. Magazines for the assault rifle are
very rare, but you can always buy more from the Three.

  On the other hand, if you already got the assault rifle from Wolfman, there is
no point in coming here.
        

# 3.5 The Road Home, Epilogue & Transcript

  

### 3.5.1 The Road Home

        
  This Location can only be reached via either the Radio Tower Bunker or the
Village, and represents a bridge between Chapter 2 and the Epilogue.
        
  NOTE: The game will autosave for the last time upon reaching the Road Home, so
if you want to make an archive save this is the time to do so.
        
  If you got here via the Radio Tower, light the OVEN (in case you somehow die
or the game crashes, in which case you have a save point), and loot the wardrobe
to find the NOTE ABOUT THE BUNDLE OF ROOTS. Head a long way east, past the
scarecrow and a backpack, to reach the military base, where you can loot a
corpse for the DAMAGED SHORT-WAVE RADIO and a nearby backpack for the SAMPLE
JAR. Inside the base you can find the NOTE ABOUT THE TREES. Head south to
another building, where you have to REMOVE THE BLOCKADE to pass through the
METAL DOOR, whereupon you should reach the Road itself.

  Alternatively, if you got to the Road by killing the Tree you will appear at
its west end, a short distance from the Metal Door (blocked from the other
side).
        
  Follow the Road all the way east, and look for a turnoff to the south at the
large truck and the second road sign on the ground. Follow this new path east
until you pass to the Epilogue.

### 3.5.2 Epilogue

                
  This is either the ultimate or penultimate area in the game, depending on
which Ending you are trying to achieve. There are two possible Endings:

#### 3.5.2.1 Bliss Ending

        
  Head all the way east until you arrive in the town. Keep heading east and look
for a dimly lit door at the bottom of the west-facing apartment building, with
your neighbour sat on a bench and looking at it. Go upstairs and head north to
find your doormat in front of your DOOR which you can unlock with your KEY. Go
inside and interact with the WARDROBE in the hall to TAKE OFF YOUR COAT, then
the WARDROBE in the lounge to find and collect the BEDROOM KEY. Go into the
BEDROOM and choose "Go to sleep" to end the game.

#### 3.5.2.2 Burn Them All (aka True) Ending

  This Ending is always achievable in the Epilogue, right up until you choose to
LIE DOWN in your BED, at which point it's too late. Before choosing this option
you can explore the apartment building in order to experience a possible four
strange events, with any two being enough to satisfy the requirements for the
True Ending:

  Event 1: The town repeats infinitely when heading north or south. Move in
either direction and pass the front door of the apartment building at least
three times before going inside.
  Event 2: Go downstairs immediately inside the front door of the apartment
building to find yourself in the basement. Follow the corridor all the way to
the last room, then INTERACT WITH THE RADIO until you can't any more. Return
upstairs.
  Event 3: Head along the corridor to the north immediately after entering the
apartment building's ground floor, and find the drunk man. Ignore him and
attempt to open the DOOR opposite him, then walk away when it fails to open. It
should then open behind you, and you can look inside to see a foetus on the
floor.
  Event 4: Look for the section of broken wall in the wall opposite your Front
Door. Peek through and watch as the room gets brighter, and wait for the lights
to go out.

  With any two of these Events having been experienced by the Protagonist, enter
your apartment and go into the living room. EXAMINE THE ROOTS after DRAGGING THE
SOFA aside, then go to the kitchen and get the LARGE SCREWDRIVER. GO back into
the living room and begin REMOVING THE FLOORING from there to the bedroom, then
EXAMINE THE BED. Instead of choosing to Lie Down, choose to SEARCH UNDER THE
BED. You can then DRAG THE BED aside, REMOVING MORE FLOORING, and ENTER THE
HOLE.

  Like something out of Planescape: Torment, you reawaken inside a giant Tree
and you must make your way around this area to find THE BEING. If you choose to
TALK TO THE BEING you have to PULL YOUR HAND AWAY when given the option, then
head south-west to find the sleeping MACIEK. TALK TO MACIEK to acquire the
FLAMETHROWER, then use it to KILL THE BEING (and everything else, including
yourself).

### 3.5.3 Transcript

                        
  This postscript is unique to your playthrough, detailing the consequences of
your various choices in relation to key NPCs. In likely order of encounter, here
are those NPCs and some brief hints about how your choices can affect them in
either Ending (assuming you did not actually kill any of them directly):

  Doctor - His ending is affected by whether or not the Protagonist visits the
Doctor's House, survives the Train Wreck Dream, or kills the Talking Tree.
  Wolfman - His ending is affected by whether or not the Protagonist talks to
him at all, kills the Sow, or dies to him at the Sawmill.
  Piotrek - You can choose to give Piotrek all the Vehicle Parts, all the Parts
including the rigged one given to you by the Wolfman, or just some of them
(which is the same as never meeting him).
  Chicken Lady - There are several possible ends for the Chicken Lady, depending
on which Path the Protagonist followed, whether or not the Protagonist kills the
Pretty Lady, kills the Musician's parents, or kills the Sow.
  Pretty Lady - Her ending is affected by which of the two Paths the Protagonist
chooses in Chapter 1.
  Musician - He is affected by whether the Protagonist talks to him at all,
kills his parents, kills the Chicken Lady, kils the Sow, or feeds him
hallucinogens in Chapter 2.
  The Three - They are affected by whether or not the Protagonist burns the
Talking Tree.
  Snail - The Protagonist can either free him or not (including ignoring him
completely).
  Elephants, Mushroom Granny, Marcinek - Their endings all depend on whether or
not the Protagonist returns Marcinek to the Swamp Cottage.
  Villagers - They are affected by whether or not the Protagonist kills the Sow,
undertakes Mushroom Granny's quest, rescues Marcinek, or traps them in the
Quarry with the boulder.
  Cripple, Talking Tree - Their endings depends on whether or not the
Protagonist burns the Talking Tree.
                
  There is also a hidden modifier which only turns up if the Protagonist kills
every one of these NPCs.
        
  NOTE: You can skip each transcript page by left-clicking; and you can speed up
the scrolling credits by holding down the left mouse button.
        
        
  Congratulations on escaping the Darkwood!

# 3.6 Dream Sequences

        
  The first five of these Dreams are encountered when the Protagonist spends
essence and acquires new Skills at the Oven. With the exception of the Forest
Dream, they appear in random order, although the Protagonist must reach at least
essence level 3 to see them.

  The other three Dreams are scripted story beats, and whether or not the
Protagonist encounters them depends on his decisions at certain points.
Depending on how you choose to leave the Swamp, you will either experience the
Cellar or Final Dream (although you can also experience both in the same
playthrough).
        

### 3.6.1 Forest

        
  This Dream is always the first one you have, and will occur upon reaching the
second essence level.
        
  Follow the tunnel until you find the shiny stones on the floor. Activate the
ARMORED DOOR and click through the text to open it, then go on through. (You may
recognise this FIRST ARMORED DOOR from the Underground Entrance if you have
already visited that location in the Dry Meadow). Follow more stones east to a
roundabout, then two more stones north-east, then look south-east to see a LAMP
(don't veer off course or you will be killed by the floor gore). 

  TURN ON THE LAMP and then attempt to leave the area by walking over the
bodies. Return to the LAMP when it glows brighter and TURN IT OFF. From here
follow more stones to the east and north-east, and LIE DOWN IN THE BED when you
find it to end the Dream. The game should reward you with a free shiny stone;
whereas if the floor gore kills you this does not happen. (You don't need to
collect any of the stones within the Dream for your reward.)

### 3.6.2 Apartment

        
  This confusing Dream apparently depicts a part of the Protagonist's life from
just before he arrived in the Darkwood. 

  Explore the living room. The TV will offer you a charming broadcast, and the
lights will go off after a while and there will be a knock at the FRONT DOOR.
Answer it and go outside to meet... yourself from the future.

  You can choose to walk around the future Protagonist and exit the Dream to the
south. You can also hug the wall on the right to find a wooden door hidden in
the wall: go through and push your way past several chairs and shiny stones to
find a TV which tells you you shouldn't be there!

  NOTE: If you began this Dream with a watch in your inventory the screen will
show the time - this is a bug.

### 3.6.3 Grave

        
  Follow the lit path until you find the corpse. It will talk to you in order to
distract you, but what you should be doing is first trying to interact with the
GRAVE to "open it", then looking for the SHOVEL next to the now open GRAVE.

  There are three ways this Dream can end, depending on your actions:
        
    Option 1: Equip the SHOVEL quickly and then click twice on the GRAVE to get
inside, thus avoiding the incoming BLACK CHOMPER. When you wake up you will find
an insect in your inventory - this can be used to gain essence.

    Option 2: Kill the BLACK CHOMPER for a free shovel blade when you wake up!
Definitely try for this if you are interested either in building your first
shovel or upgrading one you already have. However, this fight is so unreasonable
that I suggest you mash on the GRAVE as soon as you know you are going to lose,
so that you at least get the second prize of an insect. If you do try to win,
use the Grave to block the Chomper's rushes, and time your swings well!
        
    Option 3: Die to the BLACK CHOMPER. This gives you nothing.

### 3.6.4 Church

  Enter the Church and pass the Madman and the various parishioners waiting in
line. They will complain when you approach the TRAPDOOR at the back. Search the
CRATE to find a LOCKPICK which you can use to unlock the nearby DOOR. Search the
WARDROBE to find an AXE (equip this) and a STRANGE BOX.

  Collecting the AXE spawns a BLACK CHOMPER who rants about three features of
the Dry Meadow's Burned Houses (the White Dress you may already have found
there, the red see-saw in the yard, and the Strange Box you can find within this
Dream). 

  At this point you have three options, and which one you choose will affect
events at the Church Ruins (see Old Woods).

    Option 1: Run back to the clearing at the Church entrance to buy yourself
the space you will need. Deal with the CHOMPER by backing away from it while
raising your Axe, waiting for it to lunge at you, and then either smashing it in
the face just before it hits you or sidestepping at the last second and then
turning around and hitting it that way. Either way it's a hard fight, but with
some luck you can kill it with three or four swipes without losing too much
health, or before the Axe dies.

  When the BLACK CHOMPER falls it becomes a RED CHOMPER, which is much slower
and can be killed in one hit. When this one dies it drops a terrifying MEDALLION
with the TRAPDOOR keycode on it:
                
    6-9-4-2
                
  If you didn't have time to grab the STRANGE BOX earlier go back and get it
now, then use the KEYCODE on the TRAPDOOR to escape.

  Choosing this option is the most difficult, but also the best in the long run,
since when you visit the basement of the Church Ruins afterwards you will find
it populated only with huge dogs. You will also find a chomper corpse above
ground in the Church Ruins, and on its body is a less scary version of the same
Medallion, showing the same KEYCODE for the Church Ruins Trapdoor.

    Option 2: Smash down the WINDOW with the Axe in order to escape the BLACK
CHOMPER without having to fight it. You can then quickly loot a CORPSE for
another LOCKPICK which is used to unlock a GATE - walk through it to end the
Dream. The chomper can also smash open the GATE for you, meaning you don't have
to risk looting the Corpse while it chases you.

  This option is less attractive, as you can then expect to find red chompers in
the Church Ruins basement, albeit dormant unless you turn on the radio. Look for
the MADMAN in the Church Ruins afterwards, attempt to open the Trapdoor, then
let him give you the Keycode.

    Option 3: Die to the BLACK CHOMPER.
        
  This option yields the same result as the second one.

  Whichever option you chose, when you reawaken in your Hideout the Wolfman will
be standing next to you, and he is particularly interested in the Strange Box.
He tells you roughly where to go to find its Key (the Twisted Key in the Church
Ruins Cellar).

### 3.6.5 Creator's Office

        
  This Dream only occurs if the "Indiegogo" option was enabled when the game was
started. It contains various easter eggs from the developers which break the
fourth wall. To exit the Dream attempt to leave via the office DOOR once inside,
only to find it locked. Next find the unnamed BOOK on the table and read it:
this unlocks the DOOR, whereupon you can return to where the Dream began to
leave it.
                

### 3.6.6 Train Wreck

  This Dream only occurs once you enter the TRAIN WRECK after following the
Musician's path to meeting the Doctor. The first part involves a linear,
hallucinatory trek past many of the Doctor's failures of treatment. At one point
you must try and fail to CURE a patient with the MEDICAL BAG in order to be
allowed to pass. TALK TO THE PROTAGONIST at the end, back in the train car.

  The second part is where you must flee to the right, over a fence, through two
doors and a window, then over some floor gore, until you reach the red area at
the very end, pursued all the way by black chompers.
 

### 3.6.7 Cellar

        
  This Dream sequence is scripted to occur when you enter the Underwater
Corridor in the Swamp Village Cellar / Basement for the first time. It's
impossible to go wrong, just keep exploring the recurring rooms as events
progress, and make sure you collect the AXE from the floor of the fifth room. If
you choose to attack the BARRICADE with the AXE and continue doing so, a black
chomper jumps in and attacks. You can either kill it, escape it (by fleeing into
the tunnel; collect the torch from the backpack as you pass) or die to it. If
you escape into the tunnel (just run along it until you see the floor pattern
repeating, then turn around to make it close behind you) you will die anyway
from floor gore. No matter which option you choose there are no apparent
consequences, and you will reawaken in the Village Cellar.

### 3.6.8 Final (radio Tower Bunker)

        
  This Dream is important for finishing the game. If you survive the Dream and
make it to the exit you will be able to escape the Darkwood via the Radio Tower
Bunker by heading east upon leaving the Dream, potentially skipping a huge
amount of the Swamp; and you still also have the option of killing the Tree in
the Village anyway by turning back west and returning to the Swamp once the
Dream ends. However, if you die in the Dream you will be forced back to the
Swamp and you will have no choice but to kill the Tree and leave via the
Village. There is nothing you can do to prepare for this Dream, as your
equipment is scripted, so making an archive save before it starts is
recommended.
        
  You will fall asleep upon attacking the FUNGUS, and when you reawaken in the
Dream you will be controlling the Doctor. Head south and open the only METAL
DOOR which will open, then collect the military flashlight from the backpack on
the floor. The shiny stones will lead you roughly in the correct direction, but
you want to be heading generally south / south-west at each junction: if you
take the wrong way you will come to a dead end, and later dead ends will spawn
in enemies to attack and push you back onto the right path. Here is the correct
path to take at each junction:

    South
    South-east
    South
    South
    East
    South-west
    South

  This should find you in a wider tunnel with water all over the floor, and at
this point you want to head all the way west to find yourself in a small room.
The tunnel will close behind you when you approach the back wall, so loot the
SHOES to find the materials you need to BUILD A LOCKPICK and a bandage.

  Equip the bandage on the hotbar, then LOOT THE CONTAINER to find the SHOVEL.
Use it to BREAK DOWN THE DOOR and you will find yourself back in the Doctor's
House, except this time you have to escape as the Doctor. Move the wardrobe in
the radio room, then loot the CRATE in the next room to find the TABLE LEG.
Equip it as your main weapon as the black chompers smash their way out of the
locked room to the north; pop your bandage as they come at you, then run into
the room they just appeared from (loot Janek's body for the PLASTIC CHICK if you
skipped the Prologue) and jump through the WINDOW.

  Run south, through the treatment facility, OPEN THE METAL DOOR and then BASH
IT OPEN with the TABLE LEG (close it behind you to trap the chompers). Now just
follow the path all the way generally north-east, (retracing your steps),
looking out for the shiny stones:

    North
    North
    North-east
    North
    North-east
    North-west
    North-east

  You should come to another clearing with a woman praying in front of a giant,
monstrous MOUTH. Approach the MOUTH and then move away from it to make it speak
and open, at which point you can run through it (be quick, it can close on you
and kill you) and another metal door, beyond which is a SHOVEL on the floor. Use
the SHOVEL to DIG the RUBBLE AWAY, then run west to exit the Dream and return to
the Radio Tower Bunker to find the METAL DOOR to the east open and leading to
the Road Home, having survived all the way.
