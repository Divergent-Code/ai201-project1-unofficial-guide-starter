# Dead Space (2008) Collectibles, Upgrades, and Enemy Guide

## Part 1: Enemies and Bosses Reference

### Standard Necromorph Types

#### Slasher
* **Description**: The most common combatant aboard the Ishimura, equipped with long bone blades extending from its arms.
* **Strategy**: Aim for the legs to crawl and slow them down. Once on the ground, target their blade-arms to finish them. A female variant also exists which spits organic acid projectiles from a distance.

#### Leaper
* **Description**: Fast, crawling Necromorphs that use a powerful tail to leap across rooms and scale walls.
* **Strategy**: Aim low to hit their torso and sever their tails to eliminate their jump and blade attack capabilities.

#### Lurker
* **Description**: Infantile Necromorphs that shoot organic spikes from three tentacles on their backs. They can climb walls and navigate zero-gravity areas.
* **Strategy**: Shoot the tentacles directly when they extend to fire. Dismembering at least two tentacles will defeat them.

#### Infector
* **Description**: Bat-like Necromorphs that seek out human corpses to transform them into Slashers and Super Slashers.
* **Strategy**: Prioritize Infectors over other enemies in combat encounters to prevent them from creating more Slashers. Use Stasis to halt them before they reach corpses.

#### Pregnant
* **Description**: Bloated, slow-moving Necromorphs containing a swarm of smaller creatures.
* **Strategy**: **Do not shoot the belly.** Shooting the stomach will rupture it and release Swarmers. Target the arms and shoulders to defeat them cleanly.

#### Swarmer
* **Description**: Tiny, crawling parasites that attack in packs and latch onto Isaac to drain his health.
* **Strategy**: Use area-of-effect weapons like the Flamethrower, the alternate fire of the Contact Beam, or the Force Gun to clear them. If they latch on, rapidly tap the interact button to shake them off.

#### Divider
* **Description**: Extremely tall, lanky Necromorphs that emit loud, wailing sounds.
* **Strategy**: Aim for their long legs to force them to separate. Once broken apart, the head and limbs will detach and crawl independently. Use the Force Gun or Line Gun mines to quickly neutralize the crawling parts.

#### Guardian
* **Description**: Necromorphs fused into the wall tissue of the ship. They scream and spit out pods that spawn single tentacles.
* **Strategy**: Do not get close, or they will decapitate you instantly. Shoot their growing tentacles from a distance or throw a red explosive canister at them using Kinesis.

#### Exploder
* **Description**: Shuffling Necromorphs equipped with a large, glowing orange explosive sac on their left arm.
* **Strategy**: Shoot the glowing sac from a safe distance to trigger an explosion that damages nearby enemies. Alternatively, sever their right arm/legs to kill them without detonating the sac, or use Kinesis to catch and hurl their explosive sacs.

#### Twitcher
* **Description**: Human soldiers transformed by the Necromorph infection. Due to their military RIG stasis modules malfunctioning, they move at hyper-speed.
* **Strategy**: Immediately fire Stasis to counter their speed, then use high-damage weapons like the Plasma Cutter or Force Gun to sever their limbs.

### Bosses and Special Encounters

#### Hunter (Regenerator)
* **Description**: An experimental, regenerating Necromorph created by Dr. Mercer. Any limbs shot off will grow back in seconds.
* **Strategy**: You cannot kill the Hunter in normal combat. Shoot off his legs to force him to crawl, freeze him with Stasis, and immediately run to escape the room. In Chapter 5, trap him inside the cryo-freezing chamber to lock him away. In Chapter 10, position him directly behind the shuttle's engine exhaust nozzles, freeze him with Stasis, and test-fire the engines to incinerate him.

#### Leviathan
* **Description**: A massive organic mass poisoning the Atmosphere Control grow chambers.
* **Strategy**: 
  * **Phase 1**: Dodge tentacle sweeps and shoot the yellow glowing spots on the base of each tentacle when they slam down.
  * **Phase 2**: Shoot the exposed orange core inside its central mouth. Catch the explosive balls it spits using Kinesis and fire them back into the mouth.
  * **Phase 3**: Combine the strategies while staying on the move to avoid simultaneous attacks.

#### Slug
* **Description**: A giant creature blocking the communication array on the ship's exterior hull.
* **Strategy**: Man the dual ADS Cannons. Target the glowing yellow nodes on the Slug's five arms. Shoot the incoming metal panels and debris it hurls at you in mid-air. Alternate firing the left and right cannons to prevent overheating.

#### Hive Mind
* **Description**: The final boss of the game, located on the surface of Aegis VII.
* **Strategy**:
  * **Phase 1**: Dodge its tentacle slams and destroy the five glowing yellow sensory eyes surrounding its mouth.
  * **Phase 2**: When hung upside down, aim carefully and destroy the remaining yellow eyes.
  * **Phase 3**: Shoot the five orange pods inside its chest cavity when the chest plates open, using Stasis to delay chest movement while dodging sweeping tentacles.

## Part 2: Shop Items and Suits Reference

### Health, Air, and Ammo Items
Purchasable at any store terminal using credits:

| Item | Cost (Credits) | Description |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Small Med Pack** | 2,000 | Restores approximately one block of health. |
| **Medium Med Pack** | 5,000 | Restores approximately three blocks of health. |
| **Large Med Pack** | 10,000 | Fully restores Isaac's RIG health bar. |
| **Small Air Can** | 1,000 | Refills a small portion of RIG oxygen supply in vacuum. |
| **Medium Air Can** | 2,000 | Refills a large portion of RIG oxygen supply. |
| **Stasis Pack** | 5,000 | Fully refills Isaac's Stasis module gauge. |
| **Power Node** | 10,000 | Used to bypass locked security doors or upgrade gear at the Bench. |

### RIG Suits
Upgrading suits increases inventory slot capacity and armor damage reduction:

* **Level 1 Suit**: Default starting suit. (Inventory: 10 slots, Armor: 0%)
* **Level 2 Suit**: Schematic automatically available at start of Chapter 2. (Cost: 10,000 credits, Inventory: 13 slots, Armor: 5%)
* **Level 3 Suit**: Schematic found in Chapter 4 (Captain's Nest). (Cost: 20,000 credits, Inventory: 18 slots, Armor: 10%)
* **Level 4 Suit**: Schematic found in Chapter 7 (Maintenance Annex). (Cost: 35,000 credits, Inventory: 22 slots, Armor: 15%)
* **Level 5 Suit**: Schematic found in Chapter 10 (Rec Hallway). (Cost: 60,000 credits, Inventory: 30 slots, Armor: 20%)
* **Level 6 Suit**: Available only in New Game+. (Cost: 99,000 credits, Inventory: 30 slots, Armor: 30%, military design)

### Weapons Directory

#### Plasma Cutter
* **Cost**: 0 Credits (Obtained in Chapter 1 Workshop)
* **Ammo**: Plasma Energy (Cost: 1,200 credits for 6 pack)
* **Primary Fire**: Vertical or horizontal energy slice.
* **Secondary Fire**: Rotates the slicing grid 90 degrees.
* **RAG Note**: The primary weapon for dismemberment, easy to upgrade and highly effective throughout the entire campaign.

#### Line Gun
* **Cost**: 9,000 Credits (Available in Chapter 1 Store)
* **Ammo**: Line Racks (Cost: 2,000 credits for 2 pack)
* **Primary Fire**: Fires a very wide, horizontal energy beam.
* **Secondary Fire**: Deploys a timed, glowing laser mine that detonates in a localized radius.
* **RAG Note**: Perfect for cutting the legs of multiple Slashers at once.

#### Pulse Rifle
* **Cost**: 7,000 Credits (Available in Chapter 1 Store)
* **Ammo**: Pulse Rounds (Cost: 1,250 credits for 25 pack)
* **Primary Fire**: High-speed military assault rifle fire.
* **Secondary Fire**: Isaac squats and sprays rounds in a 360-degree radius.
* **RAG Note**: Weak against standard limbs but useful against small Swarmers or target sacs.

#### Flamethrower
* **Cost**: 11,000 Credits (Available in Chapter 2 Store)
* **Ammo**: Flame Fuel (Cost: 1,000 credits for 25 pack)
* **Primary Fire**: Projects a stream of fire that deals damage over time.
* **Secondary Fire**: Launches a compact fireball projectile that detonates.
* **RAG Note**: Highly effective against Swarmers, but completely unusable in vacuum zones.

#### Ripper
* **Cost**: 8,000 Credits (Available in Chapter 3 Store)
* **Ammo**: Ripper Blades (Cost: 1,800 credits for 4 pack)
* **Primary Fire**: Projects and spins a saw blade directly in front of Isaac.
* **Secondary Fire**: Launches a single saw blade projectile straight ahead.
* **RAG Note**: Highly ammo-efficient; one spinning blade can easily kill a Slasher.

#### Contact Beam
* **Cost**: 9,000 Credits (Available in Chapter 4 Store)
* **Ammo**: Contact Energy (Cost: 2,000 credits for 1 pack)
* **Primary Fire**: Charges up and releases a massive kinetic energy bolt.
* **Secondary Fire**: Slams the weapon on the floor to release a kinetic shockwave.
* **RAG Note**: Deals the highest single-shot damage in the game. Extremely valuable to sell ammo drops for credits.

#### Force Gun
* **Cost**: 11,000 Credits (Available in Chapter 4 Store)
* **Ammo**: Force Energy (Cost: 900 credits for 3 pack)
* **Primary Fire**: Releases a short-range blast of gravity force that blows back enemies.
* **Secondary Fire**: Launches a gravity grenade that pulls enemies in before exploding.
* **RAG Note**: Excellent defensive weapon to keep enemies at bay.

## Part 4: Bench Upgrade Trees

Upgrades are applied at the Bench using Power Nodes. The layout consists of connected nodes (labeled with abbreviations and blank connect-nodes `O` which must be filled to advance).

### RIG (Health and Air)
* **Node Types**:
  * **HP**: Health (increases max RIG health meter)
  * **A**: Air (increases maximum oxygen duration in vacuum zones)
* **Strategy**: Skip the early Air nodes unless playing on Hard difficulty; prioritize pathing directly to HP nodes to boost maximum survival health.

### Stasis Module
* **Node Types**:
  * **D**: Duration (Stasis field stays active on enemies longer)
  * **E**: Energy (allows more stasis shots before needing refill)
* **Strategy**: Upgrade Duration early to keep the Hunter/Twitchers frozen for long periods.

### Kinesis Module
* **Path**: Consists of 3 nodes in a single line.
* **Benefit**: Increases grab-and-throw range.

### Weapon Upgrade Keys

| Code | Upgrade Effect | Associated Weapons |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **D** | Damage (Increases shot power) | All Weapons |
| **C** | Capacity (Increases magazine size) | All Weapons |
| **R** | Reload Speed (Reduces reload animation time) | All Weapons |
| **S** | Speed (Increases firing speed) | Plasma Cutter, Force Gun |
| **W** | Width (Increases width of energy slash) | Line Gun |
| **U** | Duration (Increases active damage duration) | Flamethrower, Ripper |
| **H** | Charge Time (Reduces time required to fire) | Contact Beam |

## Part 5: Controller Cheat Codes

Cheats must be entered in the pause menu. They will only work once per playthrough, except for Stasis/Oxygen refills which can be used indefinitely.

### PlayStation 3 Button Codes

* **+2 Power Nodes**: Triangle, Square (3 times), Triangle
* **+5 Power Nodes**: Triangle, Square, Triangle, Square (2 times), Triangle, Square (2 times), Triangle, Square (2 times), Triangle
* **1,000 Credits**: Square (3 times), Triangle, Square
* **2,000 Credits**: Square (3 times), Triangle (2 times)
* **5,000 Credits**: Square (3 times), Triangle, Square, Triangle
* **10,000 Credits**: Square, Triangle (3 times), Square (2 times), Triangle
* **Refill Oxygen (Air)**: Square (2 times), Triangle (3 times)
* **Refill Stasis & Kinesis**: Triangle, Square, Triangle (2 times), Square, Triangle
