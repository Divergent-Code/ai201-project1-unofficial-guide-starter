## 1. Walkthrough overview

Hello and welcome to the TrueAchievements walkthrough for SOMA!

SOMA is a sci-fi, first person horror game from Frictional Games, the followup to their critically acclaimed Amnesia: The Dark Descent. At its core, it’s quite a linear game with not much room to get lost, but some puzzles may cause problems, which is where this guide comes in.

The achievement list is thankfully simple, with just ten story achievements to earn through the entire length of the game. They are all unmissable except one, which you could miss if you do a specific thing (which I’m pretty sure most won’t do). If you’re extra paranoid however, here’s the least spoilery message I can give to not miss it -

*** Spoiler - click to reveal ***Towards the end of the game, you will come across someone hooked up to a machine. The machine will have a screen which states ‘Life Support: Active’ with a button to disable it. **Do not do this** before speaking with them.


There are many collectables in the game but they are not required for any achievements. You’ll occasionally have a choice relating to the story which I don’t mention, these choices don’t affect the playthrough as you will always visit areas in the same order.

This guide will keep spoilers to an absolute minimum, focusing only on the essential aspects of how to solve the puzzles and where to go to progress through the game. I’ll include images where necessary to point out where certain things are. Don’t feel you have to follow the guide to the letter! If you see something you can interact with, go for it. If you see a room I don’t mention, head inside and poke around. You’ll miss most of the game otherwise.

If you’ve used the Layers of Fear Walkthrough here on TA, you’ll be familiar with the jumpscare warnings. I thought they were a good idea to include for those less keen on horror games so I’ve revamped them for this walkthrough. Instead of them appearing in the text and acting as spoilers, I’ll include them right at the top of each walkthrough page in a spoiler box to click and read if you wish.

The pages will be separated by the named story chapters as stated in the ‘Load Game’ menu. Some of the shorter, more story-driven chapters have been grouped with the previous chapter to save space. Bear in mind that SOMA does not feature a chapter select. However, it is possible to head back roughly up to five chapters through the ‘Load Game’ checkpoints in the main menu. The Save and Quit option will ensure you return to the exact same place when continuing the game, so don’t worry about losing any progress.

This is the inevitable part of the walkthrough overview where I, the owner, try to persuade you to complete the game without the aid of this walkthrough, though I’m sure you’ve most likely already made up your mind. If not however, know it’s an exceptional game with a great story, and it’s best experienced when you’re exploring areas and piecing together that story, not rushing through with a guide. If you’re not keen on horror games and want to spend the least amount of time with this game though, you’re probably best off following this walkthrough.

On your own, you can expect to comfortably finish the game within six to eight hours. Following the guide however, don’t be surprised if you’re done in under four.

## 2. General hints and tips

When you start a new game, you will have the option to choose between Normal mode or Safe mode. Bear in mind you cannot switch to a different mode during your playthrough.

**Normal mode** is the standard mode of the game. Monsters are present and can kill you, meaning you will be forced to run and hide in certain parts of the game.

**Safe mode** was added with the Xbox One version of the game. Monsters are still present but will not chase you, though they will still attack if you get too close. They can also kill you in the rare chance they trap you in a corner, so be wary of that. For the most part, they won’t be a bother.

Don’t write off Safe mode, especially if you prefer story over stealth. Here’s an article on the mode if you’re interested in learning more, especially since in it the lead developer of SOMA near admits having more docile monsters suits the game's narrative better: https://www.pcgamer.com/uk/frictional-on-designing-somas-new...

For the purposes of this walkthrough, it is assumed you are playing on Safe mode, so no references of where to hide from monsters will be made. Other than that, the guide is still fully followable on Normal.

If you choose to play through the game on Normal mode, know that stealth will be paramount in several sections of the game. Monsters freely roam around certain areas, so keep listening to make sure they’re not anywhere close. If they’re approaching, you’ll know from the visual distortion the screen. To avoid them, you’ll need to hide in the darkness, crouch and turn off your flashlight, and for some monsters, you’ll have to look away. Remember that the noises you make, such as loud footsteps and especially opening doors, will alert them to your position.

## 3. Simon's Apartment & Subway

Start off by selecting a New Game and then choosing your game mode, Safe or Normal. You can read about these two on the previous page if you skipped it. Remember, you can’t change the game mode once you’ve started!

Once you’re in control, pick up the ringing phone on the side of the bed and answer it.

You’ll be tasked with looking for an item. This area acts as a tutorial on how the open and close mechanic works, by holding when a prompt appears followed by moving in the desired direction.

The item you’re looking for has various locations it can spawn. On my first playthrough it was in a kitchen cupboard, on my second it was in the bathroom, and on the third playthrough it was in the top computer desk drawer. It may be set up to spawn in the last place you look. Anyhow, you’ll know when you’ve found it as the player character will remark on it. It’s an orange bottle as can be seen here.



Once you’ve got it, press to grab it and again to drink. From here, exit out of the front door near the kitchen.

From here will be a very short section on the subway, the only option being whether to answer or decline an incoming call. Once this section is done, you’ll spawn in the next area.

## 5. Awakening

In this new area, leave your chair and head over to the red light in front of you. Interact with the lever by pulling it down, this will turn the lights on for you. To leave this room, you’ll need to pick up a heavy object (such as one of the chairs) and throw it at the broken window twice by pressing . Jump out of the broken window with .

Head down the corridor and interact with the blue lock on the door in front of you, by turning the lever.



Once it’s off the button next to the door will turn from red to yellow, interact with the button to turn it to green and the door will unlock. Head through into the next room and interact with the robot on the left.



Head back outside and down the new opening on the right. You’ll come across another door with a blue lock. After the commotion from inside dies down, open it up and look to the right to find an ‘Omnitool’ on the desk.



Pick it up and head back to the room you started the chapter in. On the left will be a service console to plug your Omnitool into. Press to plug it in.



On the screen, select Omnitool > Unlock Tool Box. A hatch will open up on the left of the service console, pick up one of the chips and plug it into the Omnitool. Back on the screen, select Manage Tool Chip > Update, and then pick the Omnitool back up. This will upgrade your security clearance, allowing you to open the door to the exit.

From here, exit out of the room and head left, going along the passage and taking two left turns until you see a circular door on your right.



Head up to it and ‘swipe’ your Omnitool on the panel on the right to open it up. From here, head down the path until you come to a room with a ‘WAU device’, the health-giving...things that were mentioned before.



Interact with it and then press the yellow button to the left, opening up a small tunnel you need to crawl through. At the fork, head left and then right, continuing on through the door marked ‘Station Control’. After leaving the tunnel, you may have to press to un-crouch.

There are most likely multiple solutions, but here’s the one I did (with one square left unfilled):



From the terminal, head into the Cleanroom behind you. In here you will find the Structure Gel.



Activate the button on the panel, leading to an error which opens up a hatch below. Grab the chip from inside and head into the adjoining room through the glass doors. You’ll find three containment units in here.



On the middle one, press the red button to switch the light to green, and then pull out the tray. Put the chip inside, close the tray back up and press the button again to turn the light back to red, locking the tray.

Head to the lever behind you to line up the red laser over your chip. The controls are quite drifty, pretty reminiscent of doing a manual in Tony Hawk Pro Skater, so do your best to get the laser over your chip before straightening up the lever. Once you’re done, hit the red button to dispense the gel.



Open the tray back up to grab the chip and slot it back into the panel, using it to open up the machine and grab the Structure Gel.

Head back out of the Cleanroom, turning right into South Hall F1 and right again to head up the South Stairwell. Head down the corridor and into Containment F2. Access the terminal in front of you, selecting Upper and then Override. No puzzles to do this time, the doors will unlock immediately. From here, you’ll want to head to the top right of the Power Room, through the door on the right.



The thing you’re after is in the yellow box, right next to the monster. You’ll want to head left and walk around them, opening up the box and grabbing the Battery Pack inside.



Exit out and back down both stairwells to the bottom floor of Omnicron, where you first entered. Head into Containment FB, Accessing the terminal, selecting Lower and then Override. You’ll then want to head to the Workshop, or as it’s labelled on the door, the Robot Repair Bay.



In here will be a simple puzzle where you have to direct beams of light to eject the robot’s Cortex Chip. This is accomplished by directing each of the beams of light downwards. Set up the grid as shown below, before selecting the beams on the left to activate them.



Grab the Cortex Chip from the robot and head back to the Dive Room. Avoid the monster in the Medical Bay and leg it down the tunnel, the door of the Dive Room will shut automatically behind you once you’re in.

Go to the diving suit and add all three items, before heading to the terminal and selecting Retry. Head into the Remote Control room on the left, and on the terminal, select a Frequency of 1, and Offset of 5 and an Amplitude of 7.



Select Lock Parameters and sit in the seat. Grab the Omnitool, choose what you think is best and head out through the large circular door.

## 18. Path to Tau

Leave the cage by picking up and swiping the Omnitool **once** on the door. Head down the steps and along the path to the left. In this starting area is a good map of the path you’ll be taking for the rest of the game. Of course, there will be detours along the way.



At the bottom of the path will be a lever with a flashing green light. Flip this to activate the lights further along the path.



Follow the lights up to the Observatory, where they will come to a stop.



At the back of the Observatory will be a green light with a cable next to it. Follow this cable along to a new set of lights in the distance.



Use the lights as a path to the next destination, the TVS-c building.



Head through the door and flip the power lever at the back.



Head back out of the building and keep left to reach the garage terminal.



Select Control > TAU, exit out and follow the robot closely along the path. If you miss the robot, you can select Recall on the same terminal screen.

When you get to a new set of lights to the right, follow them into a cave. Follow the green flares through it, keeping to the left, and out to the other side. Head to and follow the green lights, avoiding the white one. Soon you’ll reach Tau, press the button on the left and head on through.
