# The Evil Within (2014) Collectibles and Upgrade Guide

## Part 1: Overview

### Collectible Types
The Evil Within has six categories of collectibles, plus Locker Keys:

| Type | Total | Archive Section | Notes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Personal Documents** | 16 | Archives → Personal Documents | Sebastian's personal journal entries and case notes |
| **Documents** | 16 | Archives → Documents | Notes and records found in the environment |
| **Map Fragments** | 28 | Archives → Map Fragments | Pieces of a map that assembles as fragments are found |
| **Newspapers** | 21 | Archives → Newspapers | Newspaper clippings from the real world |
| **Missing Person Posters** | 15 | Archives → Missing Persons | Flyers for people connected to the Beacon Hospital case |
| **Audio Tapes** | 10 | Archives → Audio Tapes | Voice recordings from Ruvik, hospital staff, and others |
| **Locker Keys** | 45 | N/A (tracked by count) | Hidden inside white porcelain statues — open lockers in the Safe Room |

**Total for "Every Nook and Cranny" Achievement**: 151 collectibles (all 6 text/audio types + all 45 Locker Keys).

---

### Key Mechanics
- **White Statues**: Small white porcelain figures hidden in each chapter. Smash them by kicking (approach and press X/A) or shooting to find a Locker Key inside. Some statues are in plain sight; others are behind objects or in hidden rooms.
- **Locker Room**: Located in the Safe Room nurse's station. Lockers are numbered 1–47. Two keys are found inside lockers while opening them (not in statues), so 45 statues yield 45 keys, and 2 additional keys are found as locker rewards. All 47 lockers can be opened.
- **Archives Menu**: Accessed by pausing the game and selecting "Archive." Shows total collectibles found per chapter and per type. Use this to track what you are missing.
- **Chapter Select**: Available from the main menu after completing any chapter. Collectible progress is retained across chapter replays. Re-entering a chapter resets enemy/item spawns but previously found collectibles remain counted.
- **New Game+**: Carries over all upgrades and collectible counts. Ideal for completing any missed collectibles on a second playthrough.

## Part 4: Upgrade System Reference

### The Safe Room — Upgrade Chair
The Upgrade Chair is located in the center of the Safe Room (Beacon Mental Hospital basement). Interact with it to open the upgrade menu. Upgrades are purchased with **Green Gel** — the primary currency found throughout the game.

Green Gel Sources:
- Dropped by defeated enemies (standard Haunted drop small amounts; bosses and armored variants drop large amounts)
- Found in wooden crates (kick to break open)
- Found on shelves, tables, and ledges throughout each chapter
- Awarded from opened lockers in the Safe Room

---

### Sebastian — Personal Upgrades

| Upgrade | Max Level | Gel Cost (Total) | Effect |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Health** | Level 5 | ~4,500 gel total | Increases Sebastian's maximum health pool |
| **Stamina** | Level 5 | ~3,500 gel total | Increases sprint duration before exhaustion |
| **Stealth Kill Range** | Level 3 | ~1,200 gel total | Increases effective range for stealth kill prompts |
| **Recovery Speed** | Level 3 | ~1,500 gel total | Reduces time to recover from enemy grabs and knockdowns |
| **Flashlight Duration** | Level 3 | ~800 gel total | Extends how long the flashlight stays charged |
| **Bottle Throw** | Level 2 | ~600 gel total | Widens bottle throw arc; increases distraction radius |

**Recommended Priority Order** (early game):
1. Health (Level 2) — survival first
2. Stamina (Level 2) — essential for escape sequences
3. Stealth Kill Range (Level 2) — significantly broadens stealth kill window
4. Health (Level 3)
5. Recovery Speed (Level 2)

---

### Akumu Difficulty Tips
* Akumu is the hardest difficulty — nearly all damage sources kill Sebastian in one hit.
* Stealth kills and avoidance are mandatory. Do not engage enemies in open combat unless absolutely forced.
* Explosive Bolts and environmental kills (barrels, traps) are the only safe way to handle larger enemy groups.
* Save frequently using the Safe Room. The game has limited autosave on Akumu.
* Crossbow Freeze + Snare Trap combinations are the safest method for armored enemies and boss encounters.
* The Keeper (Chapter 7) is significantly more dangerous. Use all disarmed spike traps and snares before engaging.
